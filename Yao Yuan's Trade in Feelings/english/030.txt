Alcohol Numbs the Brain but Releases Dopamine to Please the Body
The game was simple, charged with a thrill of risk and the urge to pry into each other's lives, and it got going quickly amid the cheering. What was surprising, yet somehow felt inevitable, was that tonight the "King" seemed to favor Yao Yuan in particular. In the very first round, the card her slender fingers turned over was a black card with gold lettering: the "King."
"Whoa!" the others jeered.
Gao Jieyuan, smiling, pushed a stack of command cards with intricate patterns on their backs toward her. "Boss Yao, please. Let's see what fate hands you as your first command."
A flicker passed through Yao Yuan's eyes. She drew one at random and flipped it over. The card read, plainly: Hug the person to your left.
To her left sat Wang Qian. He blinked, then split into a grin and threw his arms wide. "Yuanyuan, the card says so. Come here, let Brother Qian give you a warm hug!"
Whistles and laughter filled the room. Yao Yuan's expression didn't change. She only arched an eyebrow lightly, then leaned over with easy grace and gave Wang Qian a loose, perfunctory hug, breaking apart the instant they touched.
It was just a hug, nothing more than a trivial bit of social etiquette. Refusing would have looked petty, given people something to gossip about, and made Gao Jieyuan think he had an opening.
"There," she said, sitting back up, calm as if she'd just finished a handshake, not a ripple in her heart. To her, this kind of physical contact was merely one small step in the social game—about measure, not emotion.
But the King's "favor" wasn't over. In the next two rounds, as if by some unseen hand, the King card landed in Yao Yuan's grasp again and again. The second time, the command she drew was: Down a drink yourself. That one was easy. Without a word, she picked up the gin and tonic in front of her—still more than half full—and, under everyone's gaze, tilted her head back and drained it. The cold, sharp liquid scraped down her throat; she frowned almost imperceptibly, then composed herself. She knew this drink was for show—proof that she was a good sport, that she didn't sulk, that she could blend in better. It was also a reminder to her own clear head: she could drink, but her measure couldn't slip, and her presence couldn't lose ground.
The third time, when Yao Yuan's fingers flipped over that card symbolizing "power" for a third time, even Jin Lan couldn't help clicking her tongue. "Yuanyuan, with luck like that, you'd better hurry to the lottery shop and buy a ticket!"
The interest in Gao Jieyuan's eyes grew thicker. He pushed the command cards toward Yao Yuan once more, his voice cutting clear through the noisy music. "Third time's the charm, Boss Yao. What'll it be this round?"
A faint premonition stirred in her—luck this good was too deliberate. Perhaps Gao Jieyuan had rigged it? But she didn't call it out. Her fingertips paused, then drew a card and turned it over.
The words on the card made the air in the room freeze for an instant, then erupt into even louder jeering—
Kiss one member of the opposite sex present.
"Whoa—!!!"
"Going big, huh?!"
"Boss Yao, pick one! Pick one!"
The command card was passed around to be read and confirmed. Wang Qian let out a piercing whistle, and Jin Lan covered her mouth and laughed, her eyes roaming over Yao Yuan and the several men in the room. Gao Jieyuan leaned back into the sofa, unhurried, arms spread along the backrest, his posture relaxed but his gaze locked tight on Yao Yuan, that spectator's smile at the corner of his mouth. The certainty in his eyes was as if he'd already decided she would choose him. Even Chen Ye, who had shown little expression all night, raised his eyes and looked quietly through his glasses at Yao Yuan, who held the command card and fell into a brief silence.
Three penalty drinks, neat. The alternative was laid out plainly. But Yao Yuan—caught one round after another and now pinned at the center of everyone's attention—only lowered her eyes to the card, her cheeks flushed, whether from the alcohol or from this sudden, suggestive command. The hazy light slid across the side of her face, grazing her lightly pressed lips, yet her mind was unusually clear. On the first draw, she would choose no one. A kiss was an intimate, ambiguous act—even in a game, she wouldn't yield so easily, and she certainly wouldn't let herself fall into a passive position. If Gao Jieyuan thought he could use the game to corner her, that was far too naive. She would rather take the penalty drinks than let her rhythm be disrupted, and she would give no one an opening to misread her.
She didn't touch the three glasses of liquor already poured. She only lifted her eyes and swept a calm gaze across the room—past the smiling, ambiguous Gao Jieyuan, with a cold laugh inside: his little scheme had backfired; past Wang Qian, enjoying the show, and her old friends watching the fun; past Jin Lan, who looked faintly concerned, and a touch of warmth rose in her—the one person in this whole gathering who truly had her interests at heart; and past Chen Ye, whose expression was hard to read. Beneath that calm, what exactly was he thinking?
She was choosing.
The wave of jeering in the room peaked during Yao Yuan's few seconds of silence, then ebbed as her composed gaze swept across them, settling into a low buzz of expectant whispers. Every eye was fixed on her, waiting for her choice—would she follow this teasing, ambiguous command, or take on the punishment of three neat drinks?
Yao Yuan's fingertips brushed once along the edge of that gilded command card, and then, under everyone's held breath, she set it gently back in the center of the tea table. Without looking at anyone, without a word more, she simply turned slightly, reached out, and picked up the three glasses of liquor someone had already filled in front of her. The liquid inside was clear, refracting a cold glint under the trippy lights—obviously high-proof at a glance.
"Yuanyuan!" Jin Lan murmured, trying to stop her. Yao Yuan shook her head lightly and curved her lips into the faintest, almost soothing arc—she knew Jin Lan worried she was drinking too fast, that she'd get drunk easily. But she'd decided to do it; this was her way of controlling the room, and she would never compromise her principles for any person or any game.
She tipped her head back, her throat moving, and drained the first glass in one go. The liquid rolled down her throat, burning and stinging. Her cheeks reddened visibly, the sheen in her eyes thickening, the alcohol's heat spreading instantly to every limb—but her reason stayed clear. She paused for less than a second, giving herself little time to recover, and picked up the second glass, her movements still steady. Only as she closed her eyes and drank did her long lashes tremble faintly. Setting down the empty glass, she drew a soft breath. The stubbornness in her hardened further—this much wine couldn't trap her, this small scene couldn't faze her.
"I'll take one for her." A crisp female voice rang out—it was Liu Qingzhuo. At some point she'd already picked up the third glass, her eyes bright, carrying the brisk frankness of a woman of the world. The way she looked at Yao Yuan held the understanding and loyalty of a fellow player in the game. Without waiting for Yao Yuan's reply, she too tipped her head back and downed her glass. She drank fast and choked a little, but her manner was clean and decisive.
Yao Yuan looked at her, real warmth flickering in her eyes—Liu Qingzhuo's show of loyalty came out of nowhere, yet it was especially touching. That clarity and frankness loosened the taut strings in her chest just a little.
Seeing this, Jin Lan immediately picked up her own glass too. "Then I'll take one as well! This wretched card keeps targeting our Yuanyuan!" And she took a big gulp. Yao Yuan didn't refuse the kindness; she knew this bond was genuine. But once they had drunk, she picked up the last full glass on the table—her third penalty drink. She raised it to Jin Lan and Liu Qingzhuo, and to the whole room, then steadily drained the last mouthful of the burning liquid. She couldn't let others bear her punishment for her—this was her pride and her principle. Having chosen the penalty, she would carry it through to the end herself. Only that way could she hold her presence and her measure in front of everyone.
Three glasses of liquor down, and even with Yao Yuan's decent tolerance, she now felt a strong wave of heat surge from her stomach straight to the top of her head. The music in her ears seemed to drift further away, and the light and shadow before her wavered and blurred. She set the glass down lightly; its base met the glass tea table with a soft "tap." She lifted a hand and pressed the back of it gently against her burning cheek, saying nothing, simply settling quietly back into the sofa. Her spine stayed straight, but the air about her brows and eyes—steamed up by the alcohol and her recent choice, a mix of restraint and faint allure—grew only more pronounced.
Her mind was still clear; only her senses had been amplified by the alcohol, and the gazes fixed on her grew sharper—admiration, tenderness, frustration, curiosity. She enjoyed the feeling of being the center of attention, but that enjoyment always came with a clear boundary; she never sank into it.
The room went quiet for a moment. Gao Jieyuan arched an eyebrow, his smile thinning, his eyes complicated—perhaps with a trace of defeated disappointment that finally dissolved into a soft laugh of unclear meaning. He began to clap. "Boss Yao, what nerve." Yao Yuan nodded faintly, saying nothing more. She knew that after this round, Gao Jieyuan's interest in her would only deepen—but she was also more certain that she could keep a firm grip on this distance.
Wang Qian put away his teasing and gave a thumbs-up. Chen Ye's gaze fell on the faint rise and fall of Yao Yuan's chest and her flushed cheeks, lingered a moment, then moved calmly away as he refilled himself half a glass of ice water.
The game went on. Maybe it was a case of fortune turning after misfortune, but Yao Yuan didn't draw the King card again after that. The new "King" fell to Liu Qingzhuo. The command card she drew was, sure enough, once more: Kiss one member of the opposite sex present.
"Whoa! Are we stuck on kissing tonight?" Wang Qian yelped.
Liu Qingzhuo held the card, a radiant smile on her bright face, her gaze sweeping openly over the men present—Gao Jieyuan's smile was playful, Wang Qian wore a clowning "don't pick me" face, and Chen Ye sat in a single armchair a little further off, seemingly detached from the commotion on this side, his head bowed over his phone screen, his profile especially refined and handsome in the dim light.
"I choose…" Liu Qingzhuo drew out the syllables, her finger pointing vaguely in the air, then settled, steadily, on the corner. "Chen Ye, Mr. Chen."
"I choose… Chen Ye, Mr. Chen." As Liu Qingzhuo's voice fell, Yao Yuan arched an eyebrow slightly, understanding in her heart—Liu Qingzhuo was perceptive after all. Gao Jieyuan was flashy, Wang Qian was flighty, Chen Ye was reserved. Choosing Chen Ye was the safest option: it wouldn't drag her into Gao Jieyuan's entanglements, nor would Wang Qian's antics spoil the mood. Chen Ye's gentle reserve meant he'd most likely play along without creating any extra ambiguity.
Chen Ye, in the corner, seemed to freeze for a beat. He looked up, and a clear flash of surprise crossed the eyes behind his glasses, as if he hadn't expected to be named at all. He glanced at Liu Qingzhuo, then instinctively looked toward Yao Yuan—who was reclining against the sofa, propping her temple on one hand, with the drowsy languor of someone tipsy, paying no particular attention to this side. Yao Yuan caught his gaze and smiled faintly inside. That reflex of Chen Ye's was rather interesting; it seemed he had a barely perceptible attentiveness toward her. But it was just attentiveness, nothing romantic—perhaps merely simple curiosity.
"Qingzhuo, this…" Jin Lan tried to smooth things over.
But Liu Qingzhuo had already risen with a smile and walked over to Chen Ye with easy grace.
Under everyone's eyes and jeers, Chen Ye had no choice but to set down his phone and stand, his expression resigned but still composed. Liu Qingzhuo came up to him, her height reaching only to his chin. She rose slightly onto her toes and pressed a quick, light kiss to his cheek, breaking away the instant they touched. "There!" Liu Qingzhuo retreated to her seat, her smile brilliant, as if she'd merely completed an ordinary social courtesy. Chen Ye lifted a hand a little awkwardly and touched his knuckle to the spot that had just been kissed, smiled at everyone, and sat back down—only the tips of his ears tinged with a faint, barely noticeable red. Watching this, a touch of warmth rose in Yao Yuan's heart. This was how the game should be—light, decent, no crossing of lines, no entanglement.
This little interlude was soon overshadowed by the next round. This time, the one who drew the King card was Wang Qian.
"Hahaha! The wheel turns at last—finally my turn!" Wang Qian waved the King card, that symbol of "power," in excitement, his slightly tipsy face flushed red; the alcohol and the game had him completely loosened up. Without even looking, he drew a card from the pile, flipped it over for a glance, and cracked up first himself, slapping his thigh and showing the card to everyone. "Hahaha! This is good! This is good! I need to let loose right now!"
The card read, plain as day: Go out the door to the corridor and shout three times—I'm an idiot!
"Whoa! Brother Qian, what luck!" "Go on then, Brother Qian, we're all waiting to hear it!" The crowd jeered at once, dissolving into laughter. Jin Lan pointed at Wang Qian, laughing too hard to speak; Liu Qingzhuo covered her lips and giggled; Gao Jieyuan whistled; even Yao Yuan curved her lips at the ridiculous command, the laughter spreading wide across her alcohol-misted eyes. Chen Ye pushed up his glasses, revealing a faint, helpless, amused smile.
"Fine, I'll go! A bet's a bet—I can take it!" Wang Qian loved a lively scene to begin with and felt no embarrassment at all, only amusement. He yanked open the heavy soundproof door and strode out, valiantly, even leaving the door cracked on purpose so the laughter inside could "see him off." Watching his retreating back, Yao Yuan laughed softly to herself. Wang Qian's flighty, easygoing slickness added a touch of lightness to this slightly tense gathering. A man like that could cover all the bases in any setting—the easiest sort of company.
The corridor was laid with thick, sound-absorbing carpet, the laser-lit walls flowing with quiet, dreamlike light, that lavish cold fragrance drifting silently through it. Wang Qian walked to the middle of the corridor, a few steps from the door where it opened a little wider, planted his hands on his hips, cleared his throat, and—without the slightest hesitation—threw open his voice and shouted into the grand, empty hallway:
"I'm an idiot—!"
Full-throated and laced with laughter, his voice rang especially clear in the superb soundproofing, echoing back with a hint of loneliness.
"I'm an idiot—!"
The second shout he delivered with even more flourish, as if performing, adding some exaggerated gestures of his own, mugging at the empty air.
And just as he drew a deep breath, ready to bellow the third—the most "rousing"—shout, a group of people happened to round the corner of the corridor. The man leading them was tall and upright, dressed in a dark, finely tailored casual suit, no tie, his shirt collar left carelessly open, a coat draped over one arm. He was turned toward a friend beside him, speaking, that habitual, unhurried smile at the corner of his mouth.
It was Yu Hao.
The moment she saw Yu Hao's figure, Yao Yuan's heart gave a slight jolt. She and Yu Hao were compatible "buddies," reliable business partners. When they got together, they'd agreed—not to interfere with each other's feelings, not to meddle in each other's social lives. Yet deep down, she knew that at some point Yu Hao had become more than just a "buddy" to her; beneath his composure lay a barely perceptible possessiveness. His appearance might break the calm, controlled situation she held right now.
Wang Qian's "I'm an—" had already burst from his throat, but the words "idiot" jammed hard the instant Yu Hao turned at the sound and his gaze landed precisely on him, twisting into a strange, choked-off little noise.
Their eyes met. A clear flash of surprise crossed Yu Hao's face, and then that surprise quickly turned to comprehension, and from there into an undisguised, almost overflowing mischief and amusement. He arched an eyebrow, looking at Wang Qian, who was still frozen mid-shout in the middle of the corridor, the curve of his mouth stretching wider and wider.
"Pfft…" One of the friends behind Yu Hao couldn't hold back and laughed out loud.
Wang Qian's face went red, but he was a man used to such scenes; the awkwardness lasted only a tenth of a second. He swiftly dropped his hands from his hips, gave a dry cough, and forced out the third command in a slightly rushed but still "booming" tone: "…idiot!" Then he instantly changed his face, piling on a friendly grin and striding toward Yu Hao. "Aiyo! Boss Yu! How is it you?! Now this is just… what a damn coincidence, the coincidence of coincidences!"
Yu Hao finally laughed out loud, reached out, and gave Wang Qian's shoulder a light punch with his fist. "I was wondering why the corridor was so lively—turns out it's you out here doing… performance art?" He deliberately drew out his words, his gaze sweeping mockingly over Wang Qian. "What, President Wang's into this lately? A new way to relieve stress?"
"Get lost!" Wang Qian chuckled, slinging an arm over Yu Hao's shoulder and lowering his voice—though still loud enough for those nearby to hear. "Lost a round of the King game, so it's a penalty! A bet's a bet, your boy's just being honest! How come you're here, Boss Yu? Out with friends too?"
"Mm, brought a couple of clients over to sit a while; just saw them off." Yu Hao explained with a smile, his gaze drifting, seemingly by chance, over the "999" room door behind Wang Qian, cracked open and leaking faint music and laughter. "Your room? Sounds pretty lively—who's in there?"
"Just our usual crowd! Jin Lan, Jieyuan and the rest—and Yao Yuan's here too!" Wang Qian blurted it out and warmly extended the invitation. "Boss Yu, running into each other is fate. You done over there? Why not come in and play a while? More people, more fun! Yao Yuan's here too!" He pointedly mentioned Yao Yuan's name a second time, with a hint of unspoken understanding between men. Sitting inside the room, Yao Yuan heard Wang Qian's words clearly and thought to herself: hearing her name, would Yu Hao come in, or just leave as usual? If he came in, would it make this gathering more interesting?
Hearing Yao Yuan's name, Yu Hao's eyes shifted almost imperceptibly, though his smile turned even more casual and natural. "Yao Yuan's here too? What a coincidence, then. I won't be intruding, will I?"
"Intruding, my foot! We'd be glad to have you! Come on, come on—Jieyuan was just grumbling that we're short a real drinker!" Without giving him a chance to argue, Wang Qian half-pulled, half-pushed Yu Hao toward the room.
The heavy soundproof door was thrown fully open, and the room's dazzling lights and roaring music came rushing out. Wang Qian's booming voice rose above the music: "Everyone! Look who I caught!"
All eyes swung toward the doorway at once. Yu Hao's figure appeared at the entrance amid the interplay of light and shadow. He shrugged off his suit jacket, handing it with elegant ease to a waiter who had quietly stepped forward, a smile on his face pitched just right to blend into the atmosphere—but his gaze, like precise radar, instantly locked onto Yao Yuan, seated deep in the sofa and looking up at that very moment. Her cheeks were flushed, her eyes drowsy, the silver-gray sequined gown shimmering with seductive luster under the hazy lights. Yu Hao's gaze lingered on her for a beat, an unreadable undercurrent passing through his deep-set eyes, before he turned, unhurried, to the others and greeted them with a smile. "Boss Jin, young Gao, young Chen—long time no see. Looks like I came at just the right time, with things this lively." Moving in the same venture-capital circles, Yu Hao knew both Gao Jieyuan and Chen Ye.
She made brief eye contact with him. She caught that flash of deeper meaning in his eyes—a tangle of possessiveness and concern, with a trace of barely perceptible scrutiny. He was checking: was she "at ease" in this room? She also saw the flawless social smile he unfurled right after, sealing that hidden emotion away completely. Yao Yuan said nothing; she only lowered her lashes ever so slightly, picked up the glass before her, and drank down the last drops. The cold liquid slid into her burning throat, pressing down that faint ripple inside her. They were buddies, partners, but not lovers—at least not lovers in the standard sense. She wouldn't change her rhythm because he appeared, and she certainly wouldn't let his possessiveness sway her choices.
"Boss Yu!" "Brother Hao!" Jin Lan, Gao Jieyuan, and the others called out their greetings one after another. A flash of surprise crossed Gao Jieyuan's face, but he switched to a smile at once—only the warmth in that smile had cooled a few degrees, with an added trace of appraisal. "Brother Hao, what a rare guest! Come, sit!"
Yu Hao settled naturally into an empty seat, the spot cleverly chosen—close enough to talk with everyone, yet neither too near nor too far from Yao Yuan, his gaze able to brush over her with ease. Wang Qian loudly recounted the "chance encounter" with Yu Hao in the corridor, omitting the specific content of his shouting and saying only that it was a game penalty, drawing another round of laughter.
The atmosphere seemed even warmer now, but an undercurrent was faintly stirring. Yu Hao's joining was like a stone dropped into a seemingly calm lake. He took part in the game, the chatter, the drinking with effortless ease—but anyone observant would notice that his attention, or a considerable part of his senses, hovered around Yao Yuan, faint but constant. He noticed Gao Jieyuan's overly attentive toasts and suggestive flattery toward Yao Yuan, and he noticed the languor and allure gradually surfacing in Yao Yuan under the influence of the alcohol.
Likewise, Gao Jieyuan keenly sensed Yu Hao's unusual attentiveness toward Yao Yuan. That seemingly offhand yet appraising, faintly possessive gaze set off a quiet alarm in Gao Jieyuan's mind. He didn't know the relationship between Yu Hao and Yao Yuan, but at this moment, with Yu Hao's attitude, a male animal's instinctive competitiveness and a quiet sense of rivalry began to rise in him.
The game went on. This time, the one to draw the King card was Jin Lan. "Oh, my turn!" Jin Lan's eyes lit up. She drew a card from the pile, read it, and laughed, sweeping her gaze around the room before settling on Gao Jieyuan. "Young Gao, come on, play along." The card read: Drink a cross-cupped toast with a member of the opposite sex. "Sister Lan choosing me—what an honor." Gao Jieyuan arched an eyebrow with a smile and obligingly picked up his glass and stood.
Jin Lan rose with a smile too, and amid the room's jeering the two crossed arms and tipped their heads back, draining their glasses. The motion was clean and crisp; finishing, they exchanged a smile and sat back down, as if it were nothing more than a harmless little joke. Yao Yuan leaned against the sofa, watching quietly, a faint smile at the corner of her lips. She understood: Jin Lan choosing Gao Jieyuan was deliberate—first, to give Gao Jieyuan a graceful way out, and second, to ease the room's delicate atmosphere and keep Yu Hao and Gao Jieyuan from clashing head-on too soon. Yao Yuan saw that thoughtfulness, and she kept it in mind.
Yao Yuan enjoyed this atmosphere—the feeling of being surrounded by quality men, their gazes following her. As a professional who "analyzed men to enlighten women," she knew all too well where her charm lay, and exactly how to navigate such occasions with ease. The alcohol amplified her senses and made that subtle sense of control even clearer. She liked being watched, admired, even quietly competed over—this wasn't shallow vanity, but a double confirmation of both her own charm and her social value. She was like a chess player commanding the whole board, watching everyone in the room advance, step by step, to the rhythm she'd set in advance. The feeling left her utterly at ease.

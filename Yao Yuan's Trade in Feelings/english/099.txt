Chapter 99 — A New Year's Date, a Surprise Proposal: In the Mirror Room Beneath the Stars, a Promise of the Rest of Their Lives

The New Year's morning light filtered through the gauze curtains and gently woke Yao Yuan.
She had slept beautifully, a whole night without dreams, and woke clear-headed, her very body light and at ease. She rose, spread a yoga mat in the open space of the living room, and stretched slowly to soft music. Her breaths ran long and deep, her body finding its balance between suppleness and strength. It was a quiet ritual — a farewell to what was past, a welcome to a new beginning.
After bathing, she stood before the mirror in her dressing room. It was the first day of the New Year, and also an important day: she was going to Western World Technology to discuss the pricing model for the Pretty Big One AI with Zhao Yiming. She had no wish to meet the day half-heartedly. She wanted to greet the New Year, her beloved, and the road ahead in her very best form.
Her gaze swept the rows of crisply cut, finely tailored dresses, and at last settled on a mellow, warm wine-red in the corner.
It was a long cashmere-blend knit dress, its color not a loud, glaring true red but the deep red of an aged wine settled by the years — rich and restrained, glowing with a soft warmth of its own, understated yet full of texture. The cut was exquisite. The high collar fit snugly against her slender neck, lending its line a fine elegance; the long sleeves tapered cleanly to her wrists, dignified and refined, every proportion just right. From the shoulder line down, the fabric followed the contours of her body, cinching precisely at her slim waist, while the hem flared gently to soften any hint of severity.
The exquisitely fitted cut performed the most moving kind of "restrained sensuality." Wrapped close all over, not an inch of skin bared, it nonetheless traced every full, graceful curve of her figure — hidden yet suggested, noble allure and mature charm fused into one, growing only more flavorful the longer one looked.
The moment she slipped into the dress, the woman in the mirror was lit up by that warm red glow. Her black hair cascaded like a waterfall over her shoulders, setting off skin white as snow, brows and eyes bright and radiant, her whole presence suddenly unfolding — the composure of years settled within her, and the vivid freshness of one who has weathered a thousand sails.
Her makeup was chosen to match the dress, every measure pitched just right. Her brows were softened into a gentle, flowing arc, shorn of the sharp edge of her workdays; her eye makeup was blended layer on layer, the outer corners lifting in fine, delicate lines, every lash distinct, curled and lively, making her eyes look clear and deep; her lips wore a stroke of rich true red, full and lovely, like petals flushed by a winter sun's gentle kiss — vivid, yet never showy.
A pair of diamond tassel earrings hung at her ears, and a fine pearl sweater chain layered at her throat. The scattered sparkle of diamonds and the soft luster of pearls swayed lightly with her smallest movement, refracting a sky full of tiny starlight in the morning sun, mirroring the mellow wine-red of the dress and lending it just the right note of refined luxury — elegant, never gaudy.
Over it she wore a hip-length black fur coat, its pile fine and soft, its sheen warm as water. The classic clash of red and black blended seduction and steady strength to the utmost — at once a woman's tenderness and that nerve and presence not to be underestimated that belonged to Yao Yuan alone.
She let her long hair fall loose and easy, then lifted her eyes one last time to the bright, composed woman in the mirror. A faint smile rippled at the bottom of her eyes. She picked up a simple clutch, and pushed open the door to keep her date.
Yao Yuan brought her car to a stop and looked up. Through the clear glass curtain wall she saw a familiar figure inside the door, smiling and waving. It was Xiao Lin, the project assistant who handled her company's business with them on an ongoing basis.
A flicker of surprise crossed her mind. Western World had always run on humane management — it never ate into its staff's holidays — and Zhao Yiming, ever considerate of his subordinates, would never schedule pointless work on a statutory holiday like New Year's Day and put his people through the wringer. For Xiao Lin to be specially on duty waiting today was no ordinary business matter.
"Director Yao, Happy New Year!" Xiao Lin hurried forward, eyes crinkling, her smile bright, a barely contained excitement and delight she couldn't hide in her gaze. "Director Zhao arranged for me to wait here for you specially."
"Happy New Year. Thank you — coming in specially on New Year's Day." Yao Yuan pushed down the doubt in her heart and answered warmly. She filed it under special circumstances for now — perhaps some pricing-model data needed urgent verification — and followed Xiao Lin into the building.
The office tower on a holiday should have been empty and cold, utterly silent, yet today a warm, living current hung in the air. It wasn't the even warmth of central air conditioning, but the warm atmosphere woven from many people gathered together, hearts full of anticipation, brimming silently across the whole floor.
From the elevator hall all the way down the corridor into the company's interior, a delicate silver flower stand stood every few steps, each holding great armfuls of full, vivid red roses, their petals strung with crystal dew. A rich, sweet fragrance drifted slowly out, weaving a tender, lingering net of sweet warmth that shut out every trace of winter's chill.
Yao Yuan's steps paused slightly. The hazy guess in her heart spread, like a stone dropped into a still lake, in clear ripple after ripple. Her face betrayed nothing as she followed Xiao Lin on with the same composure. They passed through an office area likewise dotted with roses, the rows of workstations all empty, yet holding the lingering warmth of people who had only just left — clearly everyone had withdrawn early on purpose, all to prepare for this surprise.
Xiao Lin did not take her to the meeting room where they usually discussed work, but went straight toward the dedicated mirror room at the end of the corridor.
This was where she and Zhao Yiming had begun their work side by side, digging in together. Through countless days and nights they had tuned models here, broken through technical bottlenecks, polished their algorithms, and out of cold, intricate code and data had together nurtured the artificial intelligence that understood human emotion — the Pretty Big One Emotional AI. Here lay the founding heart and the milestones of their work, and here too was the hidden corner where their feelings for each other had quietly sprouted and slowly taken root.
The heavy sensor door slid open without a sound.
The instant Yao Yuan stepped inside —
"Pop! Pop!" A few soft, crisp bursts went off in turn, and a riot of confetti and fine glittering foam drifted down from the dome, dazzling across the air, settling gently all around her.
This spherical mirror room, built for absolute secrecy and holographic-projection research, had been decked out tender and romantic now, stripped of its old cold, technological feel. The eight-meter-high dome, seamlessly assembled from countless hidden hexagonal screens, flowed at this moment with a vast, magnificent sea of stars — the galaxy slowly wheeling, nebulae drifting tender and dim, gathering all the romance and tenderness of the boundless universe into this one small space.
Around the walls of the room hung weightless silver balloons and warm white string lights, their fine glow flickering bright and dim, tender and lingering. On the ring-shaped console floating at the center, the familiar blue data streams poured down like a waterfall, and through the usually cold, orderly sequences of code there now wove pulsing pink heart-shaped lights, while a line of faint glowing text quietly surfaced: Love Algorithm Running…
And at the center of all this romance stood a crowd of old friends, smiling brightly, the room full of tender blessing, all of it rushing toward her.
Zhao Yiming stood at the very front of the crowd, the irreplaceable focus. Today he wore an ink-blue bespoke suit, crisply and sharply cut, fitting his tall, upright frame to perfection, the fabric giving off an understated, fine sheen in the warm light. A matching waistcoat was buttoned neatly, and a light gray-blue tie in a Windsor knot sat trim and proper — shorn of his everyday lab casualness and workplace edge, with a few measures of the utmost solemnity added.
He had purposely combed back the loose hair from his forehead, baring a clean, full brow. The smart glasses he usually wore were swapped for a warm gold wire-rimmed pair, setting off his deep, gentle features, his bearing steady and noble. In his hands he held a great bouquet of full-bloomed red velvet roses. He stood tall and straight, his expression more solemn than ever before, even his brows carrying a barely perceptible tension, hiding a heart full of nerves and hope.
Around him were gathered those closest to them: Jin Lan held Lu Ang's arm, eyes crinkled, smiling bright and sincere; Liu Qingzhuo and Chen Ye stood side by side, the one elegant, the other steady, both with tender blessing in their eyes; Jiang Tiantian held her arms lightly crossed, the gaze behind her lenses shorn of its usual sharpness, rippling with a gentle smile; Wang Qian held up a phone, steadily recording this precious moment that belonged to the two of them. Beyond them stood the core colleagues from both companies, close in business and deep in private friendship, all standing quietly, their gazes tender, their hearts full of hope.
Yao Yuan stood frozen in the doorway, her body going rigid in an instant. The hazy guess in her heart had settled completely, yet the scene before her — so grand, so sincere and tender — struck her with a force far beyond anything she had imagined.
Zhao Yiming drew a deep breath, pressing down the nerves and gravity surging within him, and, holding the roses, came slowly toward her step by step. His stride was steady and sure; only the bob of his throat betrayed the agitation he was holding in check.
He stopped one step away from her, his eyes brilliant as a sea of stars, every measure of his tenderness focused on her alone. Under the hushed, breath-held gaze of the whole room, with no warning at all, he sank to one knee in a posture of the utmost solemnity.
"Yuanyuan."
His voice was low and clear, ringing steadily through the quiet, vast mirror room — gentle, yet weighted like a thousand pounds.
"From the time we created the Pretty Big One together, breaking through technical barriers side by side and digging deep into the emotional-AI field, right up to now, we've signed countless business contracts and technical agreements, and worked through countless data models and partnership plans."
He tilted his head up slightly, his gaze clear and sincere, looking straight into her eyes, every word earnest, every line from the heart: "But to me, every formal document and commercial contract in this world is worth less than one love white paper that belongs only to us. From start to finish it holds just a single core clause, never once amended — Zhao Yiming, for the whole of this life, will have no one but Yao Yuan."
He raised his hand and gently lifted the bouquet. At the center of the layered red velvet roses a diamond ring sat quietly, blazing out a brilliant, ardent fire in the light of the starlit sky — dazzling, breathtaking.
"This invisible white paper — I've written, revised, and polished it over and over in my heart, and in the days and nights you couldn't see, I've silently submitted it and held to it countless times."
"Today, at the dawn of the New Year, with all things renewed, I formally apply to you to launch the life project of spending the rest of our lives together, growing old side by side — to make real a lifetime of companionship and commitment."
His eyes were full of devotion and certainty, his voice growing firmer and deeper: "Yao Yuan, will you marry me?"
The instant his words fell, the hush of the whole room shattered, and a fierce roar of cheers and applause exploded out, filling the entire mirror room with tenderness.
"Say yes! Yuanyuan!"
"Marry him! It's too sweet!"
"Director Yao, Director Zhao spent so long preparing for this day!"
Jin Lan was the first to laugh and chime in, her eyes full of heartfelt blessing; Liu Qingzhuo clapped softly, a smile in her eyes; Chen Ye gave a slight nod, his face full of approval; Jiang Tiantian pushed up her glasses, showing a rare tender smile; Wang Qian held up the lens, steadily freezing each warm moment. Everyone was moved by this rational romance, this utter sincerity that belonged to Zhao Yiming alone — the whole room warm, ardent, glowing.
Yet Yao Yuan, standing at the eye of the storm, was suddenly seized by an extreme, dazed weightlessness.
The cheers and teasing, the laughter and clamor at her ears seemed to come through a hazy mist — distant and blurred. The man kneeling before her, the roses in full bloom, the dazzling diamond ring, and the unreserved hope and cherishing in his eyes — all of it was clear and real, yet beautiful as a fragile dream.
This year, she was thirty-eight.
At twenty-two she had once dreamed, heart full, of a wedding gown and vows. But in all the years that followed, reality had shattered her every tender fantasy with cold truths, time and again.
At twenty-four, her first love could not withstand the shackles of family, and she had let go in helplessness — for the first time tasting in full the bitterness of being weighed and discarded by reality, of giving up against her will. At twenty-eight, a wealthy suitor doted on her, lifted her up, yet always felt she shone too sharply, was too hard to handle, willing only to keep her in ambiguous companionship and never to grant her the name of marriage — making her see clearly her own pricing as "fit to be doted on, not to be honored." She had the child taken away and left without hesitation. At thirty, she rose into the top circles of fame and fortune, became the capable right arm of a Shanghai magnate, yet came to understand in the end that she was only a chip at the negotiating table, to be swapped out and sacrificed, never truly cherished. At thirty-six, she met a compatible "buddy," seemingly steady and well-matched, yet he treated her only as a quality "mother for his children" and "life partner" — and merely because her public IP image carried too high a risk and would affect their family business, he flatly refused any legal binding. Once again she was reduced to a functional option to be screened and weighed, rather than the one chosen with certainty.
Year after year, again and again, she had been compared, picked apart, abandoned, pushed away.
She had long since clawed her way through the thorns and mire of love and forged a hard suit of armor, polishing and freezing over her soft true heart again and again. She no longer longed for marriage as a safety net, no longer craved another's shelter, no longer needed anyone to define the worth of her life. She dug deep into her work, built up her own ground to stand on, and with her own hands raised a stretch of steady ground for herself — living as a deep-rooted, leaf-rich great tree that feared no wind or rain, strong enough to hold up alone against all the frosts of the years.
But now, when someone had pierced through her layers of armor, ignored all her past history, her worldly labels, her weak points — not weighing, not hesitating, not making do, but simply, firmly affirming her as this very person, and telling her without reserve, "It has to be you" —
What surged up in her heart was not the expected wild joy and emotion, but a sudden, defenseless panic, and an instinctive shrinking back.
A gift of beauty too grand, too pure, too utterly unreserved, threw her by habit into dread and unease. She feared this was once more a fleeting splendor of fireworks — that after the ardent bloom there would be only desolation as far as the eye could see, deeper loneliness, wounds cut deeper. All those late nights of being failed and forsaken, all those fragments of self-doubt and inner attrition, turned now into a cold tide, surging fierce upon her, trying to drag her hard away from the tenderness and warmth before her.
"Yuanyuan?"
Zhao Yiming's faint voice, wrapped in a barely perceptible tension, gently broke open her tangled, sinking thoughts and pulled her back from the shadows of the past into the present. He held his kneeling posture all the while — no urging, no impatience, only waiting quietly, the tenderness in his eyes unchanged, embracing all her hesitation and panic.
"Yuanyuan, look at you — stunned silly with emotion?" Jin Lan stepped up and gently nudged her arm, teasing with a smile, her tone warm and reassuring. "So even our Teacher Yao — decisive in battle, composed in any crisis — has a day when she's at a loss like this."
Moved.
Yao Yuan blinked slowly, her long lashes trembling, and in an instant a layer of warm tears welled up in her eyes.
It was far more than being moved. It was the release of having come full circle through a thousand sails; the comfort of having every toughness and fragility read and understood; the bittersweetness of years of grievance soothed in a single moment; a flood of disbelief and brimming joy all woven together.
She gazed quietly at the man before her. He was a top-tier helmsman digging deep into cutting-edge technology — rational, restrained, rigorous and meticulous, used to taking apart every logic, calculating every risk, controlling every variable. Yet to her, of all people, he gave all his tenderness, sincerity, and patience.
He was her most compatible ally in work, her most reliable partner, the lover who could catch all her sharp edges, embrace all her weariness, and read all her clear-sightedness and softness. He was no flawless fairy-tale prince, but the most real, the most solid, the most fitting Zhao Yiming for her. He had given the few measures of tenderness and romance he had, all of it, to her alone.
None of the past hurts and hardships had been gone through in vain. That mire and frost had honed the independent, strong, clear-eyed and transparent woman she was today; and the splendid, certain woman she was now deserved exactly this kind of love before her — equally pure, ardent, firm, and singular.
A warm sob rose in her throat, bittersweetness and warmth woven together, crashing against her heart. The Yao Yuan who calmly dissected the riddles of love in her livestream, revered by millions as the Godmother of Feelings, at last laid down all her armor before the sincere love that belonged to her, and her eyes reddened.
Scalding tears slid from her eyes, washing away the last trace of hesitation and dread, rinsing clean every shadow of unease from the past.
She drew a deep breath, pressed down the surging tide of feeling, and beneath the breath-held, hopeful gaze of the whole room, she slowly, firmly nodded. Her voice carried a faint sob, yet every word rang clear: "I will."
"Wow——!!!"
Earth-shaking cheers swept the whole room again, confetti flying everywhere, romance spilling out unrestrained.
A brilliant sea of stars lit up in Zhao Yiming's eyes in an instant, brimming with the utmost joy and tenderness. His fingertips trembled faintly — a rare lapse of composure under such joy — as he carefully took out the diamond ring and, with the clumsiness and earnestness peculiar to a science man, slid it steadily onto the ring finger of her slender left hand.
The size was just right, neither loose nor tight, locking fast the bond they were certain of for this life.
He rose and strode forward to gather her into his arms, his strength gentle but firm, holding her steadily in his embrace. Yao Yuan lifted her arms in turn to hold him back, burying her cheek against his shoulder, the clean, cool scent of his aftershave lingering at the tip of her nose — steadying and healing.
Amid the ardent teasing and blessings of the whole room, Zhao Yiming bowed his head slightly and gently kissed her lips. The kiss was restrained yet deeply tender — nothing of desire, nothing of vanity, full of cherishing, certainty, and the solemn promise to stay together for the rest of their lives, tenderly soothing the heart that had drifted half a lifetime.
The grand celebration party was set in the meeting room next to the mirror room. The originally orderly long conference table had long since been carefully arranged into an exquisite buffet station.
A crystal-clear three-tier champagne tower rose layer on layer, bubbles rustling up through the golden liquor, fine and lively. Beside it, a multi-tiered themed cake was exquisite and splendid, its sugar-sculpted code patterns and starry motifs the romantic understanding that belonged to the two of them alone — tender and distinctive. Fresh fruit, desserts, and small bites dazzled the eye, exquisitely plated, perfect in color, aroma, and taste.
At the center of the venue, a three-Michelin-star chef and his team cooked on the spot, their snow-white uniforms lending them a professional elegance. Thick-cut Angus steaks landed on the grill, sizzling, their meaty aroma mingling with the sharp scent of rosemary and black pepper, drifting freely; hand-kneaded pizza bases swelled slowly in the oven, the cheese melting and stretching, rich fragrance wrapped in ardent, lively warmth; specially mixed cocktails were poured with practiced skill, their gradient colors stunning, each glass a piece of dedicated craft.
Everyone raised their glasses in congratulation, the crisp clink of cups never ceasing, laughter and cheer filling the whole room.
"Congratulations, congratulations! May the rest of your lives go smoothly, and may you stay together year after year!"
"The two of you joining strength to strength — career and love both complete!"
"We'll be waiting for your grand wedding — you two must be truly happy!"
Yao Yuan answered each with a smile, the diamond ring on her finger heavy, real and substantial. Zhao Yiming stayed close at her side the whole time, his arm naturally around her waist, silently guarding her there. His usually cold, distant brows and eyes were now stained with a tenderness that would not melt away, the doting in his gaze impossible to hide.
The champagne left her pleasantly tipsy, warmth spreading through her, yet a happiness this ardent and grand felt too vast, leaving her always with a touch of unreality. The joy in her heart was full and scalding, yet it stayed suspended in the clouds, unable to fully land and settle. She needed a moment alone to dissolve this sudden, utter tenderness and untangle the complicated feelings churning inside her.
While everyone was gathered watching the chef show off his skills, in all the lively clamor, Yao Yuan quietly slipped away, withdrawing alone from the noisy meeting room and walking back into the mirror room where the proposal had just taken place.
The sensor door closed softly, shutting out all the clamor and laughter outside, leaving only a room of quiet and sanctity.
The sea of stars on the dome still wheeled slowly, vast and tender; the blue data streams trickled down; fine starlight flickered point by point; the sweet scent of roses had not yet faded, tender and lingering as before. Stripped of the press of the crowd, here remained only the cold of technology and the lingering warmth of the utmost romance — quiet enough to hear her own clear heartbeat.
Yao Yuan walked slowly to the ring-shaped console and brushed her fingertips lightly over the cool, smooth surface, her heart a tangle of a thousand feelings.
At twenty-two she had vainly hoped to cross class lines and seek security through marriage, believing that marrying was life's only destination. Back then she had never imagined that her road toward marriage would be so winding and thorny, marked by countless betrayals and wounds.
Later she slowly woke, slowly healed, slowly raised high walls. She shed all her obsession with marriage, no longer treating it as a life necessity, but only as a luxury that might be met but never sought. She poured all her heart into her work, building her own kingdom brick by brick, living independent, clear-eyed, and certain — needing to lean on no one, yet able to be steady year after year, blooming with each step.
But fate had always been a mysterious thing, forever bestowing surprises when least expected. Just as she had let go completely, grown indifferent to love and marriage and asked only for steady years, this most compatible, most complete, most pure marriage and love rushed toward her without warning.
No weighing of gains and losses, no added conditions, no worldly coercion — only a certainty of running toward each other from both sides, an unreserved choice, and a vow to stay.
She quietly asked herself: was this sudden completeness fate's belated compensation, or a new round of unknown trial? No one answered. The diamond ring on her finger was cold and hard, yet it refracted a warm, brilliant light beneath the starlit glow — cold and warmth woven together, just like the tumultuous first half of her life.
She lifted her head and gazed at that brilliant sea of stars on the dome, lit for her, her heart stirred beyond words. The galaxy was vast, and a person but a speck of dust; to find a kindred soul to walk alongside was already great fortune.
Just as her thoughts drifted far, her gaze unconsciously following the wheeling track of one especially bright, whirlpool-shaped nebula in the sea of stars —
An extremely familiar pull, rising from the very depths of her soul, seized her without any warning.
In her sight, the center of that whirlpool-shaped nebula suddenly rippled outward in violent ring after ring, like the surface of a lake struck by a dropped stone! Then the whole starscape in her vision, the data streams, even the outline of the mirror room itself, all began to twist, stretch, and spin, like an oil painting crumpled by an invisible giant hand.
Vertigo crashed over her like a mountain-toppling wave, draining in an instant all the strength from her limbs and bones.
Her body crumpled down beyond her control, falling onto the cool floor — itself assembled from the same hidden hexagonal screens.
In the last instant before her consciousness was wholly swallowed by darkness, a thought, clear and calm to the utmost, surfaced above her tangled feelings:
The ninth time-travel had come.
In the mirror room, the starry sky still wheeled, the data still poured down like a waterfall, the roses still gave off their fragrance. Only the figure in the red dress lay quietly against the console, as if utterly exhausted, sunk into a deep sleep that no one would disturb.
Outside the window, the New Year's sunlight was just right.

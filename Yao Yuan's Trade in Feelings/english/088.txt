A sexless, cold-violence sham marriage: a smart woman's patient counterstrike.
"All right," she said, lifting her eyes. Her gaze turned clear and focused again, her voice as steady as ever. "Let's take the next call."
The livestream went on. The light of reason kept shining down on one soul after another, each lost in the maze of feelings and searching for a way out.
[Call connected]
Caller (a woman's voice, 31, gentle and soft on the surface but with an exhaustion and a trace of confusion she couldn't quite hide underneath): "Hello, Teacher Yuanyuan. I've followed you for a long time. This is my first time calling in, I'm a little nervous… My situation might be kind of complicated."
"Take your time. We have plenty of it," Yao Yuan said, her tone level, carrying a steadying kind of strength.
The caller drew a deep breath and began. "I'm 31 this year, my husband is 41. We met right after I graduated from college. He'd already started his own business by then and was doing pretty well. We dated for four years. Back then he treated me really well — very gentlemanly, generous with money — it's just that… he was a bit of a chauvinist. He thought a girl shouldn't have to work too hard. I was young, and I took it as being looked after. We married when I was 27. It's been four years now. In three of them, I had a son and a daughter."
(Scrolling comments drifted past: [A son and a daughter — that's the winner's template for life!] [Husband out in the world, wife at home. Very traditional setup.])
"After we got married, he wouldn't let me go out to work. He said the family didn't need my salary, and told me to focus on the home and the kids with my mind at ease. I'd had my own career plans, but I couldn't win against him, and I figured the children needed their mother, so I agreed. We hired three nannies — one for the housework, two just to look after the two children. The truth is… I'm not actually very busy." The caller's voice dropped lower.
"And your daily life, your spending?" Yao Yuan asked.
"He… won't let me buy luxury goods. He says they're vanity, that there's no need. The skincare I use, the clothes I wear — he has his assistant buy them. They're some… decent-quality brands, but with no logo on them at all. He's different, though. His watches, his suits, his car — all top of the line. I've brought it up with him, and he says a man needs a presentable front out in the world, while a woman just needs to be comfortable at home." The caller paused, her voice turning a little strained. "And he often… criticizes me. Says I'm home all day, that my thinking has fallen behind his, that the things I say are childish, that I should read more. But when I do read and share my views with him, he says my ideas are shallow, that I don't understand how the real world works…"
The comments began to turn:

"How does he handle things financially?" Yao Yuan's question cut in like a scalpel, straight to the core.
"Every year he gives me a card with a fixed 400,000 yuan on it. That's the household living expenses and some of the kids' costs. The nannies' wages, the big purchases, his own spending — none of that goes through this card. And every time I spend, he gets a text alert on his end." The caller's voice grew smaller and smaller. "Sometimes I want to buy something a little nicer for my own family, or the bill runs higher when I get together with my girlfriends, and he'll ask about it. He never says anything harsh, but that feeling of being scrutinized… it's very uncomfortable."

Not a ripple crossed Yao Yuan's face. She went on: "How do the two of you get along day to day — communication, intimacy, that sort of thing?"
The caller was silent for an even longer stretch. When she spoke again, her voice carried embarrassment and a deeper bewilderment: "Communication… very little. When he's home he spends most of his time in the study, or on the phone. We barely talk, except about the children. Intimacy… even less." She seemed to make a great decision. "It was never much even before we married — he said he wanted to respect me. After the wedding, especially after the children were born, it got even rarer. Now… maybe two or three times a year. Or… once."

Yao Yuan's gaze narrowed slightly, but her tone stayed even. "You dated for four years, married for four — eight years all told. How much do you know about his social circle, his friends? Is there any woman he's especially close to, beyond the bounds of an ordinary friend? Or… a male friend like that?"
The question seemed to stump the caller. She thought back. "His friends… most are business partners, and there are a few brothers he's known for years. Women… I've never heard of anything unusual. As for male friends… there's a business partner, also his college classmate, surname Zhou, and they're especially close. They travel together for work all the time. Sometimes Mr. Zhou comes to the house and the two of them will talk in the study all night. My husband is… much more gentle and patient with him than with me." Belatedly, she added, "Oh — and at our wedding, Mr. Zhou was the witness, and he gave an especially heavy gift."

When Yao Yuan finished listening, she leaned back slightly into her chair. She didn't speak right away. She folded her hands in front of her chest, fingertips meeting, forming a calm spire. The studio held only the faint hum of electricity and the dim sound of the Yellow River flowing outside the window. An invisible pressure spread out through the screen.
A few seconds later, she spoke slowly. Her voice wasn't loud, but every word landed with the weight of a thousand pounds on every listener's heart:
"Miss, what I'm about to say may be very blunt — even cruel. I'd ask you, and everyone watching, to brace yourselves."
"Based on the fragments you've given me: low sexual demand even during the dating years; a rapid drop after marriage to near-celibacy; strict control over your social life and finances along with psychological suppression; zero interest in — even obstruction of — your personal development and self-worth; yet an urgent need for you to complete the task of childbearing (two children in four years); and a same-□□ partner he trusts and is intimate with to an extreme degree, in whom his emotional investment far exceeds anything he gives you…"
Yao Yuan paused, then stated the cold conclusion clearly:
"There is a very high probability that your husband is in a sham marriage. And it is a sham marriage he himself entered fully aware, with cool calculation."
The comments went completely berserk:
[Holy crap! It really is a sham marriage!]
[No wonder! It all makes sense now!]
[He married her just for childbearing plus a social image!]
[This is too cruel…]
From the caller's end came the sound of a sharp intake of breath, then a longer dead silence, broken only by suppressed, ragged breathing.
Yao Yuan didn't pause. She kept on in a tone so calm it was almost cruel: "This marriage, to him, is not an emotional union. It's a precise, outsourced project for childbearing and the construction of a social image. He needs legitimate heirs to pass on his wealth and to steady his business partnerships and family expectations. He needs a 'wife' to fill the social role, to maintain his public image as a 'successful entrepreneur with a happy family.' And you are the suitable 'project contractor' he selected."
"Not letting you work ensures your social ties stay simple, your dependence high, you easy to control. Not letting you buy luxury goods, monitoring your spending — that controls project costs and keeps you from developing any unnecessary 'awakening of self-awareness' or expanding your social reach because of material abundance. Suppressing you psychologically continually erodes your confidence and independent character, ensuring you settle into the 'contractor' role and never question the absolute authority of the 'client' — him — or the true nature of the project. The near-absence of sex is because he simply has no desire for you. Once the task — childbearing — was complete, this 'partnership' was over as far as he's concerned. His closeness with that Mr. Zhou is most likely where his real emotional belonging lies."
The caller finally let out a choked sob, the sound of a worldview shattering.
"So, Teacher Yuanyuan… what… what should I do? Get a divorce?" Her voice trembled, full of despair.
"Divorce? Of course. But not now." Yao Yuan's tone turned abruptly, intensely practical, with even an edge of cold sharpness. "In your current state — no job, no independent income, no competitive standing in society, two young children, possibly not even aware of the marital assets — if you rush to file for divorce, how much do you think this shrewd, calculating husband of yours will give you? You very likely won't even win custody, because he'll prove you have no capacity to support them, and he holds an overwhelming financial advantage. He might even countersue, claiming you're mentally unstable and unfit to be a mother."

"Listen, miss," Yao Yuan said, her voice dropping, carrying the certainty of a commander deploying a strategy. "What you need to do now is not cry, not make a scene. It's to launch, immediately, a two-to-three-year 'Self-Rescue and Counterstrike Preparation' plan. You are going to turn from a passive pawn into the player quietly arranging the board in this game."
"Step one: go completely calm, and play the 'role' well. Stop all the inner grievance and resistance. On the surface, return to gentleness. Let his psychological jabs go in one ear and out the other — never argue back. Play the 'wife' and 'mother' roles flawlessly, so he lowers his guard. Use the 400,000 in living expenses he gives you to quietly build up a private savings — that's one of your future startup funds."
"Step two: gather evidence, cast a wide net. This is the most critical part, and the one that takes the most patience. First, financial evidence: learn as much as you can about the family's real assets, the company's equity, the investments. Keep the spending records on the card he gave you, too — that's evidence of how he controls your spending. Second, evidence of the sham marriage: this isn't about catching them in the act. It's about noting the details of his dealings with Mr. Zhou — coming and going together, intimate gestures, special occasions — any unguarded information that might be on his computer or phone (if you get the chance, and legally), his cold attitude toward your sexless marriage (you can record it, but mind the legal risks). More importantly, gather evidence of his psychological suppression, his control over your social life and work, his economic control — WeChat chats, recordings, and so on. In court, these are key grounds for establishing 'household cold violence' and 'restriction of personal freedom.' Third, evidence for custody: record every detail of you being the primary caregiver — photos, videos, medical records, communications with teachers — proving that you are the core of the children's emotional attachment."
"Step three: better yourself, advance in secret. Use reasons like 'to give the children a better education' or 'to raise my own understanding so I don't embarrass you' to win chances to study. You can sign up for online courses — learn finance, law, psychological counseling, even a professional skill. Slowly rebuild your knowledge base and your social circle, but keep it low-key, without fail. Take care of your body. Your condition is the confidence you'll bring to future negotiations."
Yao Yuan stressed one point in particular: "I heard you mention earlier that he had you act as guarantor on a bank loan?"
The caller answered quickly: "Yes. Before we married, his company had a loan and had me sign for it. After we married there was another one — he said it was a family investment, and had me sign for that too."
Yao Yuan's gaze hardened. "Until every one of these loan debts is fully repaid and your guarantor liability is lifted, do not file for divorce under any circumstances. This is the red line. Otherwise, the moment you divorce, if he plays games with the debts, you could end up shouldering an enormous burden. Your plan must include this milestone — after the loans are paid off."
"So your timeline is this: take two to three years to quietly complete the evidence gathering, the self-improvement, the private savings. At the same time, watch the loan-repayment progress closely. The moment the last loan you guaranteed is paid off, and you already hold evidence of sufficient weight — especially evidence pointing to his sham marriage, his cold violence, his tendency to move assets — you can act."
"At that point, you don't go begging for a divorce. You go in with a lawyer, lay out your leverage, and negotiate. Your goal is not revenge. It's to secure, for you and your children, the most favorable and reasonable division of property, custody and child support, and your own future financial security. You have the evidence; he has the social image and the business. This is a game. The calmer you are, the more thoroughly you prepare, the more leverage you hold — the more secure your future and your children's will be."

Treat him as a complex 'project' that requires your careful handling. She knew that another soul, after a thorough freezing and dissection, had perhaps found that hard but necessary path of thawing and moving forward. The road was strewn with thorns; it would take calculation, take patience. But compared to slowly suffocating in a cage that looked lavish and warm yet was cold to the bone, this was already a chance to live.

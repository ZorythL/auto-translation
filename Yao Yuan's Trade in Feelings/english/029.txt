The "King's Game" at the Crimson Night Palace
Today's livestream had run half an hour over. When Yao Yuan stepped out through the company's front doors, a bitter wind cut into her, and the city's neon was already steeped in the thick, ink-black wash of a northern winter night.
She had a quick dinner with a colleague at a nearby mall, then drove over to the "Crimson Night Palace," which Jin Lan, Wang Qian, and the others had booked well in advance. After their back-to-back grind, the plan was to wring a few moments of looseness and emptiness out of the night with a few drinks and one good buzz.
At eighteen minutes past ten, Yao Yuan's figure slipped through the shimmering, light-washed doors of the "Crimson Night Palace." She crossed the glittering but clamorous lobby, and a waiter in formal dress with a textbook smile led her toward a deeper, quieter inner corridor. The floor of the long passage was laid with a Persian carpet woven in ink-black and dark-gold thread; her steps fell on it without a sound. The walls on either side were pieced together from countless dark laser-glass panels of varying sizes and dark velvet padding. Under the spotlights, as her angle of approach shifted, they gave off a deep, shifting glow like the Milky Way. From the ceiling, enormous irregularly shaped flower-shaped chandeliers cast dreamlike, ever-moving patches of light across the walls and carpet, as if one were threading through a world of crystal. The air carried a custom-blended fragrance: a top note of cold, green galbanum, a heart note that gradually gave way to expensive oud and a faint whisper of leather, and a base note that lingered with the faintest trace of amber and musk, thread by thread. Carried along by the slow, ethereal background music of the corridor, Yao Yuan—draped in a silver-mist frost-gray Arctic-fox fur—moved through this shimmering, fragrance-soaked passage like the lead of a film stepping through a veil of light and shadow toward a stage made for her alone.
At a wall ground from a single block of dark crystal, a waiter in a tailored butler's uniform stood quietly beside the heavy door of a private room marked "999." Seeing her arrive, he bowed slightly, a trained, perfectly curved smile of deference on his face, and pushed open the heavy soundproof door.
A space utterly different from the public, hallucinatory haze of the lobby outside—more private, yet lavish to the extreme—rushed at her, along with the suddenly sharp surge of sound and light, the clamor and intoxication that belonged to this one night.
The room was enormously spacious, like a miniature luxury private club. At its center sat a giant ring-shaped velvet sofa, a deep crimson-purple that flowed with a satin sheen under the shifting lights; sinking into it felt like being swallowed and held. Facing it were three walls of seamlessly joined giant laser-projection screens, now playing high-definition, visually striking abstract images of light, cleverly dissolved into the visuals of a music video so that the two blended into an immersive wraparound of sight. A top-tier surround-sound system delivered every drumbeat and every vocal cleanly to the eardrum, the volume held at a perfect threshold that could ignite the mood without ever becoming uncomfortable.
The lighting system was extraordinarily intricate. Beyond the light from the screens themselves, programmable LED strips were hidden in the ceiling, now flowing in aurora-like shifting tones of blue and violet; concealed light troughs set into the walls gave off a soft, warm yellow that lit the abstract modern art pieces hanging there. Above the coffee table, a dazzling crystal chandelier—its main light unswitched—still caught and refracted the faint glow around it through its countless facets, scattering fine glints like starlight. The air was thick with the faint mellow scent of cigars, the sweet freshness of fruit platters, and the rich, indistinguishable blend of several high-end perfumes.
As she crossed the heavy soundproof door of the "Crimson Night Palace" Room 999, a faint, almost imperceptible smile played at the corner of Yao Yuan's lips. She had long understood, in her bones, that inside and outside that door were always two different worlds—outside was the howling north wind and the order of reason, the propriety and clear-headedness she had to keep up at all times; inside was a garden of desire, carefully cultivated at constant temperature and humidity, a playground where she could release her charm in measured doses and control the rhythm. The cool-toned, silver-mist frost-gray full-pelt Arctic-fox long coat she wore underwent a curious chemical reaction with the room's over-saturated warm light the moment she stepped in: the fur was fluffy as cloud, its tips stained with the hazy tones of where moonlight meets polar night—a pale silver-white base, with smoke-frost gray and faint mist-blue interwoven in fragments and shaded together, and a soft pearl-gray luster pooled at the roots. The expensive fur caught and refracted every stray beam of light that brushed past it, as if it were giving off a cool halo of its own, instantly raising an invisible barrier between her and the boiling, worldly clamor around her. It was her protective coloring, and her ground to stand on—it kept her from looking out of place, while letting her hold on to a bystander's clarity, reading the mind of everyone in the room.
She slipped off the fur and handed it to the waiter, and the silver-gray sequined gown she'd worn for the livestream spilled out from beneath it. The cut was razor-sharp, the fabric draping soft and supple, tracing the smooth lines of her figure—elegant with a thread of sexiness running through it, exactly mirroring her state right now: stepping clear-eyed into intoxication, melting cool-headed into abandon. She knew very well what kind of looks this image of her would draw—the dazzlement, the covetousness, the probing—and she had foreseen all of them, had even deliberately engineered the effect. As the "Godmother of Feelings," who spent her years dismantling emotional riddles and teaching others to master the human heart, she understood all too well how to seize the whole room's attention with one outfit, one glance—without ever seeming to try too hard.
Right now she was like a cold-toned star that had drifted by chance into a dreamlike galaxy, walking step by step into this tender, intoxicating haze born for the night.
A fair number of people had already gathered on the ring-shaped sofa. Wang Qian sat near the song-selection console, holding a microphone and singing along to the screen, his mood running high. Beside him sat a man in a black shirt, looking not yet thirty, singing along with a bottle in hand. Yao Yuan had never seen him before. Jin Lan was tucked into the other side of the sofa, whispering head-to-head with a girlfriend she'd brought along. The girlfriend's makeup was equally exquisite; in a gold sequined cocktail dress, she glittered in the dimness—a bright, striking beauty.
In the relatively open space at the center of the room stood a man in a faintly garish printed shirt, collar wide open, flanked by two shapely, good-looking young women—by the look of it, the room's "hostess girls." Yao Yuan had seen him before; last time, at a dinner with Zhao Yiming, his name had seemed to be Gao Jieyuan, a second-generation type from the venture-capital circle. Gao Jieyuan had one hand draped casually over the shoulder of one of the beauties, the other gripping a microphone, singing along to a love song in a slightly exaggerated tone—though his eyes kept flicking toward the door, until Yao Yuan's figure came into view.
"Hey! Our gorgeous Miss Yao has finally arrived!" Gao Jieyuan's eyes lit up. Without breaking off his singing, he shouted the line into the microphone; amplified by the speakers, his voice came back with a teasing echo and instantly drew part of the room's attention to Yao Yuan, who had just walked in. His gaze roamed over her, the undisguised desire all but spilling out. Yao Yuan gave it a faint inward glance—men like Gao Jieyuan she had seen too many of: blunt, flashy, carrying the conceit of a spoiled rich kid. His interest in her sprang mostly from the conqueror's itch of "can't have her," not from any genuine appreciation.
Hearing him, Jin Lan looked up and at once waved with a smile. "Yuanyuan, over here! I saved you a seat!" She patted the soft, empty spot on the sofa beside her.
Wang Qian paused his howling too, raising his glass with a smile in greeting. His friend beside him and the woman in the sequined dress also turned their eyes her way—sizing her up, or simply friendly.
Yao Yuan paused briefly at the door, letting herself adjust to the more concentrated, more fervent atmosphere of the room.
Under the ever-shifting light of a KTV room, the silver-gray sequined gown beneath flowed with brilliance. She walked unhurriedly toward the seat Jin Lan had saved, answering Gao Jieyuan's appraising, dazzled gaze and the looks from the two beauties beside him with a calm smile, then gracefully took her seat. That composure was no act—it came from a certainty deep within her: she knew her own worth, and she knew the meanings hidden behind those looks.
"Bad traffic on the way?" Jin Lan leaned in affectionately and handed her a poured glass of clear, light-colored champagne.
"Not too bad. Nice room." Yao Yuan took the glass, her fingertips feeling the chill of the rim; that trace of cool was just enough to press down the faint restlessness rising in her at all the noise and bustle. Her gaze swept over the room's lavish fittings and the already lively crowd.
"Of course—Triple-Nine is one of the best rooms in this place." Wang Qian leaned over, his face flushed faintly with drink. "We've just been waiting for you to warm things up! Young Boss Gao's already sung a bunch!"
Gao Jieyuan set down the microphone now, swirled his glass, and ambled over, settling naturally into the seat diagonally in front of Yao Yuan. The two beauties dutifully topped up the drinks of the guests seated there. "The second Boss Yao walks in, the whole room feels brighter. Come, come—latecomers fine themselves three drinks first, that's the rule!" A slightly flippant smile sat on his face, his gaze roving over her.
Jin Lan laughed and gave Gao Jieyuan a light shove. "To hell with your rule—Yuanyuan just got here, she hasn't even caught her breath." The words sounded like a block, but there was little real intent to stop him in her tone; if anything, it was tossing another log onto this tipsy overture. The room was warm and cozy, the music restless, and on every face floated a layer of eager, loosened readiness—the fuse of indulgence had long since been lit.
Yao Yuan only curved her lips in a smile, neither accepting nor refusing the "three forfeit drinks" jest. She calmly raised the crystal-clear champagne flute and offered a vague toast toward the blurred figures in the booth. "Sorry to keep everyone waiting. Let me toast you all first." Her tone was proper, the measure pitched just right—conveying her apology without losing her poise, without a trace of flattery or stiffness.
With that, she lifted her wrist slightly, the rim of the glass meeting her lips, the golden liquid resting a moment at her mouth and leaving a small, moist gleam. She didn't gulp; she only sipped and stopped. Alcohol was a messenger of socializing, but it must never become an obstacle to controlling the room. She had to stay clear-headed even as she relaxed—only then could she read everyone's reactions, only then could she keep a firm grip on the initiative in this social game.
She set the glass down, and then came the never-ending round of introductions that no social occasion is without. Yao Yuan sat upright, her expression calm, while her mind raced beneath, making precise judgments about each person being introduced.
She set the glass down, and then came the round where acquaintances introduce new friends. Names, identities, and brief backgrounds floated through the air like pieces of a puzzle, swiftly fitting together into tonight's temporary social map.
"Liu Qingzhuo, my girlfriend." Jin Lan affectionately hooked an arm around the bright, striking woman's shoulder. "Her family's in the liquor business—all the drinks at my 'Yellow River Tower' are supplied exclusively by her family." Then she leaned to Yao Yuan's ear and added in a breath of a whisper, "Just got back to being single, the certificate's still warm." There was a touch of that subtle empathy between women in her tone.
Yao Yuan raised her eyes to Liu Qingzhuo. Those especially bright eyes held a bold, open beauty in the way their light shifted—such a vivid, brisk temperament was a rare thing. She lifted her glass in return, already clear in her own mind. "Girlfriend"—business partner, out together for some fun: deepening the bond of the alliance while keeping each other company, loosening up, and dispelling boredom.
Wang Qian clapped the shoulder of the man beside him. "Chen Ye, my buddy—he and Young Boss Gao have been close since they were running around bare-bottomed as kids."
The man wore a black shirt of crisp, well-cut fabric, the cuffs casually rolled up to reveal a watch on his wrist—simple in style but far from cheap. He raised his eyes and nodded to Yao Yuan; his features were refined and handsome, his complexion on the fair side, and he wore rimless glasses, his gaze as calm and gentle as a young professor who teaches and writes at a university. "This guy's family really does sit on a mine—a gold mine," Wang Qian said, waggling his eyebrows. Chen Ye pushed his glasses up, a slightly helpless smile surfacing, and raised his glass to Yao Yuan in greeting—an easy, unhurried motion, without the swagger so common in third-generation mining heirs. Something stirred in Yao Yuan's mind: Chen Ye's gentleness and calm were out of place in this clamorous, lavish room. He was more like a bystander, quietly watching everything in the room. What sort of thoughts lay hidden beneath that reserve? She returned the toast without a flicker, her smile proper—neither deliberately drawing close nor deliberately keeping distance.
Yao Yuan responded to each in turn, her smile gracious.
Several rounds of introductions, several rounds of clinking glasses—the crisp ring of glass against glass rose and fell, mingling with laughter and off-key singing, swiftly filling the room. Alcohol became the most capable messenger, quietly melting away the distance of first meetings and the walls of status. An eager, loosened ease had already floated up in the room; the fuse of indulgence had long since been lit. Someone picked songs, someone sang along; Wang Qian gripped the microphone and howled golden oldies, while Gao Jieyuan, arm around his companion, belted love songs in an exaggerated tone, his eyes still flicking toward her now and then. Yao Yuan simply reclined into the sofa, fingertips circling the stem of her glass, her toes tapping lightly to the beat now and then, her mind perfectly clear—she kept this distance on purpose, for the more detached she seemed, the more it stirred the conqueror's itch in men like Gao Jieyuan. It was a little trick of hers for controlling the room: effortless, yet keeping her always in the driver's seat.
The differently colored liquor bottles on the coffee table gradually emptied.
"Just singing is dull—let's play a game!" Gao Jieyuan suddenly proposed, an eager light flashing in his eyes. Yao Yuan saw through his intent at a glance—nothing more than using the game to close the distance with her, maybe even to manufacture some suggestive physical contact. She laughed inwardly but didn't refuse; it was only a game. She rather wanted to see what tricks Gao Jieyuan could pull off—and she had only to counter each move as it came, keeping the initiative all along.
At some point a clear patch had been cleared on the coffee table, and a deck of strikingly designed black playing cards with gilded edges was slapped down "snap" in the center. Gao Jieyuan was clearly well-versed in this; he shuffled the cards expertly, the corners of his mouth curling into a playful smile. "Just drinking and singing is so dull. Come, let's play something more direct—'the King's Command.' Everyone knows the rules, right? Whoever draws the King card draws one from the command cards, and whatever it says, you do. Don't do it—" he paused, his gaze sweeping over everyone, lingering a moment on Yao Yuan's flushed face, "—and it's three forfeit drinks, neat."

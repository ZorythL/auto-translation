Chapter 108 — Everything Is a Cage; To Follow One's Own Heart Is Freedom
https://www.xbanxia.cc/books/417648/72640355.html

Everything is a cage; to follow one's own heart is freedom.

But the standoff did not last—

Hmmmm!!!

A low, sweeping mechanical roar, carrying the pressure of absolute authority, came without warning from above the laboratory's pure-white dome.

The sound was nothing like the room's background hum. It was older, more majestic, more… exalted. As if it were the heartbeat of the world itself.

The faces of the six laborer clones went white as paper in an instant.

It was not fear. It was something deeper—an instinct written into their genetic code: absolute obedience.

Their bodies bent slightly, beyond their control; their heads bowed. It was the posture countless generations of clones had been preset to assume before their originals. Even with their minds full of resentment and rebellion, the chains in their genes tightened at once, until they could barely breathe.

Yao Yuan jerked her head up and looked toward the dome.

The once seamless white ceiling had split open into a web of fine golden lines. The lines spread and interlaced with astonishing speed, until they formed a complex, dazzling array of light.

The glow flared.

Six figures, accompanied by a heart-stopping ripple in space, slowly descended from the array and solidified onto the floor.

The light faded.

Six people stood quietly on the raised platform at the center of the laboratory.

Their features, their bone structure, the contours of their faces…

Identical to the six trembling laborer clones in front of them.

Identical, too, to the six men in Yao Yuan's memory—the ones she had been tangled with in love and hate inside the sandbox world.

But their bearing was a world apart.

It was the looseness, the languor, of those who have lived at the top for so long, fed by endless resources, never knowing real scarcity or struggle. Their eyes were clear, yet carried a careless detachment, as if everything before them—including the six clones who wore their faces—was no more than irrelevant background scenery.

Their very existence radiated an invisible, suffocating air pressure of "class."

The Source Realm. Native-born naturals. The originals of the six laborer clones.

The true "masters," seated at the top of the first fish pond, looking down on all living things.

The air in the laboratory froze solid.

"No wonder. For the last few Ruins Realm cycles, the 'labor attrition rate' has been fluctuating strangely, and the performance data they reported had subtle logical flaws."

The man standing in the middle—the one who wore Zhuge Jinye's face—spoke mildly. His voice was not loud, yet it carried a natural authority; every syllable pressed clearly onto every heart in the room.

His gaze swept idly over the six deeply bowed clones who dared not lift their heads, as if scanning a few malfunctioning tools. At last it settled on the only one standing upright, the only one looking back at him: Yao Yuan.

Something flickered through that gaze—a clear surprise that quickly thickened into a more probing, faintly amused scrutiny.

"So it was you six pieces of stock—quietly diverting the Source Realm's gene-bank permissions to run an illegal private experiment."

The word "stock," tossed off so lightly.

It crushed the last shred of the six laborer clones' dignity, and made their absolute place in this world utterly clear: tools. And tools about to be discarded.

The six clones went rigid all over, fingernails biting deep into their palms, yet not a single thought of resistance or protest could rise. Before the true original, the shackles of their genes were an absolute, unbridgeable chasm.

The original "Zhuge Jinye" stepped slowly down from the platform. Ignoring the six "copies," he walked straight up to Yao Yuan. He tilted his head slightly, like a man admiring an interesting work of art he had unexpectedly acquired.

"Native-natural gene sequence, copied to a high degree of fidelity… purity even exceeding those six rejects," he murmured to himself, fingertips twitching unconsciously, as if invisible streams of data were sliding past before his eyes. "No stand-in-contract contamination, a fully natural growth trajectory, extremely high consciousness integrity—and she's even broken through the lower-plane barrier…"

He lifted his eyes to Yao Yuan, the corners of his mouth curving into a smile that held no warmth, yet was striking enough:

"Interesting. You pieces of stock, blundering along, actually produced a 'sample' far more interesting than you worn-out 'tools' yourselves."

A single sentence shattered every scheme and every hope the six laborer clones had held.

And in that instant it carved out the three cruel, crystal-clear tiers of the laboratory:

The Source Realm's top-tier native naturals—they make the rules, enjoy the spoils, born to be masters.

The Ruins Realm's middle-tier laborer clones—they carry out the rules, bear the burden, born to be servants, and about to be phased out.

The sandbox-realm bottom-tier breakthrough, Yao Yuan—an accidental product, an observation sample, a new and more promising "tool" candidate.

Three tiers, sharply separated, yet all bound fast by invisible chains.

The original "Zhuge Jinye" raised his eyes, his gaze drifting lazily over the six ashen-faced laborer clones. His tone was casual, yet carried the absolute indifference of one deciding the life and death of others:

"You diverted gene sequences without authorization and ran an illegal cross-plane experiment, in grave violation of the Source Realm's Basic Law on Clone Control. By that law, the clones involved should be forcibly scrapped at once, the laboratory permanently sealed, all data erased."

The six clones shuddered violently; despair drowned them like a flooding tide.

"However," the original's tone shifted, his gaze returning to Yao Yuan, full of undisguised appraisal and interest, "in view of this 'accidental result,' I can give you a chance to atone."

He extended a finger and pointed lightly at Yao Yuan:

"Tame her. Control her. Strip away her autonomous consciousness, but preserve her growth potential and her breakthrough traits. Then deliver her to me, whole."

His voice was calm, yet it issued the cruelest of orders:

"I want her to become my new, more perfect, more malleable… ultimate stand-in sample."

"As for you—" his gaze swept coldly over the six clones, "if you do well, perhaps you'll be spared scrapping and demoted to secondary maintenance units. If you do poorly…"

The unfinished words were more terrifying than any threat.

Boom!

The six laborer clones jolted as if struck by lightning, their whole bodies convulsing. Their heads snapped up, and for the first time their eyes blazed with uncontrollable, extreme panic and the deepest despair.

For untold ages they had endured, gambling everything, scheming down to the last detail, building Yao Yuan—and all they had sought was a sliver of a chance to break free of their shackles.

And what was the ending they got in the end?

The "hope" they had raised with their own hands would replace them, becoming a tool more convenient to the master's grip.

While they themselves would lose even the right to be "tools." Their best fate was to sink into lower-grade "stock"; the more likely one, to be utterly "scrapped."

All their struggling, all their scheming, before the true rule-makers at the top, was nothing but a futile joke. They had not won. Yao Yuan had not won. The winners were always those seated calmly on the fishing platform, harvesting every fruit at their leisure.

Above the fish pond, there is another fish pond.

Above the harvesters, there are still more harvesters.

This game, it seemed, would never end.

The laboratory fell into a deadlocked three-way struggle, the air so taut it seemed it might burst at any moment:

The Source Realm original wanted Yao Yuan, the superior "new tool," and through her meant to clear away the disobedient "old tools."

The six laborer clones did not want to be replaced and scrapped, but the genetic shackles made it impossible to resist the original, and their only road to survival was to destroy with their own hands the Yao Yuan they had created—the one who might also be the only being who understood their pain.

Yao Yuan—unwilling to be tamed, dissected, or defined as a tool by anyone, whether clone or original.

Everyone was struggling.

Everyone was a pawn.

Everyone was trapped in their own tier of the "fish pond," straining to swim out, yet perhaps only swimming into another, larger net.

And there, in that suffocating dead silence.

There, as the Source Realm original frowned slightly, seemingly displeased by the "tools'" sluggishness.

There, as the six laborer clones' eyes wavered violently, swinging between genetic obedience and self-destruction.

Yao Yuan suddenly, softly, laughed out loud.

The laugh was faint, yet it pierced clearly through the congealed air and drew every gaze.

She lifted her head, her gaze passing calmly over the Source Realm original high above, over the agonized, struggling laborer clones, and finally up to this pure-white laboratory dome—the symbol of absolute control and reason.

Her eyes were astonishingly clear. There was no anger in them, no fear, no despair—only something that had pierced through all the fog and seen some essential truth…

Understanding, and compassion.

She spoke quietly. Her voice was not loud, yet strangely it sounded in the depths of everyone's heart:

"So this is the whole of the truth."

"The bottom struggles, believing it is fighting against fate."

"The middle bears its burden, believing it is searching for a way out."

"The top looks down, believing it is in control of the game."

Her gaze drifted slowly over the twelve faces before her—identical, yet utterly distinct (six originals, six clones)—and the smile at the corner of her mouth deepened, and grew colder:

"You all keep asking—where is the way out? Where is freedom? Where is the key to breaking out of the cage?"

"You look up, and there are sheer cliffs on every side. You look down, and there are weaker strugglers. So you tear at one another, scheme against one another, using the blood of those below to try to grease your own rusted-shut shackles."

She took a step forward. Though she stood in the lowest position of all, her presence seemed to rise without limit.

"But have any of you ever considered—"

Her gaze came at last to rest on the face of the Source Realm original, "Zhuge Jinye." It was so calm that even this top-tier natural, who had weathered every storm, felt a flicker of nameless unease.

"What if the 'upper tier' you imagine yourselves to be is also nothing more than 'fish' in another, larger pond?"

"What if your so-called 'original,' 'Source Realm,' 'top-tier authority' is also nothing more than an 'observation sample' or 'management tool' designed by some higher being?"

"What if these layer upon layer of fish ponds, this endless harvesting, this seemingly impregnable hierarchy and these rules—"

Her voice suddenly dropped, yet it carried an earth-shattering force:

"What if all of it is, in itself, an endless, meaningless… infinite loop?"

In the laboratory, the silence was deathly.

The pupils of the Source Realm original contracted, almost imperceptibly.

The six laborer clones lifted their heads, dazed.

Yao Yuan looked at them, looked at all this absurd cruelty, and the last trace of anger and resentment in her heart suddenly dissolved like smoke.

In its place came a calm she had never known, and… freedom.

It was freedom on the level of awareness. A freedom that rose from the depths of the soul, undeniable, once one had seen through the essence of all rules, all lies, all oppression.

I am I.

My experience is what is real.

My pain and my joy are the proof that I existed.

Whether that existence is "natural" or "manufactured," "real" or "virtual," "original" or "clone."

So long as "I" am feeling, thinking, choosing—then "I" am free.

What is free is not the circumstance, but the eyes with which one views the circumstance, and, under that gaze, the will to still choose how to "exist."

She suddenly remembered the sandbox world: the contract-breaking ceremony by the Yellow River, the bronze mirror sinking into the water, that ancient saying, the plain yet steadfast words her mother Zhang Fengxia had spoken in the hotpot shop…

All the threads, in this moment, drew together into one clear line.

All beings suffer; all beings are prisoners.

But what imprisons you was never a cage or chain of any visible form.

What imprisons you is your own acceptance of the "cage," your fixation on a "way out," your belief that only "swimming to some larger fish pond" counts as freedom.

And true liberation…

Perhaps it is realizing that you have always been in the water, yet no longer being obsessed with "reaching the shore."

It is, right here in this water, swimming at your own rhythm, feeling the current, gazing up at the light of the sky—even knowing that all around you there may still be countless other "fish ponds."

It is, within this infinitely nested absurdity, still choosing, clear-eyed, with dignity, and even with a trace of a smile, to go on…

"existing."

Yao Yuan slowly raised her hand. It was not an attack, nor a defense.

It was only to brush lightly at the lingering tear-track on her cheek—a track she did not know was real or false.

Then she looked at the Source Realm original, looked at the six clones, her gaze as calm as a deep pool:

"I will not become your new tool."

"Nor will I become the sacrifice for your self-salvation."

"And still less will I… join your infinitely descending game of tearing one another apart."

Her body began to give off a soft yet undeniable glow. It was not the light of power, but the radiance that arises naturally when consciousness is supremely concentrated and one's sense of existence has become utterly clear.

"My experiment is over."

"Your experiment is over too."

"But 'existence' itself… that greatest of all experiments, designed by no one yet joined by everyone—is far, far from over."

Amid the sudden change in the Source Realm original's expression, under the dazed gaze of the six laborer clones, Yao Yuan's figure began to blur, as if she were slowly separating from this pure-white laboratory, from these layer upon layer of planar cages.

Her voice, like a final saying, echoed through every inch of space, branded into the depths of every consciousness that heard it:

"We are all prisoner-fish; only the ponds that confine us differ.

Some are trapped in the tangles of love and hate, some in the barriers of class, some in existence itself.

But to glimpse the boundary of the pond is already the first step toward freedom.

And true freedom…

Perhaps begins with no longer asking, 'How do I break the pond and reach the sea, and rush toward freedom?'

It begins with admitting that the place this body dwells is already the sea—and then, right here in this small square of pond,

clear-eyed,

with compassion,

with a smile for yourself and for all your kind trapped in the same pond…

living as a piece of scenery that no one can define, and that needs no one to define it."

The glow swelled to its utmost.

The next moment.

In the pure-white laboratory, there remained only the Source Realm original, his face ashen, and the laborer clones, their expressions lost and dazed.

And a brand-new, possibility-filled…

silence.

(The End)

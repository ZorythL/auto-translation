Chapter 43 — Yao Yuan Uses Godmother-of-Feelings Logic to Dismantle Jin Lan's Ex
https://www.xbanxia.cc/books/417648/72640290.html

It was right then that a small commotion broke out at the edge of the dance floor. A young man — fashionably dressed, rather handsome — was struggling to push past the security guards, shouting something frantically toward the second-floor booths. You could just make out the words "Lanlan," "let me through," "let me explain." His eyes were locked hard on Jin Lan's spot upstairs.

It was Jin Lan's ex, the one she'd dumped not long ago. Yao Yuan remembered him vaguely — some small-time model and influencer, nearly ten years younger than Jin Lan. For the whole length of the relationship, everything from his clothes and shoes to his car and rent had been bankrolled by Jin Lan. Then she'd found out he was secretly using her money to court a younger college girl. Jin Lan cut him off on the spot, severed every cent of support, and tossed him out.

So losing his "sugar mama" had made life hard, and now he'd come crawling back, hungry for the pasture again. Yao Yuan stopped dancing, her breath not yet steady, her chest still rising and falling, and watched the farce downstairs with cold eyes. Up on the second floor Jin Lan had seen it too. Her face darkened in an instant, and she made a hard, cutting gesture at the guards.

The guards immediately blocked him harder. Seeing no way through, the ex actually dropped to his knees with a thud at the edge of the dance floor. Indifferent to the startled stares and the phones filming him, he started wailing at the top of his lungs: "Lanlan! I was wrong! I really know I was wrong! I can't live without you! Please, give me one more chance! I love you!"

The music played on, but his sobbing cut through part of the noise, drawing more sidelong glances and murmurs. Jin Lan went white with fury and rose to her feet to come down.

But Yao Yuan got there first. She parted the crowd and walked over. Her golden figure stood out sharply in the chaos, like a moving beam of judgment that demanded to be noticed. She stopped a few steps from the man kneeling and howling on the ground and looked down at him. Her face still carried a fading flush and a sheen of sweat, but her eyes had returned to that calm, dissecting sharpness.

"Save your energy. Stop the act." Her voice wasn't loud, yet it carried a strange clarity and force that pierced the din, making the man's wailing catch in his throat. She sized him up, her gaze like a scalpel, sweeping from the obviously last-season designer clothes on his body to his face — gaunt from agitation and probably hard living, yet still carefully groomed — as if she were analyzing the most ordinary case study. "You say you love her? Let's break down this so-called 'love' of yours."

She tilted her head slightly, as if genuinely thinking it over, her gold earring tracing a cool arc with the movement.

"In this relationship, what you ate, wore, used, and lived in — even that designer coat on your back, out of season but still designer — your social spending, all the capital that let you keep up 'appearances,' it all came from her. She was your client, your investor, the hand that fed and clothed you. And you, as the contractor, your core value and job description was to provide emotional value, to look after the client's feelings and needs, to keep this partnership pleasant and stable. It was a perfectly clear value exchange."

Her tone was even, her words precise — no insults, only calm statement, yet more humiliating and corrosive than any abuse. The people close enough to hear let out murmurs of sudden understanding and snickers. The man's face shifted from agitated red to ghastly pale.

Her words were cold and naked, like a boning knife, stripping the man's "love" costume clean off. The people who heard let out low jeers and laughter. The man's face went from red to white, then from white to green, fury and shame mixing as he jabbed a finger at Yao Yuan. "You — what are you talking about! Lanlan and I are truly in love!"

"Truly?" Yao Yuan gave a soft laugh, the sound cutting clearly through the loud music. "Your 'truly' should mean flawless performance of the contract. So how did you perform?" She went on, her tone even carrying a note of professional regret for a failed case. "During the contract term, holding the budget and resources the client provided, you not only failed your core duty as the contractor — that is, making the client feel respected, satisfied, and treasured — you used your position to go behind the client's back and develop your so-called 'personal business,' trying to use the client's investment to court another potential target. That's a serious breach of professional ethics. It's a breach of contract. It's a betrayal of the spirit of the agreement."

She paused, giving him — and everyone around with ears pricked — a moment to digest the cold logic.

"So when the client discovered your serious dereliction and your breach, and in keeping with the spirit of the contract — there was no paper contract, but social rules and basic decency are a contract by default — terminated the partnership and cut off all resources, that was a perfectly reasonable business decision. And your behavior now —" her gaze swept over his kneeling posture and the tear tracks on his face, without the slightest ripple of feeling, "— this isn't a plea for love. It's that, having lost your job, you've found you can't land another 'position' with the same pay and terms, so you're trying to use emotional blackmail and the pressure of public opinion to force your former client to overlook your major misconduct and rehire you. It's very unprofessional. And very… ugly."

Every sentence was like a cold nail, pinning his carefully groomed costume of "love" and his pose of "repentance" firmly to the pillory of "exchange of interests" and "breach of contract." The man opened and closed his mouth, wanting to argue back, only to find that any defense of "feelings" looked pale, powerless, even laughable in front of her seamless, de-emotionalized business analysis. He reached out a futile hand, pointing at Yao Yuan, his finger trembling, but he couldn't get out a single word — left with nothing but the fury and despair of someone seen through and stripped bare.

"Know your position." Yao Yuan gave him one last look, the kind you'd give the summary of a failed investment case. "A contractor has to have a contractor's self-awareness. The partnership is over, so exit with dignity, raise your own value, and go find your next suitable client. Clinging and groveling is the cheapest and most useless thing you can do."

She wasted no more time on him and turned to Jin Lan, who had hurried over and stood off to the side with a complicated look on her face. Her tone slipped back to its usual calm: "Jin Lanlan, the best way to handle a business breach is to cut clean and stop the loss in time. There's no need to pour any more emotion or time into a write-off asset. Security — please see this gentleman out. Don't let him spoil the other guests' experience."

The guards moved in at once. This time their handling had less of the roughness reserved for a troublemaker and more of the businesslike manner of "removing an unqualified supplier," hauling out the man, who had gone completely limp, his face the color of ash.

A farce wrapped up abruptly, in this extremely rational, even cold-blooded way. The music on the dance floor took over again, but the way many people looked at Yao Yuan had changed. It was no longer simple admiration for a beautiful woman, but a mix of awe, wariness, and deep curiosity — this woman could actually use the language of business to so thoroughly deconstruct and end an emotional dispute.

Yao Yuan walked back toward the booth area, her steps steady. Jin Lan followed behind her and let out a heavy breath, as if expelling a long-pent-up stuffiness from her chest, looking at Yao Yuan with a complicated expression. "Yuanyuan, I… I really don't know what to say. What you said back there was crueler than slapping him, but it worked better than any comfort."

"I just helped you sort out the facts." Yao Yuan sat back down, took the ice water Gao Jieyuan eagerly handed over, and drank a sip to wet a throat gone a little dry from all the talking and analyzing. The intense flush from her exertion was slowly fading, replaced by a lazy calm that came from spending both body and mind.

Jiang Tiantian was recording on her phone's notepad at an almost feverish speed, fingers flying, the eyes behind her lenses startlingly bright, glancing up at Yao Yuan now and then with a look full of admiration for a "master-class case study." Gao Jieyuan had put away his earlier clingy teasing too. The look in his eyes toward Yao Yuan now held, beyond desire, a clear layer of wariness and… an even stronger urge to conquer — a woman like this was too hard to control, but also too challenging, too alluring.

Zhao Yiming's gaze fell on Yao Yuan's calm profile. She had just completed a brilliant piece of "live teaching" and "crisis PR" built on her own methodology — airtight logic, a sly angle, fully weaponizing an emotional problem to striking effect. He held his wine glass, fingertips brushing lightly against the cold wall of it, the light behind his lenses deep and unreadable. He suddenly found himself wanting to know: within her "emotional business model," how would she define himself — or rather, the complex relationship between them, built on AI model development and tangled with technical discussion, mutual interest, and a subtle attraction? Client and contractor? Partners? Or… some new kind of experimental project not yet classified?

"A very efficient solution." Zhao Yiming finally spoke, his voice still even, betraying little. "Converting a nonstandard emotional problem into a standard case of contract and risk management cuts down on unnecessary emotional drain. Your 'emotional firewall' logic applies just as thoroughly to real-world relationships." His words were both an analysis of what had just happened and, it seemed, a faint echo of their earlier discussion about an AI emotional model's "immune rejection."

Yao Yuan raised her eyes to him, and their gazes met briefly in the air again. She could feel the unspoken probing beneath his words. She smiled, the smile carrying a trace of after-the-fact weariness and a subtle interest, like meeting a worthy opponent. "Thank you for the recognition, Mr. Zhao. The underlying logic is simply the same. Protect core interests, define the boundaries of rights and responsibilities, build a firewall — whether in the world of code or in human relationships, those are the foundations for lowering system risk and keeping things running stably. Only, the variable of the human heart is sometimes harder to predict than data." Her words were part answer, part a new riddle tossed back, about "variables."

Gao Jieyuan watched the subtle atmosphere flowing between them once more — the kind built on some high-dimensional understanding that left outsiders no room to enter — and his brow furrowed almost imperceptibly. Then it smoothed into an even brighter grin, carrying a touch of boyish, unwilling-to-lose spirit, as he leaned back in, his voice going softer, full of deliberate dependence and flattery: "Sis~ you were amazing back there! I was completely stunned! You're basically my idol! From now on, when I'm out there, I've got to learn from you so I don't get played. Sis, you have to teach me…" He was trying to use this low-posture flattery and plea for help to pull Yao Yuan's attention back into a more ordinary mode of interaction that he led — to break the unsettling, "above-it-all" rapport between her and Zhao Yiming.

The night was still deep, and the waves of sound and shifting lights of Liuyue Palace surged on. The contest of desire, the duel of reason, the testing of identities, and that subtle, never-fully-calculable variable called "attraction" — against this intoxicating backdrop, they kept sketching out a complex and seductive scene. Yao Yuan sat serenely within the fortress she had built — reason as her client, charm as her blade — enjoying control, analysis, and the occasional indulgence. Only, when Zhao Yiming's calm eyes — eyes that seemed able to see through every surface — turned her way, somewhere inside her solid "emotional firewall," in some deeply hidden protocol layer, there seemed to ripple a faint, barely detectable need to reassess risk.

The clamor of Liuyue Palace hit some saturation point in the small hours, then began to slide slowly toward a slack ending. The music still thundered, but the dance-floor crowd had thinned considerably, leaving behind a floor of empty glasses, flickering shards of light, and a sense of post-revelry exhaustion. The few people in the booth all showed varying degrees of drunkenness.

Jin Lan leaned back into the sofa, her eyes a little unfocused, but a habitual host's smile still hung at the corner of her mouth — only beneath it showed a deep fatigue. Clearly the aftershock of her ex's farce was far greater than she let on. Zhao Yiming still sat upright, only he had taken off his glasses and was slowly pressing the bridge of his nose with his fingertips. Under the cold white light, the usual layer of detachment on his face had been softened a little by the alcohol, revealing a rare, human kind of weariness.

Gao Jieyuan was the most visibly drunk. He talked more than before, his gestures bigger, sitting against Yao Yuan with nearly half his body leaning into her, calling her "Sis" in an ever sweeter, stickier tone, his eyes fixed on her, burning with alcohol and an unsatisfied, intensifying desire. Yao Yuan felt light-headed too, her temples throbbing, as if a warm, lazy fire were slow-cooking inside her, making her thoughts drift and her reactions lag a beat. But the alertness in her bones remained. When Gao Jieyuan tried once more to wrap an arm around her shoulders, she used the motion of reaching for her glass to slip away without a trace, leaving him only a golden-sheened profile, close at hand yet untouchable.

Jiang Tiantian was still relatively sober, though she had six or seven parts of a buzz on. She insisted on calling a designated driver herself, and before leaving she confirmed once more with Yao Yuan her intent for the follow-up interview. Then, swaying but thoroughly satisfied, she left hugging her briefcase.

"That's about it. Let's break it up." Jin Lan pushed off the sofa's armrest and stood, swaying once. Zhao Yiming reached out to steady her lightly.

"I'll take you." Zhao Yiming's voice was a little low and rough, but clear. He and Jin Lan lived in roughly the same direction.

Jin Lan didn't refuse. She nodded, then looked at Yao Yuan, her eyes carrying concern and a trace of understanding. "Yuanyuan, you…"

"I'll take Sis!" Gao Jieyuan cut in at once, springing up so suddenly that his body swayed before he found his footing. He thumped his chest, working hard to look reliable. "Sis Lan, don't worry, I promise I'll get her home safe! My driver's waiting right outside, and the car's nice and roomy!"

Yao Yuan raised her eyes, her gaze pausing for a second on Gao Jieyuan's eager (or rather, anxious) face, then skimming over Zhao Yiming, who stood quietly to the side. Zhao Yiming was looking at her too, the eyes behind his lenses unreadable in the dim light, only the gaze calm, carrying a clarity that cut through the alcohol, somehow steadying her drifting mind. But then she thought: let him take her? No — that would introduce more complicated variables. Gao Jieyuan was a nuisance, but he was straightforward; the upside was he was easy to manage and easy to get rid of.

"Mm, then I'll trouble Young Boss Gao." Yao Yuan smiled at Jin Lan by way of reply, then braced herself on the edge of the coffee table and slowly stood. The hem of her long golden dress dragged a little, and she stumbled slightly. Gao Jieyuan immediately reached out to support her, but Yao Yuan had already steadied herself. She only rested her hand lightly on his offered arm, borrowing a bit of his strength, her bearing still graceful, still keeping its distance. "Let's go."

Stepping out through the heavy doors of Liuyue Palace, the predawn chill poured over them like ice water dumped on their heads, jolting everyone. Gao Jieyuan's flashily styled sports car was already parked at the entrance, and the driver respectfully opened the door. Yao Yuan waved at Jin Lan and Zhao Yiming and bent to slide into the back seat. Gao Jieyuan wanted to squeeze in after her, but Yao Yuan had already naturally scooted inward, setting her clutch on the empty seat between them, and said with a smile, "I get a little carsick. I want to lean against the window for some air."

Gao Jieyuan's "I'll sit in the back too," already at the tip of his tongue, had to be swallowed. He rubbed his nose sheepishly, turned, and went to the front passenger seat.

The car slid soundlessly into the empty predawn streets. Outside the window the city's neon still glittered, but it had shed the peak-hour clamor of the night, looking quiet and remote. Yao Yuan leaned against the cold window and closed her eyes. The drink and the fatigue surged up like a tide. The lights and sounds of the club, the tear-streaked face of Jin Lan's ex, Gao Jieyuan's scorching stare, Zhao Yiming's silent gaze… all of it spun and wove through her mind in fragments, then slowly settled and blurred.

Gao Jieyuan kept turning back from the front seat to find topics, his voice buzzing a little in the sealed cabin. Yao Yuan mostly just put him off with an "mm" or an "oh," answering once or twice, her eyelids growing heavier and heavier.

She didn't know how long it had been when the car stopped. "Sis, we're here." Gao Jieyuan's voice pulled her back from the edge of half-sleep.

Yao Yuan opened her eyes. Outside the window stood her familiar upscale apartment building, looming silently in the faint predawn light. "Thank you." Her voice was slightly hoarse as she pushed open the door. Gao Jieyuan immediately hopped out too, wanting to help her, to walk her up.

"No need, Young Boss Gao." Yao Yuan straightened up, and a gust of night wind cleared her head a good deal. Her tone was gentle but firm. "It's very late. You should head back and rest soon too. Thank you for today." She smiled at him, and even in the cold predawn light that smile was still perfect — yet like a pane of transparent glass, it kept every possibility of closeness shut outside.

Gao Jieyuan looked at her and opened his mouth, as if he still wanted to say something, but meeting the unquestionable clarity in her eyes, he finally swallowed his words and forced out a somewhat reluctant smile. "Then… get some rest soon, Sis. Good night."

"Good night."

Watching the taillights vanish around the corner, Yao Yuan finally turned, pulled her black cashmere coat tighter around her, and walked toward the apartment lobby on still-unsteady steps. The night-shift guard nodded to her in greeting, and she dipped her head slightly in return.

The elevator car was as bright as a mirror, polished to a shine. Yao Yuan stepped in, pressed her floor, and then out of habit looked at herself in the mirror. The woman in the glass — her makeup, washed by a night of revelry and sweat, had grown a little patchy, with clear traces of weariness under her eyes. But those eyes, even now with the drink not yet faded, still kept a sliver of sharp core. Under the elevator's ceiling light, the long golden dress still shimmered with a luxurious yet faintly tired luster, like a costume after the curtain falls on a magnificent play.

She looked at herself in the mirror, at those features at once familiar and somehow strange, at the yellow diamond at her throat that even now glittered with a deep glow. The alcohol made her thoughts slow and viscous, and some distant, deeply buried fragments of memory seemed to surface unbidden — Shanghai at thirty, that ambition that felt as if it could own the whole world…

The elevator rose smoothly.

Suddenly, the mirror's surface, like a lake into which a stone had been dropped, rippled out a circle of strange, soundless waves. The center of those ripples was aimed straight at her face.

Yao Yuan froze for a moment, thinking it was the drink blurring her eyes. She blinked.

The ripples didn't vanish — instead they spread, faster and faster, more and more violently. The image in the mirror began to twist, stretch, deform. Her face, the hem of her golden dress, the elevator wall behind her, all flowed and intertwined like a melting wax figure. A familiar pull — stronger and more irresistible than the time at "Crimson Night Palace" — surged suddenly from deep within the mirror!

The world spun and reeled. She sank into unconsciousness.

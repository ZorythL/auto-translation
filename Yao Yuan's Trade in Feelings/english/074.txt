A Bond Sealed in Midsummer, Love Plain to See
Early July, midsummer blazing like a furnace. At six in the evening the sun still hung arrogantly high in the western sky, pouring down scalding gold without mercy. The air felt set solid, as if baked by fire, and every breath drawn into the lungs carried a searing heat. Summer in Jinshi was dry and violent, its fierce, scorching edge sparing no one.
Yellow River Tower stood on the riverbank, and on its top floor the "Guanlan room" had floor-to-ceiling glass on three sides, taking in without reservation the Yellow River winding away to the north and the vast, hazy mountains on the far shore. Inside the room the air conditioning sent out a soothing coolness without a sound, drawing a clean line between this space and the world outside, where the sun raged unchecked. The setting sun was sinking slowly in a posture both tragic and magnificent, dyeing the whole length of the Yellow River the color of molten gold. The water shimmered, broken flecks of gold dancing on it, so bright it was almost impossible to keep one's eyes open. In the distance, on the iron-boat tea stalls built out over the river's shallows, you could see figures sitting at ease on the boats, leisurely sipping covered-bowl "three-steep" tea against the grand sweep of the river. That ease, and the coolness inside the room, were like two parallel worlds — neither disturbing the other, yet both belonging to this fierce midsummer.
Almost everyone had arrived. Wang Qian sat alone on the sofa by the window, the cup of plain tea before him long gone cold, gazing at the endlessly surging Yellow River outside, his eyes vacant, his thoughts drifting somewhere unknown. Liu Qingzhuo and Chen Ye sat close together; Liu Qingzhuo was holding up her phone to show Chen Ye a new wine-list design, and Chen Ye leaned in, tapping and pointing at the screen, the two of them murmuring to each other. Jiang Tiantian had taken hold of Jin Lan and was rattling off, at top speed, the new in-depth interview series she was planning, her eyes glinting with excitement.
Just then the door was pushed open by a waiter, and Yao Yuan stepped gracefully in. A wave of heat seemed to try to slip in with her, only to be swallowed instantly by the cold air. Today she wore a halter-neck slip dress in true red silk that perfectly showed off the lovely line of her shoulders and neck and her clean-cut collarbone, paired below with a pair of white high-waisted, side-slit suit trousers — crisp and yet sexy. Her thick, long curls had been carefully styled, draped loosely behind her shoulders, swaying gently with each step. Her makeup was bright and retro, lips like fire, skin fairer than snow, and the two gold tassel earrings at her ears swung and shone as she moved, the whole of her dazzlingly, flamboyantly beautiful.
Right behind her came Zhao Yiming, in a light gray-blue cotton short-sleeved shirt with dark gray casual trousers and a plain black watch on his wrist, giving off a clean, fresh air that came straight from the laboratory. The two walked in one after the other, and though there was no show of intimacy between them, Zhao Yiming, very naturally, turned to one side and lightly steadied the doorframe, not letting go until Yao Yuan had fully entered. That small but protective gesture did not escape several sharp pairs of eyes in the room.
"Aiyo! We've been waiting forever for you two!" Jin Lan was the first to spring up from her seat, her gaze sweeping over the pair like a searchlight, the corner of her mouth instantly curling into a mischievous "I knew it" smile. "Boss Yao, you've got the sunset itself wrapped around you, haven't you? Such an eye-catching red! And Boss Zhao, in that outfit… standing next to Boss Yao, you're a tall, upright green pine — steady as anything!"
Yao Yuan shot her a smiling, chiding look but said nothing, greeting everyone with easy poise. Zhao Yiming, too, nodded politely to the company, his expression as composed as ever.
After the round of greetings, everyone took their seats. Exquisite cold dishes and ice-chilled drinks were soon on the table, and the mood in the room livened up at once.
Beside Jin Lan sat a tall, handsome face no one knew — Lu Ang, twenty-nine, once a professional basketball player, with a buzz cut, wheat-colored skin, and a bright, easy laugh. These days he ran several chain fitness brands. Through the meal he looked after Jin Lan with the utmost care, pouring her tea, serving her food, passing her napkins, his movements practiced and natural, his eyes barely leaving her for a moment, a warmth intense enough to melt a person. In front of him, Jin Lan's usual queen-of-the-business-world edge had softened considerably; her eyes curved, and now and then he made her laugh out loud, and she would even give his arm a light, half-petulant tap.
"Sister Lan, this is… aren't you going to introduce him?" Liu Qingzhuo asked with a smile, raising an eyebrow.
Jin Lan took a light sip of chilled champagne, her gaze sliding sideways to Lu Ang, her tone carrying its usual swagger and lazy pride: "Lu Ang, the trainer from my gym. Well? Pretty good physique he's built, don't you think?"
Lu Ang at once grinned wide, showing a mouthful of white teeth, and raised his glass on his own initiative: "Hello, everyone, I'm Lu Ang. Learning a lot from Sister Lan."
Chen Ye smiled and clinked glasses with him: "Trainer Lu looks full of energy. Sister Lan has quite an eye."
"Of course!" Jin Lan said, not a hint of modesty, swirling the glass in her hand. Lu Ang smiled beside her, his eyes glued to her the whole time.
On Liu Qingzhuo and Chen Ye's side, Chen Ye was carefully cutting the tenderest meat off the roast lamb leg and setting it in the small dish in front of Liu Qingzhuo. Liu Qingzhuo, meanwhile, said in a low voice to Yao Yuan, "Yuanyuan, do you have time soon to get afternoon tea together? There's something I'd like to ask you about in private." Her voice was soft, but Yao Yuan caught at once, in the depths of her eyes, that particular caution belonging to a mature woman.
"No problem at all, anytime." Yao Yuan understood immediately, raising her glass to touch it lightly against Liu Qingzhuo's. Liu Qingzhuo's wine business was thriving, Chen Ye came from a well-off family, and the two were steady together. Though Liu Qingzhuo was on her second marriage, both were in their thirties, and the move toward marriage had come naturally. What this so-called "asking for advice" meant, both of them knew perfectly well. Adult love, however ardent, still requires — at the threshold of marriage — a clear-eyed weighing and protecting of one's own interests; that is both maturity and necessity. All the more so since Liu Qingzhuo was stepping into marriage a second time, and would naturally treat it with greater care.
Gao Jieyuan had come uninvited.
This young master, ever since the last New Year's gathering, had launched against Yao Yuan a loud and, in his own mind, romantic "campaign of pursuit." Nine hundred and ninety-nine air-freighted roses piled up at the company front desk, frenzied attention-seeking in her livestream room, asking around everywhere about Yao Yuan's schedule to engineer all sorts of "chance encounters," even an attempt to serenade her in public at a banquet once… an extravagant, dazzling array of tricks. Yet Yao Yuan's only response was polite silence. The roses, once signed for, she had her assistant distribute to the entire staff; in the livestream room she set his tips to be blocked; at the "chance encounters" she would nod in acknowledgment and walk on by; and at the public serenade, she simply left early, citing an important work call.
It was probably the first time he had ever run into such a tough bone to chew on the "field of love," and the colder Yao Yuan was, the more his roused competitiveness and urge to conquer twisted and swelled. Later, when he learned that Yao Yuan had once been together with Yu Hao, it all became clear to him — he finally understood why Yu Hao had sparred with him that time at Crimson Moon Palace. Then he heard the two had already broken up, and secretly rejoiced, thinking his chance had come, getting ready to save up for one big move.
But tonight, the moment he stepped into the room and saw the kind of wordless rapport between Yao Yuan and Zhao Yiming — a single glance, a small gesture, and their hearts were understood; saw Zhao Yiming pour Yao Yuan's tea so naturally (she tapped the rim of the cup lightly with her fingertip, and he stopped at exactly the right moment, the amount of tea just right); saw the times their eyes met, and that calm smile that belonged to the two of them alone, as if silently exchanging some complex message… the careless, cynical smile on Gao Jieyuan's face froze in an instant, and the little unrealistic fantasy in his heart shattered, with a "crack," into bits.
He forced himself to sit down, putting on a show of calm as he tossed out jokes and banter, but his eyes, beyond his control, kept stealing glances that way. He saw Zhao Yiming explaining in a low voice the low-temperature slow-cook technique used in one of the innovative dishes, Yao Yuan listening with full attention, her profile especially soft in the light; saw how, when Yao Yuan spoke, Zhao Yiming's gaze stayed fixed on her face — a focus built on intellectual equality, on deep mutual understanding and appreciation, nothing at all like his own kind of pleasure-hunting gaze that saw only the surface.
Gao Jieyuan felt as though a jar of mixed flavors had been knocked over inside him, every taste surging up at once. He, Gao Jieyuan — illustrious family, young and handsome, always smooth-sailing on the field of love — yet facing Zhao Yiming, a man who had built a vast commercial empire from nothing in the hardcore tech field, found himself crushed across the board: in wealth, in the scope of his enterprise, in personal ability, in that steady, reserved aura of having everything under control. Apart from the trifling advantage of being "younger," he could not find a single point on which he could stand against the man. And Yao Yuan, clearly, wanted not "youth" but the "height" and "depth" of someone who could resonate with her soul and look down on the world at her side.
After a few rounds of drinks, the mood grew livelier still. The unholy fire in Gao Jieyuan's heart, mixed with the liquor, came surging straight up, and he couldn't help himself; with a hint of provocation, he spoke up toward Zhao Yiming: "Boss Zhao, I hear your 'Western World' has been tinkering with some kind of emotional AI lately? This thing… can it really be reliable? Cold, lifeless code — can it understand love between men and women, the scheming of the human heart?" His tone was full of a wastrel's contemptuous doubt toward a field he didn't understand.
The laughter and chatter at the table died down at once.
Zhao Yiming set down his chopsticks, picked up a wet wipe and cleaned his hands, then lifted his eyes to Gao Jieyuan, his gaze calm as still water, his tone carrying the steadiness of a researcher laying out the facts: "Mr. Gao, affective computing is a frontier field at the intersection of cognitive science and artificial intelligence. Our work is not about making a program 'possess' feelings. Rather, through algorithmic models, we attempt to understand and simulate the role that emotional factors play in human beings' complex social relationships and decisions, and the logical patterns behind them — the goal being to provide more rational analysis and decision-making support. As for the scheming of the human heart," he paused, a flicker of sharpness passing behind his lenses, "that is a complex problem on another level, involving game theory, psychology, and several other disciplines. Our model takes it into account as an important variable, but the original intent is to understand the underlying laws and provide a tool — not to encourage scheming."
This speech, clear in logic, neither obsequious nor overbearing, both answered the question and, without a trace of effort, drew a clear line — making Gao Jieyuan's amateurish, frivolous doubt look hopelessly pale and shallow. Choked, Gao Jieyuan went red and then white in the face, opened his mouth wanting to retort but had no idea where to begin, and only after a long while muttered sullenly, "…Making it sound so mystical."
"Mystical or not, it comes down to actual results." Yao Yuan suddenly gave a light laugh, picked up her glass, let her gaze sweep past the sour-faced Gao Jieyuan, and turned to Zhao Yiming, her eyes flowing with appreciation and a faintly perceptible intimacy. "Boss Zhao's emotional AI is precisely a project he's collaborating on with me." On the surface this was about a product, but everyone at the table heard the other layer of meaning in it.
Gao Jieyuan's expression grew worse still; he tipped his head back and threw down a big mouthful of liquor.
Zhao Yiming looked at Yao Yuan, the corner of his mouth lifting just slightly, the tiniest arc, and raised his glass to her — everything understood without a word.
By the time they were warm with drink, the stars were already mirrored in the water outside, the night scenery along both banks of the Yellow River brilliant with light, like a glittering ribbon of silk.
Zhao Yiming picked up his glass and lightly rapped its base against the table. The laughter and chatter gradually stopped, and everyone's eyes turned toward him.
"Borrowing Boss Jin's fine spot here to gather with old friends," Zhao Yiming said, his voice steady and clear, looking around at everyone before his gaze came at last to rest on Yao Yuan's face beside him, the deep affection in his eyes plain to see, "there's something I'd like to share with you all — and to make an official announcement of, you could say."
He paused, and amid the knowing or curious looks of the company, reached out and gently took hold of Yao Yuan's hand where it rested on the table. Yao Yuan's fingers were slender, done in red nail polish; they stirred slightly in his palm but did not withdraw, letting him hold them, only the roots of her ears flushing the faintest red under the brilliant lights, her face wearing a frank, bright smile.
Zhao Yiming looked at everyone, his tone solemn, and said, word by word: "Yao Yuan and I — we've decided to officially be together. On the premise of sharing a life and supporting each other, we are seriously seeing each other."
The moment the words fell, the room went quiet for a beat.
"Whoa——!" Jin Lan was the first to cheer, raising her glass high. "Fantastic! Congratulations, congratulations! Didn't I say it! The two of you standing together — that presence is an absolute perfect match! You'd better be fiercely happy!"
"Congratulations, Boss Zhao, and to you, Boss Yao!" Chen Ye also raised his glass with a smile. "This is strong joining with strong, a match made in heaven!"
Liu Qingzhuo and Jiang Tiantian likewise offered their sincere blessings at once. Wang Qian, holding his teacup, smiled and nodded: "A good thing. A perfect couple — my blessings to you both." Only Gao Jieyuan's face changed and changed again; he forced out a smile uglier than crying, raised his glass along with the rest, mumbled a "congratulations," and tipped his head back to down the harsh white liquor in one go. The burning rushed straight down from his throat, yet it could in no way press down the churning sourness and defeat at the bottom of his heart.
Amid the chorus of blessings, Yao Yuan too rose smiling to her feet, glass in hand. Under the brilliant lights, her bright face took on a moving flush, light flowing in her eyes — both the calm of dust finally settled and a certainty about the future. "Thank you, everyone. We… will walk this road well." Her words were short and firm. She and Zhao Yiming looked at each other and smiled, that smile full of rapport, trust, and a deep affection that needed no words. The two glasses touched lightly, ringing clear and sweet, and the two of them tipped their heads back and drained their wine.
The mood at the table was extraordinarily lively, and everyone began toasting and exchanging pleasantries. Yao Yuan, glass in hand, walked toward Wang Qian. The "heart-guarding" old jade she always wore against her skin, tucked inside her clothes, had at some point slipped out a small way from her collar. The lustrous white jade, set off against the intense red, looked all the more warm and pure, and the few natural threads of pale purple staining within it turned with a mysterious sheen under the crystal chandelier overhead.
"President Wang, I'd like to drink to you." Yao Yuan teased this old friend she'd known since her younger days with a bright, smiling word.
Wang Qian had looked up with a smile on his face, but in the instant his eyes touched that gleam of white at her throat, it froze stock-still! His pupils contracted sharply, and a flash of extreme shock — mingled with horror, with sudden realization, and with something of a deeply buried memory struck hard — shot at astonishing speed through the depths of his ever-calm eyes! But he was, after all, a man long seasoned in the world, and that storm-like surge of emotion was forced back down almost in the same instant. Using the motion of lowering his head to set down his teacup, he perfectly concealed that moment's lapse.
When he raised his head again, his face had recovered its mild, smooth smile, as if he were merely a connoisseur who had spotted a piece he admired, and he asked in an ordinary tone: "Yuanyuan, this pendant of yours… looks very special. It's old jade, isn't it? Where did you come by it?" He deliberately used the respectful term insiders reserve for old jade.
Yao Yuan hadn't expected him to notice the jade. She started slightly, instinctively raising a hand to tuck it back inside her clothes, then smiled and brushed it off to cover herself: "An elder gave it to me, said wearing it would calm the spirit. As for where it came from exactly, I never asked in detail — I liked the look of it, so I've worn it every day."
"Calm the spirit… mm, fine jade really does have a warm, nourishing effect." Wang Qian nodded, his gaze leaving her collar to lift his own glass, touching it against the rim of hers, his tone still casual, as though just making idle conversation. "Especially old jade with this kind of 'burial-shroud staining' — that's not often seen. This elder of yours must be a person of great fortune." He deliberately used the insider's term "burial-shroud staining"; anyone truly in the know would have heard the probing in his words.
Yao Yuan knew only a little about appraising jade and thought nothing of it, taking it for a casual compliment, and laughed: "You really know your stuff. If even you say it's good, then it can't be wrong." With that, she drained her glass.
Watching the slender, graceful line of her neck as she drank, Wang Qian's eyes were churning with waves at their depths. He recognized this jade! This was clearly one of the treasures Old Chen of the Guangu Hall kept at the very bottom of his chest — unless it were for a closest of friends, or a matter of the most special and perilous kind, the old man would never lightly give it to an outsider! Now this jade had appeared on Yao Yuan, and linking that to what he'd faintly heard of late — that someone was quietly asking after "the descendants of the mirror-masters" and "the Kui-Dragon Cloud-Pattern Mirror"… the fingers Wang Qian had around his glass actually went a touch stiff.
At that very moment, Jiang Tiantian timely raised her glass, the clear ring of the toast breaking this brief swell of tension. "Boss Yao, perfect timing — taking advantage of this happy day, I'd like to formally extend you an invitation," Jiang Tiantian said, her eyes blazing, full of professional passion. "Over this past half year, I've studied no small amount of your livestreams and the 'Pretty Big One' case library — I've filled a thick stack of notes. Your insight into contemporary women's emotional predicaments, into the games people play, and into the meeting point of traffic and commerce — it's just so profound, so inspiring! I'm pouring everything into building a brand-new in-depth profile interview series, and for the very first heavyweight guest, I truly feel it can only be you. Let's talk about the power of women in this era, about clear-eyed growth, and about how you've turned your understanding of human nature into a remarkable business. Next month, early August — does the timing work for you? Let's record a proper episode."
Yao Yuan was a little taken aback, but quickly saw the sincerity and professional anticipation in Jiang Tiantian's eyes, and she nodded gladly: "For Tiantian to invite me in person — that's my honor. We'll pin down the timing later; I'm very much looking forward to this conversation."
The gathering went on, the night deepening, the Yellow River's nighttime scenery outside growing ever more enchanting. Yet beneath this lively surface, the inner state of everyone in the room had quietly shifted. Some were immersed in the sweetness of love, some savoring the warmth of companionship, some calmly planning the future, some despondent and grieving, some who had discovered an earth-shaking secret yet chose to spy on it in silence, and still others rubbing their palms together, ready to open a new chapter in their careers.
This seemingly ordinary midsummer gathering of old friends was like a pebble dropped into the long river of fate; the ripples it raised were quietly spreading, unseen, beneath the surface, inevitably affecting and altering the course of many a story. And Yao Yuan, who had just confirmed a new relationship, while feeling her happiness and security, had not the slightest sense that a person who knew her greatest secret — and the danger she was in — had, like the most patient of hunters, quietly taken a seat at her side, and had chosen the most dangerous silence of all.

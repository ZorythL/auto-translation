Pretty Big One Emotional AI Sets Sail: A Battlefield of Fate With Everyone in Their Seats
October in Jinshi, and the sky was that lofty, crystalline blue that only autumn can scrub clean. The wind carried a dry scent of grass and leaves and a faint sweetness of ripened fruit, brushing every corner of the city — and brushing, too, the enormous standee planted before the Western World Technology headquarters, printed with "Pretty Big One Emotional AI 1.0 — Launch Gala."
Months of preparation, and at last the moment had arrived.
The launch was held in the new-media hall of the city arts center. Inside, the design was full of futurism — silver-gray and deep blue as the dominant tones, the lines crisp and clean. On the vast circular main screen, a pale-gold "Tracing the Light" tree turned and grew in slow rotation, streams of data flowing quietly among its branches like stars. The guests had gathered in force. Besides the familiar faces from tech, investment, and media circles, what drew the eye most were the women — exquisitely dressed, each of a different bearing — the first hundred core members of Yao Yuan's "Sisterhood League." They were the first batch of "experience officers" for "Pretty Big One," and the most solid support network Yao Yuan had drawn together over the past years through clear-eyed wisdom. In one corner of the venue, in a dedicated trial zone, a few were already being guided by staff into early conversations with "Pretty Big One."
Yao Yuan wore a champagne-gold off-the-shoulder satin fishtail gown that traced her graceful, upright figure. Her long hair was swept into an elegant chignon, a few loose strands framing her cheeks. Her makeup was a carefully drawn "goddess look," emphasizing bone structure and poise, the lip color a warm-gold mauve that echoed her dress. A long diamond chain swayed and caught the light at her ear with every movement. She stood at the center of the crowd, exchanging pleasantries and greetings with guests from every quarter, her smile gracious, her eyes bright — unquestionably the focal point, and the queen. Zhao Yiming, in a perfectly tailored dark-gray suit, stood a little apart, talking with several important technical partners, his expression focused. Now and then he glanced toward Yao Yuan, and when their eyes met, there was a steadiness between them that needed no words.
Everything was going to plan — grand, smooth, full of anticipation.
Until the leadership from the Provincial Department of Science and Technology arrived.
When that newly appointed deputy director — famed for a deep background and for being young and accomplished — stepped into the venue with several aides, the once-flowing crowd produced a subtle stall and a ripple of murmurs. Zhao Yiming immediately went to meet him, a genuine smile on his face, reaching out to shake his hand — clearly the two were not strangers.
Yao Yuan adjusted her expression too, wearing the proper, deferential smile of respect for a senior official, and turned her gaze that way.
And then her breath, for a thousandth of a second, hitched almost imperceptibly.
Jiang Hai.
After all these years, time had left little wear on him; if anything, it had added a more composed weight. He wore a well-fitted navy civil-service jacket, his bearing still straight, his face leaner and more refined than in her memory, and deeper. That ease of one inside the system — yet somehow beyond its usual frame — gave him a halo of his own even standing in a crowd. He was shaking hands and talking with Zhao Yiming, the line of his profile the familiar arc from Yao Yuan's memory, and yet strange, as if a lifetime lay between.
Zhao Yiming led Jiang Hai toward the head-table area, gesturing toward Yao Yuan at the same time. Jiang Hai turned his head, and his gaze fell, naturally, on her.
Their eyes met.
For that instant the air seemed drained of all sound.
The moment Jiang Hai's gaze touched Yao Yuan's face, it visibly trembled. Not deliberate — an unguarded astonishment yanked up violently from the depths of memory. He clearly hadn't expected that the "extraordinarily accomplished female partner," the "top insight-leader in emotional AI" Zhao Yiming had spoken of, would be Yao Yuan. There was a flicker of blankness in his eyes, quickly replaced by a complex, swiftly suppressed scrutiny. In that scrutiny was an appraisal of her startling transformation and achievement, a faint stir at the sudden surfacing of the past, and perhaps a thin trace — one even he might not admit — of lament at how the people endure while everything has changed.
After its brief loss of balance, Yao Yuan's heartbeat steadied with even greater force. She was the first to compose herself. She stepped forward, extended her right hand, her smile flawless, her voice clear and steady: "Director Jiang, welcome — we're honored to have your guidance. I've heard so much about you." She used the most standard, safest social formula, locking everything past and personal firmly beneath a professional shell.
Jiang Hai recovered just as quickly. He took her hand — the pressure measured, the duration exact. His palm was dry and warm, his eyes now a deep, steady warmth, carrying a senior's approval for an outstanding entrepreneur. "Boss Yao, a pleasure. Yiming often mentions you. Seeing you today, you are indeed remarkable. Congratulations on the successful launch of 'Pretty Big One' — this is a direction in our province's AI-application field very much worth watching." His words were airtight, but Yao Yuan could feel that, as he held her hand, there was an almost imperceptible, subtle weight laid on the two words "Boss Yao" and "remarkable."
"You flatter me. None of this would be possible without policy support and the backing of all our leaders." Yao Yuan answered with ease, withdrawing her hand, naturally returning the lead to Zhao Yiming. In a handful of seconds, an unexpected reunion that spanned young love, the wounds of class difference, and long years was compressed into a standard, dignified piece of official small talk with no fault to find. Only in each of their hearts did they know what storms had once raged beneath that calm surface, and into what complicated grit they had settled.
This was only the first surprise.
The launch ran smoothly. Yao Yuan's address was clear in logic and broad in vision, positioning "Pretty Big One" as "a professional, deployable emotional solution + full-cycle growth companionship," stressing both its nature as "a problem-solving tool" and its humane care as "emotional companionship," winning applause several times over. Zhao Yiming's technical explanation made the profound simple, and paired with the stunning demonstration of the "Tracing the Light" tree, it married hard-core technology and visions of the future perfectly. The provincial department leader's brief official remarks affirmed the project highly from the policy level and lent it authoritative endorsement, empowering its development.
The whole program came to a satisfying close, and the networking dinner opened in turn.
No one had foreseen that the careless arrangement of seats at the head table would push the "surprise" of this launch toward a climax of dramatic tension that even Yao Yuan had not anticipated.
Whether or not it was coincidence, the head table held the provincial department leaders, the key investors, the core media representatives — Yao Yuan and Zhao Yiming naturally among them — and Jiang Hai, seated there as an important guest. Then Shuai Hongqiang and Yu Hao, of all people, appeared at the table one after another. Both claimed they had heard the news and come especially to offer congratulations; neither had received a formal invitation, but given their standing, the staff naturally dared not turn them away, and seats were arranged.
So, when Yao Yuan settled in beside Zhao Yiming and lifted her eyes to the far side and both flanks of the round table, an absurd, almost black-comedy sensation seized her.
Going clockwise: Zhao Yiming (current boyfriend and business partner), Jiang Hai (first love, now deputy director of the Provincial Department of Science and Technology), one empty seat (investor representative), Shuai Hongqiang (ex, tied to the Mirror Pact, local entrepreneur), Yu Hao (former "arrangement partner," would-be father of her child), and back round to herself.
The ex, the ex before that, the ex before that, and the current. All gathered under one roof to celebrate the new chapter of her career. This was no celebratory banquet — this was an offline, live-action retrospective of the "Annals of Yao Yuan's Love Life," staged meticulously by fate, with a high-end social battlefield thrown in as a bonus.
Shuai Hongqiang was the first to come over of his own accord. He was decently dressed today, his color reasonable, but his eyes, when they met Yao Yuan's, still carried a complexity he couldn't fully hide. He first raised his glass to her, a forced openness in his smile: "Yao Yuan, congratulations! An achievement this big — I had to come beg a glass of wine off you." Then his gaze turned to Zhao Yiming beside her, and to Jiang Hai in the seat of honor, his manner respectful and courteous.
Yao Yuan grasped it at once: this was the cue for her to "introduce." Unruffled, smiling, she turned first to Zhao Yiming and Jiang Hai: "Yiming, Director Jiang, let me introduce — this is Boss Shuai, Shuai Hongqiang, an outstanding local entrepreneur from Jinshi, and an old friend of mine of many years." She defined her relationship with Shuai Hongqiang as "old friend" — clear, safe, and drawing a sufficient distance.
"Boss Shuai, this is Boss Zhao — Zhao Yiming, founder of Western World, and now my business partner." She introduced Zhao Yiming first, her tone natural. Though Zhao Yiming was a little surprised by this suddenly appearing "old friend," he at once showed a host's courtesy, rising to shake Shuai Hongqiang's hand: "Boss Shuai, welcome. Thank you for your support."
"Boss Zhao, I've long admired your name — glad to finally meet you." Shuai Hongqiang's grip was firm, his eyes sweeping quickly over Zhao Yiming's face. In that look was a sizing-up of this "current one," a comparison, and perhaps a trace of indescribable dejection — all masked beneath a polished social face.
Then Yao Yuan looked toward Jiang Hai and went on: "This is Deputy Director Jiang Hai, of the Provincial Department of Science and Technology."
"Director Jiang, hello! It's an honor, truly!" Shuai Hongqiang stepped up at once, his posture lowered a touch further, clasping Jiang Hai's hand in both of his. Jiang Hai kept his usual warmth and sense of distance, nodding with a smile: "You're too kind, Boss Shuai. The growth of enterprise depends on the efforts of people like you."
At that very moment, Zhao Yiming's gaze shifted, almost imperceptibly. He had caught the complex look in Shuai Hongqiang's eyes when they fell on Yao Yuan — a look that was anything but that of an ordinary "old friend" — and he had noticed the overly "standard" detachment in Yao Yuan's introduction. The keenness honed in the marketplace let him sense in an instant some invisible thread of the past winding among these three. But he said nothing. He merely lifted his glass and, under cover of drinking, quietly tucked that curiosity away, his face still wearing the proper smile of a host.
Amid the pleasantries, Yu Hao arrived too. He still carried the air of a Shanghai elite. Seeing Yao Yuan, his eyes brightened — and then, noticing Zhao Yiming beside her and Shuai Hongqiang exchanging greetings with Jiang Hai, that brightness quickly dimmed, veiled in a proper but unmistakable shadow of loss. He walked over, aiming squarely for Yao Yuan, his smile a little strained, his tone carrying a hint of intimate reproach and the sourness of one shut out of an important moment: "Yao Yuan, congratulations. A moment this important — I nearly missed it."
Yao Yuan sighed faintly inside, but on the surface her smile was full and bright, drawing a line with her form of address: "Boss Yu, you joke. You're a very busy man — how could I dare disturb you lightly?" She smiled, using the honorific and the courtesies to draw a clear boundary, then turned to Zhao Yiming and Jiang Hai: "Director Jiang, Boss Shuai, this is Boss Yu — Yu Hao, an investor from Shanghai, a friend." She used "friend," a degree more distant than "old friend," and buried their never-public "arrangement" completely.
Seeing Yu Hao, Zhao Yiming did in fact recognize him, and rose to shake hands courteously: "Boss Yu, we meet again. Thank you for your support." His manner was that of a pure business associate reunited — but Yu Hao's overly familiar "Yao Yuan," and the look in his eyes when they fell on her, a look of regret and reminiscence he couldn't quite hide, set the alarm bell in Zhao Yiming's mind ringing faintly once more. Without a flicker, he shook hands and exchanged pleasantries with Yu Hao, while his gaze slid, in passing, from Yu Hao's face over to Shuai Hongqiang's side, then lightly across the unreadable Jiang Hai in the seat of honor, and finally back to Yao Yuan beside him — her bearing impeccable, yet faintly radiating a sense of "command over the battlefield."
Yu Hao shook hands with Zhao Yiming, his smile a little dry: "Boss Zhao, congratulations. This project of yours has tremendous momentum." But his gaze drifted helplessly toward Yao Yuan, and seeing the natural, close warmth between her and Zhao Yiming, he quickly drew it back, his fingertips tightening slightly around his glass.
Jiang Hai merely gave a faint nod. Yu Hao sat down, but his eyes kept drifting toward Yao Yuan, and each time she and Zhao Yiming shared a natural low word or glance, he quickly looked away and bent his head to drink. His mood now was no doubt complex beyond words — loss at how quickly she had moved on (and to someone so outstanding), regret over the absurd suggestion he had once made, and above all a wistfulness at watching her ascend a higher stage while he had already lost the standing to stand beside her.
Zhao Yiming looked at these few men at the table, and the way they looked at Yao Yuan let him taste something out of the ordinary.
But he was Zhao Yiming — a man who had weathered great storms, who could command the situation in both the lab and at the negotiating table. He pressed down the complex tangle that surged up in an instant — curiosity, scrutiny, and a very faint displeasure — and his smile stayed flawless, even more composed than before.
As host, he warmly attended to every one of these "guests." He earnestly discussed with Jiang Hai the details of the province's AI-support policies; courteously sought Shuai Hongqiang's views on the local business environment; even tried to explore some investment trend with the apparently distracted Yu Hao. He introduced Jiang Hai as a "good friend" to Shuai Hongqiang and Yu Hao, and tended attentively to Yao Yuan's "old friend" Shuai Hongqiang and her "friend" Yu Hao. That very attentiveness, instead, added a touch of irresistibly comic absurdity to the subtle awkwardness pervading the table.
Yao Yuan took it all in.
She watched Jiang Hai, beneath the easy bearing of an official as he handled Zhao Yiming's technical-policy questions, glance now and then at her with a thoughtful eye; watched Shuai Hongqiang, as he worked to join the conversation, look at her with a guilt that hovered on the edge of speech; watched Yu Hao, behind his forced cheer, with a loneliness and unwillingness he couldn't hide; and then looked at Zhao Yiming beside her, keeping the mood alive...
The initial tension and absurdity gradually faded, and a strange, almost transcendent calm rose from the bottom of her heart.
Once, every one of these men had written a stroke into some chapter of her life — bold and vivid, or light and sketchy, or piercing to the bone, or never to be reconciled. They stood for the longing, struggle, choice and being-chosen, hurt and growth of her different stages.
And now they sat at the same table, gathered because of the career achievement that "Yao Yuan" the person had created (whatever private thoughts each of them carried). She was no longer the "object" they scrutinized, judged, chose, or abandoned. She was the protagonist of this banquet, in name and in substance — the one who had built the stage, set the rules, and moved among them all with ease.
She smiled, raised her glass, and rose with grace itself. Her gaze swept calmly across each differently-shaded face at the table.
"Today, that 'Pretty Big One' can set sail successfully is owed to everyone present, and to all the friends here, for their unwavering support and trust." Her voice came clear and pleasant over the glass, carrying the sincerity and force of one who owns the room. "This is not merely the launch of a product, but the beginning of a new journey about understanding, growth, and connection. I drink to you all — in gratitude for the past, and in still greater anticipation of the future. May we all be more clear-eyed, and more free."
She drained the glass in one go, her manner free and clean.
Jiang Hai looked at her long and deep, raised his glass in acknowledgment, and drank slowly to the bottom, the last ripple in his eyes settling into a deep-pool calm and the recognition that comes after scrutiny. Shuai Hongqiang hurried to drink along, his eyes complex, but his blessing genuine. Yu Hao drank too, his smile still a little strained, yet some fixation seemed to go down with the wine. Zhao Yiming raised his glass as well, unhurried in his movements, his gaze never leaving Yao Yuan — a gaze of clear admiration, of tacit agreement, and of a wordless support that said, "I understand. I'm here." He drank with composure, as if everything subtle he had just sensed were merely an inconsequential, flavor-adding side dish at this banquet of success.
A "gathering of exes" that should have churned with hidden currents and brimmed with awkwardness was quietly defused — through Yao Yuan's absolute core presence, her clear sense of her own role, Zhao Yiming's wisdom in seeing all yet choosing to play along as guardian, and the unspoken understanding between the two of them. It was even turned inside out into a silent proof of how solid their relationship was, how deeply they trusted each other, and a coronation of Yao Yuan's personal charm and command.
The banquet went on. Lights, music, the sound of conversation. Those past debts of feeling, knots of the heart, unfinished stories — all became the liquid swaying in the glasses of this moment, swallowed, digested, and then made part of the energy that keeps rushing forward.
Yao Yuan sat back down beside Zhao Yiming, and under cover of the tablecloth, slipped her hand lightly into his palm. Zhao Yiming caught it at once, steadily, his fingertips brushing the back of her hand with a warmth of comfort and recognition. He tilted his head and said, low, in a voice only the two of them could hear: "Brilliant." He paused, then added, with a trace of teasing and understanding, "Your 'old friends' all carry a lot of weight."
Yao Yuan glanced at him sidelong, laughter flowing in her eyes, and lowered her voice too: "So now — do you feel your 'business partner' is a little more trouble than you imagined?"
Zhao Yiming looked at her, his eyes deep, and slowly shook his head, his tone earnest and low: "No. I feel my judgment is far better than even I gave it credit for."
The two smiled at each other, and some understanding and connection that needed no words quietly deepened amid the clink of glasses and the lingering undertow. Yao Yuan turned her head back, looking toward the center of the venue. There, the "Tracing the Light" tree still turned its radiance quietly, a symbol of reason, insight, connection, and infinite possibility.
However many entanglements lay in the past, however many complicated gazes surrounded her now, she knew clearly where her true heading lay. Those men were coordinates along the road she had come — bright or dim, marking the detours and shoals she had once crossed. And the future — her course — would, like the "Pretty Big One" she had helped create with her own hands, point toward a vaster, clearer, freer deep sea of human nature.
And so the launch came to its satisfying close, in this strange and eerie atmosphere.

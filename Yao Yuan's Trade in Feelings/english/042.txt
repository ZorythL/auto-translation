Chapter 42 — The Hunting Ground of Liuyue Palace

Liuyue Palace sat at the far end of the city, a club that had been hailed as top-tier the moment it opened. As the car pulled up to the entrance, Yao Yuan looked through the window at the building's exterior — a minimalist, futuristic black geometric mass, its outer walls clad in some new kind of smart material that reflected and refracted light. At that moment, in time with the muffled waves of sound seeping out from inside, it streamed with shifting, dazzling, dizzying colored light — like mercury, like lava, like the aurora — as if the building itself were alive, breathing, pulsing. The entrance was a narrow, towering black doorway that seemed to swallow light whole, and above it glowed three Chinese characters in seal script — "Liuyue Palace." The forms were ancient, but the light was a cold, ghostly blue: heavy on design, carrying an air of mystery and seduction.

The instant she stepped out of the car, the dull, massive bass — still perfectly audible even after passing through those thick walls and doors — rose up through the soles of her feet, like a heartbeat from deep within the earth, hammering at everyone's chest. A long line had already formed at the door, kept in order by tall, stone-faced security guards in black uniforms. The men and women waiting were all fashionably dressed, their makeup immaculate; even in the biting wind of a winter night, they held their backs straight, showing off their best selves, their eyes a mix of anticipation, anxiety, and a faint, barely perceptible vanity. Jin Lan was clearly a regular. She led them straight to the VIP entrance on the side, where waiters in black suits with earpieces were already waiting respectfully, silently pulling open that heavy, glossy black door that seemed to absorb every sound.

Sound, heat, light and shadow, and a thick smell that mixed expensive perfume, alcohol, hormones, and some kind of hallucinogenic fragrance came crashing into their faces like a tangible tide, swallowing them whole in an instant.

Unlike the lavish, dreamlike KTV atmosphere of the "Crimson Night Pavilion," Liuyue Palace was a pure, contemporary nightclub space, full of futurism and sensory impact. In the center of the vast hall, whose ceiling rose nearly ten meters, stood a huge floating DJ booth made of multi-tiered metal. On it, a DJ in a fluorescent skintight outfit and enormous headphones worked the turntables in a frenzy, body convulsing to the heavy beats he was creating, like a priest performing some mysterious rite. From the ceiling, hundreds of programmable LED light spheres and laser emitters formed a complex web of light, which now — with every drumbeat, every scream of the music — shifted into dizzying geometric patterns, explosive trails of light, and sharp beams that sliced through space. All around were split-level booth areas, divided by semi-transparent glass with light patterns flowing inside. Every table held a glowing ice bucket, oddly shaped drinkware, and softly burning mood lamps. The air was so thick it seemed to have texture; every breath carried a cloying, intoxicating scent that quickened the pulse.

The dance floor was already a churning, writhing sea of people. Young bodies swayed, collided, and pressed together under the strobing, eerily colored lights, faces drunk or frenzied, every one of them written over with an unreserved chase after tonight, after this very moment, after sensory thrill. The music was industrial, dark electronic house, the drums heavy and dense as machine-gun fire, the high-frequency synths shrieking and tearing at the eardrums, as if striking the nerve endings directly.

The booth Jin Lan had reserved was in the best vantage spot on the second floor, directly facing the DJ booth and looking down over the whole dance floor. A waiter showed them to their seats and quickly brought out the drink package they'd ordered in advance — a full bottle of Ace of Spades champagne, single malt whisky, and several specialty cocktails that changed color with temperature, served in bizarrely shaped glassware, dry ice bobbing in the liquid and hissing up wisps of white mist like some witch's brew.

Yao Yuan slipped off her black cashmere coat and handed it to a waiter, leaving that brilliant golden gown once again fully exposed to the club's kaleidoscopic light. Under the strobes, the lasers, and the self-lit tabletops, the dark patterns woven into the silk seemed to come alive, the golden sheen shifting unpredictably — now like flowing molten gold, now like cold metal — forming a striking visual contrast with her snow-white skin, her crimson lips, and her glittering jewelry. She sank into the soft deep-purple sofa of the booth, long legs elegantly crossed, the diamond-studded toe of her silver high heel tapping lightly at the floor in time with the heavy beat. Her gaze swept calmly over the seething, self-forgetting sea of people below, then over her companions with their various expressions, finally settling on the cold-misting, blue-violet gradient cocktail in her own hand, the corner of her mouth curving into a barely perceptible arc — the arc of an observer.

Jiang Tiantian sat beside her, body slightly stiff, clearly not used to this direct, intense sensory bombardment. But her eyes were busy, darting around like a high-precision camera, recording every subtle expression and movement of Yao Yuan's, recording the environment, recording Gao Jieyuan eagerly pouring drinks, recording the profile of Zhao Yiming calmly sipping his whisky. Jin Lan had already begun swaying to the music, laughing as she clinked glasses with Gao Jieyuan. Zhao Yiming sat in the shadows across from them, whisky in hand, his gaze seeming to rest on the DJ booth — or perhaps to pierce through all the noise and land somewhere in the void.

Gao Jieyuan had quickly gotten into the mood. A few drinks in, with the environment as a catalyst, he was thoroughly worked up, shoulders rocking to the music. He poured himself a full glass of champagne and tipped his head back to down it, his throat bobbing. Then he turned, leaned forward, and over the roar of the music shouted at Yao Yuan: "Boss Yao! This place is electric, right?! Want to dance?"

Yao Yuan raised her eyes to him. The man's eyes blazed with a naked light kindled by alcohol, desire, and the hormones in the air, his smile fervent, carrying an air that brooked no refusal. The deafening music and flashing lights blurred the everyday courtesies of the social scene and amplified a kind of primal aggression. She didn't move. She only lifted the cocktail glass slowly to her lips and took a sip, the cold liquid sliding over her tongue with its complex fruity notes and the sharp burn of spirits. Then she set the glass down, curving her red lips at Gao Jieyuan, the smile heart-stoppingly beautiful in the dreamlike light, her voice carrying through the music — low, but clear:

"I'm tired. I'll sit a while. Go enjoy yourself, Young Boss Gao."

Gao Jieyuan's smile and his movements both froze for a beat, a clear flash of frustration crossing his eyes, followed by an even fiercer flare. But he quickly shrugged it off, dragging out a bigger, devil-may-care grin and raising his voice: "Fine! Then I'll go have fun on my own! Music this good, it'd be a waste not to dance!" With that, he turned and squeezed into the passage that led down to the dance floor, and soon the figure in his wine-red velvet suit vanished into the writhing sea of people and treacherous light below.

Jin Lan watched his back disappear at the stairwell, then leaned in to Yao Yuan's ear, breath warm with perfume against the curve of it, voice carrying a laugh and a meaning only the two of them would catch: "So Jieyuan still hasn't passed your 'risk assessment'?"

Yao Yuan neither confirmed nor denied it. She swirled her glass lightly, watching the blue-violet liquid blend with the dry-ice mist, her voice flat and even: "All investment carries risk; enter the market with caution. Especially an asset whose emotional value swings too wildly."

Jin Lan burst out laughing and clapped her on the shoulder, picking up her own glass: "Brilliant! Come on, forget about them — let's drink to ourselves!"

Jiang Tiantian took in every word and gesture of their exchange, quickly jotting a few keywords into the notes app on her phone. Then she picked up her own glass of juice, which she'd barely touched, and asked Yao Yuan in a low voice — a voice that took real effort to hear over the music: "Teacher Yuanyuan, in a setting like this, how do you usually... position yourself? As an observer, a participant, or... something else?"

Yao Yuan turned her head, and a strobe flash swept across her face just then, lighting the yellow diamond up like a small sun. She looked into Jiang Tiantian's knowledge-hungry eyes behind the lenses and smiled faintly, the smile in the shifting light somehow both genuine and remote:

"All of them, and none of them. Here, you can be anyone, or you can be no one. What matters is knowing exactly why you're here, what you want to get — and what price you're willing to pay." She paused, gaze drifting to the dance floor below, where Gao Jieyuan seemed to have found a partner and was dancing close with a girl in a glittering short dress. "Take him, for instance. He wants a brief sensory thrill and the rush of conquest, and he pays in money, time, and possible aftermath. Perfectly fair."

Jiang Tiantian fell into thought.

Down on the dance floor, the music shifted abruptly, the drums growing denser and heavier, as if pounding directly on the heart. The crowd let out a frenzied roar of cheers and screams. The lights went black, leaving only a few ghostly pale spotlights sweeping the floor. Then a single, blinding white pillar of light slammed down onto a small round stage rising in the center of the dance floor —

A figure in near-transparent fabric covered in silver sequins and tassels writhed up onto the stage like a water sprite, moving to the exotic drumbeat that suddenly exploded into life. Every motion was full of wild provocation, the tassels flying, the body shimmering under the spotlight, igniting the whole room's emotion to boiling point in an instant.

Cheers, whistles, screams pitched high with excitement surged up like a tsunami, almost lifting the roof off.

In this deafening flood of pure sensory clamor, Yao Yuan sat quietly at the border of shadow and flash in the second-floor booth, the golden hem of her gown streaming with a ghostly glow, like some deity standing utterly apart yet impossibly radiant. She watched, listened, felt the tremor shared by her chest and her eardrums, the alcohol burning slowly and steadily inside her.

Zhao Yiming, seated across from her, had drawn his gaze back at some point too. Through the swaying light, the drifting dry-ice mist, the writhing bodies and the rims of glasses, he looked toward Yao Yuan. His gaze was calm, deep, without any trace of admiration or desire — more like he was observing an extremely complex dynamic model, one driven jointly by emotional data, social strategy, biological instinct, and environmental variables.

Yao Yuan seemed to sense his attention. She drew her gaze back from the dance floor and met his across the space.

The music thundered; no one could hear anyone speak.

But, looking at him, Yao Yuan suddenly raised her glass — so faintly it was almost impossible to see.

Then she tilted her head slightly, her gaze turning back to the seething sea below, the symbol of all the uncalculated, chaotic, vivid desire. Her red lips parted and closed; she said something without a sound.

Zhao Yiming read it clearly.

She was saying:

"Happy New Year."

Then she tipped her head back and drank down the blue-violet, ice-cold liquid in her glass in a single swallow. The cold drink collided thunderously with the searing blood inside her, with this seething place, with this brand-new year that had only just begun — a year destined, like every other, to thread its way through precise calculation and uncontrolled risk.

The deafening music soaked every inch of Liuyue Palace like a viscous fluid. The second-floor booth offered a little distance from the crowd, but the low-frequency drumbeat still struck at the spine, setting off waves of resonance. Yao Yuan had drunk down that cold specialty cocktail, and the blue-violet liquor seemed to carry a current, burning all the way from her throat to her stomach, mixing quickly with the alcohol already in her and boiling up into an even fiercer heat. The flush on her cheeks deepened and faded in the shifting light, and as her eyes moved, that clear, scrutinizing gaze softened at the edges into a misty glow, revealing a languid, dazed quality rarely seen in her.

Gao Jieyuan, after going wild on the dance floor for a while, squeezed back to the booth carrying a fresh wave of heat and the scent of someone else's perfume. His hair was damp at the brow, his eyes startlingly bright. He moved straight to Yao Yuan's side again, this time sitting even closer — close enough that she could almost feel the heat radiating off his body. "Sis," he said, switching to a more intimate address, his voice in the gaps of the music deliberately sweet and clingy, holding out a small dish of fruit as if by magic, delicate silver forks stuck in it, "tired from dancing? Have some fruit, sober up a little."

The word "Sis" he drew out with endless twists, both pointing to the fact that Yao Yuan was a few years older than him and wrapping it in a sugar coating of both coquetry and challenge. Yao Yuan turned her head to look at him. In the young man's eyes was an undisguised, conquest-tinged affection, along with a certain self-assurance of "I know I'm young and I have the capital." She smiled and didn't take the dish of fruit, only lifting her champagne glass for a sip, her voice low but clear: "As long as you're enjoying yourself, Young Boss Gao. No need to look after me."

"How could I not," Gao Jieyuan said, setting the fruit dish on the table in front of her and seating himself on the armrest of the sofa beside her. The spot was half-height, letting him look down at her while seeming less overbearing. "Of course I should look after you, Sis. The way you look tonight, you're the center of attention wherever you sit. I have to keep off all the wandering bees and butterflies who don't know any better." As he spoke, his gaze swept pointedly over the others in the booth, lingering half a second on Zhao Yiming, drinking alone a little way off.

Zhao Yiming acted as if he hadn't heard, hadn't seen. He leaned deep into the sofa, long legs crossed, still holding that glass of whisky, its ice nearly melted away. His gaze rested on the shifting light and tide of people on the dance floor below, the line of his profile looking somewhat cold and hard in the flickering light. Only when Yao Yuan gently dodged Gao Jieyuan's hand offering the fruit, and when Gao Jieyuan called her "Sis," did the fingers around his glass tighten almost imperceptibly for an instant, then loosen again, his gaze still resting calmly on the distance, as if none of it had anything to do with him. But a kind of invisible, cold barrier seemed to quietly congeal around him, forming a silent standoff with the searing aggression radiating off Gao Jieyuan.

Yao Yuan took in every silent subtlety between the two men. Gao Jieyuan's attack was blunt and fervent, like a fire burning recklessly with no care for anything; Zhao Yiming's silence was like the deep sea — calm on the surface, but hiding beneath it an immeasurable, perhaps more dangerous power. She was genuinely curious about Zhao Yiming — a curiosity drawn to a top-tier mind and an explorer of unknown territory. Occasionally — watching his profile as he focused on tuning a model under the lab's cold white lights, or catching that flash of near-pure excitement in his eyes after they'd talked through an idea late at night — she would even feel a flicker of something beyond their working relationship, a physical, man-and-woman appreciation. He was clever, calm, possessed of a focus stripped of worldly restlessness, and to Yao Yuan, who had seen every mask of desire there was, that held a distinct appeal. But she had Yu Hao — an easy, well-matched, no-trouble bed partner. More importantly, she knew full well that her collaboration with Zhao Yiming involved enormous interests, and introducing a romantic relationship was the highest-risk variable of all, one that would wreck every carefully calculated balance. So she kept that subtle attraction and curiosity carefully confined within the bounds of "appreciation" and "the flirtation game." She enjoyed that faint, intangible pull, enjoyed the gaze he occasionally turned on her that went beyond data observation — but she never took a single step further. Just like now: she was happy to see the tension formed between Gao Jieyuan's attentiveness and Zhao Yiming's silence, which made her feel she stood at the center of the eye of the storm, safely in command of every flow of emotion.

"Wandering bees and butterflies don't worry me," Yao Yuan said, swirling her glass and giving Gao Jieyuan a radiant smile, a smile of devastating force in Liuyue Palace's hazy light. "What worries me are the little butterflies who think their young wings are strong enough to go crashing about everywhere."

Gao Jieyuan's heart swayed at her smile, and the soft thorn buried in her words pricked him lightly — yet far from annoyed, he found it all the more thrilling. He leaned in closer, almost breathing the words against her ear: "Then teach me, Sis — how should I fly so it doesn't count as crashing about?"

Just then the music changed to another track. No longer the heavy industrial pounding from before, but a rhythm blending retro funk with modern electronics, the melody sexy yet lively, the beat full of bounce, instantly kindling a different mood on the dance floor.

Yao Yuan didn't answer Gao Jieyuan. She suddenly drained the rest of the champagne in her glass, set it down, and stood up. The golden gown flowed like water with her movement, refracting the light sweeping in from every direction; she was like an ornate candelabra suddenly lit in the dim booth.

"This song's not bad," she said, her voice carrying a slight post-drink huskiness and a faint, eager lightness. She didn't look at Gao Jieyuan, didn't look at Zhao Yiming, her gaze cast toward the dance floor below, now writhing all the more merrily to the new music.

"You want to dance, Sis?" Gao Jieyuan's eyes lit up, and he immediately stood too.

Yao Yuan ignored him. She was already swaying her shoulders lightly to the beat, stepping in time, heading toward the stairs down to the dance floor. The ground trembled, and every step she took seemed to land on the music's pulse. Jin Lan let out a whistle, and Jiang Tiantian dropped her observer's posture too, watching with startled, rapt attention. Zhao Yiming's gaze finally drew back from the distance and settled on that golden figure walking step by step into the whirlpool of light below.

Yao Yuan didn't rush eagerly into the densest part of the crowd like the young girls. She found a relatively open spot at the edge of the dance floor and planted herself there. The waves of music wrapped completely around her, the flickering beams sweeping over her whole body now and then. She closed her eyes and drew a deep breath, as if soaking in this baptism of sound and light, and as if shutting out, for now, all the subtle calculation, probing, and rational analysis of the booth.

Then she began to move.

No deliberately choreographed steps, not even a fixed pattern. She simply surrendered herself completely to the music. Her neck and shoulders, her waist and hips, her arms, her legs and feet — every joint, every inch of muscle, seemed to understand that retro-yet-fresh rhythm. Her swaying was gentle at first, like water plants drifting in a deep current, her waist and hips the axis carrying her upper body through smooth, rounded arcs, the golden hem of her gown rippling out in tender waves. Her arms moved naturally and expressively, now stretching like wings, now curling as if to embrace herself, even the faint tremor of her fingertips seeming to speak the breath of the melody.

As the music moved into a fiercer passage, her movements opened up and intensified too. A crisp toss of her head sent a few strands of long hair tumbling loose from their soft knot, brushing across her flushed cheeks and throat. Her footwork turned nimble and springy — forward, back, turning — pivoting, stamping, tapping on the smooth floor, every step steady and precise. With each spin, the golden hem bloomed into a brilliant flower, leaving a dazzling afterimage under the strobes. Her waist was unbelievably supple, swaying to the side, arching back, rising and falling in waves, displaying the beauty of a woman's curves to the fullest — yet without the faintest hint of vulgarity, only a confident sensuality born of powerful control over her own body.

Sweat gradually beaded on her clear forehead and collarbones, glittering in fine flecks under the lights. Her cheeks grew redder and redder, her lips parting slightly with her breathing, moist and crimson. Her eyes were misty, their focus dissolved, no longer carrying their usual clear-eyed scrutiny, replaced by a wholly immersed intoxication captured by the music and the pleasure of her own body. She was no longer the Godmother of Feelings weighing gains and losses, no longer the queen of the social scene navigating men's gazes — she was simply a woman who had utterly released her body's instinct in the music, beautiful, vivid, full of a primal, irresistible allure.

Many on the dance floor noticed her and instinctively cleared a little space for her, their eyes admiring, or envious, or simply struck by her beauty. Gao Jieyuan had long since squeezed in near her, twisting clumsily to the music, his gaze glued to her like adhesive, full of undisguised craving and certainty of victory. He even tried to move in close and dance with her, but Yao Yuan seemed immersed in another dimension; a light pivot turned her naturally away from his approach, and she carried on with her own dance. That self-possessed focus, oblivious to everyone around her, only made her more mysterious and seductive.

Zhao Yiming was still seated in the second-floor booth. From this angle, he could see that shining figure on the dance floor clearly. He watched her spin and sway in the light and shadow, watched the rare look of intoxication on her face with every mask stripped away, watched the sweat-damp hair at her temples and her chest heaving violently with each breath. The hand holding his glass was steady, his expression still calm — only the eyes behind the lenses were deep as a bottomless icy pool, with something settling slowly, coldly, inside them. He didn't show the searing interest Gao Jieyuan did, but that long, unblinking stare carried, in itself, a presence stronger than any words. He watched her as if assessing the running of a most complex dynamic model — and as if simply... watching.

One song ended, and another, slower and stickier, followed at once. Yao Yuan's movements eased with it into a more casual sway, breathing hard, lifting a hand to tuck the damp hair at her cheek behind her ear — a simple gesture that, in her current state, was full of allure. Gao Jieyuan seized the chance and tried to move in close again.

Heir of the Mirror Masters
The gathering broke up, and the guests left one after another.
Yao Yuan settled the bill, but she didn't leave right away. She understood people deeply, with a keen eye for human nature. Ever since she'd moved her company back to Jinshi from Shanghai, she'd gotten to know Jin Lan. At first, Jin Lan had been a client coming to her for advice on matters of the heart. While helping her sort through her troubles, Yao Yuan learned that Jin Lan was the owner of Yellow River Tower. From then on, whenever Yao Yuan hosted a dinner or a gathering, she always booked a private room through Jin Lan. She did this not to chase the discount a friendship might bring, but out of her own particular philosophy of cultivating connections. For one thing, Yellow River Tower was well known in Jinshi's dining scene, and its quality matched Yao Yuan's high standards for a restaurant. For another, money spent anywhere is still money spent — spend it at a friend's place, and you let the friend profit while deepening the bond and connection between you. In the three years she'd known Jin Lan, Yao Yuan had never once asked her to comp a meal. When friends gathered at Jin Lan's place, even when she wasn't the one who'd organized it, she would often quietly pick up the tab. Because she knew well that, at bottom, the maintenance of any relationship is a value exchange. And it was precisely for this reason that, over these three years, her friendship with Jin Lan had grown steadily closer. Of course, Jin Lan also did right by Yao Yuan on certain occasions — showing up at her dinners as the owner, sending over dishes and fruit platters, or comping the bill on the spot, letting Yao Yuan save plenty of face.
Yao Yuan stood side by side with Zhao Yiming on the open-air terrace of Yellow River Tower. The night breeze drifted past, stirring their hair, and the distant rush of the Yellow River carried faintly to them, as if playing a gentle melody for their love.
"I really had a wonderful time today. Everyone was genuinely happy for us." Zhao Yiming drew Yao Yuan's shoulder gently to him, his gaze tender as it rested on her.
Yao Yuan nestled against him, a happy smile on her face. "It's true. With you beside me, everything feels so wonderful. It's just — I kept feeling like the way Wang Qian looked at me today was a little off."
Zhao Yiming frowned slightly, thought for a moment, then said, "I didn't notice anything. Are you sure you're not overthinking it?"
Yao Yuan nodded, but that thread of unease wouldn't leave her. She reached up, almost without thinking, and touched the jade at her neck. Its warm, smooth feel seemed to be quietly reminding her that this jade hid a secret no one knew.
At that very moment, Wang Qian sat in his car, both hands clenched tight, his knuckles white from the force. The look of that jade at Yao Yuan's throat, and her offhand reply, kept circling in his mind.
How could a treasure from Old Chen of the Guangu Hall end up on her? Could that bronze mirror really possess the power to turn back time and shift one's fortune, just as his family's legends said? Wang Qian's mind was full of doubt and wariness. He knew well what that jade signified, and he understood even better that, if the legend of the bronze mirror were true, then Yao Yuan might be...
In fact, Wang Qian was himself a scattered heir of the "Wang"-surnamed family of mirror masters. Though he hadn't inherited the craft and never made a mirror with his own hands, the family's secret lore and the skill of appraising old objects had long since seeped into his very blood.
Some months earlier, when Shuai Hongqiang had failed to collect the money the city owed him and was being pressed by the bank to repay his loan, frantic and at his wit's end, he had come to the bank office to find Wang Qian, who by then had been promoted to deputy president, begging him to broker some leeway — a little more time — and promising to pay off the loan the moment he recovered the debt. Wang Qian knew Shuai Hongqiang's situation: the city had owed him fifteen million in project payments for three years now, while the eight-million loan now being called in was one Wang Qian himself, back when he was a manager, had brazenly talked Shuai Hongqiang into taking out. At the time Shuai Hongqiang's business had been booming, and he hadn't needed the money; Wang Qian had used the low interest rate and the appeal of "helping out a brother" to persuade him to borrow. Watching the once-spirited Boss Shuai brought so low, Wang Qian felt a great deal stir in his chest. But the marketplace is a battlefield, and one bears the risk of success or failure alone. In recent years, the bank had seen countless clients in straits like Shuai Hongqiang's — there was simply no room left for pity. Shuai Hongqiang was an open-handed man, loyal to his friends, but rules were rules, and this was not something Wang Qian could meddle in too much. All he could do was offer a vague promise to do his best to file an application for him.
After chatting in the office for a good while, when quitting time came, Wang Qian — whether out of sympathy or some sense of making amends, he couldn't quite say — invited Shuai Hongqiang to dinner. Over the meal, Wang Qian brought up, as if in passing, the success Yao Yuan had achieved these days.
Back then, Wang Qian had witnessed with his own eyes the past between Shuai Hongqiang and Yao Yuan. After the two broke up eight years ago, they'd had no contact at all. In recent years, Wang Qian and Shuai Hongqiang had drifted apart too — but after Yao Yuan returned to Jinshi three years ago, he'd had frequent dealings with her. After all, the circles of businesspeople and bank presidents overlap a good deal, and crossing paths is unavoidable.
A few cups of wine in, Shuai Hongqiang was already tipsy, and he mentioned that the year he and Yao Yuan broke up, he'd won a bronze mirror at an auction, meaning to give it to her as a gift — but in the end it had become something he'd stashed away at the bottom of a trunk. Wang Qian knew that bronze mirror; he'd been at that auction too. Even then, going by the secret lore handed down in his family, a suspicion had stirred faintly in his heart.
So when Shuai Hongqiang, hazy with drink, kept muttering "bad luck, an unlucky year," Wang Qian obliquely brought up that the mirror was "said by the old folks to lend you fortune." Half joking, half egging him on, he said, "Boss Shuai, the bank's pressing hard. Why not try this strange little method? You've got nothing to lose — and what if it does bring you some good luck? Besides, that mirror was meant for Yao Yuan in the first place, so it'd count as returning a thing to its rightful owner."
His thoughts as he said this were complicated, hard to read. Part of him wanted to watch coldly from the sidelines and give things a push — to see just how much of a stir this mysterious family "legend" could raise in reality, to verify certain hidden records. And part of him genuinely wanted to give Shuai Hongqiang a sliver of hope. Afterward, as if guided by some unseen hand, Shuai Hongqiang gave the bronze mirror away — and not long after, he actually settled his debt and smoothly repaid the bank loan. From that point on, Wang Qian withdrew completely behind the scenes, watching quietly from the shadows like the most patient of observers.
Now — Old Chen's old jade, stashed at the bottom of a trunk; the rumors of someone secretly hunting for the mirror masters' heirs; Shuai Hongqiang's inexplicably improved fortunes; the subtle, hard-to-notice drain on Yao Yuan's vital energy (he knew a little of physiognomy and could read a thing or two); and her sudden union with Zhao Yiming... countless clues pieced themselves together in his mind like fragments, at lightning speed, and a startling, dangerous conjecture gradually took shape. He felt that an invisible, uncanny net was slowly drawing closed, with Yao Yuan at its center. But he gave nothing away, burying this earth-shattering discovery and suspicion deep in his heart, and went on playing the detached, mild, friendly banker and old friend.
Wang Qian leaned back in his seat and slowly closed his eyes. He understood that Yao Yuan had, perhaps, already been swept into a storm she knew nothing of.
On the other side, Shuai Hongqiang's business was sailing along more smoothly than ever, flourishing on every front. The old-city redevelopment project he'd been handling was nearing completion, and the payments had been settled promptly, without the slightest delay. After the New Year, the national fitness center project the city had invited him to join was also finalized and successfully launched.
Shuai Hongqiang had once extended an invitation to Yao Yuan, hoping she'd take part with him. But by then Yao Yuan had thrown all her focus into developing the "Pretty Big One" Emotional AI, and she gently declined him. Shuai Hongqiang didn't press her — after all, now that he'd landed this project, people seeking him out to partner were flocking to his door, and when it came to funding, he hardly needed Yao Yuan's share. In truth, in seeking to work with Yao Yuan, somewhere deep down he also harbored a quiet wish to make amends for the mental drain the Mirror Pact had inflicted on her.
At present the national fitness center project was racing ahead in full swing, and Shuai Hongqiang seemed to have rediscovered his old style, becoming once more that spirited Boss Shuai surrounded and praised by everyone. Yet, having lived through this rise and fall in his fortunes, Shuai Hongqiang was no longer the reckless, arrogant young man he'd once been. He grew ever more able to treasure the present, turning steady and reserved. On top of that, Old Chen had once solemnly warned him to hold this "borrowed" good luck in awe. And so, with a kind heart, Shuai Hongqiang donated a large batch of daily necessities to impoverished students in the mountain districts. He did this partly out of a genuine wish to help those children, and partly in search of some inner peace — hoping that through such an act he might tip the scales of cause and effect a little closer to balance. If he truly had to bear the reckoning someday, he asked only that it come a little later.

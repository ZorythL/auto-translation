The Clear-Eyed Hunting Ground: A Dance of Golden Wind and Jade Dew
Next round, the King card landed in Yao Yuan's hands again. "Boss Yao again!" "The King card's stuck to you tonight, huh!" Yao Yuan only curved her lips into a smile and drew a card from the command deck. She flipped it over. It read: Perform a talent. "Oh, this one's good!" "Yuanyuan, give us a dance! You have to dance!" "Yeah! Dance—Yuanyuan's a pro at it!"
Amid the cheering, Yao Yuan's gaze drifted across the smiling faces around her. She didn't put on a coy show of refusal. With a faint smile, she set her wine glass down lightly on the tea table, the crystal base meeting the glass top with a crisp little chime. She rose, bent down, hooked her slender fingers under the thin strap of a silver high heel, and slipped it off carelessly, planting her bare feet on the thick, soft carpet. The cool, fine plush sent its touch up through her soles, a subtle contrast to the warm air of the room. She'd decided to dance—not to please anyone, but to take the room more firmly in hand, to use her own allure to lock every gaze onto herself, and to make Yu Hao and Gao Jieyuan understand that her charm was hers to command, no one else's to steer.
She didn't bother walking to the center of the private room. She simply found her footing in that small patch of open space between the sofa and the tea table. Someone—she didn't know who—queued up "Golden Wind and Jade Dew." As the lingering melody of the intro poured from the top-tier speakers, she closed her eyes, drew a deep breath, and when she opened them again, the hazy languor in them had cleared, replaced by an absorbed, tender glow.
Evening wind, oh, plucking the strings of a lover's heart / Play me a tune and bring you to my side.
White dew, oh, soaking a lover's gentle face / Unlocking the thousand words of love I've hidden in my heart.
Golden wind and jade dew, oh, in a single meeting / It is you and I, two hearts in mutual delight.
Softly we whisper our trickling longing / White dew nestles into the evening wind.
Golden wind and jade dew, oh, in a single meeting / It is you and I, walking the mortal world together.
Gently we link hands, slowly moving forward / The evening wind sings the dusk down, sleeping beside the white dew.
Evening wind, oh, you can't dispel the longing in a lover's heart / One gust, and I gather you into my arms.
White dew, oh, you can't rinse away a lover's stirred eyes / We gaze at each other, and before we know it, tears have soaked them.
Golden wind and jade dew, oh, in a single meeting / It is you and I, two hearts in mutual delight / Softly we whisper our trickling longing.
The music was like water, and she was a lotus slowly opening within it.
Yao Yuan made no large movements. She only stretched her shoulders and neck back in the faintest degree, lifted her chin a touch, and let an elegant curve run from collarbone to jaw. The wrist of her right hand—holding the imaginary "evening wind"—turned lightly over, her fingers like orchids, as if she really were plucking unseen strings. A fine tremor ran from her shoulder to her wrist and on to her slender fingertips, every joint speaking of softness. Her left arm hung naturally at her side, palm turned slightly up, as if cradling the "white dew."
With "Play me a tune and bring you to my side," her waist began to sway slowly with the rhythm. It was not the hot, unbridled writhing of a nightclub, but a swaying like ripples on water, like willow branches—pivoting on the hips, drawing the upper body into soft, winding arcs. The silver-gray sequined gown streamed scattered light with every movement, like moonlight scattered over a faintly rippling lake. Her core control was superb: stillness within motion, motion within stillness. The range was small, yet it captured that restrained invitation and that long, drawn-out longing to the fullest. She sank into her own world, setting aside all her calculation and control for the moment, simply savoring the looseness the dance gave her. That oblivious focus, that disregard for everyone around her, was itself the deadliest pull.
Her bare feet moved across the carpet, steps light and full of rhythm. Each one landed in the snuggest spot in the beat. Forward, back, turn—she used the small space to the utmost. One light spin, and her hem swung out a ring of flowing light. She angled her body slightly, her arm extending, her gaze trailing her fingertips as if looking toward a distant lover. "Unlocking the thousand words of love I've hidden in my heart"—in that instant of pause and gazing, her eyes were dazed yet intent, as if she truly had endless words of love trembling on the verge of being spoken.
She knew that, in this moment, she had to be beautiful—beautiful enough to set every man in the room aching.
"Golden wind and jade dew, oh, in a single meeting, it is you and I, two hearts in mutual delight…" Now the movements clearly opened up. The lines of her arms stretched and reached further, like two lovers twining neck to neck. The backward lean and forward tilt of her body, paired with the circling of her arms, made it seem she was dancing with an invisible beloved. The beginnings of a back bend—her supple waist curved into a heart-stopping arc, then rebounded lightly just as it neared its limit, like a reed brushed by the wind. Her head and neck swayed gently with the melody, strands of hair grazing her faintly flushed cheeks. The shifting KTV lights swept now across her closed lashes, now lit her dewy red lips—that mood poised between clear-eyed and intoxicated, magnified to its peak.
Every turn of her wrist, every opening and closing of her fingers, carried the flavor of an opera gesture. The line of her neck lengthened when she turned her head, looking fragile and lovely. The rise and fall of her chest moved faintly with her breath and motion, the sequined gown tracing alluring shadows. As her bare feet touched down and her insteps arched taut, even her slender anklebones and graceful arches, in the interplay of light and shadow, gave off a kind of silent temptation.
She could feel clearly how the gazes fixed on her were growing more scorching, more rapt—Gao Jieyuan's infatuation, Wang Qian's astonishment, Jin Lan's pride, Chen Ye's appreciation, and Yu Hao's depth. Yu Hao most of all: his gaze was like a sea under the night, calm on the surface but with undercurrents surging silently in its depths—and within them, possessiveness, appreciation, and a faint, hard-to-catch tension.
When the song reached "Softly we whisper our trickling longing," her lashes lowered, casting a small shadow beneath her eyes, yet at the corner of her lips hung a barely-there curve, sweet and wistful. At "Gently we link hands, slowly moving forward," she lifted her eyes toward some point in empty space, her gaze tender and lingering, as if she truly saw the one walking beside her. That focus created a strange sense of isolation between her and the noisy room around her. She sank into the world built of her own dance and music, and that oblivious focus, that disregard for everyone there, was itself the deadliest pull.
Gao Jieyuan had long forgotten to sing. He held the microphone and stared, fixated, his throat bobbing once without his meaning it to. The two "club princesses" beside him had stopped their giggling too, their eyes carrying that sizing-up between women and a faint, hard-to-catch envy. Wang Qian swayed his head gently to the beat, humming along soundlessly. Jin Lan wore a look of pure pride, as if showing off a treasure of her own. Chen Ye, the third-generation mining heir, let a look of intent appreciation surface on his usually refined, calm face. He leaned slightly forward, his fingers unconsciously tapping the beat on his knee, his gaze trailing Yao Yuan's dancing form—especially that absorbed, moving expression on her face. Behind his glasses, his eyes deepened a shade. And Yu Hao set down his glass, a look of appreciation on his face, his gaze deep and dim, flickering with a light that came and went.
"The evening wind sings the dusk down, sleeping beside the white dew…"
The last line sung, its echo curled in the air. Yao Yuan's movements came to rest with the music.
It was the foundation of a professional, yet without any of a performer's stiffness; it was free, improvised swaying, yet everywhere it showed a beauty carefully honed. There in that small patch of ground, in the midst of the gazes of friends and half-strangers, she had danced an entire dance through.
As the last note fell, she gathered herself in slowly, in a wholly natural pose, her arms folding softly before her like wings drawing in. She bowed her head a little, her chest rising and falling lightly from the brief exertion. Then she lifted her face, a thin flush of post-exercise color on her cheeks. The dazed, intoxicated expression faded; her eyes recovered their clarity. Only a trace of watery sheen lingered at their depths, as though that tender, alluring woman who had just turned into a wisp of wind and a drop of dew with the music was nothing but a dazed dream at the dim edge of the lamplight.
The room fell silent for an instant, with only the last echo of the music's accompaniment. Then—
"Bravo!" Wang Qian was the first to clap hard, and he whistled.
"So beautiful, Yuanyuan! The old blade hasn't dulled!" Jin Lan clapped excitedly.
Only now did Gao Jieyuan snap out of it. He set down the microphone and applauded, his gaze even more scorching. "Eye-opening, truly eye-opening! Boss Yao, that dance was incredible—no worse than those dancers on TV! I have to toast you again!"
Chen Ye clapped softly too, a mild, appreciative smile at the corner of his mouth. He said nothing, only picked up his glass and tipped it toward Yao Yuan. Yu Hao applauded as well, a look of appreciation on his face—but that look didn't fully reach his eyes. His gaze settled on her cheeks, faintly flushed from the dance, and on the fine beads of sweat at her neck, lingering a moment, the meaning in his eyes growing only heavier.
Yao Yuan merely gave a faint smile, as if that soul-stirring dance had been nothing but a casual whim. She walked back to her seat, slipped her high heels on again, picked up her glass of champagne, raised it to the room's cheers, and took a shallow sip. The cool liquid slid down her throat, easing the slight heat the dance had stirred up. She knew how the dance had landed, and she knew that, after it, her grip on this gathering had grown a few degrees firmer. But her expression stayed calm. Only in the flow of her gaze did that allure—coaxed up by the music and the dance, not yet fully dissolved—still ripple gently under the KTV's hazy light and shadow.
The dance done, before the applause and cheers had fully faded, a new round of toasts came one after another. Maybe that dance had unwittingly lit something; maybe the alcohol had long since quietly loosened the locks of restraint. The mood grew visibly livelier, even reckless. Gao Jieyuan raised his glass first, his eyes bright, his words carrying undisguised admiration. Wang Qian joined the clamor; Jin Lan smilingly drank one along too. Even the refined, quiet Chen Ye lifted his glass again, tipping it gently toward Yao Yuan before drinking it down in silence. And Yu Hao only held his glass and looked at her, deeply. Yao Yuan turned no one away. That faint, unhurried smile stayed at her lips as glass after glass of champagne and crafted cocktails slid down her throat, turning into the crimson slowly blooming on her cheeks and the ever-mistier sheen in her eyes.
Alcohol really is one of humanity's great inventions—a brief numbing of your brain, a quick release of dopamine to please your body. In this moment, catalyzed by the dance's slight tipsiness and the dazzled gazes of the crowd, it plucked at certain heartstrings, making the night subtle and unpredictable.
A new round of the game began. Yao Yuan leaned back into the sofa, taking in the gazes fixed on her—appreciative, scorching, playful, deep. She liked this feeling, liked this sense of control that came from being surrounded, from being the focus. As a woman who "understood" men, she knew all too well how to move with ease through a setting like this—how to release her charm offhandedly, and how to keep just the right distance. But at the same time, a faint weariness welled up at the bottom of her heart. This endless game of maneuvering, this being gazed at like a "trophy," sometimes left her feeling jaded.
Another draw. This time, the one who drew the King card was Gao Jieyuan. His eyes lit up. He pulled a card from the command deck, read it, and a meaningful smile curved his lips as his gaze went straight to Yao Yuan. "Kiss one member of the opposite sex present," he read out, then showed the card to everyone—though his eyes stayed locked on Yao Yuan's face. "Boss Yao, earlier when you drew this card, you chose the penalty drink. Now it's my turn to draw it—" He paused, his smile deepening. "I choose to exercise the right."
The room fell silent in an instant, every gaze flicking back and forth between Gao Jieyuan and Yao Yuan. Yao Yuan lifted her eyes to look at him, that faint, composed smile still on her face, as if waiting for him to go on. But in her heart she'd long since guessed it: Gao Jieyuan wouldn't let this chance slip. He wanted to use the game to force her into yielding, to break through her boundaries. His bluntness, carrying a streak of childish stubbornness, stirred a faint, hard-to-catch disgust in her.
"The member of the opposite sex I want to kiss," Gao Jieyuan said, slowly and clearly, "is Yao Yuan. Boss Yao."
The air froze. Jin Lan's brow knit faintly; Wang Qian raised an eyebrow; a playful glint crossed Liu Qingzhuo's eyes; Chen Ye pushed up his glasses. And Yu Hao—the habitual, composed smile faded from his face. His gaze settled calmly on Gao Jieyuan, then slid slowly to Yao Yuan's face, waiting for her reaction. Within that gaze were suppressed anger, possessiveness, and a faint, hard-to-catch anticipation—he was waiting to see how she would choose, whether she would yield to Gao Jieyuan.
Yao Yuan gave the glass in her hand a light swirl, the ice clinking crisply against the sides. She lifted her eyes and met Gao Jieyuan's, and in those eyes—misted with watery sheen from the wine and the dance—was now reflected, clearly, his face and its confident, this-is-mine smile. "Young Boss Gao is sure he's choosing me?" Her voice was a little husky, carrying the languor of drink, yet every word was crisp, her tone laced with a barely perceptible distance and amusement.
"Of course." Gao Jieyuan smiled and leaned forward slightly. "What, Boss Yao doesn't dare?"
Yao Yuan curved the corner of her mouth, and that smile held a heart-stopping beauty under the hazy lights. She set down her glass and rose slowly. The silver-gray sequined gown streamed scattered light with her movement. Barefoot on the carpet, she walked toward Gao Jieyuan, one step at a time. The room was quiet enough to hear the faint drumbeat behind the music. Every gaze focused on her. Yu Hao's body tensed slightly, his fingers unconsciously tightening around his glass.
She stopped in front of Gao Jieyuan. He stood up too. They were very close—close enough to feel each other's breath. A confident, this-is-mine light glittered in his eyes as he lowered his head slightly and waited. Yao Yuan lifted her hand, but she didn't touch him. She only tapped her own cheek lightly with a finger. "Here," she said, her voice calm, yet her gaze carried a commanding, playful amusement.
Gao Jieyuan stalled for a beat, then laughed. "Boss Yao, you mean—"
"Didn't Young Boss Gao say he'd kiss one member of the opposite sex present?" Yao Yuan tilted her head, her long hair sliding off her shoulder. "I agreed. But—" she paused, her smile deepening, "since it's a game, let's go by the game's rules. A kiss on the cheek should fit the rules, shouldn't it?" She said it deliberately—both giving Gao Jieyuan a way to save face and making her own boundary clear: she could play along with the game, but never let it cross the line. Her body, her feelings, had always been hers to command, and no one could force them.
Gao Jieyuan looked at her, a tangle of emotions flickering through his eyes—surprise, amusement, and the chafing of having been outmaneuvered, but more than anything, a thicker, more intense interest rising up at having been challenged. He laughed, the sound carrying clearly through the quiet room. "Boss Yao's right—the game's rules." He nodded, then leaned in slightly and pressed a light, fleeting kiss to the cheek she'd designated. It was very soft, very quick—it barely even touched her skin, just a symbolic gesture. But it was enough. He'd kept his face, and he hadn't truly offended Yao Yuan. And Yao Yuan had guarded her boundary and kept the situation in hand.
After he drew back, Yao Yuan still stood where she was, her smile unchanged, only with a victor's composure added to her eyes now. She turned to walk back to her seat.
"Wait." Yu Hao's voice rang out suddenly. Yao Yuan stopped and turned to look at him. In her heart she knew: Yu Hao couldn't hold back after all. His possessiveness wouldn't allow anyone—even under the cover of a game—to get close to her.
At some point Yu Hao had already risen. The smile was still on his face, but it didn't reach his eyes. He took the bottle from the table and poured a glass each for himself and Gao Jieyuan, the liquor swaying in the glasses, refracting the hazy light. "Jieyuan," Yu Hao said, pushing one glass toward him, his voice level yet carrying a force that brooked no argument, "since the game's been so much fun, let me toast you."
Gao Jieyuan raised an eyebrow at him, an ambiguous half-smile curving his lips. "Brother Hao, this is—" He knew it too: this drink Yu Hao was offering wasn't a toast. It was a declaration of ownership, a warning—stay away from Yao Yuan.
"Nothing. I just feel like toasting you." Yu Hao raised his own glass, looking at him calmly. "What, won't you give me face?"
The air tightened in an instant. Everyone felt the silent standoff between the two men. Wang Qian and Jin Lan exchanged a glance; Liu Qingzhuo watched with relish; Chen Ye pushed up his glasses, his gaze behind the lenses moving back and forth between Yu Hao and Gao Jieyuan. Yao Yuan stood where she was, watching the two men, that composed, playful smile still on her face—as if watching a play that had everything and nothing to do with her.
She enjoyed this feeling of being fought over, of being seen as a target by quality men. But at the same time, a faint weariness rose from deep within—weariness at this beast-instinct contest between males, at this posture that treated her as a trophy. What she wanted was appreciation, pursuit, a willing surrender—not this childish, possessive scramble. Yu Hao's possessiveness, Gao Jieyuan's lust to conquer—both made her feel as if she'd become an object, snatched back and forth between them, rather than a person with her own will and her own rhythm.
Gao Jieyuan looked at Yu Hao, then at Yao Yuan standing off to the side, and suddenly laughed. He picked up the glass Yu Hao had pushed over and raised it. "Brother Hao's offering a toast—of course I'll give you face. Come on, bottoms up." The two glasses met in the air with a crisp clink. They tipped their heads back at the same time and drained them.
Setting his glass down, Yu Hao filled two more. "This one's to tonight's gathering," he said, his gaze still calm. Gao Jieyuan raised an eyebrow but didn't refuse, lifting his glass again. "To the gathering." Another drained. Yu Hao kept pouring. "This one's to…" he paused, his gaze sweeping past Yao Yuan and returning to Gao Jieyuan's face, "to Boss Yao's dance just now. It was beautiful." Gao Jieyuan's gaze darkened, but the smile at his lips deepened. "It really was. This one's worth drinking to."
The third went down. The drinking contest had, just like that, formally begun beneath all the seemingly polite small talk and toasting. From then on, the game gradually faded into the background. Yu Hao and Gao Jieyuan seemed to have found a contest more interesting than the game. They no longer needed the command deck; reasons to toast each other came in endless supply—to the reunion, to the collaboration, to the weather, even to a song that had just finished playing. One glass for you, one for me, drinking fast and hard, as if what was in the cups wasn't liquor but some kind of will that had to overpower the other.
Yao Yuan walked back to her seat, sat down again, and watched in silence. She watched Yu Hao's steady yet relentless press of an offensive, and Gao Jieyuan's face going gradually red while he refused to concede. The alcohol burned inside her, sending her thoughts drifting one moment and turning them oddly clear the next. She knew exactly what these two men were contesting—her, and that laughable possessiveness. Yu Hao—even though they'd agreed not to restrict each other's social lives, it seemed that the moment a man got involved with a woman, he was born with a kind of possessiveness, feeling that as long as he was present, she was by default his property. And Gao Jieyuan—she, a person who made her living analyzing men to untangle other women's troubles, had, at their very first meeting, effortlessly sensed his curiosity and his liking for her. That liking carried a wastrel heir's arrogance, more like a desire to conquer: "If I want it, I have to have it."
Did she like this feeling? Yes. As a woman, she knew her own charm too well, and understood too well how to enjoy this sense of being surrounded, being fought over. But at the same time, she felt a subtle disgust—disgust at this beast-instinct contest between males, at this posture that treated her as a trophy. What she wanted was appreciation, pursuit, a willing surrender—not this childish, possessive scramble. They both wanted to control her, yet neither had ever asked her what she wanted, or how she wanted to be together.
Another round, and Gao Jieyuan's tongue had clearly gone stiff, his eyes unfocused, yet he still forced himself to pour Yu Hao another glass. Yu Hao's face was flushed with drink too, but his eyes stayed deep and clear. He pressed down the hand pouring the liquor and said evenly, "Jieyuan, that's really about enough." "Enough—enough of what! Brother Hao, you look down on me?" Gao Jieyuan flung his hand off, splashing some of the liquor.
Yao Yuan watched the scene and suddenly found it a little funny, and a little dull. This contest was getting more and more childish, drifting further and further from the elegant script she'd set out in her mind. She stood, her figure swaying slightly, and picked up her clutch and the silver-fox fur that had been draped over the armrest the whole time. Her voice husky with the heavy weight of the drink, she said to Jin Lan, who sat closest to her, "Sister Lan, my head's a little dizzy. I'm going out for some air—and to touch up my makeup."
With that, in her high heels, her steps somewhat unsteady yet stubbornly held straight, she walked directly to the door of the private room, pulled it open, and stepped out. She shut that roomful of luxury, of noise, and of the invisible war those two men had kindled over her behind her, for the moment. She needed air, needed to sort out her thoughts, needed to find that sense of control again—away from this childish scramble.
In the corridor, the cool, lavish laser-glass wall pressed against her back, bringing a touch of sobering chill. Yao Yuan drew a few deep breaths of air laced with crisp fragrance, then turned and headed toward the restroom. The woman in the mirror still wore exquisite makeup—only the sheen in her eyes was fuller now, the flush on her cheeks like the finest rouge had been brushed across them, carrying a heart-stopping allure steamed up by the drink and emotion. But more striking was the sharpness, clear and faintly playful, that had gathered again at the bottom of her eyes.
She touched up a little lipstick, pressed her lips together, and looked at her own alluring reflection—a "spoil of war," you might say—a faint, knowing curve rising at the corner of her mouth. Good. Yu Hao's appearance, the instant tensing of Gao Jieyuan's nerves, and those invisible sparks colliding in the air… all of it was within expectation—or rather, a situation she'd permitted and even unwittingly nudged along. The alcohol magnified her senses, and magnified this subtle sense of control too. She enjoyed this feeling of being noticed by quality men, of having them quietly compete over her—not out of shallow vanity, but as a double confirmation of her own charm, her social value, and her judgment of feelings. See, they're bidding for me. That recognition, carrying the warmth of tipsiness, pressed soothingly against certain places deep in her heart.
She was just… a little tired of Gao Jieyuan's overly blunt, even clumsy advances, and felt a slight, subtle displeasure at Yu Hao's "protective" posture and its air of entitlement. Contesting was fine, but the posture had to look good, and the rules had to be set by her. That was her inner standard. Clearly, those two in the room had both drifted somewhat from the elegant script she'd set. She needed to recalibrate the situation, to make them understand who was really in control of this game.
With that recalibrating mindset, she turned to walk back. The corridor was still long, the lighting dreamlike, the doors on both sides shut tight, only faint music seeping out. The alcohol had, after all, affected her judgment, and with her thoughts more sunk in savoring her own charm and assessing the situation, she walked back along memory and similar decor, her gaze searching for that "999" door plate. From a distance she saw the metal numbers set into one door seem a little loose; under the shifting light, the shapes looked somewhat blurred—the top "9," its lower round metal piece seeming to droop slightly, looked, at a certain angle, oddly like a "6."
Yao Yuan's steps faltered, and she narrowed her eyes. Was this it? She didn't remember walking this far on the way out… but maybe she'd misremembered. The alcohol had dulled her sense of time and space, weakening her usual absolute precision. She didn't dwell on it. All she wanted now was to get back to the room quickly, retake control, and end that childish contest. She glanced at her own reflection in the smooth mirrored surface beside the door, reached up to grip the cold handle, and pushed it open.
The deafening music and familiar din she'd expected didn't rush out to meet her.
The room was dark—not pitch black, but the deep dark of all the equipment on standby, with the faint breathing glow of electronics' standby lights. There was no smell of alcohol, fruit, or perfume in the air. The full-length mirror on the wall facing the door seemed startled awake by her intrusion, suddenly rippling out rings of eerie, water-like ripples, their center pointed straight at her. An instant later, a familiar, powerful pull appeared out of nowhere. Her soul was yanked hard, and dizziness washed over her.
"?!!" Yao Yuan's heart seized, and all her leisurely thoughts of emotional gamesmanship froze in an instant. One high heel turned under her foot, and her whole body lurched forward out of control, tumbling into the sofa in front of her. The silver-fox fur and clutch in her hands slipped free, falling soundlessly onto the cold floor. She stretched out a hand in vain, trying to grab hold of something, but her vision filled in an instant with twisted light and shadow, and she lost consciousness.
At the bottom of her heart there was only one thought left—she had, uncontrollably, traveled through time again.

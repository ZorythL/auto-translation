She knew the lights would come on again, the camera would open again, and once more countless souls swept along by the silt of their feelings would yearn to receive from her a filter — perhaps sharp, perhaps painful.
And she would still sit here, with that silent, silt-laden Yellow River behind her.
Using the most clear-eyed and most merciless language, teaching them how, in this turbid river of life, to sift out their own small, solid measure of "gold."

The ring light flared to life, its cold white glow like the shadowless lamp over an operating table, wrapping Yao Yuan in its beam. Behind her was a wall-to-wall window. Beyond the glass there were no buildings, no neon — only a vast, churning expanse of muddy yellow stretched out below: the Yellow River. From this height it didn't roar and surge the way it did in promotional films. Instead it looked like some great, weary beast tamed by the enormous window frame, grinding silently past the edge of the city, countless grains of unseen silt sunk in its turbid sheen.
At the edge of the frame, a gold-thin-bordered diagram of "the Energy Pyramid" hung on the wall, its lines cold and heavy with implication.
Yao Yuan was dressed all in red. A flowing dark-red silk cape set off skin white as snow. Beneath the cape, the neckline of her crimson slip dress was cut with restraint, yet the soft drape of the fabric traced a heart-stopping rise and fall as she moved. The diamonds on her collarbone chain caught the light with every motion, throwing off sharp little points of light. She faced the camera, unhurriedly reapplying Armani lipstick No. 400 — a red that was aggressive, that announced its own presence.
On the iPad beside the phone stand, the live viewer count ticked upward: 82,156.
The light stretched the shadows of her lashes long, casting them beneath her eyes like two silent, watchful wings. This carefully built space — the precise lighting, the symbolic backdrop, the eternal flowing chaos beyond the window, and the woman in red within it — together formed an instrument, a precision instrument for dissecting the human heart, taking apart desire, and measuring the exchange rate between feeling and gain. Stripped of every excess emotion, she was the clear-eyed observer, and also the surgeon who, without a flicker of expression, cut open every truth and lie.
She blotted her lips even, pressed a fingertip lightly to the peak of her mouth, finishing the last step. Then she lifted her eyes, her gaze a probe driving straight into the lens.

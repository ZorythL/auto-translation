Chapter 82 — Pretty Big One Emotional AI Passes Full-Chain Testing
https://www.xbanxia.cc/books/417648/72640329.html

After telling Shuai Hongqiang that secret, Yao Yuan didn't give herself much time to wallow. That eight-years-late "delivery of the truth" was like a stone dropped into a deep pool: it stirred violent ripples, then sank fast to the bottom, and after a brief churn the surface forced itself back to a cold calm. She didn't know how Shuai Hongqiang was digesting the news — whether in pain and regret, lying awake at night, or whether he'd used a businessman's logic to quickly file it away as an "unchangeable sunk cost." She forced herself not to guess, not to care. The adult world doesn't leave much time to chew over old pain, especially the kind of past that has long since become a museum specimen. She crushed that tangled mix of old wounds, resentment, a secret thrill of revenge, and an even deeper emptiness down into her chest, turned, and focused all her energy like a laser on the one thing that mattered most right now — the final testing and launch of the "Pretty Big One" Emotional AI.
Early September. The dry heat of late summer hadn't fully lifted, but the wind carried its first dry, unmistakable hint of harvest season. Yao Yuan brought several executives and core engineers from the "Pretty Big One" team back to the Western World Technology headquarters in Jinshi's high-tech district. In the afternoon sun, the matte-black smart-glass curtain wall was as severe and self-contained as ever, like a great beast lying in wait.
This time her state of mind was nothing like that first visit. Back then it had been cautious appraisal, a probing test driven by business instinct. Now it was rushing toward a celebration — the birth of something carefully nurtured, about to come into the world.
The elevator went straight to the top floor. They crossed the empty, silent corridor lit only by the cold glow of linear strips, and once again stood before that unmarked metal-grey door. The heavy door slid open without a sound —
That familiar smell hit them: ozone, the heat dissipating off precision instruments, and a kind of metallic, after-rain earthiness. The huge spherical "Mirror Room" came into view again. Walls, ceiling, floor — countless fine hexagonal screens now displayed a deep, flowing starscape. At the center, the floating ring-shaped console gave off a soft glow. Zhao Yiming stood with his back to the door, gazing at the waterfall of blue data streaming through the void ahead. Today he wore a light-grey polo and khakis, a touch more of the lab's austerity and a little less of late-summer ease than last time, though the focused line of his back was as clear as ever.
Hearing footsteps, he didn't turn around. He raised his right hand and made a brief gesture. In an instant the rushing data froze, contracted, and reorganized into an intricate three-dimensional architecture diagram hovering above the console.
"Right on time." Zhao Yiming turned. His eyes swept over Yao Yuan's crisp off-white suit, lingered briefly on the team members behind her, then settled back on her face, pausing for an almost imperceptible instant — there was concern in it, a question, but both were quickly reined in and turned into a partner's focus. "The last full-chain closed test starts in five minutes. If it passes, the 'Pretty Big One' base model and its fusion interface with the Genesis Engine are truly solid."
Yao Yuan nodded and looked toward the intricate visual map, its blue-and-gold light faintly interwoven. It was more seamless than when she'd first seen it months ago, as if it were alive — breathing and growing slowly.
The team got into position fast. Yao Yuan stood a little to the rear, in an observation spot where she could take in the whole picture without interfering with the technical work. Today she'd pinned her long hair up low, with two minimalist pearl studs at her ears, every inch the calm air of a commander. Only the freshly applied haze-blue polish on her fingertips, flecked with fine gold, shimmered with a subtle sheen in the Mirror Room's dim light.
"Begin the test," Zhao Yiming said.
At once a flood of data poured across the ring of screens. A simulated "storm of problems" — drawn from countless extreme emotional cases across every culture in the world — slammed into the just-formed "Pretty Big One" AI core at millions of entries per second. On screen, the fusion map began to heave violently; certain nodes lit warning-yellow, and now and then flickered a glaring red.
The control area was dead silent, nothing but the clatter of keyboards and Zhao Yiming's occasional calm command. Yao Yuan held her breath, feeling the warm comfort of the heart-guarding jade against her chest. This was more than a technical trial. It was the ultimate test of whether she could turn half a lifetime of insight into human nature, her "clinical dissection" of feelings, into a quantifiable, reproducible AI model.
Time seemed to stretch. One by one, the warning points were resolved under the deft scheduling of the algorithms. After violent shaking, the map showed astonishing resilience — like a great tree in a storm, its roots (the Genesis Engine's computing power) gripping the earth, its branches (the "Pretty Big One's" emotional logic) swaying in the gale but not snapping, instead adapting, adjusting, evolving.
"Emotional-intent recognition accuracy, holding steady at 93.1 percent!"
"Self-consistency of complex-relationship reasoning, past 91.5 percent!"
"Humanization deviation rate of strategy recommendations under extreme scenarios, below threshold!"
The metrics kept coming, the voices going from taut to excited. The last one — the "ultimate test question" Yao Yuan had helped design — loaded: an utterly chaotic case mixing betrayal, deceit, financial entanglement, emotional dependence, and self-doubt.
The map heaved again. Countless possible paths radiated, interwove, collided, recombined like beams of light. For a full three minutes, you could have heard a pin drop.
Then the turbulence settled. In the region representing the AI's output, a text report began to generate — clear, calm, rigorously logical, and even carrying a subtle "empathy" and "strategy." The report not only untangled the snarled mess and pinpointed the core demands and weak points; it laid out several highly feasible courses of action, with clear cost-benefit breakdowns, even accounting for the psychological tolerance of whoever would carry them out. Some of the suggestions were so seasoned and precise that even Yao Yuan, the one who'd set the problem, was secretly startled.
"Final scenario test… passed!"
"Full-chain simulation test, passed! All preset metrics met, and some exceeded!"
After a brief, almost suffocating silence —
"Yesss—!"
"It worked!"
Cheers and applause exploded. Months of tension, countless sleepless nights, enormous pressure — all of it turned in that instant into the purest elation. The young engineers high-fived; the women had tears in their eyes. The members of Yao Yuan's own team couldn't hide their excitement either.
Yao Yuan stood there, spine ramrod straight, as if a thousand-pound weight had lifted off her. A scalding rush surged from her heart up to her eyes, and she forced it back down. She drew a slow, deep breath. It had worked. It had truly worked. From a vague idea, to building a team, pouring in a fortune, meeting Zhao Yiming, the sparring, the partnership, the fusion… she had finally reached a milestone on this road.
She turned her head to look at Zhao Yiming.
He was looking at her too. His usual composure was broken by a bright, fulfilled smile, even something boyish in it. Across the cheering crowd he nodded at her, then raised his right hand, made a fist, and tapped it lightly over his heart — the small private gesture between them, born of starry Gobi skies and the tacit understanding beneath their rational pact.
At last, beyond her control, the corners of Yao Yuan's mouth lifted into a smile of pure, dazzling, vivid life.
The celebration was spontaneous and fierce. Champagne someone had stashed got "produced" out of nowhere, the golden liquor merrily fizzing in the glasses. Everyone raised a toast, laughter filling the futuristic space. Yao Yuan held her glass and clinked it with every core member, elegant in her bearing, the absolute center of the room. Zhao Yiming, surrounded by engineers hashing out details, wore the pure satisfaction of a tech obsessive who has hit his target.
After the brief celebration, Zhao Yiming signaled for quiet.
"Everyone, this is only the success of the first-stage model test — the 'zero to one' breakthrough." His voice returned to calm, but it carried an uplift. "Next, we need to dress the 'Pretty Big One' in even prettier 'clothes,' design the most human interaction, and prepare the most solid commercial rollout. So—" he looked at Yao Yuan, their eyes meeting, "Director Yao and I have decided to hold the official launch of 'Pretty Big One Emotional AI 1.0' in Jinshi on October first. We'll invite industry partners, the media, and—"
He paused, and Yao Yuan picked it up right on cue, her voice clear and strong: "And the first hundred new and old members of the 'Sisterhood League' that has supported me all along. They'll be the first batch of core experience officers for the 'Pretty Big One.' On October first, let's witness the launch together!"
Even warmer applause broke out. With the launch date set, the project had entered its true sprint phase.
The crowd gradually dispersed. Yao Yuan and Zhao Yiming were the last to linger, both, by unspoken agreement, not leaving the Mirror Room right away.
Inside the spherical space, the starscape slowly began to flow again, gently covering over the traces of the data flood. They stood side by side beside the ring console, looking at the data map before them — calm once more, yet holding infinite possibility.
"How does it feel?" Zhao Yiming tilted his head toward her, his voice soft.
Yao Yuan gazed at those deep "stars," and only after a moment said quietly, "Like finishing a marathon with no finish line in sight and finally spotting the flag of the next aid station. Tired, but… worth it." She turned back to look at him, her eyes clear. "Thank you, Yiming. Without the Genesis Engine, the 'Pretty Big One' would just be a pretty castle in the air."
"Likewise." Zhao Yiming shook his head, his gaze frank. "Without your near-'cruel' precision in dissecting human feelings, and your ability to structure them, no matter how powerful the Genesis Engine, it would still just be a cold supercomputing machine. We created it together." He used the word "created."
Yao Yuan's heart gave a faint tremor. Created. It was almost a kind of… shared "giving birth." The thought made her look away, her ears a little warm — fortunately the Mirror Room's light was dim.
"The next month is going to be busy," she said, changing the subject, her tone back to brisk.
"Mm." Zhao Yiming nodded. "Technical support — absolutely no problem."
A simple exchange, yet the firmest mutual backstop.
As they left Western World Technology, the setting sun was staining the building's black glass curtain wall a warm gold-red. Yao Yuan got into the car and looked back at this structure that lay like a crouching beast.
The heart-guarding jade rested warm against her skin. The shadow of the Mirror Pact still lurked in the distance, but right now her heart was filled with another kind of full, grounded strength.
The "Pretty Big One" was about to break out of its cocoon. This was a new height for her career, the tangible result of refining personal pain into universal value, and a deep interweaving and mutual proof — between her and Zhao Yiming, between reason and feeling, technology and human nature.
The car merged into the evening traffic as the city's neon began to light up one stretch at a time. The headlights cut through the thickening dusk, pointing the way home — and toward the coming dawn, a brand-new one that belonged to the "Pretty Big One."

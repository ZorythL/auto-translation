Starry Sky Over the Desert
Far from the light pollution of the city, the night sky here was vast, deep, and so pure it made the heart ache. A person felt gently wrapped in the boundless universe, and at the same time small as a speck of dust. All the daytime scheming, the worries, the unsolved puzzles — under this sky they all seemed to shrink to nothing.
"Every time I feel boxed in by some specific problem and can't see my way out, I drive out here alone, sit for a while, and look at the stars." Zhao Yiming spoke suddenly, his voice soft, as if half to her and half to himself. "I look at this 'fortress' I helped build with my own hands — one that tries to understand intelligence, even to create it — and then I look up at this universe overhead, billions of years old, still silent and full of mystery. And I realize that humanity's so-called hard problems, technology's so-called frontiers, set against that scale, are nothing more than a child learning to walk. But there's a kind of solemn, tragic beauty in that first walking, and it's worth giving everything to."
Yao Yuan turned her head to look at him. The firelight danced across his face. He gazed up at the stars, his eyes focused and faraway, holding the reason and hunger to explore that she knew so well, and something she rarely saw in him — the loneliness and rapture that belong to an idealist. In this moment he was no longer the rigorous scientist or the shrewd businessman, but more like a child of nature gazing up at the sky from the wilderness.
"You've done things most people wouldn't even dare to imagine, let alone pull off," Yao Yuan said quietly, her tone sincere with genuine admiration.
Zhao Yiming drew his eyes back and looked at her. The firelight flickered deep in his dark eyes. "Yuan." He said her name, dropping her surname in private for the first time, his voice steady but carrying a gravity she had never heard from him before. "Do you remember my 'white paper'? About the system interaction protocol, about the risks, the returns, about… shared governance and trust."
"I remember." Yao Yuan's heart gave a small jump, though her face stayed calm.
"All the rational reasoning, the risk assessments, the protocol clauses — tonight, under this campfire and these stars, they all feel a little pale and clumsy." Zhao Yiming looked at her, his gaze open and direct, the way it was when he dissected a technical problem. "But I still believe in the value of that method, because at the very least it guarantees that the foundation we start from is solid and clear-eyed. Right now, though, I want to skip all the complicated reasoning in between and ask you something straight — something rooted in my own feelings, not in a business deal."
He paused, as if gathering his courage, or making a final check, and then said clearly, "Yao Yuan, I like you. Not admiration, not approval — the way a man likes a woman. I want to build a connection with you that's deeper, more intimate, and more complicated than business partners. When I explore that unknown frontier between technology and human nature, I want you beside me. And in the moments I feel lonely — like now, watching the stars — I want to turn my head and see you there. Will you give me a chance? Let's try and see whether two complex, independent systems, beyond a rational protocol, can build a coordination that's emotional, profound, and exclusive."
His confession still carried that Zhao Yiming flavor — clear, direct, goal-driven, not even forgetting to mention "systems" and "coordination" — but its core was scalding and sincere. No flowery words, no romantic build-up; and there beneath the desert sky, in the crackle of the campfire, it carried a weight all its own.
Yao Yuan didn't answer right away. She lifted the rough clay bowl and tossed back the last small mouthful of strong liquor inside. That burning sensation swept down through her again, making her cheeks hotter while her mind stayed strangely sharp. She looked at Zhao Yiming — this man she respected in his field, who made her feel steady in the way he dealt with people, and who now showed her a rare, tender side.
The old jade against her chest lay warm on her skin, a reminder of those unresolved tangles of fate. But in this moment, with the sky vast and the fire warm, with strong liquor running through her veins and his eyes fixed on her, full of expectation, she suddenly felt that perhaps she didn't have to wait for every last fog to clear before reaching out to touch a possible happiness. The future was always unknowable; the only thing she could hold was a clear-eyed choice in the present.
"Zhao Yiming," she said, using his name too, her voice slightly hoarse from the liquor and the emotion but clear in every word. "I admire you as well, and… I even like the way it feels to be with you, to work with you. I still agree with the principles of your 'white paper,' especially 'shared governance' and 'boundaries.' So my answer is: yes, we can try. But just as the protocol says, public and private must stay separate. Our feelings are a chapter of their own. They shouldn't sway the partnership decisions between the 'Pretty Big One' and the 'Genesis Engine,' and they can't become a stumbling block to each other's careers. If one day the feelings are gone, the basis for working together still has to remain — we have to be able to step back gracefully into the position of partners. Can you accept that?"
It was a classic Yao Yuan answer — even at the very start of accepting a relationship, she clear-headedly drew the boundaries, set the rules in advance, and laid out a way back. But that was exactly the kind of candor Zhao Yiming could understand and admire.
A smile broke across his face — relieved, and brilliantly, expansively bright, as if he'd just solved some major technical problem that had plagued him for ages. "Of course. That was part of the protocol to begin with, and it's what I expect too." He nodded gravely, then added, "And I think a healthy relationship should make both people better, not drag each other down. Our partnership will only grow steadier and more efficient because of this extra trust and understanding."
The deal was struck. No embrace, no kiss — just a smile shared across the dancing light and shadow of the fire. A brand-new relationship, full of exploration and anticipation, quietly took shape beneath that ancient sky.
"Shall we dance?" Zhao Yiming suddenly held out his hand, an invitation, laughter and challenge in his eyes. "Purely a private celebration. Nothing to do with the partnership."
Yao Yuan looked at his outstretched hand, then at the lively, exuberant crowd dancing nearby. Some lock inside her seemed to loosen quietly. She placed her hand in his palm, stood up, and gave a radiant smile. "All right. Let's dance."
Yao Yuan watched the boisterous but utterly chaotic "free-for-all" in front of her and raised an eyebrow with a laugh, the smile carrying a touch of professional teasing in the firelight. "This improvised stuff — I really can't do it. But," she said, turning sharply, a sly glint in her eyes, "if you'd like a taste of the professional version, I could improvise a little something."
Zhao Yiming was clearly drawn in by her words and her manner, and asked with real interest, "Oh? A top graduate of the Beijing Dance Academy improvising — now that I can't miss."
Yao Yuan shook her head with a smile and didn't take up his "professional" thread, only put her hand in his outstretched palm and, riding the liquor and the mood, rose to her feet with easy abandon. "Come on. When in Rome — let's just dance for the fun of it."
Zhao Yiming led her by the hand into the joyful crowd wrapped in firelight and music. The music was an unrestrained folk rhythm with no fixed steps. Yao Yuan hadn't taken a stage in years, but the sense of rhythm and bodily control carved into her bones were still there. She didn't try to show off; she simply moved with the beat, swaying her body freely and easily. Simple steps and gestures, done by her, took on an indescribable fluidity and grace. In the glow of the fire her figure unfurled, carrying a charm that was both effortless and bewitching.
Zhao Yiming was no dancer, but he was clever and well-coordinated, and he quickly caught her rhythm. The two of them faced each other, moving freely with the music, their eyes tangling in the air, the smiles flowing in their gazes. The firelight stretched their shadows long, casting them onto the golden dunes, weaving them together with countless other swaying silhouettes. The kick of the strong liquor, the joyful atmosphere, the new relationship under the stars — all of it let Yao Yuan relax completely. Her smile was bright, light shifting in her eyes, as if she'd returned to a younger, freer time. Zhao Yiming's gaze followed her the whole while, full of undisguised admiration and delight.
The night deepened. The campfire slowly died down, and the crowd drifted off one by one, returning to the comfortable inns of the small town to rest. Zhao Yiming and Yao Yuan lingered at the very back, walking slowly along the edge of the dunes; neither said anything about heading back. The starry sky hung low, as if within reach. The land lay silent on every side, broken only by the faint sound of wind brushing over the sand. The lights of the town glowed gently in the distance, sketching out a peaceful outline.
Zhao Yiming stopped before a few sturdy, comfortable stargazing tents, set up specially for tourists who came to watch the sky. "Want to take a look? The view here is the best, and it's absolutely quiet." He pointed at one of them.
Yao Yuan nodded. The two of them stepped inside. The space within was roomy, the floor laid with a thick, soft faux-fur rug, the furnishings simple but complete. The top was made of a special transparent material, letting you gaze up at the stars with nothing in the way. They lay down side by side, the soft rug beneath them, the flowing Milky Way overhead. There was no lamp — only starlight to pick out the dim shapes inside the tent.
A strange hush and intimacy filled the small space. They could hear each other's faint breathing.
"It's beautiful," Yao Yuan sighed softly, her whole spirit carried off by that pure, vast sky.
"Mm." Zhao Yiming answered, turning onto his side, gazing at her profile in the starlight. His eyes no longer hid anything; they held warmth as they slowly traced the lines of her brows, her nose, her lips.
Yao Yuan felt his gaze and turned her head too. Their eyes met. Under the starlight they could see clearly the reflection of themselves in each other's eyes, and the attraction rising between them that reason could no longer fully hold down.
Zhao Yiming's hand gently covered hers where it rested at her side, fingertips touching, then slowly lacing together. His hand was warm and dry, lightly calloused. He made no further move, only held her like that and watched her, as if waiting for a final permission, as if confirming that this was real.
Yao Yuan didn't draw her hand back. She looked into his eyes, close enough to touch — and beyond the light of reason in them, they were now brimming with tender starlight and a longing and restraint she had never seen in a man before. The old jade at her chest grew slightly warm, but it was no longer a warning, more like some silent resonance. She closed her eyes, then slowly opened them again, and then, ever so slightly, leaned forward.
It was a silent, unmistakable signal.
Zhao Yiming's breath seemed to catch for a moment, and then he hesitated no longer. He drew slowly closer, his warm breath brushing her cheek, and then a kiss landed gently, tentatively, on her forehead, light as a feather. Then her brow, the tip of her nose, and at last, with the greatest reverence, he covered her slightly parted lips.
At first it was a tender brushing, full of careful searching. The cold, bracing aftertaste of the liquor still lingered between their lips, mingling with each other's breath. Starlight spilled through the top of the tent, flowing over their tangled breaths and their slowly warming skin. Yao Yuan's hands climbed unconsciously to his shoulders, feeling the taut, powerful lines of muscle beneath his shirt. Zhao Yiming's kiss deepened, growing hot and certain, his arm circling her waist to pull her tighter against him. The fibers of the rug rubbed at her skin, sending faint shivers through her.
At some point their clothes had grown loose and bothersome. Beneath the silent stars, in the heart of the desert far from all the noise of the world, the levee of reason quietly gave way before primal instinct and surging emotion. There were no more words — only breathing that grew quicker, body heat that twined together, and the indescribable thrill of skin against skin.
In the instant their bodies pressed together, both of them gave an almost imperceptible tremble. That closeness broke through every spoken agreement and every boundary of reason; their souls seemed to embrace tightly in that moment. He moved with the lightest touch, with careful cherishing, as if handling the rarest treasure in the world. His warm breath wrapped around her, his restrained feelings surging without end, all his tenderness and depth poured into every small point of contact.
Starlight like flowing water settled quietly over the two of them lying entwined inside, the air thick with a tenderness that would not dissolve.
As the rhythm gradually slipped out of control, a primal cadence replaced the early tenderness. The air in the tent turned scalding, breaths weaving together, mingled with the faint sounds of bodies meeting. Yao Yuan tilted her head back, her neck drawing a lovely arc, her gaze passing through the transparent top of the tent to fall into that boundless, turning river of stars. In that moment, the joys and sorrows of the individual, the unsolved puzzles, the distant threats — all of it seemed to dissolve into insignificant dust against the backdrop of the universe. Only this — the body's utmost pleasure, the soul's brief merging, and the eternally watching sky overhead — was the only real thing.
After the storm of feeling passed, everything slowly settled into calm. Yao Yuan lowered her eyes and lay quietly in his arms, her fingers resting lightly on his back. Zhao Yiming circled her waist, and a faint sigh escaped his chest.
The night wind drifted over carrying starlight, and the two of them, holding each other, gradually caught their breath. A lazy warmth wrapped around them. No words were needed; simply leaning together like this, they felt utterly at peace, and time itself turned soft and long.
After who knew how long, Zhao Yiming propped himself up slightly, found her lips in the dimness, and pressed a few gentle, desireless kisses to them, then drew her close again, his chin resting against the top of her hair.
"Sleep," he murmured, his voice husky and tender.
Yao Yuan found a more comfortable spot in his arms and closed her eyes. The body's fatigue and the spirit's contentment rolled in like a tide and quickly dragged her down into deep sleep. In her haze she felt him seem to rise, carefully draw a soft thin blanket over them both, then come back and gather her into his arms once more.
Outside the tent, the desert night wind blew on, and the river of stars turned slowly overhead. This ancient, silent land witnessed tonight the most primal and most beautiful union of a man and a woman after the boundaries of reason dissolved away. The future was still full of fog and challenges, but at least in this moment, they had each other, they had this boundless sky, and an attempt just begun — one bound by a clear-eyed promise.

Chapter 53

Early the next morning, just as the sky was growing light, Meng Wan turned up at the Lin household.

Lin Dong had no jobs today; for these three days his sole task was ferrying Lin Xi to and from the exam. Hao Xiangping had also risen at the crack of dawn to make her daughter breakfast.

Meng Wan ate breakfast with Lin Xi, said a few words to help her relax, then set out with Lin Dong to see her off to the exam hall. Hao Xiangping said she had to prepare lunch and so wouldn't come along.

In truth, she was afraid her own overwrought nerves would rub off on her daughter. She couldn't let her daughter down at the crucial moment.

On the way, Meng Wan told Lin Dong and Lin Xi about the celebration banquet she was planning for Lin Xi, and asked Lin Dong to tell Auntie Hao she needn't cook that day. She'd also invite Xu Qiuyun, James, and Zhou Nian along, so they could all give Lin Xi a lively, festive celebration.

Once Lin Xi was safely inside the exam hall, Meng Wan had Lin Dong drop her off at Xiaohai's place. Today was the day to take Qin Yi'e and Zhou Da to pick up their medical exam results.

Sure enough, the doctor had found a tumor in Qin Yi'e's stomach—but it was early-stage, and a simple operation would clear up any real danger. As for Zhou Da, his wound had become infected, and he'd need surgery as well to clean it out properly.

Meng Wan didn't dare waste a single moment. She immediately got the two of them admitted and their paperwork in order. Qin Yi'e had never been in a hospital before and was a little frightened to stay there alone, so Meng Wan spent a long while comforting her. With a nurse's help, she found a kindly nursing aide to look after her.

She also pressed some money into Zhou Nian's hand, asking her to go home and bring back fresh changes of clothes and daily necessities for the hospital. It would mean a few extra trips for her over the next few days—Meng Wan was grateful. Once Lin Xi's exams were over, she'd move into the Lin household to study.

After a day of running back and forth, Meng Wan finally made it back to the travel agency and at last got to set her nameplate on her office desk: "Manager: Meng Wan."

The more she looked at it, the more pleased she was.

James was especially attentive to Meng Wan's affairs. She'd only mentioned the matter of the contracts to him yesterday, and today he'd already brought the lawyer to the travel agency.

Meng Wan conferred with the lawyer for a while, laying out clearly her thoughts on employee rights and the protections the agency could provide its staff. The lawyer said he'd draw up a lawful, fully compliant labor contract according to her requirements.

Having asked the man to come all this way to help, Meng Wan had meant to pay him—she couldn't very well let him put in the work for nothing. But James said there was no need: the lawyer was a good friend of his, and he'd said he was just there to help out. Besides, drawing up a contract was child's play to them.

Hearing James out, Meng Wan didn't make any more fuss over it. She arranged to treat the lawyer to a meal sometime when he was free—that way they'd grow closer, and she could repay a little of the favor.

"Oh, that's right." James suddenly remembered something. "About what you asked me to look into before—the matter of the renovation worker—I asked the foreman of the construction crew for you."

It was about Qin Yizhong.

"How'd it go? Do you need more people over there?"

James shook his head. "The hotel doesn't need anyone at the moment. But the foreman knows other renovation crews and can refer Qin Yizhong to them. The city's right in the middle of a commercial-housing building boom, so renovation workers are in very short supply. He won't have any trouble finding work once he comes."

Hearing this set Meng Wan's mind at ease. Now she could call Qin Yizhong's family over without worry.

That same evening, Meng Wan sent a telegram to Qin Yizhong and his wife Ding Chunling, asking whether they'd be willing to come make a fresh start in Yuyang. If they were willing, they'd need to hurry up and settle their affairs back home and sort out the children's schooling.

The lawyer was very efficient. He sent the contract over the next day, and Meng Wan printed out a dozen-odd copies to have ready, planning to sign them whenever everyone was gathered together.

Luo Zhigang was picking up a new tour group today. Before he left, Meng Wan asked him about that college student, Jiang Ke, she'd run into at the train station some time back. At the time he'd said he was using a guide's license borrowed from someone at this agency, and Meng Wan guessed it could only have been Luo Zhigang who'd do such a thing.

Sure enough, Luo Zhigang admitted it. That student had only just graduated, and it had been a good while since he'd last rented the license—he was probably out job-hunting.

Meng Wan asked Luo Zhigang to check whether the student might be interested in working at their travel agency. After all, he had experience, so he could start right away without training—and right now, hands were exactly what they were short of.

Liu Fei was still escorting Xiaohai around with an inspection delegation and hadn't been back to the agency for several days. Xu Qiuyun was hard at work learning bookkeeping and account-balancing from Accountant Bi, run off her feet every day.

Meng Wan took several more calls. Two tour groups would be arriving the following week, both large—more than twenty people each. And Xiaohai's aunt, Chen Hui, had referred a group her way as well; this one wasn't so big, only seven or eight people, and Meng Wan decided to let Xiaohai try handling it on his own.

The three days of exams passed quickly, and with them came the celebration banquet.

Master Hai, punctual to a fault, turned up at the Lin household first thing in the morning, his vehicle loaded with fresh meat and vegetables he'd bought at the market at dawn.

For once Hao Xiangping ceded her kitchen to someone else—but she couldn't sit idle, so she made herself Master Hai's kitchen helper.

Around midday, Meng Wan arrived at the Lin household with Xu Qiuyun, Zhou Nian, and James in tow.

Lin Dong had set up a big round table right in the middle of the courtyard, borrowed from a neighbor.

Meng Wan had prepared a jade pendant carved with Lin Xi's zodiac animal, which she'd had Old Bai make. James had brought a Parker fountain pen—fitting for his big-spending style. Xu Qiuyun, going by Lin Xi's measurements, had bought her a dress; back when Lin Xi had gotten into the junior college, she'd wished for a new dress, but the wish had never come true.

It was the first time Lin Xi had ever received so many precious gifts. The girl was so moved she was nearly in tears.

Meng Wan introduced Zhou Nian to Lin Xi, hoping she might help tutor and coach the girl before she went off to university. Lin Xi thumped her chest and promised Meng Wan she'd make a fine pupil of Zhou Nian—she'd leave all her textbooks and notes to her.

Once they'd chatted their fill, Master Hai's dishes were just about all out of the pot. Everyone took their seats and began to savor the meal.

Meng Wan had another purpose in throwing this celebration banquet for Lin Xi: to let everyone sample Master Hai's cooking. If it truly measured up to Yuxiang Tower's standard, then she could open a restaurant of her own in Yuyang—that way she could bring tourists straight there to eat, guaranteeing a steady stream of customers. It was a surefire, can't-lose proposition.

They say it's hard to please every palate, but on the subject of Master Hai's cooking, everyone reached an unprecedented consensus. James went even further in his praise: in his view, though Master Hai's craft was rooted in Yuxiang Tower, he'd folded in his own distinctive refinements, putting his own heart into it, so that his dishes carried more of the warmth of real, lived-in home cooking.

With everyone's endorsement behind her, Meng Wan, set at ease, invited Master Hai into a room and told him what she had in mind.

"Have a seat, Master Hai." Meng Wan held out the unpaid balance to him. "This is the rest of your fee."

Master Hai glanced at the two hundred yuan she was offering and took only one bill. "That's too much—one note is enough. I haven't even spent all the ingredient money yet."

Meng Wan pressed the money into Master Hai's hand with a smile. "Take it, please. Consider this my way of currying favor with you."

"Why would you want to curry favor with me?" Master Hai asked bluntly. He was just a cook—who'd ever bother trying to win him over?

"I'd like to hire you as head chef at my restaurant." Meng Wan painted a grand pie in the sky out of thin air.

"You run a restaurant?" Master Hai looked at the young girl before him in astonishment.

Meng Wan smiled with a guilty conscience but kept her composure. "Our restaurant is short exactly one head chef with skills like yours. If you're willing to come, we'll be able to open our doors."

Subscribing to the principle of luring the man in first and worrying about the rest later, Meng Wan let the tall tales flow.

Seeing how earnestly Meng Wan spoke, Master Hai took her at her word. "I never imagined a young girl like you could be capable of so much. May I ask where your restaurant is?"

Meng Wan planned to open the restaurant near what would become the largest hotel in Yuyang in the years to come, so that getting back and forth between hotel and restaurant would be all the more convenient—because the restaurant's target customers were precisely the tourists.

And that hotel was the very Yunlai Hotel where James was staying, which in the future would likely be taken over by James himself. That way she'd have all three links—travel agency, hotel, and restaurant—connected end to end.

"Do you know the Yunlai Hotel? Our restaurant is right near there."

Master Hai laid out a map in his mind, gauging the distance from there to his home. It wasn't far, really—the commute would be manageable.

Seeing him still hesitating, Meng Wan went straight for her core selling point. "Your wage will be eighty yuan a month, and depending on how many customers we get each month, the restaurant will pay you a corresponding bonus. At year's end you'll also receive a share of the year's profits. If there's anything you're unhappy with, you can bring it up at any time, and we'll do everything in our power to put it right."

The terms Meng Wan offered were a world apart from the noodle shop's. Steady as he was, even Master Hai's heart was stirred. For the first half of his life he'd cooked the canteen pot for the state, drawing a fixed wage. He'd never dreamed that in his old age he'd stumble onto a job this good.

"You're not pulling my leg, are you?"

Master Hai still couldn't quite believe such a fine opportunity could land in his lap. It felt like a pie falling out of the sky—not the least bit real.

"Of course not. We run an honest business. If you find anything not to your liking, you can walk out then and there. We live in a society ruled by law now—you have your personal freedom."

True enough. If she failed to honor the promises she was making now, then he'd just leave. A job paying eighty a month might be hard to find, but he could always turn up another paying thirty or forty.

Master Hai studied Meng Wan, deep in thought, for a few seconds, then answered with conviction, "All right then—I'll throw in my lot with you!"

Having reeled in another able general, Meng Wan was in high spirits. She'd have to speed up her search for a site for the restaurant.

On the map she circled an area near the Yunlai Hotel, planning to find a good storefront somewhere within it for the restaurant.

No sooner said than done—she was gathering her things to head straight out when there came a knock at the office door.

"Come in."

It was Xu Qiuyun. The moment the door opened, her face was alarmed and she cried out in great agitation, "Manager Meng, this is bad—our travel agency's in the newspaper!"

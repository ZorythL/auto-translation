Chapter 4

The Meng family's dining table was a square one. Cheng Xuehua and Meng Qingyan sat at the head, Sun Jing and Zhizhi on the right, the Meng siblings on the left, and the guest, Song Qingyang, alone on one side.

Seeing that Meng Xingyang was still in the kitchen, Meng Wan naturally left the seat nearest Song Qingyang for him, to spare herself any repeat of the awkward moment from earlier. But no sooner had she pulled out her chair than Cheng Xuehua spoke up. "You sit over there, next to Xiao Song, and look after our guest."

Meng Wan's hand froze in midair. She looked at Cheng Xuehua, baffled. Since when was it her turn to look after the guests in this house?

Just then Meng Xingyang set the last dish on the table. Glancing at Meng Wan, half-seated and half-standing, he generously offered, "Mom, let her sit here. I'll look after the guest."

The moment the words left Cheng Xuehua's mouth, Sun Jing had grasped her mother-in-law's intent. Now, looking at her clueless husband, she shot him a stay-out-of-it look and cut off his words. "Xiao Song and Xiao Wan are close in age—sitting together, they'll have things to talk about. What are you butting in for?"

Meng Xingyang clearly still hadn't caught on, but seeing his wife making faces at him, he knew there was something he'd failed to understand. So he tactfully took his seat and said to Meng Wan with a chuckle, "Your sister-in-law's right. You young people sit together, it's easier."

Meng Wan shot Meng Xingyang a glare, then obediently sat down beside Song Qingyang. She wasn't the person she used to be—it was only looking after a guest, after all. Easy.

She picked up her chopsticks, sprang up from her seat, and one by one, from the farthest dish to the nearest, served Song Qingyang's bowl, narrating as she went. "Section Chief Song, this is my mom's specialty, braised fish. This is my sister-in-law's specialty, sweet-and-sour cabbage. And this is my brother's specialty, steamed pork with rice flour."

Then she poured him a cup of tea and said brazenly, "I can't cook, so I'll just pour you some tea. And since you've probably got work to get back to, I won't pour you any wine."

Having done all this, Meng Wan flashed Song Qingyang a textbook smile and said in a gentle voice, "Go on, try it. If there's not enough, I'll serve you more."

That was hospitality, wasn't it—as warm as could be? Compared to the old her, it was a world of difference. Today she'd show them all that she, Meng Wan, was no longer the Meng Wan of before.

Song Qingyang watched, scarcely able to keep up, as Meng Wan filled his bowl one dish at a time. Overwhelmed by this fierce barrage of hospitality, at a loss for what to do, he could only express his thanks through action, stuffing food into his mouth without pause.

Every so often he looked up, only to find Meng Wan smiling at him with such fervent expectation that, somehow, it was a little unsettling.

Watching his daughter being so attentive, Meng Qingyan couldn't keep the corners of his mouth from lifting. He remembered Song Qingyang was still single. If the chance arose, it might be worth nudging the two of them together—but that sort of talk was best left to his wife. He was a grown man; it wouldn't do for him to bring it up.

To keep the table from falling silent, Meng Qingyan, as head of the household, had to say something too. As it happened, he'd heard a piece of news a couple of days back, and Song Qingyang was in charge of exactly this—it would make good conversation.

"I hear Yuyang is aiming to become a tourist city, developing tourism as a pillar industry?"

Yuyang, the city where Meng Wan lived, had been the capital under three dynasties. Prosperous since ancient times, it boasted scenic and historic sites beyond counting, and the relics unearthed there drew scholars from far and wide to study them.

In recent years the people's material standard of living had climbed steadily, and their hunger for spiritual fulfillment grew ever more pronounced. Every weekend and holiday, tour groups came to Yuyang, the number of visitors rising year after year—and many foreign tourists made the trip especially to see for themselves.

Song Qingyang gratefully set down his chopsticks, glad for the excuse to eat a little less. On the subject of his own field, his expression turned earnest. "The directive from above is to suit measures to local conditions—where there are coal deposits, mine the coal vigorously; where there's farming, keep cultivating it deeply. Our Yuyang is rather special. Its most outstanding resources are these cultural relics. So the leaders have unanimously decided to develop tourism in a big way and build Yuyang into a tourist city."

Meng Xingyang had caught wind of some rumors too, and seized the chance to ask the man himself. "I hear that plot of land out in the suburbs is going to be used to build a hotel?"

At the mention of that land, Meng Wan's eyelid twitched. The bite she'd just been about to put in her mouth she quietly set back down.

In her past life, for her ex-husband's sake, she had pulled strings through Song Qingyang via her father and gotten that plot for him. Later the hotel was built and made him a fortune—and yet she, the one who'd done him the favor, was discarded the moment her usefulness ran out. The instant he learned she wasn't the real heiress, he'd divorced her, then turned around and married some high official's daughter.

The thought alone made her head throb dully. In this life she would never again work herself ragged sewing wedding clothes for someone else. A deal like that—pure profit, no risk—if only she could swallow it up herself. A pity that, as she was now, she not only had no money but owed someone else a debt she had yet to repay.

At the thought, she couldn't help sighing. Money, money, money. What was there to do to earn more of it?

Song Qingyang noticed, out of the corner of his eye, the chopsticks Meng Wan had set down. He replied, "Yes, that plot happens to be mine to manage. These past few days I've met with quite a few contractors, hoping to win the villagers the greatest possible benefits."

At that, Meng Wan felt even guiltier. In her past life, one of the biggest reasons Song Qingyang had agreed to give her the land was that her ex-husband had promised that, beyond the land-use payment, he'd arrange jobs at the hotel for all the able-bodied young people in the village. But not long after the hotel opened, he found one excuse after another to dismiss the villagers, and within half a year there was scarcely a single local left working there.

After that Meng Wan had deliberately avoided Song Qingyang, skipping any occasion where he might appear, terrified that he'd ask about it and she'd have no way to explain.

Meng Qingyan nodded with satisfaction—truly the student he'd handpicked. "Do good work. The people's better days rest on you young pillars of the next generation."

Just as the conversation had risen to the lofty plane of careers and ideals, Cheng Xuehua spotted her opening and dragged it right back down to the level of household chitchat. With an eager smile she asked, "Xiao Song, I remember you must be twenty-seven this year?"

"I'll turn twenty-seven once my birthday passes."

Cheng Xuehua nodded, then circled around to ask obliquely, "And how many years have you been seeing your girlfriend?"

For a moment Song Qingyang didn't follow—where had he gotten a girlfriend? Out of the corner of his eye he glanced at Meng Wan's reaction, then quickly explained, "Madam, I'm still single. I don't have a girlfriend."

Only now did it click for Meng Wan. Every cell in her body went on red alert as though facing a great enemy. Her slouched back snapped abruptly straight, and she stared at Cheng Xuehua at the head of the table with eyes gone round as saucers. Her mother wasn't trying to match the two of them, was she?

Spooning food into Zhizhi's mouth, Sun Jing privately gloated. So her foolish sister-in-law was only now catching on—just as dense as her brother.

Song Qingyang's answer was exactly what Cheng Xuehua had expected. She curved her lips in satisfaction and, pointedly ignoring her daughter's pleading gaze, put the question on her mind straight to him. "So what do you think of our Xiao Wan?"

Ever since Song Qingyang had started working, relatives at home had introduced him to blind dates from time to time, so he'd long grown unflappable in situations like this.

He'd had his own notions about his future partner. But he was always too busy to spare any thought for romance, and so had stayed single—really, it was that he'd never met anyone he liked. If he ever truly did, he'd take the initiative.

Take the Meng Wan before him, for instance. It hadn't quite reached love at first sight, but at the very least he didn't mind spending time with her—he even wanted to know her better. He just couldn't show such feelings after a single meeting, or they'd take him for a ravenous wolf pouncing on anything that came its way.

So Song Qingyang fell back on his usual stock answer to handle Cheng Xuehua. "Meng Wan and I don't know each other yet. For me to pass judgment on her would honestly be unfair to her. But I'm certain that any child raised by you and Teacher Meng couldn't possibly be anything less than wonderful."

A single sentence that both respected Meng Wan and flattered the two Meng elders.

Meng Wan shook her head furiously inside. You've got it dead wrong there. Even with parents like Meng Qingyan and Cheng Xuehua, the child they raised wasn't necessarily anything remarkable.

The Meng Wan of her past life was a textbook example.

Cheng Xuehua promptly dropped another bombshell. "You can get to know each other plenty later on. Let me tell you a bit about our Xiao Wan first. Our Xiao Wan is twenty-two this year, works at the cultural center, loves reading and painting in her spare time, and she's a hardworking, good-natured girl—the moment she's off work, she pitches in with the housework."

Meng Wan listened with her brows knotting tighter and tighter, her face heating faintly. Was this even her they were describing? Wasn't this just false advertising?

Beside her, Sun Jing silently wiped Zhizhi's mouth, listening to her mother-in-law hawk her wares so energetically and fighting to keep the corners of her own mouth down. She grumbled inwardly: lay it on this thick now, and when the man discovers the goods don't match the label, it's this sister-in-law of hers who'll catch it.

Meng Xingyang glanced at Sun Jing, knowing full well she was holding back laughter—since even he could barely control his own. He simply buried his head in his bowl and started eating.

Song Qingyang noticed nothing amiss among the Mengs, and listened to all this praise without the slightest disbelief. As he saw it, for a scholarly household to raise a child like this was perfectly natural.

He glanced at Meng Wan's faintly flushed cheeks and assumed she was shy. Girls, after all—her skin was a touch thinner than his.

"That's far better than me. My mom's always complaining I'm never home."

Meng Qingyan waved a big hand in defense of his beloved student. "You're just busy. Your position carries heavy responsibility, so naturally it demands more of your time. A man should put his career first."

Seeing Cheng Xuehua about to open her mouth again, Meng Wan shot to her feet in her panic and seized on a hasty excuse. "I'm finished eating. I just remembered there's some work I need to deal with—I have to make a trip back to the cultural center. Section Chief Song, please excuse me. Take your time with the meal."

Meng Wan grabbed her coat and headed for the door, hearing Cheng Xuehua's voice drift from the living room behind her. "The child's actually gone shy."

Shy?

Meng Wan grumbled to herself. The two lifetimes of her added together made her older than Meng Qingyan—shy, she was not. Mortified, more like.

Chapter 44

"You two—what do you think you're doing?"

Meng Wan stamped down on her pedals and shot forward, squeezing the brakes hard right in front of them and skidding to a stop with all the fury of an avenging spirit.

The moment Lin Dong saw her, it was as if he'd seen a ghost. Terrified, he scrambled into his car in a puff of smoke, gunned the engine, and fled the "scene of the crime" on the spot.

Choking on the stinking exhaust, Meng Wan coughed a couple of times and fanned the air in front of her nose. Then she turned her head and demanded, like an interrogator grilling a suspect, "What's going on here? Confess!"

Xu Qiuyun knew there was no hiding it now, so she came clean. "It's just what you saw. Xiaodong and I—we're seeing each other."

"How come I didn't know?" Meng Wan's eyes went wide, her voice full of wounded reproach. "Isn't this the kind of thing you're supposed to tell me first?"

She was the matchmaker who'd introduced them, after all!

Xu Qiuyun ducked her head and pulled at Meng Wan's wrist, her voice soft and coaxing. "I did mean to tell you, but..."

"But what?" Meng Wan huffed, eyes glaring.

"But that day you told us about your past." Xu Qiuyun looked at her with aching sympathy. "How could I just bask in my own happiness and rub salt in your wound?"

Xu Qiuyun had originally been planning to confide in Meng Wan that day, but she hadn't expected Meng Wan to beat her to it and pour out the story of her own origins. After that, bringing up her own news would have felt rather out of place.

In an instant, half of Meng Wan's anger melted away. "And here I thought you two didn't count me as a friend."

"How could you think that?" Xu Qiuyun protested loudly. "Without you there'd be no Lin Dong and me today—we'd never even have met. You're our matchmaker. When we get married, we'll be sure to raise a few good toasts to you."

"Really?" Meng Wan pursed her lips, just a little smug.

"Of course." Xu Qiuyun knew exactly what Meng Wan liked to hear. "We'll even save you a special seat—you'll sit at the head table and give the speech on behalf of my side of the family."

Meng Wan mumbled, "Now that's more like it."

"All right, don't be cross anymore." Xu Qiuyun shook Meng Wan's wrist and asked with a wink, "So what brought you here to find me today? Was there something?"

"Oh!" Only then did Meng Wan remember her actual errand. "I came to recruit you under my banner."

And then she told Xu Qiuyun everything that had happened these past few days, down to the last detail.

Xu Qiuyun's eyes went round with shock. "A few days out of touch and you've already become a boss! No wonder you had me get my accounting certificate—you'd been planning this all along, hadn't you?"

Ever since Meng Wan had signed that contract, Xu Qiuyun was still the first friend to hear of it. Hearing her astonishment and praise, Meng Wan was simply over the moon, her vanity bursting at the seams.

She grinned and nodded. "It's just that it all came a lot faster than I'd imagined."

Xu Qiuyun was genuinely happy for her. She thought it over for a moment, then looked at Meng Wan and said in earnest, "I'll go talk to Director Wu about resigning this afternoon. If it goes smoothly, I can report to the travel agency tomorrow."

"That fast?" Meng Wan was overjoyed. "Don't you need to talk it over with your family first?"

Xu Qiuyun gave a soft snort. "My affairs are none of their business. Anyway, I'll do whatever you say."

"All right then." Afraid she'd dredge up painful memories, Meng Wan quickly changed the subject. "Whatever you do, don't tell Director Wu I'm the one who poached you—otherwise he'll blame me for playing dirty."

Xu Qiuyun laughed lightly. "Don't worry. I guarantee I won't so much as mention your name."

------

Meng Wan made another trip to Meng Xingyang's workplace. She'd listened with only half an ear the day before; today she went back to get the details straight and to thank him properly while she was at it.

Arriving at the Surveying and Mapping Bureau, Meng Wan found her way to Meng Xingyang's office with practiced ease. His colleague said he was in a meeting and told her to go in and wait.

As a section chief at the bureau, Meng Xingyang had his own private office—small, but reasonably well furnished.

Meng Wan paced the room with her hands clasped behind her back. Wandering this way and that, she came to a stop in front of his desk and curiously took in the traces of her brother's work. Several stacks of documents were squared away neatly, and across the desktop were spread a few drawings he hadn't finished reviewing, his annotations beside them.

She rested her arm on the back of his chair, and in a haze she noticed a single long black hair caught on the chair back.

The chair was upholstered in black fabric, so unless you looked closely you'd never spot it. Frowning, she carefully pinched the hair loose and let it dangle freely in the air.

And it was a wave of hair, too, of moderate length!

Her warning radar instantly went off. Instinct told Meng Wan this was no simple matter.

This was Meng Xingyang's desk. Apart from him, who would sit here? Not a subordinate, certainly. And a superior would never take a subordinate's seat. Only someone on very intimate terms would do such a thing—and Sun Jing had straight hair.

Which meant that, apart from Sun Jing, Meng Xingyang had someone else close to him.

Who could it be?

Knock, knock, knock!

A burst of knocking suddenly rang out, and Meng Wan jumped like a thief caught in the act, her heart pounding. She quickly clenched the strand of hair in her fist, hid her hand behind her back, stepped to the door, and opened it with her other hand.

"Section Chief Meng."

It was a young woman who'd knocked. Before Meng Wan could even make out her face, a sweet, simpering voice reached her ears. The moment the woman saw that it was Meng Wan who'd answered, the smile at the corners of her mouth gradually drew back, and, a little taken aback, she stepped out to glance at the nameplate again.

This was Section Chief Meng's office, no mistake.

Meng Wan read her confusion and explained, "My brother's gone to a meeting. He'll be back in a bit. Did you need him for something?"

At that, the smile climbed back to the corners of the woman's eyes and brows. "You must be Meng Wan. I've heard Section Chief Meng mention you."

"Have you?" Meng Wan bent slightly at the waist to invite her in, her gaze fixed tightly on the woman's head.

This young woman just happened to have shoulder-length waves.

Could it really be her?

Meng Wan went on sizing her up without letting it show, ushered her to the sofa, and tucked the hair into her trouser pocket.

A single strand of hair really didn't prove anything. She'd just have to draw her out and see whether she could pry anything loose.

Feigning idle chitchat, Meng Wan asked, "I don't think I caught your name, miss—and what position do you hold here?"

Hearing herself called pretty by a girl as lovely as a flower, Lan Xiuli couldn't suppress the upward curve of her lips. "My name is Lan Xiuli. I'm with the Archives Section."

Lan?

Meng Wan caught a whiff of something off. She suddenly remembered that in her past life, her sister-in-law Sun Jing had once mentioned a pretty young woman at Meng Xingyang's workplace. She couldn't recall the woman's given name, but the surname was Lan—because it was such an uncommon surname, she remembered it that clearly.

Sun Jing had brought up this Miss Lan more than once. The time their conflict had flared up was after Meng Xingyang and Lan Xiuli went on a business trip together; afterward, while packing up his luggage, Sun Jing had found a "love letter" Lan Xiuli had written.

It was over that very incident that the two of them had, for the first time ever, had a blazing row. Sun Jing said Meng Xingyang had someone on the side; Meng Xingyang insisted he'd had no idea the letter existed—otherwise he'd never have handed his luggage over to her without a care.

Ugly as his words were, the logic did hold up. If he'd known the letter was there, he'd surely have either kept it on his person or left it at the office. Bringing it home was just asking to be found out.

But Sun Jing still wouldn't let it go. She said that even if he hadn't known about the letter, something must have happened between the two of them—otherwise the woman wouldn't have slipped a letter into his luggage for no reason at all.

It was only then that Meng Xingyang told the truth: Lan Xiuli had indeed declared her feelings to him, but he had rejected her outright, stating plainly that he was a married man and would never do anything to wrong his wife and child.

He'd kept the matter hushed and never reported it precisely because he was afraid Sun Jing would read too much into it—only to have it exposed by that letter anyway.

A few words alone still weren't enough for Sun Jing to believe him. She'd met Lan Xiuli and knew just how irresistible a woman that pretty and gentle could be to a man—the average man couldn't possibly refuse her.

And in Sun Jing's eyes, Meng Xingyang was exactly an average man.

In a fit of anger, Sun Jing took the child and went back to her mother's home.

In the end it was Cheng Xuehua who'd stepped in, made Meng Xingyang write a written pledge, and gone to the Sun family to coax Sun Jing back, giving her plenty of face.

Meng Wan had later asked Meng Xingyang about it, and his answer was the same as the explanation he'd given Sun Jing—he swore he'd done nothing to betray his family.

Sun Jing aside, even Meng Wan couldn't fully trust her brother. That baser streak in men simply couldn't withstand the test of the word "trust."

Later still, at Meng Qingyan's insistence, Meng Xingyang had Lan Xiuli transferred to a different work unit, and only then did the whole affair finally die down.

True, Sun Jing and Meng Xingyang hadn't divorced, but a crack had been left in their marriage all the same. Every time they quarreled, Sun Jing would dredge up the past, and Meng Xingyang would wear a look of weary impatience, the two of them wearing away at their feelings for each other.

Judging from this strand of hair, Meng Wan reckoned the two of them must already be teetering right on the warning line—a single push at this point would send them forward, while a single pull would mean nothing had happened at all.

Even if the hair was only a coincidence, Meng Wan couldn't just sit and wait to be struck down. She wanted her brother and his wife to be happy.

"You're so pretty, Miss Lan—surely you're already spoken for?" Meng Wan probed, feigning innocence.

"Spoken for, ha." A shadow crossed Lan Xiuli's eyes, and she sighed. "My husband passed away a few years back. I'm a widow now."

Meng Wan raised her brows in mild surprise and offered comfort. "I'm sorry—I didn't mean to bring up something painful."

"Ah, I turned that page long ago. A person can't live in the past; you have to look ahead."

Those were words Meng Xingyang had said to her.

A false smile on her face, Meng Wan thought to herself: looking ahead is fine, but not down the wrong path.

"And you've never thought about finding someone again?"

"Thought about it, yes." A name flashed quickly through Lan Xiuli's mind. "I'm just afraid the falling flower yearns while the flowing stream feels nothing."

Meng Wan caught the scent of something. She was about to press further when the door was pushed open—Meng Xingyang was back.

Their conversation was cut short, and Lan Xiuli came back to herself with a jolt. She really shouldn't have said so much to Meng Wan just now.

"Brother, what are you doing back?"

What terrible timing!

Meng Xingyang looked baffled. "This is my office—why shouldn't I come back?"

"I mean, why are you back so late? Sister Xiuli's been waiting quite a while for you," Meng Wan said, testing his reaction.

Only then did Meng Xingyang notice there was another person sitting on the sofa by the wall. A flicker of displeasure passed through his eyes, and he turned to shut the door.

"I didn't wait long." Lan Xiuli hurriedly stood, clutching her files. "I came to deliver the archives Section Chief Meng needed. Xiao Li was tied up, so he asked me to bring them over."

Meng Xingyang sat down at his desk, his tone flat and cool. "Understood. Just leave them there. I'll return them myself once I've looked them over."

Lan Xiuli bit lightly at her lower lip, looking as if she wanted to say something, but a glance at Meng Wan beside her made her swallow it back down. All she said was, "All right, Section Chief Meng."

Meng Wan watched the two of them, every word and gesture. There was a problem—there was definitely a problem! This was not how ordinary colleagues spoke to each other. She didn't talk to Liu Fei or Luo Zhigang like that.

Once Lan Xiuli had gone, Meng Wan went straight over and sat down across from Meng Xingyang. She said nothing, arms crossed over her chest, chin tilted high, fixing him with the unblinking stare of an interrogator.

Her gaze made Meng Xingyang's skin crawl, and at last he couldn't take it. "Little princess, I'm not your prisoner."

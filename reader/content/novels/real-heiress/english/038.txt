Chapter 38

In an instant the room dissolved into chaos.

Qin Yizhong and Ding Chunling stared at Meng Wan in shock, honestly unsure whether she'd come to congratulate Zhou Nian on her marriage or to blow the whole thing up. And Qin Yi'e, of all people, hadn't said a single word—what on earth were these two playing at?

The Sun mother watched the match she'd worked so hard to arrange for her son come undone at a few words from this slip of a girl, and stood frozen on the spot, her hands trembling uncontrollably. She grabbed Qin Yi'e's arm, trying to make her say something.

Qin Yi'e turned her face aside, not daring to look at the Sun mother. Meeting the eyes of her brother and sister-in-law, she let her own gaze dart guiltily back and forth. She'd meant to find some excuse to break off the marriage herself—but she'd never expected Meng Wan to settle it so airily, with just a few words, catching her completely off guard.

Zhou Nian, too, stood there dazed. That very morning Qin Yi'e had been about to take her to the market again to buy red paper for cutting out wedding characters—so how had the marriage gone, in the space of a breath, from on to off? What in the world was Meng Wan scheming?

Zhou Da, who'd been in the inner room the whole time, couldn't hold still any longer either. He picked up the crutch leaning against the wall and rapped it against the door frame. "What's going on out there? The marriage is off, just like that?"

Qin Yi'e pulled her arm free of the Sun mother's grip, went inside, and soothed Zhou Da with a few words. Whatever she said, by the time she came back out, Zhou Da had fallen quiet.

Meng Wan glanced at Zhou Da, then held out a hand to Qin Yi'e. "Where's the money?"

"Ah!" Qin Yi'e had been so focused on calling off the marriage—the money was long gone, spent buying medicine for Zhou Da. "I've spent it all..."

A few days back Zhou Da's wound had flared up again. She'd originally meant to keep half the money for her daughter, but not a cent was left; it had all gone to Zhou Da's doctoring.

Hearing this, the Sun mother felt as if she'd grasped another piece of driftwood. From what she knew of the Zhou family, there was no way they could produce a sum like that.

The instant Zhao Xiuqin heard the money was gone, she half-rose from her seat in alarm. Meng Wan quickly made a gesture, signaling her not to panic.

She took her wallet out of her pocket and asked, "How much bride-price did the Sun family pay in total?"

"Three thousand yuan."

Meng Wan's brow furrowed slightly. That truly was no small sum. That the Sun family would put up such a large bride-price genuinely caught her off guard.

Her gaze fell once more on the evasive-eyed Qin Yi'e. Surely it hadn't been Qin Yi'e who'd demanded a figure this high?

Meng Wan hadn't anticipated needing so much money, so she'd only brought two thousand when she set out—and even that she'd agonized over for ages. At first she'd planned to carry only a thousand; out here, far from anywhere, with the mountains high and the emperor distant, it never felt safe to have that much cash on her.

Luckily there was James. She turned to throw him a pleading look, and he understood at once.

James drew his own wallet from his pocket and handed it straight to Meng Wan.

Meng Wan's eyes thanked him first; then she counted out ten hundreds, added them to her two thousand, and returned the lot to the Sun mother.

"Take this money." She turned to Zhao Xiuqin. "Go home and settle among yourselves how it's to be divided. Once you've reached a decision, it won't be too late to talk about the marriage then—otherwise Zhou Nian will have no good life even if she does marry in."

That truly hit the mark. In her past life Zhou Nian had said as much: from the day she married in, her eldest sister-in-law had made her life difficult, and being the meek sort, she'd never dared push back.

"Sis, are you really going to just stand there and watch her cancel Nian'er's wedding?" Qin Yizhong looked on, utterly baffled by the scene before him.

Qin Yi'e shot him a look. "You saw it all yourself, didn't you? It's the Sun family's own affairs they couldn't sort out. Do you want to send Nian'er off to suffer?"

"But—" Qin Yizhong was about to say more when Ding Chunling tugged his arm and shook her head, signaling him not to interfere. Clearly Qin Yi'e and Meng Wan had reached some understanding and were set on calling the marriage off.

The Sun mother clutched the three thousand yuan, at a loss for what to do. The wedding was this Saturday; to give back the bride-price now—what would that look like? How was she to explain it to friends and relatives? And most important of all, how was she to explain it to her son?

Jianxing was thoroughly pleased with Zhou Nian as a bride; he'd long been looking forward to Saturday's wedding.

Unwilling to give up, the Sun mother pleaded again. "Zhou Nian's mother, since Xiuqin insists on taking back half the bride-price, then you keep the other half. Half each—that way, when Zhou Nian marries in, Xiuqin won't have any cause for complaint. What do you say?"

The bride-price hadn't originally been three thousand at all; Qin Yi'e had forced it up to three thousand, refusing otherwise to let Zhou Nian marry in—which was what had moved the Sun mother to dip into her own dowry.

Qin Yi'e had hardened her resolve too: since Meng Wan said the marriage couldn't go ahead, then it couldn't. "Eldest sister-in-law, when I asked you for that much bride-price, it was so you'd value our Nian'er and not treat her lightly. But from the look of things now, even with this bride-price, she'd still be bullied."

As she said it, Qin Yi'e glanced sidelong at Zhao Xiuqin.

Zhao Xiuqin gave an indifferent snort. "Pretty words. In the end you spent it all anyway, didn't you?"

His face twisted with pain, the Sun mother made one last struggle. "Nian'er, Auntie Sun's always treated you well, hasn't she? Can you tell Auntie what's in your heart? Jianxing is so very fond of you—he's been longing to bring you home as his bride. Don't you want to marry him?"

Zhou Nian looked round at the roomful of people, helpless, one hand stroking back and forth over the jade bracelet, turning the Sun mother's question over in her mind.

Did she want to marry Sun Jianxing?

She supposed she did. Sun Jianxing was good-looking and an educated man; the way he spoke and carried himself was unlike any other man she'd ever known.

They'd met only twice, but each time, Sun Jianxing had brought her a bunch of flowers.

That was the whole of it.

She'd heard Qin Yi'e say before that her future sister-in-law, Zhao Xiuqin, was hard to get along with. She hadn't taken it to heart at the time—they'd be one family, after all, living together and helping each other, seeing each other day in and day out; surely the relationship would warm over time.

But now, having met the woman in person, she gradually realized there was no chance of their growing close. With this money wedged between them, Zhao Xiuqin would never give her a kind look. Even her future mother-in-law was helpless against Zhao Xiuqin—let alone meek Zhou Nian herself, who'd surely never be able to cope.

In her heart she began to choose: was she to endure being bullied and be with Sun Jianxing, a man she felt some fondness for—or to be free of bullying and find someone of comparable circumstances instead?

After a few seconds' thought, she chose the latter.

She wasn't even married in yet, and Zhao Xiuqin had already come to the house to make her suffer. Once she married in, who knew what else might be lying in wait for her.

"I'm sorry, Auntie Sun." Zhou Nian paused. "The money and the bracelet—please take them all back."

With that she worked the jade bracelet off her wrist and returned it into the Sun mother's own hands.

Meng Wan let out a quiet breath of relief. At last it was done.

Off to the side Zhao Xiuqin curled her lip and said snidely, "See that? No matter how good you are to people, they don't appreciate it one bit."

Holding the bracelet, the Sun mother's eyes reddened. "Nian'er, won't you really reconsider?"

"Auntie Sun, let's just say Jianxing and I weren't meant to be. He's sure to find a far better match."

Having said her piece, Zhou Nian turned on her heel, fled back into the inner room, and shut the door.

"Let's go—nothing more to see here." Zhao Xiuqin stood, brushing off the seat of her trousers. "Once we're home, let's split that money quick. Whatever kind of bride you find for your son after that is no concern of mine."

The Sun mother, money clenched in one fist and bracelet in the other, glared hard at Zhao Xiuqin, stamped her foot in fury, and turned to march out the gate.

Zhao Xiuqin trailed sulkily after her.

Who'd have thought it could be settled so fast. Meng Wan returned the wallet to James and called Zhou Nian back out.

In front of everyone, she laid out her plan. "Pack your things now. Tomorrow you're all coming with me."

Qin Yi'e looked at her, uncomprehending, and asked, "Where are you taking us?"

"To Yuyang." Meng Wan spelled out what she had in mind. "Zhou Nian is going to go back to school—I'll arrange a place for her. As for you and—"

Meng Wan glanced at Zhou Da, who lay on the kang watching her from the inner room. "—I'll take you to a hospital in the city for a checkup, and after that I'll arrange somewhere for you to live. You don't need to worry about anything else. I'll cover all the costs."

"No—I won't go!" Qin Yi'e's stance was firm. If all three of them went, wouldn't they just be a burden to the girl? "You take Nian'er. Her father and I will stay."

"Ma." Zhou Nian stepped up and took hold of Qin Yi'e's arm.

"What about you?" Meng Wan turned to Zhou Da. "What do you think?"

"I'll go—I'll go with you!"

Zhou Da's heart pounded wildly. A stroke of luck like this, falling to him? And his foolish wife had actually turned it down—she truly didn't know good from bad.

Listening in, Ding Chunling felt her own heart stir. She'd love to follow Meng Wan back too. Her sister-in-law was simply too narrow in her outlook—she had no idea how well Yuyang was developing these days; in her eyes there was nothing but Dongping Village and her own little patch of ground.

Qin Yi'e went on refusing, stubborn as ever. "The rest of you go. I'll stay behind and keep watch here. I'm not going anywhere!"

The moment she set foot in that city, she would have to be ready for the day Meng Wan and Zhou Nian's true origins came to light. How would she face Nian'er then? How would she face the Meng family? How would she face Meng Wan?

And once she was there, she'd have nothing to do, nothing to rely on but Meng Wan to get by—wasn't that just adding to the girl's load? A young thing like her, how could she possibly shoulder the upkeep of several mouths?

Meng Wan understood what she was resisting, but no matter what, she meant to take her in for a full physical examination—she wouldn't rest easy until she knew the woman was well.

She pressed on with her persuasion. "You've as good as broken off Zhou Nian's engagement just now. Word of it will be all over Dongping Village in no time. Do you think Zhou Nian can go on living here without the village talking? Will anyone of better standing still come to propose a match? If you won't go with her—if you leave the two of you old folks behind here—how could she ever be at ease? Even if you won't think of yourself, you have to think of her."

"Ma." Zhou Nian gave Qin Yi'e's arm a wheedling little push. "Just come with us."

She'd long heard her little aunt talk of how fine Yuyang was, and had dreamed of one day seeing it with her own eyes. She never imagined someone would suddenly appear out of nowhere and say she could go to Yuyang to study—before today she wouldn't even have dared to dream it.

Just moments ago she'd been fretting over what her future held; now, in the blink of an eye, the way had opened bright before her.

Qin Yi'e's eyes grew moist as she stroked Zhou Nian's hand. The greatest wrong she had done in this life was to Nian'er. If the chance came, she wanted nothing more than for the girl to have the very best, too.

After a long hesitation, Qin Yi'e finally spoke, reluctant. "All right. I'll go with you."

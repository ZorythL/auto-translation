Chapter 24

Qin Weimin's eyes spat sparks as he glared at Xiaohai. If not for him, Qin Weimin wouldn't have been hauled in alongside Chen Hui for a dressing-down by the administrative supervisor. He never should have agreed to let Chen Hui's nephew come work here in the first place. If it hadn't been for the fact that Chen Hui was a longtime fixture at the hotel asking a favor for the first time, he'd never have let someone with a record set foot in the place.

Xiaohai's face flushed crimson at once. The back he'd only just straightened slumped over again in an instant, and he didn't dare meet Qin Weimin's eyes.

"Manager Qin." Meng Wan rapped her knuckles on the counter. "Xiaohai is learning the trade under me now. We're here on business today, so I'll thank you to mind your manners."

"You're teaching him the trade?" Qin Weimin's eyes went wide with disbelief. "Surely you know that he—"

Before he could get it out, Meng Wan cut him off at once. "I know. He made some mistakes in the past, but he's paid the price for them. Does doing something wrong mean a person never gets a chance to turn over a new leaf? He's only twenty—is he supposed to shoulder that mistake his whole life?"

Qin Weimin was struck dumb. He'd been looking out for Meng Wan's interests out of goodwill, and here she was lecturing him. To think he'd once considered her a young woman with real promise; now he didn't know what wire had come loose in her head.

The hard words said, it was time for the soft ones. Meng Wan dropped her earlier sternness and put on a warm, friendly face. "Manager Qin, the words just ran away with me—forgive me if my tone was a bit sharp. It's only that I can't bear to watch a fine young man keep wasting away. If I can lend a hand, I will. He'll be in and out of the hotel with me often from now on, so do me this favor and let bygones be bygones. Harmony brings prosperity, after all."

With that, Meng Wan took the pack of Marlboros she always kept on hand out of her bag and pressed it into Qin Weimin's grip. "Manager Qin, don't keep that face on—give us a smile."

Meng Wan was playing to her advantage as a woman, laying on a bit of coquetry with Qin Weimin, and the effect was plain to see. Qin Weimin's ears went suddenly hot; flustered, he stuffed the cigarettes into his trouser pocket, his face no longer as sour as before, his tone considerably gentler.

"Group tour or walk-ins?"

With the topic shifted to work, Xiaohai finally lifted his head a little. He knew this meant Qin Weimin had decided to let the matter drop.

"Group tour." Meng Wan grinned and produced a slip of paper from her bag, handing it to Qin Weimin.

Qin Weimin took it and gave it a glance, mildly surprised. "Another big group, twenty-some people. You've got quite the connections."

For a guide as new as Meng Wan, the agency would at most assign a few walk-ins, or a group of ten people at the very most. One large group after another like this—her client base was nearly catching up to guides who'd been at it three or four years.

A bit guiltily, Meng Wan trotted out the excuse she'd long since prepared. "Someone in my family is in this line too. These came through a relative's introduction."

Qin Weimin quickly registered Meng Wan and set the rooms aside for her.

Meng Wan thanked him with a grin, then tugged Xiaohai over and had him bow to Qin Weimin. "Xiaohai really did do wrong before, and caused you no end of trouble. From now on, judge him by how he behaves—he definitely won't let you down."

Watching the youth bend his back, the knot of resentment in Qin Weimin's chest dissolved along with it, though his mouth still wouldn't yield. "I've got no hopes pinned on him, so there's nothing for him to let down. Now take your prize apprentice and clear off—the supervisor later on won't be as easy to talk to as me."

*

As usual, Meng Wan finished her work and got home to find that Cheng Xuehua had already made dinner.

"Wash your hands and come eat."

Meng Wan looked around and noticed the house was empty. She went to her room to put away her bag, then peeked into the kitchen. "Mom, how come it's just you at home?"

"Your dad has an academic conference—it'll run a little late." Cheng Xuehua carried a dish out to the living room. "Your brother and sister-in-law took Zhizhi to her grandmother's place."

Meng Wan washed her hands, and she and Cheng Xuehua sat down to dinner together.

Watching her daughter dig in heartily, Cheng Xuehua couldn't hold back the thing she'd meant to save until after the meal.

"Guess who I ran into at the hospital a few days ago?"

"Who?"

"Little Song and his mother."

Song Qingyang? Was he sick, or was his mother ill?

Puzzled, Meng Wan waited for Cheng Xuehua to go on.

"Little Song's mother turned up high blood sugar on her physical and came in for a follow-up. I happened to run into them, so I had a word with the doctors and nurses to take extra good care of her."

Meng Wan kept eating, unable to make heads or tails of where this was going.

Cheng Xuehua continued, "Today Little Song's mother came to pick up her lab report, and she made a special trip to my office to thank me—said she wants to treat me to a meal this weekend."

"And you agreed?"

Cheng Xuehua wasn't one to chase after small advantages; she wouldn't accept an invitation over something this minor.

"I'd actually turned her down." Cheng Xuehua said haltingly, watching her daughter's face. "But Little Song's mother said he's off this Saturday and she'd bring him along, and she asked me to bring you too—so we could all have a meal together."

So that was it.

"And that's why you agreed?"

Cheng Xuehua said nothing, just smiled and nodded.

So this was a meet-the-parents, then? She and Song Qingyang weren't anywhere near that point. The two families' matchmaking intentions couldn't have been more obvious.

A bit irritated, Meng Wan set down her chopsticks. "Song Qingyang and I aren't suited."

Song Qingyang was clearly the sort who put great weight on a woman's family background. Right now she was still the young miss of the Meng household, so he and his mother could arrange to meet her. But once they learned Meng Wan's true origins, whether they'd still be satisfied was anyone's guess. She had no wish to take that gamble.

"Why aren't you suited?"

Cheng Xuehua had no idea what was weighing on Meng Wan's mind. In her eyes, her daughter was a more than worthy match for Song Qingyang. The two families were well matched in standing, and her daughter wouldn't suffer a day if she married into his.

Meng Wan had no way to explain to Cheng Xuehua, so she could only fumble for a handy excuse. "I've only got a high school education, and Song Qingyang's been to university. He's sure to want someone with the same level of schooling."

Hearing that this was her daughter's concern, Cheng Xuehua relaxed at once. "Don't you worry. Little Song has no such notions—last time he saw me he even asked how you'd been doing lately. And besides, you may not have gone to university, but your job is nothing to scoff at. Not just anyone can land a post at the cultural center. Your dad even heard your supervisor praising you not long ago, saying you're doing very well there."

Cheng Xuehua had assumed her daughter was an unambitious sort who just coasted along at work, and here she'd actually drawn praise from her supervisor. That meant her daughter was doing just fine—no shame brought on the family at all.

At the mention of the cultural center, Meng Wan grew uneasy, her eyes darting all over the place.

"Oh, that's right." The mention of Meng Wan's job stirred up a fresh question in Cheng Xuehua. "Why have you been so busy lately, out early and back late every day? Has your supervisor piled a lot of work on you?"

"Huh?" For a moment Meng Wan didn't know how to answer.

"And another thing." Cheng Xuehua pressed on. "Doesn't it seem like you've gotten darker lately?"

The cultural center was indoor work—how could she possibly be getting a tan?

"Have I gotten darker?" Meng Wan spread the back of her hand and inspected it from side to side. "Maybe it's because I've been biking to work lately, so I'm getting tanned on the way."

Seeing that Cheng Xuehua was still staring at her hand, Meng Wan, afraid that another question would give her away, quickly changed the subject.

"Saturday, was it? If I'm free Saturday I'll go with you."

Sure enough, the moment Cheng Xuehua heard this, she threw all her earlier questions out the window. Nothing mattered more to her than her daughter's personal affairs.

"You'll definitely be free Saturday." Seeing her relent, Cheng Xuehua decided it for her on the spot. "It's settled, then. The two of us will go together Saturday."

*

The tour group was arriving that afternoon, so in the morning Meng Wan planned to go nail down the time with the driver and head to the train station together to meet them.

Bright and early that day, Meng Wan had just come downstairs to the front gate when she got a fright—Xiaohai was crouched against the wall, lying in wait.

"What are you crouching here for?"

Xiaohai pointed at the bicycle. I came to pick you up.

Yesterday Xiaohai had ridden her home, and Meng Wan had told him to take the bike back and just meet her straight at the train station today. The two homes weren't close—riding over meant crossing half of Yuyang. He'd have done better to ride straight to the station instead.

She hadn't expected the boy to be so earnest, going so far out of his way to fetch her, dripping with sweat.

Meng Wan didn't know whether to laugh or cry. She pulled out a handkerchief and handed it to him. "Wipe yourself off."

Afraid of dirtying the handkerchief, Xiaohai gave his forehead a couple of clumsy swipes with the back of his hand.

Meng Wan slapped his hand away in distaste. "When I tell you to wipe, you wipe. Just wash it clean and give it back to me, simple as that."

Only then did Xiaohai obediently take the handkerchief and carefully wipe the beads of sweat away until he was thoroughly clean.

When he'd finished, Xiaohai folded the handkerchief neatly and tucked it into his trouser pocket, then pointed at the back seat, signaling Meng Wan to get on.

Meng Wan couldn't help but smile. Maybe taking on this apprentice had its perks after all.

The two of them reached the coach depot but, after searching a while, couldn't find any sign of Wang Hongqiang. Asking around, they learned Driver Wang had fallen ill these past few days and hadn't been in to work.

Meng Wan was thrown into a panic. It was peak season lately, and most of the drivers were booked solid with no time to spare—right now she truly couldn't dig up anyone else to help.

As she stood there spinning in circles, a taxi suddenly drove past, and a solution flashed across her mind in an instant.

That's right—there was still Lin Dong!

But at this hour Lin Dong would surely be out picking up fares already, and she had no way to reach him. All Meng Wan could do was take a chance and head to Lin Dong's home to try her luck first.

Xiaohai simply followed Meng Wan's orders, dutifully serving as her driver, going wherever she pointed. He pedaled the bike back to the old city district he'd set out from, and before the two of them had even reached the mouth of the alley, someone called out to them.

Hao Xiangping's eyes had recovered nicely; during the day she'd sit at the mouth of the lane with the neighbors, shelling vegetables and chatting. From a distance she spotted a bicycle, and only when it had ridden up close did she realize Meng Wan was on the back. So pleased she was that she cried out at once.

Learning that Meng Wan had come looking for Lin Dong, Hao Xiangping told her that Lin Dong had gone out first thing in the morning. Before Meng Wan's disappointment could even set in, she heard Hao Xiangping add, "But Xiaodong got himself a secondhand pager not long ago, so it's easy for me to reach him if anything urgent comes up. If you're in a hurry, you can just page him."

Meng Wan's heart dropped right back into her belly. She was saved!

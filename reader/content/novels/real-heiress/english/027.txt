Chapter 27

Meng Wan was startled too—she'd never expected such a coincidence, running into a colleague here.

Accountant Bi had told her their agency had only three guides counting herself. It seemed this was the other third.

Only now did Meng Wan take a proper look at the man before her. His skin was on the dark side, his eyes set a little wide apart, his lips somewhat dry. When he smiled he looked like an honest, simple farmhand.

"Hello, I'm Meng Wan." She introduced herself first.

"Hello, I'm Luo Zhigang."

Luo Zhigang looked at Meng Wan with delight. "A few days back I heard from old Bi that the agency had taken on a new guide, but I've been so busy I never got the chance to meet you. Who'd have thought I'd run into you here today?"

When old Bi had told him a young, pretty woman guide had joined, he hadn't believed it. He couldn't exactly trust old Bi's taste—the man called even cross-mouthed Auntie Xu across the street good-looking.

"I've only been back a few times, and Accountant Bi was always there on his own. I never managed to meet anyone."

Once the two of them had finished their friendly small talk, Luo Zhigang fished around for information. "Did old Bi set you up with this group?"

A resource this good and old Bi hadn't thought of him first? That was hardly decent of him.

"No," Meng Wan explained. "A friend introduced this one to me."

So that was it. Luo Zhigang wanted to ask more, but just then the shop manager suddenly raised his voice and called out, "For every customer who's bought a Three-Tiered Cloud pendant today, we'll also throw in a rabbit jade pendant as a free gift for everyone."

He held up a crystal-clear little rabbit in his hand.

Everyone looked toward it—a vivid little rabbit jade pendant, its eyes specially dotted in with red pigment.

Meng Wan's gaze fell on the little rabbit too. It really was awfully cute, carved most lifelike, and those two red dots for eyes were a stroke of genius.

"How come we don't get one?" complained someone from the group next door who'd bought a pendant.

Everyone had paid the same money—why should others get a free gift?

In his excitement the manager had forgotten there were other customers next door. He shot Meng Wan an awkward glance and, faced with the choice between taking the blame himself or letting the guide take it, spent zero seconds choosing the latter.

"Actually, it's not a free gift." The manager guiltily avoided Meng Wan's eyes. "This lady guide paid for them herself, out of her own pocket, for her group members. She's so modest she wouldn't even let us mention it."

Meng Wan's face went dark. I told you not to mention it, and you mention it anyway.

Hearing this, the people in the group next door turned their eyes on Luo Zhigang, their looks seeming to say: look at how their guide does it, then look at you. Shouldn't you make some gesture too?

Luo Zhigang hadn't expected to be put on the spot like this. His face flushed crimson at once, while his mind raced to tally up what buying these few pendants would cost. By the workmanship they wouldn't be cheap, and he wasn't about to part with that kind of money.

It was all Meng Wan's fault—playing the big spender and dragging him into it, leaving him caught in a bind.

Just as he wavered in his dilemma, Meng Wan spoke up. "Put them all on my tab—I'll settle for theirs too."

There were only about five or six over there who'd bought pendants; this much she could bear. Besides, she and Luo Zhigang were colleagues. They'd be seeing each other all the time, so there was no need to leave him in an awkward spot.

Hearing how generous Meng Wan was, Luo Zhigang quietly let out a breath and gave her a big thumbs-up. Not bad at all, this little miss—quite the stand-up sort.

Seeing someone foot the bill, the tour group next door happily received their gifts too, and amid the general warmth they all thanked Meng Wan.

That little rabbit pendant really was adorable. Before paying, Meng Wan made a point of asking for a few extra to take home for Zhizhi to wear—she was sure to love them.

She also made a point of asking the manager about the craftsman who made the rabbits. He told her it was an old artisan past fifty, who borrowed the shop's storefront to display his carvings on consignment; the shop took a cut of the commission. They didn't even know his address—he always came in punctually on Sunday mornings to deliver new stock.

Meng Wan made a note of the time, meaning to come try her luck then. A craftsman this fine couldn't be left to languish in obscurity.

The manager also told Meng Wan that the kickback would be tallied up at the weekend and given to her all at once—she should remember to come collect it then.

——

The group boarded the bus back to the hotel. The return train was first thing the next morning, so tonight was their last in Yuyang.

Apart from that greasy middle-aged man being a bit of a nuisance, Meng Wan felt the trip had ended on a fairly satisfying note.

The greatest gain was getting to know the Xia and Guan sisters. They'd even exchanged addresses with Meng Wan, with a promise that she'd come visit their hometowns when she had the chance.

They'd also said the Yuyang market was huge, and that if the opportunity arose she could come open a shop there. Meng Wan said she'd welcome it warmly—they could grow the business big together when the time came.

Back at the hotel, Meng Wan said her goodbyes to everyone and watched the tourists head off to their rooms. Li Jianshu waited patiently for them all to disperse so he could speak to Meng Wan alone.

"Have a seat, Tour Guide Meng." Li Jianshu glanced at her and went straight to sit on the sofa.

Meng Wan eyed the envelope clutched in his hand and dragged her heavy steps over to sit across from him. Her two guardians flanked her left and right, little Meng Wan wedged in the middle like the filling in a sandwich cookie.

"This is the tour fee." Li Jianshu set a white envelope on the table.

Looking at the thick envelope, Meng Wan couldn't keep the corners of her sullen mouth from lifting a little. "Thank you, Boss Li. I appreciate your trust."

"You're being far too formal, Tour Guide Meng." Li Jianshu leaned back into the sofa and crossed his legs. "We're well acquainted now—just call me Brother Dashu."

"Bro—"

Meng Wan honestly couldn't get it out. He was nearly as old as Meng Qingyan—Brother Dashu? Grandpa Dashu was more like it.

Li Jianshu smiled. "It's all right. You'll manage to say it eventually."

Eventually? Wasn't he heading back tomorrow?

As if he could read Meng Wan's thoughts, Li Jianshu cleared up her confusion for her.

"I'm not leaving this time." Li Jianshu tapped his fingers lightly against his knee, studying her expression. "I'm planning to open a new shop here in Yuyang—and to start a new family too."

As he said that last part, he gave Meng Wan a long, meaningful look. Slow as she could be, even Meng Wan understood his meaning now.

She sat bolt upright with a jolt. He wanted her to be his second wife?

A divorced man with a child, with one foot in the grave—where did he get the confidence?

Meng Wan kept up the pretense of not understanding and brushed him off with a glib reply. "Then I hope Boss Li rakes in the gold and soon weds the woman of his heart."

Li Jianshu narrowed his eyes, his mouth quirking to one side. "Soon or not isn't up to me—it depends on whether the lady's willing."

He was angling at her with that!

"Aiya!" Meng Wan really had no wish to keep tangling with him. She grabbed at the first excuse she could find. "I've got something to take care of at home, so I'll be off. Thank you for the tour fee, Boss Li."

With that she tucked the white envelope into her bag and pushed Lin Dong to hurry on ahead. She couldn't stand to stay there another second—any longer and it felt like Li Jianshu's naked gaze would strip her down and swallow her whole.

She'd barely reached the door when a deep, resonant voice came from behind her. "We'll meet again, little Wan."

Ugh!

Meng Wan's whole scalp prickled, her back stiffened, and in her distraction she missed her footing—caught her toe on the doorframe and pitched forward. Luckily Lin Dong and Xiaohai were quick on the draw; each grabbed an arm and held her suspended steadily in midair.

What a nightmare. In a thoroughly foul mood, Meng Wan boarded the bus.

It dawned on Lin Dong belatedly. "Sister Wan, is that old geezer interested in you?"

Xiaohai gave a blank nod too—it was exactly what he'd wanted to ask.

"Probably?" Meng Wan didn't quite know how to answer; after all, the man hadn't come right out and said it.

"He's old enough to be your dad and he's got designs on you? What an old lecher—just watch me sort him out!" Lin Dong rolled up his sleeves and made to get off the bus.

Xiaohai immediately answered the call. "I'm coming too!"

A wretched old man daring to covet his master—what wishful thinking.

"Stop right there!" Meng Wan called them back. "He didn't actually do anything to me—what could you even do to him? You're grown men, act with a little composure. Get over here and sit down!"

Meng Wan gave the two of them a commanding look, watched them sit down reluctantly off to the side, then took the envelope out of her bag. Making no secret of it, she counted the money right in front of them.

By the price she'd quoted earlier, it came to five thousand yuan total. Minus the two thousand handed over in advance last time, the balance was three thousand.

Meng Wan took out her little account book. Lodging, transport, meals, and admission fees came to four thousand two hundred all told, leaving her a clean profit of eight hundred—not bad.

She drew out three hundred and gave it to Lin Dong. The price for chartering his bus was a bit above the market rate. Lin Dong tried to refuse. "No, this is too much."

He'd asked around among the bus drivers before; they made only thirty or forty yuan a day. Three hundred was enough for ten days, and he'd only worked five.

"Take it when you're told to take it—no more of your fussing." Meng Wan pressed it into his hand. "Little Xi's exams are coming up. Buy some extra meat and feed her up properly."

Lin Dong gripped the money in his hand, knowing this was Meng Wan looking out for him on purpose. He owed her far too much.

"Xiaohai." Meng Wan peeled off a hundred. "This is yours."

Xiaohai had been running himself ragged at her side these past days, and he'd put in real effort. Even though she'd said earlier she wouldn't pay him wages, that depended on the circumstances; do good work and there ought to be a reward, or it would be hard to keep anyone motivated.

"Master, I can't take this money." Xiaohai waved his hands. "I haven't even paid you any tuition—how could I turn around and take your money?"

"This is what you've earned." Meng Wan stuffed the money into his hand. "Go buy yourself some good food and build yourself back up too. A few days following me around and you've already slimmed down a size. You need to eat more, get yourself big and strong—there'll be plenty of hard days for you ahead."

Once the money was divvied up, Meng Wan gave each of them one of the little rabbit jade pendants. "Wear it—for good luck."

*

Nearly eight o'clock, Meng Wan came home clutching her money, head high and chest out.

The Meng family was sitting in a tidy row on the sofa watching television as usual. At the sound of the door they all turned to look at once.

"What kept you out so late?" Cheng Xuehua couldn't help frowning. The child had been coming home later and later lately—out all day, who knew doing what, always so mysterious about it.

Meng Wan shut the door and toed off her shoes, fobbing them off. "Qiuyun's been in low spirits lately, so I kept her company on a walk."

Sorry, Qiuyun. Using you as my shield.

Hearing it was about Xu Qiuyun, Cheng Xuehua pressed no further. That child had her own sad lot in life.

Meng Wan scanned the living room and saw no sign of Zhizhi. "Sister-in-law, where's Zhizhi?"

Sun Jing, knitting a sweater in her hands, jerked her chin toward the bedroom. "She tore around the courtyard with Feifei from next door all day. She got sleepy early tonight."

Meng Wan fished the little rabbit jade pendant from her pocket and handed it to Sun Jing. "Sister-in-law, give this to Zhizhi to wear when she wakes up tomorrow—she's bound to love it."

Sun Jing set down her needle, took the pendant, and looked it over, unable to help praising it. "Oh my, this pendant is lovely. Where did you buy it?"

"A jade shop in the north of the city. If you like it, sister-in-law, I'll take you to browse there sometime."

Meng Xingyang spoke up tactfully. "Tell me the address—I'll take your sister-in-law."

"Ooh, ooh, ooh." Meng Wan teased, "My brother really is one thoughtful, devoted husband."

Listening to her sister-in-law's ribbing, Sun Jing lowered her head a little shyly. It had been so long since she and her husband had gone out together; a faint flutter of anticipation rose in her heart.

Cheng Xuehua chimed in at the right moment. "Watching your brother and sister-in-law so loving makes you envious, doesn't it? Dress up nicely tomorrow and come with me to meet young Song and his mother."

Cheng Xuehua had already settled on Song Qingyang in her mind, and seeing that Song Qingyang's mother seemed to have the same idea, she was all the more eager to push things along. Once her daughter's marriage was decided, half the great weight on her heart could finally come down.

Meng Wan had nearly forgotten about this business. She pleaded, "Do I really have to go?"

Cheng Xuehua gave her a hard look and said in a tone that brooked no argument, "This isn't up for discussion!"

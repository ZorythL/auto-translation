Chapter 17

The whole meal, Song Qingyang couldn't taste a thing. His thoughts churned and churned and churned again—a hundred twists, a thousand turns, and then some. All the goodwill toward Meng Wan he'd quietly built up before now scattered to nothing in the span of those few exchanges.

He'd never imagined that Meng Wan, a young lady of such good breeding, would slander someone groundlessly for the sake of money. He was deeply disappointed.

Meng Wan could tell, too, that Song Qingyang's mood had shifted. All of that she could accept—but the flicker of contempt that rose in his eyes made her deeply uncomfortable.

Taking a man's money to do his bidding—she didn't feel she'd done anything improper. Everything she'd said was grounded in fact. Giving the land to James was a win-win-win-win outcome: for her, for James, for Song Qingyang, and for the local people.

Of course, the "facts" she was basing it on hadn't happened yet, so from Song Qingyang's perspective there was no way to verify the truth of her words. She knew she shouldn't have leaned on her memory of future events to negotiate with him in such an absolutely certain tone.

But still she couldn't accept that look of contempt. Deep down, Song Qingyang simply didn't trust her.

Meng Wan brought the result of her negotiation back to the hotel where James was staying. James gladly agreed to her proposal—he was willing to hire a batch of local young people as hotel staff, and would even set up a training program and a promotion track.

Meng Wan told James to prepare a contract, and she insisted the section dealing with the young people's employment arrangements be written in fine, thorough detail.

For instance, under what circumstances an employee could be dismissed, the onboarding training program, the promotion mechanism—all these concrete plans had to be spelled out on paper.

James understood Meng Wan's intent: she meant to use this section as their advantage in ultimately winning the land-development rights.

That very afternoon, James contacted his lawyer in Yuyang and commissioned him to draft a contract according to her wishes.

Meng Wan went over the contract carefully from start to finish. It encompassed nearly every detail she could think of—more than enough to expose the gaps in Xu Tian's offer.

At the contract submission the next day, Meng Wan did not appear. If Xu Tian learned it was she who'd helped James win the land, he'd surely assume she'd pulled strings through her connection to Song Qingyang and would never recognize that the fault was his own.

Besides, she had some sort of connection to all three of these men, which made such a gathering rather thorny.

Meng Wan waited anxiously in the hotel lobby for news—and then a familiar face walked in.

It was Wan Qing, the woman she'd met at the Yuxiang Restaurant that day. Today she wore a yellow plaid skirt paired with a diamond-pattern sweater, white leather shoes with thumb-width heels, a fluffy waist-length perm, a pair of sunglasses, and a classic black double-C leather bag slung over her shoulder. Meng Wan recognized it at a glance as a Chanel—she'd owned one herself once.

At this point in time, luxury handbags hadn't yet made their way into the Yuyang market. A bag like this had either come from Hong Kong or been brought back from abroad.

Meng Wan couldn't help but speculate: could James have given it to her?

The next second she cut off her own runaway imagination. Even if James had given it to her, what did that have to do with her?

Wan Qing had no doubt come to see James. The front desk told her James wasn't in his room at the moment and asked her to wait in the lobby lounge.

Meng Wan sank a little deeper into the sofa and turned her head aside, not wanting to make eye contact. For some reason, even though the two of them had only crossed paths briefly last time, the other woman's gaze hadn't been friendly. Maybe she was overthinking it—or maybe Wan Qing had taken her for a potential rival in love.

Wan Qing's heels clicked across the floor as she walked to the other end of the sofa. With slender fingers she removed her sunglasses and shot a careless glance at Meng Wan, who sat turned away at the far side. She seemed vaguely familiar, but Wan Qing couldn't quite place where she'd seen her before.

The two of them sat in silence. After a long while, just as Meng Wan's patience was about to run out, she finally heard movement.

"James, you're back!" Wan Qing called out gladly.

Sitting with her back to the door, Meng Wan twitched her ears at the sound, listening to their exchange of pleasantries.

"Wan Qing?" A flicker of disappointment crossed James's eyes, and he glanced around in search of Meng Wan. "What brings you here?"

He'd thought he'd be the first to see Meng Wan—he could hardly wait to share the good news with her.

Hearing his words, Wan Qing's smile instantly froze. "My grandfather heard you were back and specially asked me to invite you over for a meal at our home."

James declined politely. "I'm sorry, I have other matters to see to today. In a few days, once I've cleared the things on my plate, I'll definitely call on you in person."

A trace of disappointment flickered through Wan Qing's eyes. Then she added, "By the way—I've found out who can help with that land."

"I've already secured the land." James held up the signed contract in his hand as he spoke.

Meng Wan's pricked-up ears finally caught the good news, and she let out a soft breath.

"You secured it?" Wan Qing was astonished. Just yesterday there'd been no sign of progress, and today it was so easily done? It made no sense. Song Qingyang's leanings had clearly favored Xu Tian—how could James, a foreigner, possibly change Song Qingyang's mind in the space of a single night?

James didn't bother explaining to Wan Qing. His eyes were still scanning the room for Meng Wan, and after one full sweep they finally settled on a back turned toward him on the sofa. He walked straight over to confirm. "Meng Wan?"

Hearing the footsteps approach, Meng Wan promptly faked a yawn, as if she'd just woken, and put on a look of surprise at the sight of James. "James, when did you get back? I waited and waited and fell asleep."

James could finally share his joy with her, though he expressed it with composure, a faint note of satisfaction in his voice. "Take a look—your handiwork."

He handed the signed contract to Meng Wan.

Even though she already knew the result, Meng Wan's heart still thumped uncontrollably. With trembling fingers she flipped straight to the last page, where the bright red official seal leapt into view.

"It's done?"

"It's done!"

Meng Wan clenched both fists in a little gesture of celebration, her whole body sagging with relief.

"Um..." Amid her delight, Meng Wan hadn't forgotten her chief purpose in coming today. "My payment—could I have it today?"

James looked at Meng Wan with a doting smile and drew a white envelope from the inner pocket of his jacket. "I had it ready for you long ago."

Meng Wan took the thick envelope, a little surprised. "When did you prepare this?"

"This morning."

Before going to the land administration bureau, James had made a special trip to the bank. He knew Meng Wan would get this money without fail.

"So you'd already decided I could pull it off." He'd believed in her—and that struck Meng Wan right in the heart.

"Of course."

From the very first day he'd met Meng Wan, he had entrusted his trust to her.

Meng Wan grinned foolishly at those two simple words. She straightened her back, clapped James on the shoulder, and adopted the tone of a seasoned old-timer. "Right. Then you keep up the good work, and don't let me down."

Wan Qing gripped the strap of her bag tightly, listening to their conversation with disbelief written all over her face. This had actually been Meng Wan's doing? How could a lowly little tour guide have such ability?

The question hung over her, and the look she turned on Meng Wan grew colder still.

*

After heading back that day, Meng Wan didn't see James again. She had no idea whether he'd ended up visiting Wan Qing's home afterward, or how far things had progressed between them. But anyone with eyes could see it—Wan Qing liked James.

That was only natural. A rare sort of man like James was bound to have plenty of women showing him favor.

That day Xu Qiuyun had asked Meng Wan whether she had feelings for James, and Meng Wan answered honestly that she did—but only that far, no further. She knew very clearly that her foremost goal right now was to create the conditions she needed.

James was a businessman, and what businessmen valued most was profit. Everything between them so far had been built on the basis of fair exchange: Meng Wan had value to him.

So Meng Wan had to keep creating that value, to remain useful to him; and by the same token, she needed to make use of him to achieve her own ends. A relationship of mutual exploitation wasn't quite suited to anything more. And besides, the moment her true identity came to light, the resources she possessed would collapse and her usefulness would vanish. Would James still want anything to do with her then?

On Saturday, Meng Wan went to her interview, and as it happened Xu Qiuyun took her accounting exam that same day. Two weeks later, both of them received good news.

Though Xu Qiuyun still didn't know what use the certificate would be for now, one more skill was always a good thing.

The day she received her tour-guide certificate, Meng Wan bought some nourishing tonics and brought Xu Qiuyun along to the hospital to visit Auntie Hao. Lin Xi was there taking care of her.

The doctor said she'd improved a great deal since the surgery and would be able to go home to recuperate in a few more days. In just over a month, looking at Auntie Hao on the hospital bed with her cheeks slightly fuller, Meng Wan could plainly see she'd put on some weight and that her spirits had picked up considerably. To see her face clearly, Auntie Hao pulled Meng Wan close and talked with her for a long while.

Before leaving, Meng Wan called Lin Xi outside and asked what subject she wanted to study and whether there was a particular university she hoped to attend.

Without a moment's hesitation, Lin Xi said she wanted to study medicine—she wanted to become an outstanding doctor, just like Ms. Cheng Xuehua.

Meng Wan wanted to tell her that studying medicine was grueling and that she'd better be mentally prepared—but on second thought, hardship was probably the very thing Lin Xi feared least. As long as the opportunity was there, enduring hardship would likely be the easiest thing in the world for her.

Meng Wan gave her another sum of money, telling her to buy some nourishing food to build herself up before the college entrance exam, or to buy study materials for one last sprint.

Lin Xi truly felt too embarrassed to take Meng Wan's money again—but she genuinely needed it, so without any fuss she accepted it outright, silently resolving in her heart that once she earned money of her own, she would repay Meng Wan a hundredfold, a thousandfold.

Learning that Auntie Hao was about to be discharged, Meng Wan went to the billing window and settled her entire hospital bill in one payment. Because Cheng Xuehua had applied on Auntie Hao's behalf for the urban low-income medical-hardship subsidy, more than half the cost of the medicine was covered, easing Lin Dong's burden considerably.

With all that done, Meng Wan and Xu Qiuyun left the hospital together.

Now that she had her tour-guide certificate, Meng Wan's next step was to open a travel agency of her own and start taking on clients and leading tours.

Once she began guiding work, she'd no longer have time to clock in at the cultural center, so it was time to resign.

When Xu Qiuyun learned she meant to quit, she urged her to think it over carefully—a stable, respectable job wasn't easy to come by, and once she let this one go, the chance might not come again.

But Meng Wan had made up her mind to leave. The truth was, stability itself was the greatest shackle of all.

Early Monday morning, Meng Wan splurged on two bottles of Wuliangye and brought them to Director Wu's office.

The gift landed squarely on Director Wu's soft spot—the man loved his liquor more than life itself, and at the sight of such fine wine his eyes lit up.

Seizing on his good mood, Meng Wan immediately stated her purpose for coming. Surprised, Director Wu lifted his gaze from the bottles to Meng Wan's face.

"You want to resign?"

Director Wu was thoroughly taken aback. Just a few days ago he'd run into Meng Qingyan and praised Meng Wan's work to his face—after all, he hardly assigned her any tasks. Tidying up books, filling in lending records: jobs anyone with a pair of hands could manage.

She'd been at the cultural center only a few days and already wanted to leave. Could something at work have rubbed her the wrong way?

Meng Wan spun a little white lie, saying she still wanted to take the college entrance exam, so she intended to study in secret without her family knowing and surprise them later. She hoped Director Wu would keep her secret too—and if she hadn't still needed something from him, she'd never have parted with two such expensive bottles of liquor.

Hearing this, Director Wu relaxed. Thank goodness it wasn't his doing—otherwise he wouldn't have known how to account for it to his son. Relatives-by-connection really were a thorny business.

He'd been about to suggest Meng Wan could prepare for the exam while keeping her job, but then he remembered her poor grades. Splitting her focus between the two was probably asking too much of her.

In the end he merely offered her a few words of an elder's advice and wished her into a good university.

Meng Wan thanked her superior, then returned to the library to say goodbye to her colleagues. She didn't have much—a cup, some tea, a coat, a cushion.

She handed it all to Xu Qiuyun, telling her to dispose of it as she saw fit. Xu Qiuyun walked her out to the front gate, watching her go with deep reluctance.

Meng Wan told her not to worry, and to use this idle stretch to brush up on her bookkeeping—there'd be plenty for her to do down the road.

Xu Qiuyun still had no idea what Meng Wan was planning. She only nodded blankly—whatever Meng Wan said, she'd go along with.

Bathed in the morning sun that spilled across her face, Meng Wan finally set out on her brand-new road.

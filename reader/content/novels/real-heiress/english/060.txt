Chapter 60

The greetings and introductions over, it was time for Meng Wan, as manager, to say a few words. She gave a light cough and said, "Everyone should be here now, and I've finished the introductions. Next I'll keep it brief."

Luo Zhigang glanced around. "Lan Xiuli isn't here yet."

Meng Wan paused, then offered an excuse. "She's got some family matters today, so she asked me for leave."

Lan Xiuli had worried she wouldn't be able to control her emotions if she saw Meng Xingyang, so she'd taken the initiative to ask Meng Wan for the day off. Meng Wan had agreed without hesitation. Sun Jing had a sharp, subtle mind—she might well read something off their faces, and then all of Meng Wan's careful scheming would go to waste.

She cleared her throat and went on. "Today is the day our Youth Travel Agency reopens. By rights, since the agency has changed hands, I ought to give it a new name. But I think the name our agency already has is wonderful. Youth Travel Agency—it speaks to the vigor and life that fill our agency, to our guides forever young and striving upward, and to Yuyang's tourism industry flourishing day by day like youth itself. So I've kept the name. In a little while my family will be bringing over an inscribed plaque, and together we'll witness it hung up. From this day forward, we're all passengers aboard this great ship called Youth Travel Agency. When storms come, we'll weather them together; when beautiful sights appear, we'll take them in together. I believe that as long as we twist ourselves into a single rope, both our agency and we ourselves will have a bright future ahead!"

"Hear, hear!" Luo Zhigang was the first to respond loudly, clapping with every ounce of strength he had in a clattering burst.

Not to be outdone, the others followed the rhythm with hearty applause. Everyone was buoyed by Meng Wan's words, as if they could already see the sun rising over a golden, gleaming sky, beckoning them onward, the beautiful future right before their eyes.

The applause went on for a long while, and Meng Wan smiled from the heart as she looked out at them all. This was her team, her troop, her comrades-in-arms.

James clapped along too, gratified. Meng Wan was even better suited to leading than he'd imagined. He believed every promise she'd made today would come true. The earnest are people ablaze, and wherever they go, the fire of life burns on, inexhaustible.

"It's here, it's here—the plaque's here!" Old Bi, by the window, shouted out in excitement.

Everyone turned to look out the window at once, and there were Meng Xingyang and Meng Qingyan lifting a plaque draped in red cloth down from the back seat of a taxi.

Meng Wan hurried out to greet them. "Dad, Brother, Mom, Sister-in-law, and Zhizhi too—welcome, all of you!"

Cheng Xuehua peered into the room, her face full of smiles, and saw a cluster of unfamiliar people crowded at the doorway, every one of them beaming with joy and looking warmly their way. Among them was one familiar face—the foreigner she'd seen before at the Western restaurant.

Xiaohai and Lin Dong immediately bounded over, three steps at a time, and took the plaque from Meng Xingyang and Meng Qingyan's hands.

Brimming with delight, Meng Wan ushered everyone inside and introduced them all around. Meng Qingyan, deeply moved, looked over the agency and, out of long habit, launched into a little speech. "I'm very glad to meet you all. As Meng Wan's father, I'm delighted to see my daughter has a career of her own. None of this could have come about without your help and support. If there's anything she doesn't do well, I hope you'll all bear with her and give her some time to improve, little by little. On her behalf, I thank you all for your support."

With that, Meng Qingyan bowed to the group. That single gesture was enough to convey his respect, and everyone present was a little at a loss. They all knew Meng Wan's father was a distinguished, highly respected university professor, an intellectual—and now this intellectual was actually bowing to them for his daughter's sake. Who could see it and not feel a warmth rise in their chest?

Not only had the agency's people not anticipated that bow—neither had Meng Wan. That the ever-proud Meng Qingyan would actually bend his back for her sake moved her so much, for a moment, that she forgot the order of proceedings.

At the crucial moment it was Cheng Xuehua who held the room together. With easy poise she said, "All right. We've come today to celebrate the agency's reopening and its new journey ahead. The auspicious hour is nearly upon us—let's go hang the plaque first."

His nose stinging, Meng Xingyang chimed in, "Hang the plaque, hang the plaque."

In Sun Jing's arms, little Zhizhi clapped her hands excitedly and piped up in her soft, milky voice, "Watch the plaque, watch the plaque."

Meng Wan blinked, forcing the tears back down with effort, and lightly tweaked the tip of Zhizhi's nose, smiling tenderly. "Yes, let's watch the plaque go up."

With that, everyone filed out the door and lined up in two rows. Xiaohai and Lin Dong climbed the ladder Old Bi had prepared ahead of time, inching their way up rung by rung.

Watching the ladder wobble a little precariously, Xu Qiuyun steadied it while urging them to be careful.

Once the two of them had the plaque hung, they each held a corner of the red cloth with one hand and waited for Meng Wan's word.

Meng Wan clenched her fists, her heart racing, and tipped her head back to gaze up at the red-draped plaque in excitement. As the second hand swept up to twelve—ten o'clock sharp—she announced loudly, right on time, "I hereby declare Youth Travel Agency officially open!"

The words had scarcely left her mouth when the red cloth was whisked away with a sharp swoosh, and at the same instant Luo Zhigang lit a string of firecrackers at the mouth of the alley.

Applause, cheers, and the crackle of firecrackers all burst forth together. At that moment, gazing up at the five gleaming golden characters on the plaque, Meng Wan's heart welled over with a thousand feelings, and two streams of clear tears rolled down her cheeks. She really and truly had an agency of her own!

James stepped in as photographer, taking a big group photo of Meng Wan and all the agency's members in front of the plaque, then another of the whole Meng family.

The photos taken, Meng Qingyan asked with mild puzzlement, "And this gentleman is...?"

At Meng Wan's agency's current standing, surely things hadn't reached the point of specially hiring a foreign photographer—and besides, when Meng Wan had introduced the agency's people to him just now, she'd made no mention of this foreign friend.

Meng Wan slipped her arm through James's with a smile and introduced him in a gentle voice, "Mom, Dad, Brother, Sister-in-law—his name is James. He's my boyfriend. We only got together a little while ago, so I hadn't had the chance to tell you yet."

"Boyfriend?" Meng Xingyang said in astonishment. "You've got yourself a foreign boyfriend?"

She didn't make a sound, but when she did, she made a splash—just like his little sister.

Sun Jing had seen from the moment she walked in that there was something between these two. The way this James looked at his little sister-in-law, honey all but dripped from his eyes—if it wasn't unrequited pining, then they were dating.

Cheng Xuehua, by contrast, was quite calm. She'd met James already, and with a daughter as outstanding as hers, surrounded by so many suitors, it was no surprise she'd ended up with James.

Meng Qingyan, for his part, first froze for a moment, then took a while to digest it. What should have been a happy occasion, yet the corners of his mouth refused to lift. For the first time it struck him that his daughter had reached the age where she could leave home and start a family of her own, and for a moment he couldn't accept it.

Wholly oblivious to his future father-in-law's inner turmoil, James was immersed in the joy of finally having his title made official. With a smile he introduced himself. "Uncle, Auntie, Brother, Sister-in-law, hello. My name is James, and I'm Xiaowan's boyfriend. Today's meeting was rather sudden and I didn't prepare any gifts. Next time I'll be sure to call on your home properly."

Cheng Xuehua was quite pleased with this match for her daughter, and she beamed so wide she could hardly close her mouth. "What gifts—just you coming is enough. Whenever you'd like to visit, have Meng Wan let us know, and Auntie will cook you something delicious."

"Thank you, Auntie." Hearing himself accepted, James's heart lifted with joy.

It was only then that Cheng Xuehua noticed something off about Meng Qingyan. He was usually the most particular about etiquette, yet here was his daughter's boyfriend right in front of him and he hadn't said a word—which made no sense at all.

She nudged Meng Qingyan with her elbow, a smiling signal for him to say something.

Meng Qingyan came to as well, realizing he'd been a touch rude, and squeezed out a smile. "Welcome to visit our home."

As brief as brief could be. Even Meng Xingyang, no great reader of faces, noticed his father's odd behavior.

"Thank you, Uncle." James kept his smile and replied politely.

Meng Wan thought she understood how Meng Qingyan felt. In her past life, when she married, he'd worn the same crushed, soul-lost look—worse, even, because he hadn't liked Xu Tian. His own daughter marrying a man he disliked had dealt him quite a blow.

After they'd seen Meng Wan's family off, even the ever-confident James rarely showed a flicker of self-doubt. He leaned close to Meng Wan's ear and asked quietly, "Your father—does he not like me?"

Meng Wan dispelled his doubt. "It's not your identity he dislikes. It's that he still can't accept that I'm already a grown-up, that I've reached the age where I could leave home at any time."

James's appearance had given Meng Qingyan a sense of crisis.

James understood then too. This was a road every parent had to walk. If one day he had a child of his own, he might not be able to bear letting her leave home either.

"Oh, right." James suddenly remembered something. "I've already moved out of the hotel and into my new house. I wonder if I might have the honor of inviting Miss Meng Wan over to warm the home?"

Madam Ye had told him this was a Yuyang custom—when moving into a new home, you held a simple housewarming.

"Of course!" Meng Wan said, delighted. "Having a home of your own is one of life's great milestones. I'll throw you a proper celebration, and show off a couple of my skills while I'm at it—light the stove and warm your hearth for you."

The words had barely left her when Xu Qiuyun passed by the two of them and remarked airily, "Having a home really is one of life's great milestones. Let me take a couples' photo for you two—you can hang it in the new place later."

James gave Xu Qiuyun a thumbs-up, thoroughly approving of her suggestion. He gave her a quick lesson on using the camera, then drew Meng Wan over to stand in front of the plaque.

It was the first time Meng Wan had taken a photo alone with James, and she didn't quite know how to react or what to do, so she simply stood there ramrod-straight without moving. Xu Qiuyun couldn't bear to watch and called over to them, "What are you doing—get closer, and smile."

James tilted his head to glance at Meng Wan, reached out to drape an arm around her shoulder, and tucked her head against the side of his neck. He leaned his own head over to follow, his lips moving as he murmured softly in her ear, "Meng Wan, I love you."

That much Meng Wan understood. Hearing this sudden confession, her heart pounded, and a happy smile couldn't help but bloom at the corners of her mouth.

"Perfect."

As Xu Qiuyun spoke, she swiftly pressed the shutter with a click, catching in a snapshot a sweetly smiling, perfectly matched pair of lovers.

The two people in that photo wore the most innocent of smiles, as if to embody the beauty of pure love in that era.

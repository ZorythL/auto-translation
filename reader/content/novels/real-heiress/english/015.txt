Chapter 15

The exam fell on a Saturday. Lin Dong handled the driving, and Xu Qiuyun stayed by her side the whole time.

This was the first exam Meng Wan had ever seriously prepared for. Although she was seventy or eighty percent confident in her review of the material, she couldn't help feeling very nervous—proving the old saying that the more you care about something, the more you fear botching it.

The moment Meng Wan walked into the examination room, she found it was uniformly male—she was the only woman taking the test. Under a chorus of curious stares, she made her way to her seat.

Her seat was in the very back row. As soon as she sat down, she automatically tuned out the irrelevant gazes and closed her eyes, running through the knowledge points to ease her nerves. She didn't open them again until the proctor came in to hand out the papers.

One second her legs were still trembling nonstop, and the next, Song Qingyang's face barged into her vision without warning. Beside him stood another woman who looked rather older, middle-aged.

Meng Wan momentarily forgot all about the exam and cocked her head at him, her face full of confusion.

Wasn't this the tour-guide certification exam? Why was someone from the land administration bureau here proctoring? Did his job really reach this far?

Song Qingyang, holding the exam papers, was equally taken aback. He stood frozen at the lectern, locking eyes with Meng Wan for several seconds, dearly wanting to ask her on the spot why she was there. But he had a task to do, so he could only swallow his boundless curiosity, tear open the bag of papers, and prepare to distribute them.

Seeing the person in front pass back the papers, Meng Wan reeled in her thoughts and stopped wondering, focusing entirely on the exam. That was what truly mattered today.

She began answering. The questions were divided into single-choice, multiple-choice, true-or-false, and short-answer. The single-choice ones went smoothly for Meng Wan, but the multiple-choice gave her trouble—some options felt ambiguous, could be right or could be wrong, so she just selected them all.

The exam lasted three hours, and Meng Wan wrote right up to the last second. Not until the proctor announced, "Time's up," did she set down her pen.

In the interval before the papers were collected, Meng Wan let out a long breath at the densely filled exam sheet. Rubbing her hand, she marveled with satisfaction at her own masterpiece. She was confident she could pass this written test.

The one who came to collect her paper was Song Qingyang. Thoughtfully, Meng Wan picked up her sheet and handed it to him. Taking the paper, Song Qingyang murmured, "Wait for me outside."

Meng Wan had known he'd want to get to the bottom of it. She didn't plan to hide it from him anyway—she only hoped he could keep her secret for a while.

She walked out of the examination room full of worry. The moment Xu Qiuyun saw her, she came trotting over to meet her.

"What's wrong?" Xu Qiuyun asked carefully. "Was the exam very hard?"

Meng Wan shook her head, pushing the scenarios she'd been dreading out of her mind, and exhaled deeply. "It's nothing. Let's go sit by the flower bed for a bit—someone wants to talk to me."

"Who?"

Meng Wan told Xu Qiuyun about running into Song Qingyang. Chatting as they went, the two of them sat down by the flower bed, and Meng Wan slipped one of the two books she'd brought under each of their seats.

Xu Qiuyun immediately read the meaning behind Meng Wan's earlier expression. "You're afraid he'll tell your father, aren't you?"

"That's right." Meng Wan stretched out her legs and rested her hands on her thighs. "But sooner or later it has to come out. The exam's over anyway—there's nothing he can do to me now."

"Sorry I'm late."

Their conversation was cut short as Song Qingyang trotted up and stopped in front of them.

Meng Wan and Xu Qiuyun stood, and Meng Wan introduced the two of them.

Then she asked, "How is it you're here?"

Song Qingyang explained, "The friend who was supposed to proctor today—it's his girlfriend's birthday, so he asked me to fill in for him."

"You're quite the good Samaritan," Meng Wan muttered under her breath.

Song Qingyang gave a slight smile. "For friends, I really am very willing to help."

"Then could you be willing to help me a little too?" The moment the words left her mouth, Meng Wan regretted them—it sounded as if she were confessing feelings.

"That's not what I—"

"How can I help you?"

The two of them spoke almost at the same time.

Since he'd asked, Meng Wan made her request without ceremony. "About me taking the tour-guide exam... could you not tell my father?"

"Of course."

This was Meng Wan's private business. He wasn't some tattletale schoolkid who ran to report every little thing to the teacher.

That easily? Meng Wan silently gave him a thumbs-up in her heart.

"But," Song Qingyang said, his eyes meaningful, "if it were my own personal curiosity, would you be willing to tell me?"

He couldn't understand why Meng Wan, with such a respectable job at the cultural center, would still want to take the tour-guide exam. Among everyone who'd just sat the test, she was the only woman.

Meng Wan twisted her fingers behind her back, having anticipated his question. "You want to ask why I'm taking the tour-guide exam, don't you?"

Song Qingyang gave a slight nod.

Meng Wan stated her thinking without holding anything back. "I want to make money—lots and lots of money. And I've realized I can make money this way, so I took the exam. It's that simple."

Song Qingyang found Meng Wan's bluntness a little hard to accept. It completely upended his image of what a child raised in a scholarly, cultured household should be like.

Afraid he'd keep pressing, Meng Wan changed the subject. "I want to ask you a question too, if it's all right to answer."

"Go ahead."

"That plot of land you mentioned at our house before—has a buyer been settled on yet?"

In her past life, around this time, Meng Wan had helped Xu Tian persuade Song Qingyang to sell him the land. Without her help this time, Xu Tian would surely have found other channels—but she didn't want him to get that land. If only she had money of her own.

Not having expected this to be what she'd ask, and since it was hardly a secret, Song Qingyang answered truthfully. "It's narrowed down to two buyers now, still in final negotiations, to see who can offer the better terms."

One of them was surely Xu Tian. She just didn't know who the other was. Whoever it was, she'd back them—as long as it wasn't Xu Tian.

Meng Wan dropped a subtle hint. "Then you'd better keep your eyes sharp. Some people who look shrewd are very good at exploiting loopholes in a contract. They'll turn around and burn the bridge once they've crossed it, leaving you to swallow your grievances in silence."

In her past life, following Meng Wan's suggestion, Xu Tian had proposed to Song Qingyang that he'd help solve the employment problems of the village's young people, and had even put it into the contract. But in the end he dismissed them on various pretexts—pure exploitation of a contract loophole.

Song Qingyang didn't know why Meng Wan was saying this. Seeing how earnest she looked, as if she were describing something that had actually happened, and—more absurdly—Xu Tian's face floated unbidden into his mind. Could it be these two knew each other?

"Do you... know something?"

"No, no, no." Meng Wan hastily waved her hands. "I read about a case like this in the newspaper. I was just afraid you might get cheated too, so I'm giving you a friendly heads-up."

Song Qingyang knit his brow slightly and nodded, lost in thought.

"Beep, beep, beep."

A car horn sounded. Meng Wan and Xu Qiuyun both turned to look—Lin Dong was waving at them from the car window.

Meng Wan gathered up her books and bid Song Qingyang farewell with a smile. "We'll be off, then. See you next time."

Song Qingyang watched the two of them leave with a smile. What he really wanted to ask Meng Wan was: when would next time be?

*

The written results came out quickly. Meng Wan's score met the passing line set uniformly by the tourism bureau—she'd cleared the written exam and advanced to the interview.

On the day the results were announced, Meng Wan happened to receive that month's wages. She invited both Lin Dong and Xu Qiuyun out to the Yuxiang Restaurant for a proper celebration, and to reward herself for all those grueling weeks.

The moment they walked in, the familiar male waiter came to greet them, smiling. "The foreign friend who came with you last time is upstairs right now. Are you here to see him?"

Hearing that James was here too, Meng Wan froze for a moment, her heart suddenly pounding.

He was back? Why hadn't he told her? Could he have already forgotten her? And what had he come back for? Had he not had a good time last time, and come to find a different guide for another tour?

Meng Wan's mind churned with wild guesses, her feet rooted to the spot.

Seeing her zone out, the waiter gently called again, "Miss Meng?"

Lin Dong had been quietly watching Meng Wan's expression the whole time, and a flicker of disappointment crossed his eyes.

"Hm?" Meng Wan's eyes flew wide as she snapped back. "No, never mind. We're not with him—just the usual. Take us to a private room."

The private room was on the right-hand side of the second floor. Last time, she and James had sat on the left, by the window.

With expectant steps, Meng Wan climbed the wooden staircase one rung at a time, unable to keep her head from turning left. Sure enough, James was still sitting in the spot the two of them had shared last time—except he was in the seat Meng Wan had occupied, so she could only see his back. Across from him sat a beautiful, elegant woman. The two of them were chatting away happily, their smiles practically spilling onto the floor.

A wave of sourness welled up in Meng Wan's chest, an emotion she couldn't quite name flooding through her. She pulled her gaze away, turned, and walked briskly toward the private room.

Partway through the meal, Meng Wan got up to use the restroom. On her way back downstairs, her eyes drifted of their own accord toward the window seat—and found the people were no longer there. A little deflated, she continued down, her heart a jumble of feelings. Just as she stepped onto the last stair, she saw the two of them saying goodbye outside the door.

As if guided by some unseen hand, she slipped behind the door and started eavesdropping.

"I'll get someone to ask around about the land for you. When you have time, do come by the house," the woman said.

"Thank you. Next time I get the chance, I'll definitely pay you a visit."

A visit, no less. Meng Wan pursed her lips. If you didn't know better, you'd think he was a son-in-law calling on the family.

Meng Wan was so absorbed behind the door that she failed to notice the surprise attack coming from across the way.

"Miss Meng, why have you come down?" the waiter called loudly toward the door Meng Wan was hiding behind. "Did you need something?"

The shout startled Meng Wan. She made frantic faces and gestures at him, signaling for him to keep it down, but the waiter didn't catch on at first and, thinking she felt unwell, came forward to ask, "Miss Meng, are you all right?"

Meng Wan made desperate shushing motions and was about to bolt when a familiar voice suddenly reached her ears.

"Meng Wan?" James said, delighted. "We meet again!"

Chapter 61

Xue Guangming's apology and clarification ran in that week's issue of the Yuyang Radio and Television Gazette, just as promised, restoring the good name of their Youth Travel Agency. As a result, the city leadership decided to convene another meeting to officially vindicate the agency.

The travel-agency heads who showed up this time were largely the same as before, and Meng Wan's seat was the same too—that inconspicuous spot at the very bottom of the table.

"Well, well." Qian Zhenyu glanced over in Meng Wan's direction, his voice dripping with sarcasm. "If it isn't the head of the famous Youth Travel Agency, the one that made the papers. I hear her family's got some real connections—she can even pull strings at the newspaper, and she's got the leaders wrapped around her little finger. What a talent. Looks like it won't be long before she works her way up from the bottom seat to mine."

There it was—the very thing Meng Wan had worried about had come to pass. That was the nature of rumors: they spread with terrifying speed and people believed them without question, yet when the truth came out, it only invited endless doubt. At the end of the day, people were always more willing to believe the worst.

There was surely no shortage of people in the room thinking exactly that, and with Qian Zhenyu stirring the pot, even those who hadn't believed it would start to harbor suspicions.

At a moment like this, defending herself was the worst possible move, because these people didn't actually care whether the rumor was true. As long as you were strong enough, someone would always rise to argue your case for you.

Meng Wan didn't get angry. Her expression perfectly composed, she replied, "Justice lives in people's hearts. I can't sway what people think. But Manager Qian's last words were right—me taking your seat is only a matter of time. I just hope that when the day comes for you to clear out for me, you'll be smiling as happily as you are today."

Meng Wan curved only one corner of her lips, but to Qian Zhenyu it was a smile loaded with provocation. He was the head of the second-largest travel agency in all of Yuyang—even the city leaders showed him a measure of deference. Who did she think she was, this Meng Wan, daring to challenge him to his face?

"Fine! When you take this seat of mine, I'll hire an opera troupe to sing outside your agency's door for three days and three nights, so all of Yuyang knows our city has produced a once-in-a-century prodigy!"

Qian Zhenyu worked himself up as he spoke, spit flying from his mouth. The man sitting diagonally across from him wiped his face with his forearm, a look of disgust on his face.

Meng Wan ignored the sarcasm packed into his words and kept her smile. "All right then—I'll let Manager Qian splurge."

"That's enough." Bai Yuexi, seated at the head of the table, spoke up just then. "The leaders will be here any minute. What does it look like, the two of you bickering like this? Whatever you have to say, say it in private."

By virtue of running the largest travel agency in Yuyang, Bai Yuexi held a certain standing among these agency heads. His position was something like the president of a chamber of commerce, and people did tend to listen to what he said.

Qian Zhenyu forced down his anger, the fury blazing in his eyes as if it could burn Meng Wan to a crisp.

The silence didn't last long before the leaders pushed open the door and entered, kicking off the day's proceedings.

At the meeting, Deputy Mayor Wang rescinded the penalty against Youth Travel Agency and brought everyone a piece of good news on top of it.

The city had decided to allocate funds to increase the number of public toilets and to put up bilingual Chinese-English signage, all to vigorously attract foreign tourists.

After that, the proposals Meng Wan had laid out in her report would also be implemented one by one, pending further study.

To everyone present this was rousing, welcome news—more tourists meant good things for them all.

From the leaders' remarks, anyone with sense could immediately draw one conclusion: foreign-language tour guides would be a hot commodity in the future.

At the meeting, Deputy Mayor Wang also singled out Meng Wan for praise, saying she was meticulous in her work and had a grasp of the bigger picture. He called on everyone to learn from her and to always put the development of Yuyang first.

As the other agency heads listened to Deputy Mayor Wang heap praise on Meng Wan, they all turned their gazes her way—some congratulatory, some contemptuous, some perfectly indifferent.

This time Meng Wan had truly made her name in a single stroke. Not only had she made a strong impression on the leaders, she'd also become the focal point of every other agency's attention. From now on, her agency probably wouldn't get a moment's peace.

After the meeting ended, James appeared outside the gates of the Tourism Bureau, just as promised—he'd come to take Meng Wan to the housewarming at his new home.

The moment Meng Wan stepped out, she saw him in a black tracksuit, leaning casually against the car door. His superb proportions made him stand out all the more from a distance, his legs stretched impossibly long by his shadow.

By contrast, Meng Wan turned to look at the cluster of middle-aged men around her—some fat, some balding, some unkempt. Truly, each had his own distinctive flaw.

Among them, a female comrade seated not far from Meng Wan spotted James across the street at a glance and gasped, stars in her eyes. "Oh my, this foreign young man is downright handsome. Yuyang's got someone like him and I've never laid eyes on him before?"

A man beside her said sourly, "That's you toadying up to the West. A foreigner looks good and suddenly he's 'someone'? You're just a sucker for a pretty face."

"A sucker for a pretty face, am I? I bet you wish you looked like that yourself. You've got a head like a gourd and you're going to police where I look? I'll look at whoever I please—what's it to you?"

"You—!" The man, rendered speechless by her tongue-lashing, snorted and strode off.

Meng Wan struggled to hold back a smile. James surely had no idea that simply standing there, he'd set off such a quarrel.

"I say, Big Sister Qin, don't just talk about it—go on over and introduce yourself. Don't you have a daughter? If he turns out to be single, there's your son-in-law right there." Qian Zhenyu, true to his nature of never minding when a scene got bigger, egged Qin Lan on for the fun of it.

To everyone's surprise, Qin Lan actually gave his words serious thought. "You're right. I'll go over and introduce myself right now."

With that she marched off toward James, bold as brass, before Meng Wan could even call out to stop her—she was already at the gate.

When the others saw that Qin Lan was actually rash enough to go pitch herself as a matchmaker right out on the street, language barrier and all, they followed along too, ready to enjoy the show at her expense.

From across the street, no one could make out the two of them talking; they could only judge their reactions from their expressions.

Meng Wan hesitated in the crowd, unsure whether to go over and explain, when she saw Qin Lan turn to look at her—James must have been explaining.

Before long, Qin Lan came back beaming, not a trace of annoyance on her face, lavishing praise on Meng Wan. "You've done well, young lady, landing yourself such an outstanding boyfriend. If the two of you ever break up, be sure to give me a heads-up—I've got two daughters of my own."

Meng Wan had no idea what sweet talk James had fed Qin Lan to make her praise her like this. She could only smile and say, "I hope your daughters find their special someones soon."

There was no way she was ever breaking up!

The rest of the crowd, who'd come hoping for a spectacle, didn't get their show but got their fill of gossip instead: Meng Wan actually had such a handsome foreign boyfriend, and she ran her own travel agency on top of it. This young woman was really something—juggling love and career and doing both well.

Meng Wan said goodbye to Qin Lan and, under everyone's watchful eyes, got into James's car.

Once the car had slowly pulled away, Meng Wan buckled her seatbelt and asked curiously, "What did you just say to her? She looked so pleased."

James turned the steering wheel, a mysterious smile tugging at the corner of his mouth. "Just paid her a few simple compliments. I didn't expect her to be so delighted."

Qin Lan had asked whether he was married and said she had two daughters she'd like to introduce to him. James had replied that he'd assumed she was asking on her own behalf, since she looked to be about his age.

That had thrilled Qin Lan to no end. Then James said he already had a girlfriend—Meng Wan—and though Qin Lan was a little disappointed, getting such a sincere compliment from James, she figured she hadn't lost out.

Meng Wan thought it over. James really did have a gift for flattery, and on top of that he was good-looking, with a face that always looked utterly earnest when he spoke—so no matter how outrageous the compliment, he could make people genuinely believe it.

Meng Wan and James first made a trip to the market and bought some vegetables to cook the first meal at his new home. Lighting the stove in a home for the first time was an event with real significance.

James's new place was located in the South City district at the center of Yuyang. This area had once been a maze of old alleyways before it was demolished and rebuilt into commercial housing.

James's building was among the first batch of these commercial units to be handed over, and the moment they went on sale they sold out instantly. The real-estate market could only be described as booming and flourishing—all the hot money came tumbling and scrambling in, waiting to burn itself up and kindle an even fiercer blaze.

At the door, James handed Meng Wan the key and let her open it herself. With a gentle turn of the key, a bright, simply furnished, spacious living room came into view.

Delighted, Meng Wan began wandering through the place, while James carried the vegetables into the kitchen and set them down.

"Your place is huge!" Meng Wan spun in circles in the living room, thrilled. "You're so lucky, having such a big home all to yourself."

James leaned against the doorframe with his arms crossed, smiling. "For now it's just me. There'll be more people later on."

The truth was, while she'd been wandering around just now, Meng Wan had already been picturing herself living here—especially that big bed; she could practically roll around on it. But she'd be too embarrassed to say so.

Feeling as though her thoughts had been read, Meng Wan played dumb and changed the subject. "I'm going to start cooking. Come give me a hand."

"Whatever Chef Meng says."

The kitchen wasn't very big, and the two of them standing in it already blocked the passage. James reached over and took down the apron hanging on the wall, bending slightly to tie it on her—just as Meng Wan turned to grab the cutting board. And so, in a muddle, their lips met.

It had only been the lightest brush at first, but James deepened the kiss. The cramped space filled with rolling heat. Meng Wan felt as if her heart would burst from her chest; a wave of warmth swept between their lips, and the touch of his tongue left her mind blank, her whole body weak and tingling. If James hadn't had his arms around her, she might well have crumpled to the floor.

The kiss went on for a long time—from the kitchen to the living room to the bedroom, a crisp, neatly made bedsheet crumpling beyond recognition as the two of them tumbled across it. The kiss, too, evolved: from James leading, to Meng Wan tentatively responding, until at last it became a give-and-take, the two of them perfectly in sync.

She didn't know how much time had passed. Meng Wan felt utterly drained of strength, yet the threshold of her excitement still burned, hungry for more—until James was the first to pull back.

His forehead was beaded with sweat, his collar soaked through, his prominent Adam's apple bobbing up and down as he gulped in heavy breaths. In a hoarse voice he said, "You lie down. I'm going to take a cold shower."

If he kept kissing her, he was afraid he'd lose all his self-control.

Meng Wan pressed a hand to her pounding chest, staring up at the snow-white ceiling, and couldn't help but wonder aloud: "Wasn't I just in the kitchen getting ready to cook? How did I end up in bed?"

Chapter 42

Zhang Yuqing approved of Meng Wan's report and took her to see his own superior. A matter this big he couldn't decide on his own; he had to report to the leadership first.

He even stood by helping Meng Wan explain to the leader: if Meng Wan could truly develop the agency according to the plan, then the value it would yield down the line would far exceed a hundred thousand yuan.

If their western district really did become a pioneer in the city's tourism development, their subdistrict office would deserve no small share of the credit. It would surely draw the leadership's attention, and might even bring a tilt of resources their way—several bold, vivid strokes in their ledger of achievements.

But for now, all of this was still talk on paper; there had to be at least something tangible to show. So under Meng Wan's bargaining, the leadership still insisted on a transfer fee. That way, even if Meng Wan ultimately produced no results, the money would prove his decision sound—he'd weighed both the transfer fee and the agency's future, and when the higher-ups asked, he'd have an answer to give.

In the end the transfer fee was set at thirty thousand yuan. As long as Meng Wan came to the subdistrict office a week later with the plan and thirty thousand to sign the contract, the agency would from then on be theirs to run privately.

Thirty thousand was no small sum either, but compared with a hundred thousand, Meng Wan gritted her teeth and agreed. This was already the best outcome she could have fought for.

Out of the administrative building, Meng Wan and Liu Fei tallied things up: between the two of them, never mind thirty thousand, they couldn't even produce ten.

Luo Zhigang had a whole family to support and was basically broke by month's end, while old Bi was already on the verge of retirement—she couldn't trouble the old fellow further.

Meng Wan told Liu Fei to head back first; she'd go think of something herself.

Heavy with worry, she returned to the Lin household. Just as she reached the mouth of the alley, she came upon a familiar figure pacing back and forth outside the Lins' door.

"Ma?"

Cheng Xuehua's body stiffened at the sound. She turned, and her eyes met Meng Wan's delighted gaze.

"What are you doing here?" Meng Wan came closer and asked teasingly, "Did you miss me?"

So many days had passed that Meng Wan had long since put the unpleasantness behind her. Since Cheng Xuehua had come looking for her today, it meant her mother was willing to offer her a way to climb down gracefully. She couldn't make an elder lose face by forcing her into words of contrition; as the younger one, she had to spare her elder's dignity, so she needed to take the initiative.

Cheng Xuehua had assumed her daughter was still angry, and had been turning over how to humble herself and coax the girl home. She hadn't expected Meng Wan to act as if nothing had happened at all, as if they'd never quarreled, and to be so happy to see her—which lifted a good deal of weight off her chest.

"You little brat." Cheng Xuehua shot her daughter a half-petulant look and seized the offered way down. "You knew perfectly well I missed you, and still you wouldn't come home to see me. If I hadn't come looking for you today, how much longer were you planning to stay out?"

"I've just been so busy lately." Meng Wan caught Cheng Xuehua's hand and swung it back and forth. "Even if you hadn't come today, I was going home tomorrow anyway."

"Hmph." Cheng Xuehua clearly didn't buy her glib tongue, but both of them understood—this was each granting the other a graceful exit; what had happened before was now turned past.

"I told you to stay at the cultural center and you wouldn't, and now you're off being some tour guide. If you're run ragged, it serves you right." Cheng Xuehua pursed her lips.

The quarrel might be behind them, but where their daughter's career was concerned, the two of them still weren't satisfied.

"Yes, yes, yes." Meng Wan laughed and draped an arm over her shoulder. "It's all my fault for not heeding my elders' words—now I'm paying the price right before my eyes."

"Who are you calling old?" Cheng Xuehua slapped the hand resting on her shoulder, then asked, "What price are you paying? Has someone been bullying you?"

A thought struck Meng Wan, and she nodded with great solemnity.

Cheng Xuehua flared up at once and demanded, "Who? Who dares bully my daughter?"

Putting on a wronged little pout, Meng Wan said, "Money's been bullying me."

------

When it came to her children, Cheng Xuehua was the most generous of all—especially toward this youngest daughter. The very day Meng Wan brought up the money, she went straight home, fetched her savings passbook, and withdrew thirty thousand yuan to bail her daughter out.

Meng Wan didn't take it for nothing, either; she promised Cheng Xuehua that once the agency turned a profit, she'd pay back every cent with interest.

Cheng Xuehua had no real expectation of being repaid. As long as she could help Meng Wan out of her immediate bind and spare her daughter further worry, she was content.

That same day, Meng Wan gathered her things from the Lin household and moved back to the Meng home.

The family was living together once more. Meng Qingyan's temper had nearly cooled; she'd been fretting over her daughter for some time but had held back from going to find her out of pride. Seeing her daughter home safe, she didn't want to dredge up the old unpleasantness either—the child was grown; let her do as she pleased.

Meng Wan also took the chance to mention the inspection groups to the family, hoping that if anyone's work unit had the resources, they might help make an introduction. Meng Xingyang said his unit seemed to have a batch of inspection groups coming in from another province, and that he'd ask around for her when he went in to work on Monday.

The household was as warm and harmonious as ever, but Meng Wan came back this time with a changed state of mind. She had already brought Zhou Nian and her birth parents here; their reunion was now only a matter of time.

She hadn't yet decided when to arrange that reunion, so the matter hung over her head like an axe, keeping her constantly on edge and breeding a faint unease in this home.

After dinner, Meng Wan drew Sun Jing and Zhizhi downstairs for a walk, meaning to ask Sun Jing to look into the matter of Zhou Nian's schooling.

Zhou Nian was twenty-two this year. She'd dropped out in the second year of high school, and hadn't touched a book in five or six years; whatever she'd learned had likely all leaked away. Forget the first-year high school material—she'd probably long since handed her middle-school knowledge back to her teachers.

But Meng Wan didn't tell Sun Jing the truth. She said only that Zhou Nian had been studying hard on her own all this while, and that it was her family who hadn't wanted her to continue, forcing her to drop out—otherwise, with too poor a foundation, no school would be willing to take her.

Meng Wan had given Zhou Nian the cover identity of a country cousin of Lin Dong's. Sun Jing had heard the name before when she'd gone to Xu Qiuyun's, so she wouldn't suspect anything. Meng Wan planned, once Lin Xi had finished the college entrance exam, to have her help Zhou Nian cram and make up the ground she'd lost.

This was a matter Sun Jing could indeed look into for her sister-in-law, but if the person's household registration wasn't in Yuyang, she could only enroll as a guest student—come the college entrance exam, she'd still have to go back to her registered hometown to sit it.

Meng Wan knew this. She'd thought about transferring the registration too, but it was a complicated business; better to first nail down the matter of schooling. If Zhou Nian wanted to transfer later, the Meng family knew plenty of people, so it would be easier to arrange then.

With the money problem solved, the great stone weighing on Meng Wan's heart had come to rest. On Zhou Nian's end, her sister-in-law would help arrange things, and from the way Sun Jing spoke, it shouldn't pose much difficulty.

Meng Wan placed a call to old Bi. The money was taken care of, she told him; have everyone gather on Monday to go to the subdistrict office together and sign the contract.

The one thing still hanging over her was taking Qin Yi'e and Zhou Da to the hospital for their checkups.

To keep Cheng Xuehua from finding out, Meng Wan took the two of them to a different hospital, where she arranged a comprehensive physical examination for them both.

Zhou Nian looked after Zhou Da, and Meng Wan after Qin Yi'e. The two of them ran up and down all day and finally completed every item on the list; the doctor said to come back for the results in a week.

When it was done, Meng Wan took the group to the department store and bought each of them a few sets of new clothes, then treated them to a welcome meal at Yuxiang Tower.

So far, everything was unfolding according to her plan, and that sense of control gave her a measure of comfort—she no longer felt quite so much at sea.

Early Monday morning she arrived at the agency with thirty thousand yuan and the proposal, and all four of them presented themselves at the subdistrict office, neat and complete.

Because the money and the plan had both come from Meng Wan, naturally it was her name that went down where the transferee's name was to be signed—which meant that from here on, in this travel agency, it was she who called the shots.

Luo Zhigang harbored some discontent. Meng Wan was a newcomer to the agency, young on top of that, and a woman besides; from now on she'd be his superior, and he'd answer to her command—a hard thing for a grown man to swallow.

But hard to swallow or not, with no money to put up and no plan to offer, all he could do was bide his time and stay put for the moment, wronged or not.

Liu Fei's thinking was the exact opposite of Luo Zhigang's. From his observations over the past few days, he reckoned Meng Wan was a capable leader, fit to take over the agency, and he believed that under her guidance the Youth Travel Agency would only grow and grow.

Accountant Bi had even fewer reservations. As long as he got to retire in peace and the agency kept running smoothly, it made no difference to him who held the reins.

With a flourish of bold strokes, Meng Wan signed her name and pressed down firmly with the seal bearing the words "Youth Travel Agency." Looking at the contract in her hands, she could scarcely believe it.

This agency was hers now?

She finally had a travel agency of her own?

She was the boss!

A new official lights three fires, and Meng Wan set hers blazing.

The first fire: Meng Wan announced to everyone her plans for the agency's future. Luo Zhigang would handle the reception of inspection and internship groups connected to government units and schools; Liu Fei would handle tourist groups, with Xiaohai placed under Liu Fei's wing.

Since she would henceforth be devoting more of her energy to management, she'd be taking on fewer groups herself—and the agency was badly short of guides right now, so they had to train up a few good ones quickly.

The second fire: she had Accountant Bi compile all the agency's tourist resources, along with a full itemized list of every kickback arrangement with hotels, shops, restaurants, car rentals, and the like; the agency's current profit-and-loss situation was to be drawn up into a report as well.

It was no small workload. Meng Wan planned to bring Xu Qiuyun over to organize it together with Accountant Bi, and to have her learn the ropes from him along the way—so that before old Bi retired, she'd be thoroughly familiar with the agency's operations and could take over seamlessly when the time came.

The third fire was to redecorate the agency. Their office might be small, but they'd be hiring people in the future, and looking as shabby and run-down as it did, no one in their right mind would want to stay.

This was called sweeping out the old to welcome the new—a fresh face, and a fresh start!

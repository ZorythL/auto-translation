Chapter 71

Meng Wan arranged a time for Qian Zhenyu and James to meet for the first time.

Compared with having his agency bought out by Bai Yuexi, Qian Zhenyu naturally leaned toward working with James. After all, James was a foreigner with no tangled web of personal connections in Yuyang and no interest in pulling rank by seniority. As for his girlfriend, Meng Wan—she was just a small fry, someone Qian Zhenyu never gave a second thought.

There was no telling what James said to him, but Qian Zhenyu, resistant at first, had changed his tune by the time he walked out the door. He said he'd give James's proposal serious thought and let him know in three days.

Meng Wan asked, brimming with curiosity, "What on earth did you say to him?"

James answered with perfect composure. "I offered him terms he couldn't refuse, of course."

"What terms?"

"You'll find out when the time comes." James put on a mysterious air.

Sure enough, on the third day Qian Zhenyu came to the hotel of his own accord to find James, and the two finally settled on the acquisition. James had his lawyer draft a contract first; if Qian Zhenyu had no objections, it could be signed on the spot.

It was, after all, the agency he'd spent all these years in, and selling it off all at once left Qian Zhenyu reluctant. He clutched the contract and gazed at it with teary eyes for a long while before finally signing his name with a flourish.

No sooner had James finished signing the contract with Qian Zhenyu than he signed an equity transfer agreement with Meng Wan, the two of them redistributing the shares according to their capital contribution and her expertise stake.

Meng Wan's Youth Travel was likewise merged into Chaoyang Travel—though the name was still kept as Youth Travel.

With that, Manager Meng vaulted up to become the head of Yuyang's second-largest travel agency. The leap was a big one indeed, leaving her with a feeling of stepping on cotton—light, floaty, and not quite solid underfoot.

But James had great faith in her abilities, giving her not only broad authority but plenty of encouragement.

It was only after signing the contract that Meng Wan learned the terms James had given Qian Zhenyu: a job as manager at the hotel.

Qian Zhenyu had gone straight from travel-agency manager to the hotel trade. The leap wasn't huge, but there was a difference all the same.

Meng Wan asked James why he'd hired him, and James gave her three reasons.

First, Qian Zhenyu had his own knack for managing subordinates, which suited the service industry.

Second, he was well versed in Yuyang's social niceties and had plenty of acquaintances in the tourism trade; for the banquets and drinking sessions, he'd be indispensable.

Third, he'd spent a lifetime in the travel-agency business and had never made it to number one. It was time to switch tracks, and James had promised him his hotel could become Yuyang's best.

Taking those three points together, Qian Zhenyu really was a suitable choice. Sometimes when you can't climb any higher in one position and always feel something's missing, it isn't that you aren't good enough—it may simply be that the job doesn't suit you. At a time like that, switching tracks just might let you soar, riding on all the experience you've accumulated over the years.

Meng Wan agreed wholeheartedly with James's view, but she still harbored a grudge against Qian Zhenyu, and learning he'd be working at the hotel gave her a flash of displeasure.

James had foreseen that Meng Wan would feel let down, so he had a word with Qian Zhenyu, hoping the man would offer her a sincere apology. Qian Zhenyu, a man who could bend as well as stand tall, said straight out that on the day the new agency opened, he'd do exactly as he'd said in the conference room—hire a gong-and-drum troupe to come banging and clanging to her door in congratulation, and he guaranteed it would satisfy Meng Wan.

Meng Wan was a little surprised that Qian Zhenyu would set aside his pride for the sake of his career. It seemed he genuinely liked his new job. And since he was willing to swallow his face and sue for peace, she supposed she ought to show a bit of grace in return.

A few days after the contract was signed, word of the buyout of Qian Zhenyu's agency got around—but somewhere along the way, someone had embellished it with no small amount of spice, and Meng Wan heard several versions in a single morning.

Some said Meng Wan couldn't stand Qian Zhenyu and had James buy out his agency.

Some said Qian Zhenyu had latched onto a better patron and sold his agency off at a rock-bottom price.

And some said James had long had his eye on the prize of Yuyang's tourism industry, and that getting together with Meng Wan was just a way to get up to speed on the local scene quickly.

Some of them even sounded plausible to Meng Wan herself.

Everyone at Youth Travel gathered together, chattering away about what they'd each heard.

Jiang Ke said excitedly, "Manager Meng, does this mean we get to move to a bigger office?"

"Do you all want to?" Meng Wan pulled over a chair and sat down. "If you don't want to, we can stay here—worst case, I'll just run back and forth between the two."

"We want to, of course we want to." Jiang Ke jumped in to answer. "Our office here is old and cramped. A big agency's office has got to be nicer than this."

Yu Fei disagreed. "Not necessarily. Guides all work outdoors—when things get busy you don't set foot in the office for a whole month. Their office probably isn't much better than ours."

After the two youngsters, Liu Fei spoke up. "Let's all go over together. Manager Meng's busy enough as it is—running back and forth between two places would be too hard on her."

Xu Qiuyun agreed too. "That's right, let's all go over together. It'll make it easier for Manager Meng to work, too."

"All right." Meng Wan clapped her hands decisively. "Then we'll pack up the whole household and move!"

In truth there wasn't much to move—the most important things were the account books and the cash. Early the next morning the group took everything they'd packed, called in Lin Dong's coach bus, and hauled it all over together.

Just as Yu Fei had predicted, because the office had stood empty year-round, Chaoyang Travel's premises really were shabby. The desks and chairs were each missing an arm or a leg, with gap-toothed chunks chipped out here and there, and even the banner hanging on the wall sagged crooked, yellowed with age.

Yu Fei, clutching her stack of materials and her little flags, pulled a smug down-turned face at Jiang Ke. See? She'd said it might not even be as nice as their own agency.

Jiang Ke, for his part, had never imagined that the second-largest agency in the whole city would be so... hard to put into words.

When Qian Zhenyu's underling, Deputy Manager Li Yang, saw Meng Wan's group arrive, he was first taken aback, then quickly recovered and gave the young new manager a warm welcome.

Qian Zhenyu had upped and left, dumping this whole mess on him, and Li Yang had long been praying for someone to come quickly so he could extract himself from it. So when he saw Meng Wan, his smile was especially friendly.

"Manager Meng, why didn't you let us know you were coming? We should have prepared a welcome for you." As he spoke, Li Yang spotted a crumpled ball of wastepaper out of the corner of his eye, and without moving his upper body, swiftly toe-kicked it off to one side.

Meng Wan pretended she'd seen nothing and said with a smile, "We're all one family here—no need for those empty formalities."

Ever since the summer break ended, the agency had been in complete disarray, everyone single-mindedly chasing tourists. There wasn't even anyone to clean the office; with people coming and going all day, no one paid the slightest attention to the shared space.

Chaoyang Travel had twenty guides in all—fifteen Chinese-speaking guides for domestic tourists and five foreign-language guides. A few days before he left, Qian Zhenyu had told all the guides to finish up the jobs in hand and then stand by for the new boss to assign them new tasks.

So today half the agency's guides hadn't gone out and were gathered together discussing their new manager, Meng Wan. They were right in the middle of debating which of the rumors were true when the door opened and the woman herself walked in. Every one of them went mute on the spot, not daring to make a sound.

Meng Wan greeted everyone one by one, then brought her own people forward to make a brief round of introductions. She'd met quite a few of these people before—they'd crossed paths at scenic spots and restaurants over the years, and several of them had recently been poaching tourists right out from under Luo Zhigang and the others.

"Everyone go on back to work—I won't take up your time. I just need a few words with Manager Li."

When Meng Wan finished, everyone looked to Li Yang, waiting for him to give the order. Li Yang put on a baffled expression. "What are you all looking at me for? Manager Meng's told you—go and get on with your own work."

Only when he'd said so did the crowd disperse. Li Yang watched Meng Wan's expression carefully and hastened to explain, "Manager Meng, don't take offense—they just haven't gotten used to your new position yet."

Meng Wan knew he was afraid she might be holding a grudge, so she came right out and reassured him. "Don't worry, I'm not so petty a person."

Only then did Li Yang relax, and he led Meng Wan into his office.

"Please, have a seat." Li Yang lifted the thermos, found it empty. "We're out of water—I'll have someone bring you some right away."

"No need to bother. I'll only be a moment."

Li Yang had no choice but to set the thermos down and sit across from Meng Wan, his hands resting somewhat awkwardly on his knees.

Meng Wan could see his nervousness, and reassured him with a smile. "Don't be tense, Manager Li. I'm not here to call anyone to account—I just want to get a sense of how the agency stands at the moment."

"Whatever you want to know, I'll tell you everything, hold nothing back," Li Yang pledged his loyalty at once.

That wretch Qian Zhenyu had sold the agency off without a word and gone straight over to a new boss. Word was this Manager Meng was on bad terms with Qian Zhenyu, and if she happened to be the small-minded sort, she might fire him—one of Qian Zhenyu's old people—the moment she took office, and there'd go his livelihood. Where would he find a suitable job again on such short notice? So he had no choice but to bow and scrape to Meng Wan, praying she'd show mercy and not transfer her anger at Qian Zhenyu onto him.

"Is there any money left in our accounts?"

Li Yang's eyes went wide; for a moment he didn't know how to answer. Leading off with such a sensitive question?

"There's still a bit, I think." Li Yang spoke vaguely. "Things have been a complete mess these past few days—I haven't had a chance to look at the books."

Meng Wan nodded. "Tally up how much there is, and put it all toward new desks and chairs and office supplies. Anything that needs replacing, replace it. If it's not enough, draw from Xu Qiuyun, the accountant I brought with me. Assign all the guides to it together. Within one week, I want to see this agency with a brand-new look."

It was James's money she was spending anyway, so Meng Wan didn't feel the least bit of pain over it. Sprucing up the premises counted as a form of doing business; it didn't count as throwing money away.

"Huh?" Li Yang sat there stunned. "You're not going to assign everyone their work first?"

The agency was running this deep in the red and she was in the mood to redecorate?

Meng Wan smiled slyly. "This is the first assignment I'm giving everyone."

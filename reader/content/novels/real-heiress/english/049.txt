Chapter 49

One of the very few moments in Meng Wan's life when she wished a crack would open in the floor so she could crawl into it was happening right now.

Frowning in genuine confusion, James asked, "Happy with whom?"

Meng Wan decided she might as well break the pot since it was already cracked. "I saw the whole thing. The night of your birthday, you went back to the hotel with a woman—the two of you were laughing and chatting the whole way."

James stared at her in astonishment for a long moment, the smile at the corner of his mouth growing more and more pronounced. Laughing as he spoke, he said, "So that's why you never came to find me afterward?"

Her little secret laid bare, a blush rose to Meng Wan's cheeks, and she nodded obediently.

James grinned so wide he couldn't close his mouth. "Madam Ye would be very happy to hear you say that."

"Madam Ye?"

Was it the fashion among foreigners to refer to one's own fiancée that way?

"Madam Ye is my mother. Ye Xinzhu." James cleared up the misunderstanding for her.

"That was your mother?" Meng Wan's face was a picture of shock, her jaw practically hitting the floor. "So I misunderstood—I thought you and your mother—"

Her mind went completely blank, a roaring filled her head, and wave after wave of heat washed over her face.

James's mother was far too young. No one who looked at that face would ever guess she was his mother.

Not wanting Meng Wan to feel any more embarrassed, James put away his smile and said earnestly, "Miss Meng Wan, allow me to formally set the record straight. On my birthday, because my mother, Madam Ye Xinzhu, had arrived, I couldn't keep waiting for you. What you saw at the hotel that night was her coming to visit me—I'd gone down from my room to meet her."

Afraid she might still be turning things over in her mind, he went on. "And this morning, I was coming back from the hotel where she's staying. We had dinner together yesterday, it got very late, so I stayed the night there. As for the new house—since I'll be living in Yuyang for the long term from now on, I can't very well stay in a hotel forever."

James laid the whole matter out in a single breath. "Is there anything else you'd like to know? I'll explain all of it to you right now."

Meng Wan's nose stung, and a rush of heat welled up behind her eyes. Acting on impulse, she asked, "That day at the hotel—you still never gave me a clear answer."

A smile played at James's lips, and he gazed at her with eyes full of tenderness for a long while before he spoke slowly. "Miss Meng Wan, would you be willing to be my girlfriend? We come from different countries, with different backgrounds and different pasts—but I believe it's precisely these differences that have brought us together. We'll learn from each other, grow alongside each other, and face every unknown risk the future holds, together. And of course, we'll share the happiness that fills this long life, too—you and I."

Meng Wan had come today fully intending to make a clean break with James. She'd never imagined the scene would refuse to follow the script she'd written for it—that instead she'd receive the most sincere confession of her life.

Her heartbeat was like a drumbeat with no rhythm at all, charging recklessly around inside her chest. Her temples throbbed in time with it, and even her fingertips burned. She snatched up her coffee cup and took a sip, trying to use the caffeine to calm herself down—but her heart only pounded faster.

"Mr. James." Meng Wan breathed in small, nervous breaths, her toes curling hard against the floor. She paused for a long time before she finally got the words out. "No matter what the future holds, I want to face it together with you."

On the first day of a couple's romance, people usually catch a movie, take a stroll through the park, or share a syrupy-sweet meal together.

But when it came to Meng Wan and James, Director Meng's mind was set firmly on work—on the tables and chairs still piled up on the street outside. So once she'd finished the cake, she said goodbye to her new boyfriend, hopped on her bicycle, and rode back to the travel agency.

Before she left, Meng Wan even asked James to help her set up a time with his lawyer. She wanted him to draw up a proper labor contract for her tour guides, to guard against any labor disputes down the line.

Her travel agency was going to be hiring, and hiring meant signing labor contracts. It wasn't just the new people who'd sign—veteran employees like Liu Fei and Luo Zhigang would all have to as well.

The new Labor Law had been promulgated just this year, so if Meng Wan wanted to grow the business, every step had to comply with the procedures and regulations. Every step had to be done by the book, so that down the road no rival could find any fault to attack her with.

In her brother Meng Qingyan's words, this was called heading off trouble before it started.

James agreed, saying he'd ask the moment he got back.

Watching Meng Wan's retreating figure, he sighed quietly to himself. What was a man to do when his girlfriend had nothing in her heart but her career?

------

When Meng Wan got back to the travel agency, Xu Qiuyun and Old Bi were in the middle of hauling tables and chairs.

"Qiuyun!"

Meng Wan called out to her from a good distance away, waving as she shouted.

Xu Qiuyun turned at the sound, and her already cheerful mood lifted even higher, infected by Meng Wan's smile. She raised her hand and waved back.

"I thought Director Wu wouldn't let you go," Meng Wan said excitedly, pulling her bicycle over to the side and parking it.

After all, of everyone in the reading room, Qiuyun was the most conscientious worker and the most efficient—an outstanding employee.

"He did try to keep me at first, but my mind was made up, and he couldn't force me to stay. Besides, that job's a real plum. The day I leave, someone will fill the vacancy by the next."

That was true enough. According to Qiuyun, the day after Meng Wan had left, Director Wu had slotted his own niece into the position.

Meng Wan happily stuck out her hand. "Thank you, Comrade Xu Qiuyun, for deigning to take a post at my humble little temple. From now on, the financial management of our travel agency is all in your hands."

She'd just learned a new term and was glad of the chance to put it to use.

Xu Qiuyun shook her hand, eyes crinkling with a smile. "Thank you, Director, for giving me this opportunity. I'll work hard and do a good job of this 'financial management' you mentioned."

"You two can swear your loyalty later. This old man's back is about to fall apart," Old Bi bellowed at them.

"Coming, coming."

Meng Wan winked at Xu Qiuyun with a mischievous grin.

The three of them carried the tables and chairs inside, bit by bit. Meng Wan caught her breath and wiped the faint beads of sweat from her temples, staring thoughtfully at the tables.

What James had said today really had set her thinking. She was the head of the travel agency now—the "manager," to use James's word. She wasn't one to put on airs, but she knew that a leader without authority couldn't keep subordinates in line.

So Meng Wan decided to start small and slowly shift everyone's mindset. First of all, she needed an office of her own.

"Qiuyun." Meng Wan had made up her mind. "Help me move this table into the back room. From now on, that's my office."

Xu Qiuyun froze for a moment, then answered, "Right away, Manager Meng."

Business was business and personal was personal. From the day Xu Qiuyun had agreed to come work at the travel agency, she'd drawn a clear line between her relationship with Meng Wan and her job. She would never presume on their friendship to step out of bounds.

Inside the travel agency, Meng Wan was her superior. However she'd treated Director Wu, she would treat Meng Wan exactly the same.

She guessed that this was precisely Meng Wan's reason for setting up a separate office.

Hearing Xu Qiuyun address her that way, Meng Wan couldn't quite adjust to it. As the two of them carried the table, she said, "Qiuyun, when you call me that, I really can't get used to it. Why don't you just call me what you always have?"

Meng Wan did intend for everyone at the agency to change how they addressed her—but Qiuyun was a different case from the rest.

"That won't do." Xu Qiuyun strained a little under the weight of the table. "It's working hours now, and I'm your subordinate just like everyone else, so I should address you the same way they do. I don't want anyone thinking I get special treatment."

Meng Wan jerked her chin toward the empty patch in the middle. "Just set it down here."

The two of them lowered the table together, and Meng Wan panted in small breaths. "You are special, though. I recruited you personally."

Xu Qiuyun went to fetch the chair and set it in place. "Then all the more reason for me to mind my words and actions at all times—I can't go embarrassing our Manager Meng."

While the two of them chatted and laughed, Old Bi finished tidying up the front room. He called toward the back, "Manager Meng, I'm going to take those old tables and chairs from the street back home first. It doesn't look good having them sitting out there all the time."

Old Bi must have overheard the two of them talking. No wonder—he was a seasoned old hand who'd worked all these years; his knack for reading the room was polished to perfection.

Meng Wan and Xu Qiuyun exchanged a glance and smiled. "Got it," they called back loudly. "Off you go."

The two of them poured themselves cups of water and sat in the front room to rest. Meng Wan hesitated for a long while, then decided to tell Xu Qiuyun about her and James getting together.

To her surprise, Xu Qiuyun showed no particular astonishment when she heard. Instead, her expression said, "I knew it."

Meng Wan got worked up. "That's not fair. When I found out you and Lin Dong were together, I was plenty surprised!"

Watching Meng Wan act like a sulking child, Xu Qiuyun couldn't help laughing. "You and James are a perfect match. The very first time I saw the two of you standing side by side, I had a feeling you'd end up together."

"Really?" Meng Wan was instantly mollified. "I was afraid people would think I wasn't good enough for him."

After all, in Meng Wan's heart James was just about a flawless man. To this day she hadn't found a single shortcoming in him.

No—there was one. His handwriting in Chinese characters was pretty mediocre. At least worse than hers.

"You're Meng Wan. If you're not good enough for someone, who is? In my eyes, you're the best there is."

The praise went straight to Meng Wan's head, the corners of her mouth nearly curling up to the sky. "In my eyes, I'm the best there is too."

So much had happened to Meng Wan lately, and since she had a bit of free time now, she poured it all out to Xu Qiuyun in one go.

The trip back to her old hometown, the business at the travel agency, the misunderstanding with James.

The two of them shared their lives with each other, animated and brimming with words. Pouring your heart out to a good friend is a thing that makes a person feel happy.

Just as they were getting into the swing of it, Meng Wan was recounting the embarrassing tale of how she'd misunderstood James—telling it to Xu Qiuyun in vivid, lifelike detail—when a knock at the door interrupted their conversation.

Meng Wan turned to look. The person who'd come was Lan Xiuli.

Only a day had passed—earlier than she'd expected.

Meng Wan introduced Xu Qiuyun and Lan Xiuli to each other. Knowing the two of them had things to discuss, and with Old Bi just back, Xu Qiuyun pulled Accountant Bi aside and began studying with him.

She'd only passed the accounting certification exam; when it came to actually doing the work, she was still a novice. Before Accountant Bi retired, she had to learn everything he knew. Only then could she help Meng Wan make the travel agency bigger and better.

Meng Wan led Lan Xiuli into her office in the back room and pulled the door shut behind them.

"Have a seat." As she spoke, she poured her a cup of water. "I didn't think you'd come to me so soon."

Lan Xiuli sat down across from Meng Wan, her hand resting on the teacup. She stared at the tabletop for a long moment before she spoke. "I saw your sister-in-law today."

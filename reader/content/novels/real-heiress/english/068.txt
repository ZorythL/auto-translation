Chapter 68

On a fine, sunny day, Comrade Bi Guisheng reached the very last day of a working career that had spanned forty long years.

Meng Wan threw a banquet for him at First Cuisine; the whole agency turned out to see old Bi off together. Each of them said a few heartfelt words to old Bi, and everyone's eyes went red—especially Luo Zhigang, a big, strapping man who wept until he was a sodden mess.

Amid the toasting and clinking he'd downed quite a lot of erguotou, and now, snot and tears running together, he clutched old Bi's arm like a child who wouldn't let go.

Old Bi was loath to part from everyone too, but he was the elder after all, and he did his best to comfort them. His home was just a short way from the agency, he said; whenever he had time he'd come back to visit, and if anyone ever needed help they could come find him anytime.

Because it was midday, the restaurant was packed. Meng Wan's group had taken a private room converted out of the upper floor; the place wasn't large and had no restroom of its own, so anyone who needed to relieve themselves had to use the public toilet a short distance across the road.

Meng Wan came downstairs and was crossing the dining hall toward the exit when, in a careless instant, a liquor bottle shattered into bloom at her feet. Yellow liquid instantly soaked her white canvas shoes, slid down her ankle to the seam where her sock met her skin, and the wet, clammy sensation spread across her flesh in a flash.

She stood rooted in place for a few seconds, on the verge of snapping out a few angry words—but remembering that she was the owner of this restaurant, she swallowed her temper.

She had just turned her head to figure out what on earth had happened when she heard a crash, and a wiry, dark-skinned man toppled over at her feet, foaming at the mouth, his calves still twitching.

The short man beside him cried out at the top of his lungs, "Someone, come quick! My big brother ate the food in this place and he's foaming at the mouth!"

Screams and clamoring voices erupted all around, sweeping over Meng Wan's whole body like a sudden downpour. It was the first time she'd run into a situation like this; her feet froze where she stood, and she stood there blankly for a few seconds until Sun Jing came running over in a panic and she came back to herself.

"Xiao Wan, what do we do?" Sun Jing stamped her foot in her anxiety.

Meng Wan glanced at the man on the floor and made her decision instantly. She wheeled around and shouted Lin Dong's name up the stairs, telling him to drive the man to the hospital at once—whatever the cause, saving a life came first.

Then, composed, she gave her instructions. "Sister-in-law, you stay here, mind the restaurant, and keep the customers calm. We still don't know exactly what's going on, and it isn't necessarily our fault. Once I get to the hospital the truth will come out. Whatever you do, stay calm right now!"

Sun Jing nodded dazedly, gripping her own hands tightly as she struggled to pull herself together.

Meng Wan and Lin Dong tried to lift the man into the car together, but to their surprise the man's "family" blocked them, refusing to let them move him. Beads of sweat stood out on his forehead as he glared at Meng Wan ferociously and bellowed, "Don't you touch my brother! He got poisoned eating in your restaurant—you owe us compensation!"

He shoved Meng Wan with one palm, and she lost her footing, staggering back several steps. Luckily Lin Dong had a steady stance; he caught her arm in his solid grip, and only then did she keep from falling.

"Your brother's in this state and all you can think about is compensation? Is he even your real brother?" Meng Wan honestly wanted to pry open his skull and see what was going on inside. "You two aren't putting on an act, are you—coming here on purpose to shake down my restaurant?"

Meng Wan said this only to test him; it really wasn't out of the question. This "younger brother's" reaction was far too strange.

The short man's pupils contracted slightly. He swallowed, his Adam's apple bobbing, and pointed at Meng Wan, cursing her out. "You're slandering us! There's something wrong with your restaurant's food, that's what! I want compensation, I want compensation!"

The commotion here was so loud that the customers upstairs all leaned over to watch the show. Afraid the men might hurt Meng Wan, the whole agency came downstairs and gathered protectively around her.

Seeing the veins bulging at his temples and the desperate, hoarse look of him, Meng Wan worried he might do something rash in the heat of the moment, so she quickly raised her voice to soothe him. "All right, we'll do as you say—we'll pay compensation. But there has to be some proof; we can't just hand over whatever amount you name. Let us take your brother to the hospital first and have a doctor diagnose what's wrong with him. If it's our fault, we'll cover every last expense of yours. How does that sound?"

"No, we're not going to any hospital!" the man still refused. "Who knows what tricks you'd pull there!"

Meng Wan was so exasperated she laughed. "Brother, what tricks could we pull at a hospital? That's government turf."

"No means no. You give me the money, and I'll take my brother to see a doctor myself!"

Seeing there was no reasoning with him, Meng Wan murmured into Lin Dong's ear, "Go quick to the nearby police station and fetch the police. Tell them someone's making trouble and a life's in danger."

Lin Dong caught the message and slipped out of the crowd at once, racing for the station without a moment's pause.

Meng Wan kept up the standoff with the man, stalling for time with smooth, pleasant words. "Then tell me—how much would be fair for me to give you?"

"Two thousand. And I'll take my brother to the doctor myself!"

"Two thousand?" Luo Zhigang shouted. "Why don't you just go rob someone instead!"

What illness cost two thousand to treat? Even counting recovery afterward, two thousand yuan was no small sum.

"My brother got sick eating in your place. If you don't pay up today, I'm not taking him anywhere, and you can forget about running this business!"

His own brother had suddenly collapsed and he showed not a flicker of concern, instead naming a price that sounded worked out well in advance. No matter how you looked at it, something was off.

Meng Wan was already eighty percent certain the two of them were putting on an act to extort money. All she had to do now was keep him steady until the police comrades arrived.

She glanced at the man foaming on the floor and slowed her tone. "Two thousand yuan is fine. But after the scene you've made in my restaurant today, all the customers in here now know there's a man foaming at the mouth in my place. And you won't let me take him to the hospital, so I don't know the cause. How am I supposed to explain that to my customers? How am I supposed to keep doing business? You have to consider my side of things too."

Hearing that his two thousand was as good as settled, the man slowly eased up. "So what do you want to do, then?"

"Here's the thing—I know a bit about first aid. Let me examine your brother and see whether it really is food poisoning. If it isn't, all the customers here can rest easy, and it won't hurt my business going forward."

"You think I'm a fool?" The man threw out an arm to block her. "If it isn't, you still going to pay me?"

"I'll pay—why wouldn't I? If it isn't, all the more reason to pay. I've got plenty of money. Call it saving a life—worth more than building a seven-story pagoda."

The agency people looked at Meng Wan in bewilderment, not knowing what she was selling in that gourd of hers. Why pay either way? Wasn't that idiotic?

Meng Wan had exchanged only a few words with this man, but from the way he acted and spoke she'd sized him up as not particularly clever—otherwise he wouldn't have come up with such a clumsy excuse to swindle money. Since money was what he was after, he'd agree to anything she said.

Sure enough, the man bought it. "Really?"

"Really. My word is as good as gold. Whether it's poisoning or not, I'll give you the money to take your brother to the doctor. The condition is that I have to see for myself first."

The man looked at his brother on the floor, then at Meng Wan, and in the end he was talked over by Meng Wan's ringing, forceful patter. He shuffled aside a step and let her come forward to examine the man.

Meng Wan crouched down beside the man, and a familiar, fresh scent drifted into her nostrils on the air current.

Toothpaste.

If you were going out to swindle people, this prop was a little too crude—truly, in this wide world there was no end of strange things. Meng Wan didn't expose him right away; instead she made a show of examining him, looking for all the world as if she really did understand a thing or two about medicine.

"How is he?" Xu Qiuyun asked anxiously.

"Can't tell." Meng Wan waved her hand breezily. "Can't rule out that it's our restaurant's fault, either."

The short man secretly let out a breath of relief, his expression growing even more smug. "See? I told you there was something wrong with your food. Hurry up and pay!"

With that he stretched out his hand again, eager to get hold of the money.

Meng Wan caught a glimpse out the window of Lin Dong and the police rushing toward them, and said leisurely, "No hurry. How about a cup of tea first? Look at the state you've worked yourself into."

"Tea? My brother's dying and you want to drink tea? Is now the time for tea?" Dim as he was, the man sensed something was off about Meng Wan, and his voice turned sharp and urgent as he barked at her. "Hurry up and bring the money, or I'll smash up your restaurant. I'll come make trouble here every single day, and we'll see how you keep running this business!"

With that the man snatched up a chair beside him and looked about to bring it crashing down on a table.

"All right, all right, all right—I'll go get the money this instant, just put that down!" Meng Wan held out both hands to stop him; that table hadn't been cheap, and if he smashed it she'd have to buy a new one.

"Go, now!" The man brandished the chair threateningly.

Catching sight of the police drawing closer out of the corner of her eye, Meng Wan pretended to turn and head for the front desk.

"Police—nobody move!"

She'd taken only a few steps when a clatter and crash sounded behind her—the short man had been subdued in an instant.

The one lying on the floor had just started to rise when he was pushed back down, his wrist wrenched behind his back. "Ow, ow, ow!" he howled in pain.

The bystanders were startled at first, but once they grasped what was happening—that the man foaming on the floor had actually woken up and tried to flee—wasn't it obvious these were con men?

"Don't move!" the police comrade said sternly, his voice loud.

"Comrade officer, we're the ones who got sick eating in this restaurant! Why are you arresting us?" the short man shouted, baring his teeth.

"If you've got a problem, you can explain it at the station. What were you doing picking up a stool? With this many people around, what if you'd hurt someone?" As he spoke, the officer swiftly snapped a pair of handcuffs on him.

"I wouldn't hurt anyone—I just wanted her to pay up!" The man twisted his hands, beads of sweat raining down onto the floor without letup. "Comrade officer, let me go, please. I didn't mean to hurt anyone, I just wanted to scare her a little."

"Come to the station with me first and explain yourself!" the officer said in a tone that brooked no argument. He glanced at Meng Wan, who had spoken a moment ago. "You come with us too!"

"All right." Meng Wan finally let out a breath of relief. "Liu Fei, come to the station with me. The rest of you stay and keep the customers calm. These two are swindlers—the foam at that man's mouth is toothpaste."

So it was toothpaste—no wonder he'd "come to" so conveniently. It seemed it had all been a sham.

A buzz of discussion spread through the hall.

"You're talking nonsense! My brother foamed at the mouth because he ate your restaurant's food!" the man was still struggling.

The officer, leaning in close and catching the familiar smell, called it out in a single phrase. "It's toothpaste."

With the officer's word to back it up, everyone relaxed. So it really had just been two con men trying to swindle some money.

Meng Wan threw the officer a grateful look and went along with them to the station to sort the matter out.

Only then did Sun Jing lower the hand pressed to her chest. She raised her voice to reassure the customers. "Everyone, please go on eating—that was just a small interlude, sorry to interrupt your meal. I've had the kitchen prepare a cold dish for every table, on the house, to settle everyone's nerves."

Not only had they watched a show, there was a free extra dish too. The customers all returned to their seats, chattering away about the two men.

Old Bi couldn't help but sigh with feeling. It seemed that, having handed the agency over to Meng Wan, he could leave with his mind at ease.

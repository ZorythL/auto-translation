Chapter 59

That said, Meng Wan turned and went back to her own office, taking the labor contracts she'd drawn up out of a drawer.

Seeing everyone seated in a row, Meng Wan held up the contracts in her hand. "These are labor contracts. A new labor law was just introduced this year, so we can't go on the way we used to—signing some casual letter of employment, or, in some cases, simply renting someone else's credentials."

As Meng Wan spoke, her gaze fell on Luo Zhigang and Jiang Ke, and the two of them dodged their eyes a few times, guilty-conscienced.

"From now on, every bit of our agency's hiring, spending, and revenue must be traceable. Everything needs a receipt; everything must be compliant and aboveboard. I can make you all a promise: as long as you take your work seriously and are willing to put in the effort and learn, the money you can earn will absolutely be far more than it is now. As long as we twist ourselves into a single rope, our agency will only grow bigger and bigger. Thank you, every one of you, for being willing to walk alongside me and build our Youth Travel Agency up step by step. Each and every one of you is an indispensable, vital part of this agency. Whatever difficulties come, we'll face them together—we're one collective. This is my first time being a leader too, and if there's anything I do that you're not happy with, you can raise it with me directly. I hope we can all grow together!"

With that, Meng Wan bowed deeply before everyone.

Moved to red-rimmed eyes by Meng Wan's words, Xu Qiuyun was the first to clap, applauding excitedly. Everyone else, too, was touched by Meng Wan's sincerity and candor, and one after another they broke into applause, until the crisp sound of clapping rang clean through the little room.

Meng Wan thought of this applause as the flint that kindled a single spark.

The next day, Jiang Ke brought Yu Fei to the agency. Meng Wan took an immediate liking to this capable-looking girl with her ear-length bob, signed a contract with her on the spot, and had her start studying hard under Lan Xiuli.

Meng Wan ran the numbers in her head. As things stood, the agency's guides were arranged thus: Liu Fei led Xiaohai on the public-sector tours; Luo Zhigang led Jiang Ke on the commercial tours; Lan Xiuli and Yu Fei handled the foreign-tourist tours; and she herself, apart from foreign-language work, filled in wherever there was a shortage.

At their agency's current size, by the end of one summer vacation, she figured they could earn quite a bit.

Meng Wan grinned just imagining the scene of money pouring into the bag.

------

The auspicious day Meng Qingyan had chosen for the agency's reopening fell on a weekend, and Meng Wan came to the agency early that morning to make preparations.

In fact Xu Qiuyun had already pretty much taken care of the preparations. Meng Wan had been so busy with the restaurant's renovations these past days that she hadn't had time to mind the agency at all.

The restaurant was nearly squared away by now. It had originally been an old guesthouse that went under from poor management. Guesthouses all came with a back kitchen, so the renovations needed no major overhaul. Meng Wan had only bought new tables and chairs, enlarged the kitchen, and added a good deal of tableware. She'd already submitted the application materials for the operating license; it would take roughly a month to get approved.

There was one pressing problem left to solve: who would manage this restaurant. Run off her feet just with the agency, she couldn't possibly handle the restaurant on top of it. Hiring an outsider she didn't feel easy about, but among the people she knew, none had the ability to run a restaurant. It was a real headache.

As she was mulling it over, she saw James and two men carrying two large flower baskets outside, discussing where to set them down. Meng Wan trotted out joyfully, taking the stairs two at a time.

"How are you here so early?"

James pointed to either side of the entrance. "Just set them there."

Then he looked at Meng Wan with doting tenderness and said gently, "Today's your big day. Of course I had to come early to keep you company—and see if there's anywhere I can make myself useful, too."

Hands behind her back, Meng Wan tilted up her chin and said cheerfully, "I'm afraid you'll be disappointed. Qiuyun's so capable that even I couldn't get a hand in."

"Then congratulations on finding such a capable subordinate. As you'd put it—knowing your people and using them well, a discerning eye for talent."

"Oh-ho." Meng Wan teased him. "A few days apart and you've improved again."

"It's all thanks to Teacher Meng's good teaching. I read that Compendium of Idioms every day now."

A few days back, Meng Wan and James had gone out strolling and come across a newly opened bookshop, so they'd gone in for a browse. She'd bought nothing for herself but had bought James a Compendium of Idioms—the edition with pinyin annotations, no less.

She'd done it on a whim, really, and never expected James to actually study it in earnest.

Meng Wan gave him a thumbs-up and praised, "Worthy of commendation."

"And does Teacher Meng have any reward?"

With that, James stepped closer to Meng Wan, bending slightly at the waist and bringing his cheek near.

Instinctively Meng Wan took a step back, then glanced about in a fluster, only to find the two delivery workers had long since vanished. At that moment there was no one in the street but the two of them.

This man was really too bold—what if someone saw!

Seeing him still grinning brilliantly and fixing his eyes on her, Meng Wan's heart pounded. She shut her eyes and pecked a quick kiss to his cheek, then bolted back inside as if fleeing.

The corner of James's mouth curved up, and he touched the spot of the kiss that hadn't left behind even a trace of lingering warmth.

"My mom and dad will be coming by later. How should I introduce you to them?" Meng Wan hadn't yet formally told her family she had a boyfriend. Apart from Cheng Xuehua, none of them had met James.

James folded his arms across his chest and leaned against a desk, the very picture of ease yet not without authority. "As your boyfriend, of course. We've been together this long—am I not even entitled to a title?"

"Of course you are." Meng Wan hurried over with a placating smile. "I was just being shy, that's all. Whatever title you want, it's yours."

"Really?"

"Of course!"

"Then when can we... become legal?" But before James could finish, Old Bi pushed open the door and walked in, cutting off the second half of his sentence. "You two are here early."

Old Bi paid not the slightest notice to the sticky-sweet mood between them, simply going about the first step of his morning routine: brewing tea.

Meng Wan was still waiting to hear the rest of James's words. She turned and said to Old Bi perfunctorily, "Isn't today our agency's big day? I came early to see if there's anything to be done."

"Anything to be done, Little Xu's pretty much done it. In a bit all that's left is for you to give your speech." As Old Bi spoke, he measured a small pinch of tea leaves into the glass jar and tipped hot water in from the thermos.

"Right." Meng Wan turned back and asked in a low voice, "What were you about to say just now?"

James blinked his clear eyes and vaguely changed the subject. "Nothing. I brought a camera. Later I'll take plenty of photos for you to keep as a memento."

Now wasn't the time to talk about that. Sooner or later they'd become legal anyway.

"If you hadn't mentioned it I'd have forgotten. A day this important really does call for some commemorative photos."

James saw there was still time. "I'm hungry. Let's go grab some breakfast first."

Meng Wan rubbed her stomach. She'd come out early today and was indeed a little hungry. "Come on—Teacher Meng will treat you to steamed buns!"

After breakfast, the agency's people arrived one after another. Everyone had dressed especially formally today; even Xiaohai wore a white shirt that he'd dug up from who knew where.

"Look at you—a few days apart and I hardly recognize you." Meng Wan teased Xiaohai.

Xiaohai scratched his head bashfully. "Master Liu gave me one of his shirts. He said today's a formal occasion and I mustn't shame you, Master."

"You're no shame to me. You could wear anything and never shame me." Like an older sister, Meng Wan smoothed out a crease at his shoulder. "Look how far you've come in such a short time—our Xiaohai's so smart and well-mannered now."

Hearing Meng Wan praise Xiaohai, Lin Dong scrambled over to vie for her attention too, showing off his outfit as if competing for favor. "What about me? Do I look smart today? Qiuyun picked this whole outfit for me."

Xu Qiuyun shot him an exasperated look. "What are you butting in for."

Meng Wan laughed happily, propping her chin in her hand as she looked him over carefully and gave her verdict. "You do look sharper than usual—a much healthier glow about you, too. Seems love's been nourishing you well lately."

Lin Dong's cheeks flushed red at once. He cut a glance at Xu Qiuyun and muttered under his breath, "I asked you about the clothes—where'd you wander off to with that."

Amid the joking, everyone wore the indulgent smiles of doting aunties.

"All right." Meng Wan clapped her hands. "Let me introduce everyone. This is Master Bai, who makes our agency's souvenirs. While our guides are out running all over the place, Master Bai is leading his workers in carving souvenirs late into the night."

Facing so many people, Old Bai grew a bit nervous too. "Just call me Old Bai, everyone."

Meng Wan went on with the introductions. "And this is Master Hai, the head chef of our restaurant. Some of us have already tasted Master Hai's cooking and couldn't praise it enough. From now on we can take guests straight to our restaurant to eat. That way our guests get to taste Yuyang's signature cuisine, and the restaurant gets a steady stream of customers—two birds with one stone."

Master Hai's face was as stern as ever, but his tone today was warm. "From now on I'll be relying on all of you to bring the business. Together let's give every tourist an unforgettable Yuyang trip—great food and great scenery both."

"And this person here." Meng Wan walked over to Lin Dong. "I'm sure you all know him well already, but today I'll formally introduce his new role. He's the head of our agency's dedicated vehicle fleet. From now on, when we take on clients, we won't have to book drivers and pay kickbacks. We've got our own fleet, with the drivers' wages paid by the agency. It's not only convenient but also saves us a tidy sum."

Not long ago, the moment Meng Wan mentioned forming a fleet to Lin Dong, he'd thrown himself into finding vehicles and drivers without a moment's delay. There were a few laid-off workers in their compound who used to handle transport at the factory—their driving skills were beyond reproach—and the instant they heard about such a good job, they agreed at once, saying they could start the very next day.

The buses were secondhand and old. Meng Wan had rented one large coach and three small-to-medium vehicles, and just like that, the agency had its own exclusive fleet.

"And this person." Meng Wan exchanged a glance with James, her tone softening as she introduced him. "This is the head of Yuyang's first resort-style hotel, James. Once their hotel is built, the two of us are sure to be collaborating."

Thanks to Old Bi's unguarded mouth, James's name had long since spread to the ears of everyone at the agency. They all knew their manager had a foreign boyfriend; they'd simply never had the chance to meet him. Today they finally got the honor of laying eyes on him, and from the moment they'd come in, every gaze had kept circling back to him.

Never one to behave, Luo Zhigang stirred the pot. "This Mr. James seems to have another identity, doesn't he? Won't Manager Meng introduce that for us?"

Meng Wan had known someone would ask. Just as she was hesitating over how to broach it, James walked straight to Meng Wan's side, gently draped an arm around her shoulders, and introduced himself. "I'm not only the head of the hotel. My most important identity is that I'm your Manager Meng's boyfriend. I'm honored to attend this opening ceremony today and to meet all of you outstanding partners. I hope that in the future we can collaborate again and again, and together add bricks and mortar to Yuyang's tourism industry."

It was a thoroughly graceful little speech. Luo Zhigang, who'd meant to needle him a few more times, heard James lift things to such a lofty height and held his tongue.

Meng Wan turned and exchanged a glance with James, then faced everyone with a smile and declared firmly, "Together, brick by brick!"

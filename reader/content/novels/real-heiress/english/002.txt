Chapter 2

Meng Qingyan was a man who cared about appearances, and because his daughter had had debt collectors at the door for the first time in her life, his face was cold as he demanded to know what was going on.

This time Meng Wan didn't let Meng Qingyan's mood rattle her. In her past life she had been too rash, too desperate to prove the fault wasn't hers, and had walked straight into Xu Hongtian's trap. The two of them had ended up in a screaming match; in her anger she'd even thrown the first punch, and so what had been no fault of hers became her fault after all.

She walked unhurriedly to the center of the room and, slowly and methodically, laid out the whole story for them from start to finish, explaining the agreement she and Xu Qiuyun had made—that it was not a case of her borrowing money and refusing to pay it back.

Afraid her parents wouldn't believe her, Meng Wan even encouraged Xu Qiuyun to speak up and bear witness for her.

Seeing that Meng Wan wasn't angry, Xu Qiuyun's trembling body gradually steadied. Through her tears she explained the matter clearly to the Meng parents, making it plain that she had willingly lent the money to Meng Wan, and that Meng Wan had promised to repay her the moment this month's pay came in. So there was no question of borrowing and not returning, as her brother claimed.

Seeing his sister actually take an outsider's side, Xu Hongtian pinched her viciously, foul-tempered. How had he ended up with a sister who sold out her own family?

"Enough of your useless talk. I'm getting that money today, or I'll move right into your home and stay—I'll leave when I get my money, and not before!"

Seeing Xu Qiuyun hiss in pain, Meng Wan strode forward in a single bound, pulled her to her own side, and asked softly, "Are you all right?"

Hearing Meng Wan's concern, tears welled up in Xu Qiuyun's eyes despite herself. She pressed her lips together and shook her head.

Meng Wan gave a cold snort. In her past life, a few words from Xu Hongtian had goaded her into a shouting match, costing her the upper hand—and not only had it landed her in a furious fight with her family, it had cost her Qiuyun's friendship. This time she would not be so foolish.

She drew Xu Qiuyun behind her and, face cold, negotiated with Xu Hongtian. "There's no money to be had. It's all gone into clothes. Right now I'm flat broke. So you've got exactly two choices: wait for my pay and I'll repay you, or take the clothes."

Xu Hongtian was satisfied with neither of the two options Meng Wan offered. He was in a hurry to furnish his home and get married—where did he have time to wait around for her? "You've got no money, but your parents surely do. A son's debt is the father's to pay—that's only natural!"

Cheng Xuehua, afraid that if Xu Hongtian kept making a scene it would set the neighbors gossiping, was about to dig into her own pocket and pay off the debt herself.

She had just opened her mouth when Meng Wan cut in ahead of her. "Money I borrowed, I'll be the one to repay. You answer for your own deeds. And anyway, this is between me and Xu Qiuyun—she hasn't even spoken yet, so where do you get off butting in!"

With that, Meng Wan turned to look at Xu Qiuyun, steadied her by the shoulders to make her lift her head, and asked in a gentle tone, "Don't be afraid, Qiuyun. You choose. I'll go along with whatever you decide."

Buoyed by Meng Wan's encouragement, the courage in Xu Qiuyun's heart began to bubble slowly to the surface. She glanced at Xu Hongtian, who was glaring viciously at her, and the moments of the past flickered through her mind one after another, like scenes from a film.

Her brother, who had brought his friends to spy on her while she bathed—and when she caught them, turned it around and accused her of being indecent.

Her brother, who, on learning she'd gotten into a vocational college, had secretly thrown away her acceptance letter, unwilling to spend that tuition money on her.

Her brother again, who forced her to hand over every cent of her monthly pay, or else he wouldn't let her go to work—he'd find some man to marry her off to and trade her for a bride price.

Before she met Meng Wan, her whole life had been gray, without a glimpse of sunlight.

Meng Wan was the first person to praise her lovely handwriting and her good looks; the one who, when she'd made a mistake at work, had stepped forward to shoulder the blame with their supervisor; the one who, on learning her birthday, had taken her to Yuxiang Pavilion and ordered her a whole table of dishes she'd never tasted before.

She had decided that Meng Wan was her friend.

Every bit of the bullying she could swallow down—but Meng Wan was her friend, and she could not let her friend suffer such an injustice.

Xu Qiuyun drew a deep breath, lifted her chin, and—as if having resolved on something—said in a firm, forceful voice, her gaze steady, "I choose the clothes!"

Choosing the money would never get it into her hands anyway. If she chose the clothes, she could at least wear them herself. Since Xu Hongtian wouldn't let her have any peace, then fine—nobody would have any peace.

Meng Wan was surprised she would choose that way. The timid, frail Xu Qiuyun of before seemed to have vanished in that very instant. She liked this version of her—the one who fought for herself, who no longer wronged herself to spare others.

"Good. The clothes it is, then!"

"No!"

Watching the money slip through his fingers, Xu Hongtian shot to his feet in alarm and stepped forward, meaning to grab Xu Qiuyun.

At once Meng Xingyang moved up and put himself between Xu Hongtian and Meng Wan. He'd been keeping close the whole time, precisely for fear this hooligan would turn violent.

Meng Wan instinctively shielded Xu Qiuyun, raising her voice to throw it back at him. "Whether it works or not isn't up to you. No matter how big a scene you make, I'm not giving you any money. In a moment Xu Qiuyun is going to take the clothes and leave, and if you keep this up I'll call the police and have them lock you up for a few days. Aren't you about to get married? In that case I'll pay your future in-laws a visit and put in a few nice words for you—let's see if they'll still hand their daughter to a man who's done time in jail!"

Meng Wan had a friendly, playful face, her curved smiling eyes deeply charming, and she'd used that face plenty to sweet-talk the Meng parents. But today that face was suffused with ferocity, her gaze ready to swallow her opponent whole and tear him apart—the very sight of it sent a chill through a person.

As the saying goes, to kill a snake, strike at its vital point, and for Xu Hongtian, marriage was that vital point. His family was poor; at a full twenty-nine this year, he'd only just found someone to marry. The woman even had a limp—but fortunately that didn't affect his ability to carry on the family line, and she came with no bride price required. Her one condition was that there had to be a wardrobe, a television, a refrigerator, and a washing machine.

These appliances would sit in his own home, where he could use them himself, so of course Xu Hongtian had agreed at once. The trouble was that he was just a furnaceman at the steel mill, earning only five hundred yuan a month, and on that wage he couldn't save much. The money would surely have to come from the family—and the biggest share of it from his sister, who worked at the cultural center.

Xu Hongtian had no intention of letting four hundred yuan derail the great matter of his life. He couldn't afford to drop the ball at such a critical juncture. It was only four hundred yuan, after all—worst case, he'd go ask his aunt again. He didn't believe she could stand by and watch the Xu family's eldest son fail to marry.

He just couldn't swallow this insult. Sooner or later, he'd settle this account with Meng Wan!

That was what he thought in his heart, though his mouth still refused to concede. "Don't think you can scare me with the police station. I'm getting married soon and I don't want to blow this up, so I'll let the money go—call it a few articles of clothing I'm buying for my sister, as her big brother. This sister of mine is a jinx, you know. Just you wait—rotten luck is coming your way!"

Having spat out his threat, Xu Hongtian kicked the tea table, shot a glare at Xu Qiuyun cowering behind Meng Wan, dusted off his backside, and stalked out.

With the troublemaker gone, the Meng household fell instantly quiet. Meng Wan took Xu Qiuyun by the arm. "Mom, Dad, I'm going to pack up some clothes for Qiuyun."

Cheng Xuehua nodded quickly. "Go on, then."

After listening for so long, she'd gathered as much: this girl was a pitiable thing too. Saddled with a brother like that, life surely couldn't be easy for her. Such a shame.

By now Meng Qingyan's anger had cooled as well. At first, hearing only Xu Hongtian's side, he really had thought his daughter had gone around borrowing money irresponsibly and refusing to repay it. Borrowing money to buy clothes was no good habit, true—but today his daughter had handled things with real maturity, solving her own problem without leaning on the family. That gratified him.

*

Meng Wan led Xu Qiuyun back to her bedroom and shut the door behind her. "Sit wherever you like. I'll pack the clothes for you."

Xu Qiuyun looked timidly around her, then shuffled in small steps to the edge of the bed and sat down.

So Meng Wan had a room of her own, while she, to this day, still shared a single room with her younger brother, divided only by a curtain.

When Meng Wan opened the wardrobe, the clothes inside hung pressed one against the next, packed full—their whole family put together didn't own this many clothes.

"Meng Wan."

"Hm?"

Meng Wan bent down and pulled a blue cloth sack out of the wardrobe.

"I'm sorry."

"You're not the one who should be apologizing." Meng Wan stared at the dizzying array in the wardrobe, trying to recall which pieces she'd bought at the time. But too much time had passed; she only remembered the one dress. So she simply started going through them one by one, picking out the new pieces, the ones that would suit Xu Qiuyun, to send along with her.

Xu Qiuyun twisted her fingers together and, after hesitating a long while, finally got the question out. "Do you still think of me as a friend?"

"Of course we're friends." Meng Wan kept choosing and stuffing pieces into the sack. "As long as you still think of me as one."

Xu Qiuyun answered urgently, "Of course I think of you as a friend!"

Then she silently added, in her heart: my best friend, in fact.

"Well, there you go, then." Meng Wan crammed the blue cloth sack until it bulged and lugged it onto the bed. The zipper wouldn't close, so she had to force it. "Give me a hand."

Xu Qiuyun had no idea how many clothes four hundred yuan could buy, but this seemed like an awful lot. She'd never owned this many clothes in her whole life.

She knew how much Meng Wan loved that dress, and she didn't want to take what someone else cherished. "Keep that dress for yourself. The money's all gone into clothes now anyway, so I can do with them as I please."

The words struck Meng Wan as a little sad. The money was Xu Qiuyun's own earnings, yet only once it was converted into things nobody else wanted could it truly become hers.

Meng Wan shook her head and went on packing, while Xu Qiuyun reached over to help with the zipper.

"What's yours is yours. Don't wrong yourself for anyone's sake." Meng Wan put her weight into her arm, signaling Xu Qiuyun to keep pulling. "The truth is, I've forgotten which pieces I actually bought with the borrowed money, so I've just packed you some that'll suit you. When my pay comes, I'll give you back half the money on top of that, and then this account will be square."

Xu Hongtian didn't know this money existed either, which meant Xu Qiuyun could keep it in her own hands.

"I couldn't possibly," Xu Qiuyun refused. "I've already taken the clothes—how could I take money too?"

The zipper finally closed. Meng Wan shook out her aching arm and said, "Just listen to me. You can't only make yourself feel better and leave me feeling uneasy and guilty, now."

"But—"

"No buts. When you get home, whatever you do, don't go along with what they want again. You have to tell them: the money you earn yourself is yours alone to dispose of. Stop foolishly handing it all over, only to get not a single kind word for it in the end."

That was as far as Meng Wan could go. Whether Xu Qiuyun could see it through depended on her own resolve. Everyone had their own hurdles to clear.

Xu Qiuyun silently clenched her fingers. The moment she'd defied Xu Hongtian, she had made up her mind to resist. If she stayed this weak, they would pick her clean to the bone. She didn't want to live so meekly anymore.

"Thank you, Xiao Wan."

Meng Wan gazed into Xu Qiuyun's clear, bright eyes and at last spoke the apology that had been so long in coming. "I'm sorry, Qiuyun."

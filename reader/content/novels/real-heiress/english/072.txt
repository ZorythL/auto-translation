Chapter 72

The money in the accounts did turn out to be short, so, as Meng Wan had instructed, Li Yang drew another five hundred yuan from Xu Qiuyun. He didn't dare ask for more—better to economize where he could, or else Manager Meng might think he was lining his own pockets.

Following Meng Wan's instructions, Li Yang went to the building-materials market himself to pick out the office supplies, even pulling some strings with an acquaintance to get a discount. This was the first task the new boss had handed him, and he was determined to carry it out flawlessly.

In the meantime, all the guides set down the work in their hands and joined Li Yang in giving the agency a thorough overhaul. With so many hands, it only took three days to get the bulk of it done. For the few days remaining, everyone was out of work, waiting at the agency with eager eyes for Meng Wan to assign them their tasks.

Nobody could figure out what this new manager was playing at, leaving them all hung out to dry like this without even showing her face. One senior guide, Wang Yong, came right out and voiced his discontent to Li Yang's face.

In those seven days he could have run a tour group and earned a hundred or two at least—anything was better than sitting in the office staring blankly into space. And it wasn't just him sitting idle; twenty or thirty guides all had nothing to do. How would that sound if word got out?

Li Yang was anxious too, but he knew anxiety was useless. Meng Wan had said seven days, so seven days it was; he'd see what she had planned after the seven days were up. If she still hadn't turned up by then, he'd have no choice but to go find her himself.

Right on cue—not a day early, not a day late—Meng Wan appeared outside the agency's front door first thing on the eighth morning, a big sack of local specialties in hand for everyone.

"Manager Li, hand these out to everyone, quick. We'll eat and meet at the same time."

Meng Wan rolled her wrist, sore from the weight she'd just set down, and rubbed it as she counted heads with her eyes. This dark, dense crowd—it really was quite a lot of people.

The moment everyone saw Meng Wan, the buzz of chatter died down at once, and each person sat back in their own seat, waiting for this new boss they'd waited so long for to speak.

Meng Wan cleared her throat. Addressing this many people for the first time, she felt a touch nervous. "These are local specialties I brought back from Linjiang. Help yourselves and hear me out."

Linjiang was the capital of neighboring Linhai Province.

"Linjiang?" Jiang Ke took the square box Li Yang handed him. "Manager Meng, you went to Linjiang?"

Jiang Ke was a guileless sort who said whatever came to mind. He was close to Meng Wan—one of her own inner circle, you might say—and he understood her: whatever she did, she always had her reasons.

But the others didn't see it that way. Here they'd been, waiting anxiously, while the new boss went off on a trip for fun? What kind of way was that to behave?

Yu Fei caught on first and gave Jiang Ke a slap, telling him to mind his tongue.

Meng Wan only smiled and said, "Yes, I just got back last night."

"At a time like this, Manager Meng still has the leisure to go off sightseeing?" Wang Yong slapped away the piece of candy being passed to him and aired his displeasure outright.

"You may have misunderstood." Meng Wan explained, still smiling. "I went to drum up business."

"Drum up business?"

Meng Wan turned a pair of clear, bright eyes on the crowd and explained for them all. "As provincial capitals, both Linjiang and Yuyang carry the responsibility of helping develop their provinces' economies. Our province's pillar industry is tourism, while Linhai Province's is the food industry—their locally brewed vinegar and liquor are quite famous. They can no longer sell off all they make within their own borders, and the surplus they produce has to be sold somewhere. And as their neighbor, Yuyang is bound to be their first target."

"So their city leadership took the lead in signing an agreement with our city, for mutual benefit: they encourage their local people to come tour our city, and we place orders for their products. This time I went to Linjiang together with Vice Mayor Wang, who's in charge of tourism, and I've already made contact with quite a few cadres and government offices. When the time comes, they'll bring their families and friends to tour Yuyang."

Not long after she and James signed their contract, Vice Mayor Wang had sought her out. Secretary Little Liu had already gotten to the bottom of the matter Qian Zhenyu had reported, and the facts were exactly as Meng Wan had said: she'd merely served as a local host for a few days, with no money changing hands between the two sides, so it couldn't be counted as receiving tourists.

Because Vice Mayor Wang had scolded her a few times at that last meeting—and because he'd been so angered at the time by Qi Hongwei's inaction that he'd lost control of his measure and snapped at a young woman like Meng Wan in front of all those people—and given her past contributions to Yuyang's tourism industry, Vice Mayor Wang had decided to bring Meng Wan along to Linjiang as an enterprise representative, so she could make contact a few days early and drum up business for her own agency.

Because the spots were limited, Vice Mayor Wang brought only Meng Wan as his sole enterprise representative—which meant that this first batch of tourists was hers to corner almost entirely.

And she hadn't let his trust down. Over those few days she'd run herself nearly footsore, recording the contact information of just about every interested cadre.

The good news exploded among the guides like a burst of fireworks. They'd never imagined Meng Wan would land them such a huge piece of business—at the very least, this month's commissions were taken care of.

For a moment Wang Yong's face went a little awkward. He rubbed the tip of his nose sheepishly. He'd judged a gentleman's heart by a petty man's measure.

Luo Zhigang asked loudly, "Manager Meng, do we have to lower our prices, then?"

This question was aimed at Chaoyang Travel's guides; he'd asked it on purpose.

Meng Wan gave a sweeping wave of her hand. "No. From now on, every guide in this agency must charge the prices on the tour-route price list I provide. Otherwise—if I ever find out a guide has privately cut prices to poach customers and disrupt the market—it's dismissal, no exceptions."

The moment this rule was announced, the crowd was in an uproar. The punishment was awfully harsh, wasn't it—no allowances for personal feelings, just straight to dismissal? That sort of thing had never happened before.

"As long as you follow the rules, you're fine. Manager Meng's not going to fire you for no reason," Jiang Ke put in on Meng Wan's behalf.

"That's right." Li Yang immediately raised his voice over the rest. "As long as everyone does as Manager Meng says, no one's getting fired."

"Exactly. I hope you'll all cooperate. As long as it's not a matter of principle, everything else is open to discussion—I'm not an unreasonable person."

Then Meng Wan shifted gears and began to establish her authority. "I'm not Qian Zhenyu. I do things by my own set of principles. If anyone can't accept that, you're free to leave right now. Anyone who hasn't left by the end of today, I'll take it as a sign you mean to work with me—and from here on, let's build this agency into the number one together. Have you got the confidence?"

"Yes!" Once again Jiang Ke was the first to answer.

The others' minds didn't quite keep up; a thin, scattered, somewhat sheepish murmur of agreement followed.

Meng Wan clapped her hands smartly and asked again, louder, "Have you all got the confidence?"

This time the answer came back as a chorus of low but solid voices. "Yes!"

"Then if you've got the confidence, let's get to work." As she spoke, Meng Wan took a notebook out of her bag. "In here are all the tourists who've shown interest in coming—I got their phone numbers. In a moment, Manager Li will hand out one to each of you. When you call, just mention our agency's name and they'll know what it's about. They'll be coming with their families, and National Day is right around the corner, so be sure to make the most of this window."

The moment the meeting broke up, everyone scrambled to start making calls. There were only three telephones in the agency, instantly commandeered by the three people standing nearest; the rest gathered their things and went out to find phones of their own.

Lan Xiuli, Yu Fei, and a few of the other foreign-language guides didn't move. Lan Xiuli, the eldest among them, spoke up. "Manager Meng, what about us?"

"You'll receive domestic tourists for now too. Once we've gotten through this busy stretch, I'll go after foreign-tourist resources."

James already had a whole batch of friends wanting to come; it was just that the visas hadn't come through yet, which had held things up.

Lan Xiuli and the others nodded. Domestic tourists didn't earn as much as foreign ones, but it beat sitting idle.

These past few days of running around had left her genuinely worn out, so to shake off the fatigue Meng Wan went to Yuyin Lodge for a bath. After she'd finished soaking, James took her to a newly opened restaurant for a meal.

As she ate, Meng Wan brought up work again. "Tell me—how can I build up our agency's reputation overseas?"

In this era the internet was still primitive, and for a domestic enterprise to break into international recognition was hard beyond words—all the more so for them, who weren't even a big company.

James set a plate of sliced steak in front of Meng Wan. "Have you ever heard of computers?"

Meng Wan let out an "Ah?" and pretended she'd never heard of them. "What's that? Like a calculator?"

"Hmm..." James thought about how to explain it to her. "Actually, I don't know much about this myself. From what I understand, a computer can connect every corner of the world together. Whatever happens in one place, you just send it to the computer and everyone can know about it."

Meng Wan let her mouth fall open a little and feigned astonishment. "Wow, it's that incredible? Then could I put our agency on this computer, and everyone in the world would know about us?"

"In principle, yes." James thought it over. "But it takes a professional to make something like that. I have a classmate who studied computers—let me send him an email and see if he can help."

"What's an email?" Meng Wan kept playing dumb.

"Just think of it as writing a letter—a letter written through a computer. As long as he's got a computer at hand, he can see it."

"Amazing—it's all fields I've never even heard of. Looks like I'll have to pick up the pace of my learning so I don't get left behind."

With every advance in technology, the ones who shut themselves off and cling stubbornly to the old ways are cast aside by that monster called the times, knocked flat by the rolling tide with little hope of getting back up. Only those with the courage to embrace new things, who meet the new wave with a positive attitude, can keep their footing.

James gave a soft laugh. "All right—I'll teach you next time."

While Youth Travel's guides were working the phones in full swing, Bai Yuexi—who'd somehow learned that Meng Wan had landed a batch of tourists in advance—came straight to her door.

He'd already been unhappy that Meng Wan and James had bought out Chaoyang Travel and thrown off his plans. Now he was even more put out, a chest full of pent-up frustration, as though Meng Wan had snatched his tourists right out of his hands.

"Oh ho." Meng Wan came out smiling to greet him. "If it isn't Manager Bai. Have you graced us with your presence to offer your congratulations?"

"I've come to call you to account!"

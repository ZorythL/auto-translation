Chapter 67

The municipal government had called all the travel-agency heads together for a meeting, to give an interim summary of the city's tourism work over the past stretch of time.

That day Meng Wan had deliberately put on a crisp brown suit. Her hair had grown smoother and straighter, only curling up a little at the ends right after she washed it, so today she had simply plaited it into a thick three-strand braid that lay over her right shoulder.

The reason she had dressed up specially today was, in part, confidence.

Her agency's earnings couldn't compare with the big firms, but after the summer break it had just barely clawed its way into the middle tier. For an outfit the size of Youth Travel, earning that kind of revenue was nothing short of a miracle. There was no way she was going to sit in the very back row this time—she represented the face of her entire agency.

Ever since the last meeting, when Meng Wan had proposed that everyone sign a written pledge, she had become a "celebrity" in tourism circles. By now there wasn't a single guide who didn't know the names Meng Wan and Youth Travel; she was something of an industry star. Only this star hadn't won much adoration—on the contrary, plenty of tongues wagged behind her back.

When Meng Wan reached the conference room, more than half the seats were already filled. She glanced over the arrangement: everyone was still sitting in their old spots, nothing changed.

She hesitated in the doorway for a moment, torn over whether to claim a seat closer to the front, when Qian Zhenyu's loud, brassy voice rang out in mockery. "Oh ho, if it isn't Manager Meng of Youth Travel. Word is your agency doubled its earnings this season—beat out quite a few of us middle-tier outfits. And you've still got the nerve to leave her stuck in the back? Hurry up and clear a spot for her."

The managers already seated exchanged glances. Suddenly their stools felt scorching hot beneath them; nobody knew whether to stand up or stay put.

Bai Yuexi shot Qian Zhenyu a helpless look. Afraid the two of them would start quarreling, he jumped in before Meng Wan could speak. "If we're changing seats, let's all change. The last time we sorted seating was a year ago, and a lot has shifted in the market this past year—everyone's business has had its ups and downs. It's about time we reassigned the seats."

Although nobody knew anyone else's exact revenue figures, based on the size and activity of each agency they all had their own private estimates. There were only so many travel agencies on the market these days, and sometimes they kept closer tabs on their rivals than on themselves. Who had run how many tour groups lately—everyone knew it down to the last detail.

At Bai Yuexi's words the crowd got up to find their new seats, dutifully moving forward or back. Those who couldn't agree began comparing themselves right then and there, like students being seated by their exam ranks—nobody wanted to slide backward.

Bai Yuexi and Qian Zhenyu didn't budge; they were confident they still held the top two spots. The one who'd been third dropped back a place, and the former fifth moved up to fill it.

Meng Wan didn't go hunting for a seat herself. She waited until everyone else had settled, then walked to the spot left open for her in the middle of the room.

Her former "place of honor" had been taken by Qin Lan, who now sat there with a sour face, rolling her eyes at the empty air.

"Some people really ought to try harder. Getting shown up by Manager Meng here, a greenhorn fresh out of the gate—if it were me, I'd be too mortified to show my face." With that, Qian Zhenyu made a show of patting his own cheeks.

So this was the real Qian Zhenyu after all; the man she'd seen at the hospital that day had been the exception.

Meng Wan answered with an unhurried smile. "It's only because all of you seniors have looked out for a newcomer like me that our agency has gotten where it is today. I'll be counting on your guidance going forward—let's work together to build up Yuyang's economy."

Bai Yuexi picked it right up. "Manager Meng is right. Some agencies didn't do so well this season, but I believe that's only temporary. As long as we grow the pie bigger, everyone can have a slice."

Qian Zhenyu was about to say something when the door was suddenly pushed open and the officials attending the meeting filed in.

By rights today should have been a happy occasion, but to Meng Wan the officials didn't seem to be in particularly good moods. Every one of them wore a black expression, as if a portable storm cloud hovered over each of their heads.

Wang Zhongxian walked to his seat, his face grim, his gaze sliding smoothly over everyone present. He noticed the shuffled seating at once—it seemed people were quite honest about their own standing.

"Just now I heard you all talking about growing the pie bigger." Wang Zhongxian looked at Bai Yuexi. "That's a fine idea. Infighting is pointless; what we need is to expand outward."

Qian Zhenyu muttered under his breath, "It's not that simple."

"No, it isn't simple. But the harder a thing is, the more we ought to take it on." Wang Zhongxian straightened up and looked out over the crowd. "Today I've called you all here to commend the effort everyone put in over this past summer season."

"This summer, spending at our Yuyang hotels, restaurants, gift shops, and on public transport all rose sharply. Even the old fellow hawking snacks from his carrying-pole at the foot of the mountain made a tidy sum. This proves beyond doubt that our Yuyang has a powerful tourism advantage. And so, as its next step, the city has decided to step up its support for the tourism industry and rapidly push through the various supporting infrastructure projects."

The materials Meng Wan had submitted earlier had already been sent out for field investigation, and well over half of them addressed real, concrete problems that needed solving. Orders had already gone down to rank them by difficulty and push them through one by one.

For everyone present this was a thrilling opportunity. Once infrastructure improved, the visitor experience would rise dramatically; and through word of mouth, Yuyang's tourist numbers would only keep climbing. That was the very foundation for growing the pie.

Wang Zhongxian went on. "This summer, the number of foreign visitors also rose markedly. Many of them said our bilingual signage made a real difference, so we must keep up the good work on the bilingual front. However, because we don't have enough foreign-language guides, a great many visitors ended up with no one to receive them and had to tour on their own, which left them with a poor experience. So going forward, training foreign-language guides remains our top priority."

"Vice Mayor Wang." Qian Zhenyu shot a glance at Meng Wan. "Don't travel agencies need a license to receive foreign visitors?"

"Of course they do. That's a regulation laid down in black and white from above."

"Then if someone receives foreign visitors privately, without a license, aren't they liable to be penalized?"

"Are you trying to report someone?" Wang Zhongxian had no wish to dance around with him, and cut straight to the point.

Qian Zhenyu didn't keep him in suspense either. His voice rang out clearly. "Deputy Director Wang, I'd like to report Meng Wan of Youth Travel. She privately received foreign visitors, caused them to suffer food poisoning and end up hospitalized, and has done serious harm to the reputation of Yuyang's tourism industry!"

Wang Zhongxian's mind raced. Meng Wan had indeed obtained the license, so there was no question of receiving guests illegally—but where did the food poisoning come from? He sent Meng Wan a look, signaling her to explain.

Qian Zhenyu was only halfway through before Meng Wan realized he was talking about the time the two of them had run into each other at the hospital. She'd known no good would come of meeting that man. Back then he'd worn such a fragile, pitiful look—she'd never imagined he'd turn right around and go digging into her affairs.

"Vice Mayor Wang, those people that day were my relatives, not foreign tourists. They'd come all that way to Yuyang, so naturally I played the gracious host—showed them around, treated them to some local Yuyang food. That was my personal business and had nothing to do with the agency. As for the food poisoning, it was caused by some unclean snacks they bought at a street stall themselves. I can account for all of this clearly, and if you don't believe me, I have witnesses."

Qian Zhenyu rolled his eyes. "So you didn't do a single thing wrong, is that it?"

"Enough!" Wang Zhongxian's face fell. "I'll have someone look into exactly what happened. Right now the most important thing for all of you is unity. Only by pulling together can we build Yuyang's tourism up. Grabbing hold of each other's tails and never letting go, squabbling in the henhouse day in and day out—what kind of behavior is that!"

Before Qian Zhenyu had a chance to feel wronged, Wang Zhongxian spoke again. "And you—even though you provided the leadership with some very valuable material before, don't let it go to your head. We'll investigate the situation Qian Zhenyu reported and get to the bottom of it. Don't count on getting off lucky!"

Listening to this dressing-down rain over her head, even Meng Wan, even-tempered as she was, couldn't keep her face from darkening. She hadn't done anything wrong in the first place—how exactly had her head gotten too big?

Zhou Quan held his breath, watching Meng Wan from the side, afraid she might lose her temper and talk back to Deputy Director Wang.

Deputy Director Wang usually didn't have such a fierce temper, but before the meeting today, Secretary Little Liu had handed them a report he'd compiled himself—a survey of the street vendors and gift shops in Yuyang that fleeced their customers. Eight out of every ten establishments had this problem, and yet Qi Hongwei, the bureau chief, had taken no serious action to crack down on it. That had deeply disappointed Wang Zhongxian.

So his anger hadn't passed yet, and it had spilled over onto Meng Wan.

After the meeting ended, Zhou Quan had meant to explain things to Meng Wan and tell her not to take it to heart, but she left in a hurry today—in the blink of an eye she was gone.

Meng Wan was, after all, the manager of a travel agency now, and being criticized in front of so many people left her feeling both wronged and stifled. The moment the meeting let out she rushed straight out of the tourism bureau building.

Men's faces really could change in an instant. Not long ago he'd acted like he thought highly of her, and the next moment he was hinting, openly and otherwise, that she'd better not get cocky.

"All right, stop being angry." James carried a plate of sliced watermelon over and set it in front of Meng Wan. "Have some watermelon to cool off."

The more Meng Wan thought about it, the angrier she got. She pushed away the offered fruit, tucked her legs up onto the sofa, and launched into her case to James again. "Tell me, isn't there something wrong with this Qian Zhenyu? That day at the hospital I saw how haggard he looked and kindly asked after him, and he turns right around and reports me. How can a person be so shameless?"

"There's really only one way to deal with someone like that." James set the fruit plate on the coffee table, his voice flat and even.

Meng Wan jumped in at once. "Surpass him!"

James nodded in agreement. "The only reason he's behaving this way is that he thinks you've stolen his customers and his thunder. Young people don't know how to keep their edge sheathed. So you might as well just go all the way—give him exactly what he expects. Once you're sitting in his seat, I believe he'll shut up."

Every time she dealt with Qian Zhenyu, Meng Wan could plainly sense the contempt and scorn he radiated toward her. When it came down to it, he still saw her as a young woman with a few petty schemes—someone who, when bullied, could only swallow it down—and he didn't take her agency seriously at all. In short, he picked on the weak and feared the strong.

Meng Wan turned her grief and indignation into strength. She jabbed a forceful number "one" at James with her index finger and cheered herself on loudly. "I'm going to be number one!"

Chapter 16

Caught red-handed, Meng Wan slowly turned her head, gave an awkward little smile, and put on an utterly fake look of surprise. "Wow, what a coincidence—fancy running into you here. Didn't you leave?"

"I went back to take care of some business and only just returned a couple of days ago." James was overjoyed to see an old acquaintance again.

"And this is...?" The woman outside the door followed the voice and peered in.

James cheerfully introduced the two. "This is the wonderful tour guide I told you about—Meng Wan."

"And this is my local friend here in Yuyang—Wan Qing."

He had a local friend too?

Meng Wan and Wan Qing exchanged a smile, but Meng Wan keenly sensed the other woman's smile wasn't sincere—it was laced with hostility.

After seeing Wan Qing off, James learned that Lin Dong was also in a private room and wanted to go up and say hello.

What had been a private room for three suddenly became one for four, and Meng Wan introduced Xu Qiuyun to him.

"James, are you leaving again this time you're back?" Lin Dong had gotten used to calling James by his English name, just like Meng Wan.

James took a sip of the tea Meng Wan had poured him. "I'm planning to settle in Yuyang for now. Going forward I'll probably be going back and forth between the two places."

"Is it because of that plot of land?" Meng Wan explained, "I accidentally overheard your conversation just now."

"That's one of the reasons, you could say." James leaned back in his chair. "But I still can't be sure I'll get the land."

"The land you want to buy..." The mention of land had set off an immediate association in Meng Wan's mind. "It wouldn't happen to be the plot in the northern suburbs, would it?"

"How did you know?" James asked, astonished. He didn't think he'd mentioned the location.

So the other buyer Song Qingyang had spoken of was James. That meant in her past life she'd helped Xu Tian snatch that very plot away from James. To think there'd been this twist of fate between them all along.

The mistake she'd made in her past life—this time she would absolutely make right.

"If you want to secure that land, I can help you."

"Oh?" James's interest was instantly piqued. He straightened up and waited for Meng Wan to go on.

"But I have one condition."

"Please, go ahead."

"I help you secure the land, and in return you pay me a commission."

"That's fine. How much do you want?"

When something could be solved with money, that was the simplest method of all.

"Five thousand yuan."

Although she already considered James a friend in her heart, even brothers had to settle their accounts clearly. The returns he stood to make from this land in the future would far, far exceed this measly five thousand.

Both Xu Qiuyun and Lin Dong were stunned by Meng Wan's "exorbitant" price. Five thousand yuan was nearly a whole year's wages for them. James was wealthy, sure, but he wasn't a sucker—surely he wouldn't agree.

Business was business. This time James didn't answer right away. He laced his fingers together, his two thumbs spinning round and round—a habit he fell into when thinking.

Five thousand yuan really wasn't a small sum, but compared with the returns he'd reap once he had the land, it was honestly nothing. He could give Meng Wan this money. What he had to consider was whether her efforts were worth it. He couldn't simply let her name any price she liked just because of their personal friendship. He was a businessman, not a philanthropist.

His meeting with Wan Qing today—she'd given him information about his competitor, and after learning the rival was an old acquaintance, he was honestly a little worried.

A man like Xu Tian would stop at nothing to get what he wanted, and on top of that he was a local, far more versed in the local web of connections and how things worked. Naturally that gave him the edge.

The reason James had sought out Wan Qing today was precisely that he needed a local with connections and resources to help him win this project. Wan Qing wouldn't ask him for payment, and even if he offered, she wouldn't take it—but that would leave him owing her a favor, and favors were the hardest debts to repay. What's more, Wan Qing hadn't told him with anything like Meng Wan's certainty that she could secure the land.

All things considered, weighing a potential favor-debt—owed at some unknown time, place, and price—and an uncertain outcome against five thousand yuan to wipe out both headaches, James decided it was worth it.

"Deal. Five thousand yuan to secure that land for me."

Seeing him hesitate so long, Meng Wan had thought she'd asked for too much and frightened him off, and had been on the verge of revising her figure.

"Leave it to me. You can set your mind at ease."

"A pleasure doing business." James extended his hand to Meng Wan.

Meng Wan smiled charmingly and clasped it back. "A pleasure doing business."

Both Xu Qiuyun and Lin Dong could scarcely believe it. In the span of those few short minutes, a five-thousand-yuan deal had just been struck in this room. Their admiration for Meng Wan deepened another notch—and at the same time their sense of just how rich James must be grew sharper still.

*

The next day was the weekend. Meng Wan had originally meant to wait until Monday, when Song Qingyang would be back at work, to visit his office, but she truly couldn't wait. This wasn't only tied to her grudge against Xu Tian—most important of all, she genuinely needed that money.

She asked Meng Qingyan for Song Qingyang's home phone number and called first thing in the morning. His mother answered, saying Song Qingyang had come home very late from a wedding banquet the night before and wasn't awake yet.

Meng Wan asked her to pass along a message: have Song Qingyang call her back once he was up.

Song Qingyang's mother had an exceedingly gentle voice. She kept Meng Wan on the line, asking a string of inconsequential questions—what her name was, what her relationship to Song Qingyang was, how long the two of them had known each other, and the like.

Meng Wan had no choice but to answer them one by one. Right before hanging up, the woman even warmly invited Meng Wan to come over to their house sometime, and Meng Wan could only humor her, saying she'd surely pay a visit when she had the chance.

Not a few minutes later, Meng Wan received Song Qingyang's return call. She guessed his mother must have woken him.

Meng Wan said it wasn't convenient to talk over the phone and asked to meet him somewhere. They settled on the Yuxiang Restaurant. Song Qingyang agreed, and they arranged to meet at eleven.

"Auntie, Zhizhi wants to go to the Yuxiang Restaurant too."

It was Sunday, and Sun Jing was just taking Zhizhi back to her own parents' home.

"Next time. Auntie will take you next time." Meng Wan scooped Zhizhi up and bounced her. "Next time Auntie will bring you back some crispy pork in a bowl, all right?"

"Mm!" Zhizhi nodded hard. Crispy pork in a bowl was her absolute favorite.

Sun Jing took Zhizhi from Meng Wan's arms and teased, "Zhizhi, be good now—you mustn't get in the way of Auntie's date."

While dressing Zhizhi earlier with the door open, she'd heard every word of Meng Wan's phone call.

"What date?" For a second Meng Wan didn't catch on.

Cheng Xuehua, combing her hair, said with a grin, "Your date with young Song, of course."

It seemed her matchmaking the other day hadn't been for nothing. She just hadn't expected her daughter to be so forward—calling the house directly to arrange a meeting. At this rate, it looked like she'd have a son-in-law before long, and grandchildren not far behind.

"What on earth are you all talking about?" Meng Wan laughed helplessly. "It's nothing like what you're imagining."

"No need to be shy in front of us," Meng Xingyang put in between bites of his fried dough stick.

Meng Wan flew into a small temper and stamped her foot hard. "Think whatever you like, then." With that she spun around, went back into her room, and shut the door.

Zhizhi's fair little face was full of bewilderment. Blinking her big eyes, she asked, "Mama, why is Auntie angry?"

Sun Jing glanced with a smile at the firmly shut door and raised her voice. "Auntie isn't angry. Auntie's just embarrassed."

Embarrassed? Meng Wan curled her lip scornfully. It was men who got embarrassed around her, not the other way around.

Since she was going to discuss serious business, Meng Wan deliberately changed into a brown suit. Today she wore no hair clip, simply tucking her curls behind her ears to reveal a pair of large, round pearl earrings.

She arrived a bit early on purpose and chose a seat by the window. Today she sat in the very spot James had first taken, and the view from above was indeed lovely.

There was hardly any pollution in this era. The sky was sky and the clouds were clouds, their boundaries crisp and distinct—a clarity almost too inviting to be real.

Song Qingyang arrived right on the dot. He explained that just before leaving he'd received a work call about the land—one of the buyers wanted to meet him that evening to talk. Meng Wan already knew all of this, so he simply laid it out for her.

Meng Wan secretly breathed a huge sigh of relief. It was practically heaven-sent. Had she truly waited until the workday tomorrow to contact Song Qingyang, the other party might well have already wrapped everything up.

Song Qingyang had assumed this was just a casual lunch, so he'd come out in a set of casual sportswear, never expecting Meng Wan across from him to be dressed so formally.

"You didn't call me here just for a meal, did you?"

Meng Wan gave a slight smile. "Let's eat first."

With that she called the waiter over, asked after Song Qingyang's tastes, and ordered three dishes and a soup.

Song Qingyang's curiosity was bursting; he couldn't enjoy the meal without getting to the bottom of it. Tentatively, he ventured a guess. "You're not asking to see me because of that land too, are you?"

Unhurriedly, Meng Wan poured Song Qingyang a cup of tea and pushed it in front of him. "Section Chief Song really is shrewd—seeing through me at a single glance."

So it was exactly that. A trace of disappointment drifted across Song Qingyang's eyes. He'd thought...

"You want to secure that land?" As far as he knew, Meng Wan couldn't possibly come up with such a large sum.

"Not me." Meng Wan waved a hand. "My employer."

"Mr. Chen?"

When had he and Meng Wan become acquainted?

"That's right."

Since it was official business, Song Qingyang's expression turned serious. "The reason I've been wavering between the two of them is precisely Chen Jinghua's foreign nationality."

Bringing in foreign capital was indeed a way to inject fresh vitality into the market, and it was an economic activity the state was currently encouraging.

But for Yuyang to develop as a tourism city, it still needed people who understood this land to build it. He hoped that the bricks and tiles he left behind for future generations would be laid by the people of Yuyang—a legacy with that kind of meaning.

Meng Wan had known about this concern of his in her past life. It was, in fact, one of the key reasons she'd been able to win him over back then.

But this time she stood on the opposing side, arguing the opposite case. She'd made both the argument and the counterargument—it was like one half of her brain wrestling the other.

"I understand your thinking, and I agree with it. This is land I love too, and I want to see it flourish." Meng Wan looked at him earnestly, paused, then added, "Section Chief Song, what's the single most important thing for a city's development?"

"People."

"Exactly. For Yuyang to develop, it absolutely cannot do so without the efforts of every one of its people. So in the end it all comes back to the people. Bring in foreign capital, give the local young people jobs—when young people have prospects, the city can keep flourishing and growing."

Song Qingyang spread his hands, unconvinced. "But the Xu Tian you mentioned could do that too. This doesn't persuade me."

"Being able to do it doesn't mean doing it well."

"What do you mean?"

Song Qingyang couldn't help recalling what Meng Wan had said outside the exam room that day. Before anything had even happened, she'd been certain Xu Tian would burn his bridges. Wasn't this just her own one-sided slander?

He hadn't expected Meng Wan to be the type who'd stop at nothing to get what she wanted. What gave her the right to assume he'd take her word for it on the strength of a one-sided account?

"How about this." Meng Wan made one last push. "Have Mr. Chen and Xu Tian each bring a contract to your office tomorrow. I'm confident that once Section Chief Song sees the contracts, he'll understand the care behind what I'm saying."

Song Qingyang drummed his fingers and thought for a moment. This really was the fairest way to resolve it at the moment.

"Let's do that, then."

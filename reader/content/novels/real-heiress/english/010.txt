Chapter 10

After the visit to the tomb, Meng Wan took James to Bailing Bridge, and then it was time for lunch. Following the map, Lin Dong brought the two of them to a restaurant.

Seeing them get out, he took out his own lunch box, ready to eat his own meal.

To save money, he always brought his lunch from home. Today's was leftover scallion pancakes from the family table the night before. He'd barely cracked the lunch box open when there came a tap-tap on the car window.

He turned to look. It was Meng Wan.

Lin Dong thought she might have forgotten something. He set down his lunch box and glanced into the back seat—it was empty.

Reaching across the passenger side, he rolled down the window and asked with a guileless grin that bared two rows of white teeth, "Sister Wan, what is it?"

Meng Wan bent at the waist, her gaze falling on the still-unopened lunch box. She didn't know what his lunch was, but it surely couldn't be as plentiful as a restaurant meal—and besides, the food had been sitting since morning and was stone cold by now. Eating cold food was bad for the stomach.

"Come on, eat with us."

Lin Dong hesitated a few seconds, then refused. "Sis, I brought my lunch. You and Mr. Chen go in and eat—I'll wait here for you."

Mr. Chen had already paid to hire his car for the day; how could he have any face to let the man treat him to a meal on top of that? Besides, he ate a lot—one of him equaled two of them—and if he ended up making them think he was greedy, they'd hardly say so to his face.

Meng Wan was a straightforward sort. She knew James wasn't stingy, much less someone who'd begrudge the cost of one meal. If you could eat well, why wouldn't you?

"Grab your lunch box and get out here, quick," Meng Wan threatened. "Otherwise I won't use your car tomorrow."

That was Lin Dong's soft spot.

Sure enough, the moment Lin Dong heard that Meng Wan meant to hire his car again tomorrow, his face lit up. Worst case, he'd just eat a little less, then sneak his own pancakes later to fill up.

Watching a man over six feet tall cradle a lunch box barely bigger than his hand made for a comical picture. How did he manage such a vast gap between his actual character and his appearance?

When Meng Wan brought Lin Dong in, James was already studying the menu on the wall. Hearing them, he turned to look, and Meng Wan explained, "I asked Lin Dong to join us—you don't mind, do you?"

"Of course not." James pulled out the chair beside him. "Please, sit."

Lin Dong wasn't used to anyone being so courteous to him, and he muttered a thank-you back to James.

The place was a noodle house famous for its mutton-soup braised noodles, with its roast lamb ribs a specialty too. Meng Wan asked each of them about food preferences and ordered three bowls of mutton-soup braised noodles and a plate of roast lamb ribs from the waiter.

The moment she'd walked in she'd caught the aroma of the roast lamb ribs—the richness of the mutton fat mingled with the scent of charcoal fire—and her stomach was already growling.

While they waited for the food, Meng Wan pointed at Lin Dong's lunch box and asked what good things he had inside.

A little bashfully, Lin Dong opened it. "Just a few scallion pancakes my mom made."

Meng Wan's eyes lit up at once, and she asked without the slightest ceremony, "Can I have a taste?"

Lin Dong froze for a moment, unsure whether to let her or not. These were cold, day-old pancakes—it was fine for him to eat them himself, but giving them to someone else just didn't seem right.

Afraid Meng Wan would think he was unwilling, Lin Dong hurried to explain, "These have already gone cold—I'm worried they'll upset your stomach."

"It's fine, I'll just have one bite."

Ever since she'd been thrown out of the family in her past life, Meng Wan hadn't eaten scallion pancakes in ages. The instant the lid came off, that faint, savory scent of scallion oil coaxed the greedy worm right out of her belly.

Seeing she really did want some, Lin Dong had no choice but to push the box over to her. Meng Wan immediately picked up her chopsticks and pinched off a small piece. Delicious, sure enough—fresh out of the pan it would be even better.

Meng Wan wasn't one to enjoy good things all by herself. She turned to James and asked, "James, do you want to try some?"

James pressed his lips together and shook his head. "No thank you."

He had a great many allergies, so he never touched any food whose ingredients he didn't know.

Meng Wan had known he wouldn't eat any—she'd only asked to be polite—but afraid of wounding Lin Dong's pride, she explained to Lin Dong in dialect, "Foreigners are all like this; they won't just eat other people's food, because their skin is delicate and they get allergies easily. You know what allergies are—it's when you break out in red bumps all over."

Once, her ex-husband's hotel had had a foreign guest who'd accidentally eaten peanuts and been rushed to the hospital; in the end they'd paid out a hefty sum to settle the matter. That was the first time she'd ever even heard of the concept of allergies.

Lin Dong nodded. He knew about allergies—Hu Xianglan in his courtyard was allergic to pollen, and he'd once taken her to the hospital himself.

James said nothing, watching the two of them talk in dialect. He very much wanted to tell Meng Wan that he actually understood it, because his mother and grandmother were both from Yuyang and always spoke Yuyang dialect at home; he couldn't speak it himself, but he could understand it.

But he thought this was rather amusing. After all, he'd never said he couldn't understand—she'd simply assumed he couldn't, so it couldn't really count as deceit.

*

After lunch, the group set off on the afternoon's itinerary.

The two morning stops had leaned toward history; the afternoon was more about sightseeing—strolling, pausing, taking photos—so Meng Wan didn't have to explain much, and the afternoon passed quickly.

It was already nightfall by the time they got back to the hotel, with only the occasional car threading through the streets. Lin Dong brought the car to a smooth stop at the hotel entrance. Before getting out, Meng Wan sneezed three times in a row, and Lin Dong reminded her to dress more warmly tomorrow—the nights were still quite cold.

Meng Wan nodded, though she didn't feel cold herself; she only felt an eerie chill down her back, as if someone were talking about her behind her back.

Lin Dong asked how she planned to get home and whether she needed a ride back. Meng Wan said she'd come by bike and there was no need.

She took the token the doorman had given her that morning, meaning to go fetch her bike, when she saw James exchange a few words with the doorman at the entrance and come back.

"You'll ride home in Lin Dong's car, with your bike in the trunk."

It was a bit late getting back; a girl riding a bike alone at night truly wasn't safe.

"It's all right." Meng Wan waved a hand. "It's only ten-odd minutes by bike."

"Mr. Chen, here's the bicycle." Having pocketed a generous tip during the day, the doorman was being especially friendly to James.

James looked at Lin Dong, who'd rolled down his window, and gestured toward the back. "Open the trunk—Miss Meng's riding home with you."

Lin Dong had been hoping for just this. He pressed the button at once, got out to help the doorman lift the bike in, and lashed it down tightly with rope.

Seeing this, Meng Wan couldn't very well keep refusing—it was, after all, a kindness on their part.

She was just about to get in when, turning, she caught out of the corner of her eye a man and a woman climbing out of a car, the man with his arm around the woman as they walked toward the hotel. Even reduced to ashes, she'd have known that man's silhouette anywhere.

"Stop right there!" Meng Wan shouted at the man's back, pulling the cry up from her core, the veins at her temple bulging in an instant.

Everyone was stunned by that powerful, booming voice, and they all turned, bewildered, to follow the sound.

The man's body went rigid. He immediately dropped the arm around the woman and quickened his pace inside, pretending he hadn't heard.

"Xu Tian!" Meng Wan shouted as she snatched a wrench from Lin Dong's trunk. "Take one more step and see if I don't smash your car to bits!"

The man did stop in his tracks. After a long pause he turned reluctantly to face her, walking toward Meng Wan with a face plastered with ingratiating smiles. "Xiao Wan, what are you doing here?"

Just the sight of him made Meng Wan sick. She pointed the wrench at the empty patch of ground ahead. "Don't come any closer. Stand right there and don't move!"

Cowed by Meng Wan's force of presence, Xu Tian froze on the spot, not daring to budge.

Seeing that familiar face, James couldn't help knitting his brows. What was Meng Wan to this man?

Meng Wan glanced at the woman slowly turning to face them. Wasn't this his "cousin," Sun Keke? So the two of them had already gotten tangled up together by this point!

In her past life, Xu Tian had told her this was his cousin and that he wanted to make money together with her, so he'd arranged a position for her at the hotel. Later, after Meng Wan and Xu Tian divorced, she'd gone back to get her clothes and walked in on the two of them in the act in her own bedroom. Only then had she realized: there was no cousin—the woman had been his lover all along, trailing after him the whole time.

At the time Meng Wan had assumed they'd gotten together after the marriage. It seemed it had started even earlier than she'd imagined—what a devoted pair. So this was his true love, then.

"Xiao Wan, let me explain—"

Meng Wan gave a cold laugh. Explain? Weasel his way out, more like. She cut him off flatly. "I have eyes—I can see perfectly well. You don't need to rack your brains for excuses. You want her to be your cousin, she's your cousin; you want her to be your girlfriend, she's your girlfriend. It has nothing to do with me. Don't ever contact me again. I don't want to see you."

"Xiao Wan!" Xu Tian stepped forward, reaching to grab her. "It's not what you think."

Afraid Meng Wan might get hurt, James and Lin Dong both stepped up. James, being closer, planted himself directly in front of her, while Lin Dong halted behind her.

By the look of things, Meng Wan must be this man's girlfriend, and she'd just witnessed her own boyfriend cheating with her own eyes—and James happened to be able to confirm that her boyfriend really had wronged her.

A moment ago James had been in the shadows and Xu Tian hadn't seen his face clearly; now, watching him appear so suddenly, the memories tied to that face came rushing back.

Wasn't this the foreigner who'd complained about him last night? What was he doing here?

Xu Tian's eyes darted back and forth between James and Meng Wan. Could these two have gotten together behind his back? So Meng Wan was trying to turn the tables and pin the blame on him!

"Well, Meng Wan, you've got some nerve—cuckolding me and then trying to flip it around and blame me. I nearly fell for it!"

James realized he might have done more harm than good. Anything he said now would only make matters worse, so to keep Meng Wan from being put on the back foot, he chose to keep quiet and wait for her to speak.

Meng Wan could only marvel at how blind she'd been. How had she ever fallen for him back then?

This was nothing but a hypocritical, sanctimonious, fickle, two-faced, use-and-discard bastard!

"Whoever's got a guilty conscience knows it. Your aim in getting close to me was never pure in the first place. I was just a fool to believe you really cared about me. Watching me be toyed with in the palm of your hand—you must have been very pleased with yourself, weren't you?"

Xu Tian was momentarily blinded by rage—then remembered he still needed something from Meng Wan. The hard line he'd just taken softened again. "Xiao Wan, don't let this man bewitch you. I'm the one who loves you most. As long as you break up with him right now, in front of me, I won't even hold against you whatever's happened between the two of you. We can go back to the way we were before—all right?"

Meng Wan ignored him and tipped her chin toward Sun Keke. "What do you think? If he and I get together, you become the other woman all over again. Sneaking around just to share a hotel room, never able to bring him home for the holidays, the 'father' line left blank when you have a child—are you really happy? Is this really what you want?"

The barb struck home. The woman standing not far from Xu Tian quietly lowered her head. Seeing no reaction, Meng Wan administered another dose of strong medicine. "And don't go dreaming that once he's used me up and cast me aside, you'll get to move up. Without me there'll be a Zhang Wan, a Li Wan, a Wang Wan—anyone but you."

"Enough!" His little scheme exposed, Xu Tian cut in anxiously.

Meng Wan ignored his existence and took a step closer. "Someone said to me today that other people's love is always conditional, and if you want their love, you can create the conditions yourself. Right now your condition is being young and pretty, so you've taken the shortcut—but that road has an end. Only by creating a condition that makes people love you forever, a condition no one can take from you, can you keep them from looking down on you or throwing you away."

James watched Meng Wan's profile in silence, and he saw on her face no contempt for the other woman—on the contrary, she was trying to save her.

The woman across from her still said nothing, just like Meng Wan herself the night before: silent, an endless silence.

It wasn't that she didn't know Meng Wan spoke the truth, but deep down she still clung to a sliver of illusion—the illusion that one day the man could marry her openly and bring her home. Today that last thread of hope was finally severed.

"I used to imagine how it would go if you ever caught me," the woman said with a self-mocking smile. "I thought you'd give me a beating. Meng Wan, you really are an interesting woman."

With that, Sun Keke cast a meaningful look at Xu Tian and turned resolutely to leave.

"Keke." Xu Tian called after her retreating figure, unwilling to let her go.

Meng Wan watched Sun Keke walk away, then fixed her gaze back on Xu Tian, swinging the wrench in her hand as she stepped forward.

"I don't hit women. I save my fists for scum like you—the kind who keeps a foot in two boats!"

Seeing the look of her, Xu Tian's nerve faltered and he backed up a few steps, bucking himself up inside. It was broad daylight—Meng Wan surely wouldn't dare actually hit him. "Meng Wan, think this through. If something happens to me, you won't get off easy either!"

Afraid Meng Wan might really act on impulse and hurt the man—which would cost her far more than it was worth—Lin Dong made to step in and pull her back, only for James to block him with a shake of his head.

He trusted that Meng Wan wouldn't do anything foolish.

A wicked little smile curved Meng Wan's lips. She couldn't hit the man—but she could certainly hit his car. She remembered that Xu Tian had this car smuggled in from Hong Kong; today she'd make him swallow the loss in silence, with no way to complain.

She circled around to the car window, gripped the wrench tight in both hands, drew a deep breath, and—imagining the glass was Xu Tian—swung with all her might.

With a crash, the intact window shattered in an instant, bursting apart as brilliantly as fireworks.

Before Xu Tian could even react, another crash followed.

Xu Tian shouted in a panic, "Stop it!" He was about to rush forward and stop Meng Wan when there came yet another crash.

He instinctively stepped back, throwing up both arms to shield his face from the flying shards of glass.

"Stop smashing it, stop!"

Crash!

In the blink of an eye, all four windows had met a heroic end.

After shattering four windows in a row, Meng Wan was a little out of breath. "You got off cheap. We're square now."

With that, Meng Wan spun around to meet James's admiring gaze and Lin Dong's horrified one, broke into a crescent-moon smile, gave the two of them a playful wink, and pressed the wrench into Lin Dong's hand.

Well? Conquered by your big sister, aren't you?

"I'm calling the police!" Xu Tian raged, hysterical in his impotent fury.

Meng Wan didn't even look back. "Then let's see whether the police arrest me or you," she said indifferently.

And with that, Meng Wan smugly yanked open the car door and got in.

Lin Dong, savoring the moment, followed her in. Afraid Xu Tian might chase after them, he floored the gas and tore off down the long hotel street.

His face ashen, Xu Tian stared at the carpet of shattered glass, both fists clenched, and let out a furious roar in the direction Meng Wan had vanished, even throwing a flurry of punches at the empty air.

Looking at the mess all over the ground, James couldn't help the corners of his mouth lifting, his mood turning bright. It looked like he'd get a good night's sleep tonight.

Xu Tian shot him a venomous glare, as if he wanted to swallow whole anything connected to Meng Wan, James included.

James paid his look no mind. Hands in his pockets, he strode over to the front desk, drew fifty yuan from his wallet, and handed it to the manager. "Sorry for the trouble. Please be careful when you clean up."

The manager looked at the floor of broken glass left for her, her face still drawn—but the moment she saw the bill James held out, a smile leapt to her cheeks. Taking the money with both hands, she said with a chuckle, "It's no trouble at all, no trouble at all."

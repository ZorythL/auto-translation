Chapter 65

After that little interlude, Meng Wan went on busying herself with the agency's affairs. Everyone else was run off their feet, but the two guides handling foreign tourists had things relatively easy, so Meng Wan had them keep practicing their spoken English. In a few days the first official foreign tour group, introduced through James, would set off for Yuyang.

This group was made up of James's classmates. They were all intensely curious about the historical sites of Yuyang that James had described, and wanted to come see them for themselves.

In the past, because no one at the agency spoke English—and because foreign tour groups like this were usually received directly by the big agencies, never reaching the likes of them—this counted as the first time Meng Wan's agency would officially host a foreign tour group.

Meng Wan had heard before that receiving foreign tour groups required filing paperwork in advance, so while she had the time she went to the Tourism Bureau to ask, and only then discovered she'd overlooked a major formality.

Earlier that year, Yuyang City had issued a new regulation requiring all agencies intending to receive foreign visitors to obtain a Foreign Tourist Permit; otherwise they didn't have the qualifications to receive foreign tourists. Soliciting and receiving overseas tours without it counted as acting without authorization, which violated the rules—and once discovered, it carried the risk of having one's business license and guide certifications revoked.

The Tourism Bureau staff explained that this permit had to be processed at the Industry and Commerce Bureau. In addition, a foreign tour group had to have its reception plan approved by the Tourism Bureau through a domestic agency, after which—armed with the Tour Group Reception Plan, plus the members' passports and visa information—they had to file with public-security border inspection and the culture-and-tourism department.

The most complicated part of all this was processing the permit—it couldn't be done in under ten days to half a month. But with the tourists set to arrive next week, Meng Wan didn't want to leave a gaping hole and hand such a wonderful opportunity to someone else—especially when these were James's friends.

Upon learning this dreadful news, she came out of the agency with a face so long it nearly dragged to the ground, her steps heavy as if weighed down by a thousand pounds.

"Meng Wan?" Zhou Quan planted both feet down and stopped his bicycle. "You here at the Tourism Bureau on business?"

Seeing how preoccupied she looked, she must have run into some thorny problem.

Meng Wan looked toward the voice and answered dully, "Section Chief Zhou, I came to take care of something."

Zhou Quan parked his bike in the shed and strode over to her, asking carefully what was the matter. After all, Meng Wan was Song Qingyang's friend, and this was the Tourism Bureau—his word here carried at least a little weight, and maybe he could help her.

Meng Wan heaved a heavy sigh and laid out the whole situation honestly. Three cobblers with their wits combined could out-think Zhuge Liang—maybe he'd have some idea.

Zhou Quan led her to the shade beneath a tree, where the birds chirped away nonstop.

He put his hands on his hips and thought it over seriously, then suggested, "I think Deputy Mayor Wang has a good impression of you. You could go ask him to help speed up the process."

Meng Wan drew her gaze back from the dappled patches of light filtering through the leaves on the ground, lifted her eyes to him, and said, "His impression probably is decent. But Deputy Mayor Wang is such an upright, incorruptible man—would he pull strings for me through the back door?"

For a brief moment she hadn't not thought of finding someone to help, but Deputy Mayor Wang's stern, imposing manner made her flinch just thinking about it.

"This doesn't count as going through the back door—at most it's special handling for a special case. After all, you gave Deputy Mayor Wang such a valuable report last time. I don't believe he'd refuse you this favor. Besides, just bring out that silver tongue of yours from the meeting that day, and this is sure to work out."

Through his job, Zhou Quan had dealt with Deputy Mayor Wang many times and had a rough sense of the man's character. Though he looked stern on the surface, at heart he was warm-hearted; when it came to matters that genuinely helped ordinary people, he'd surely take them up.

Meng Wan certainly counted as an ordinary person—and one contributing to Yuyang's economic development at that. There was no reason Deputy Mayor Wang wouldn't help.

Listening to Zhou Quan's encouragement, Meng Wan slowly felt her courage filling back up. Whether it worked or not, as long as there was a chance she had to try. She broke into a brilliant grin. "Section Chief Zhou, could I trouble you to take me there?"

——

Zhou Quan often went to the municipal administration building for meetings and to report on his work, so the gate guard was already familiar with his face. After a few questions, they were let in.

The moment they entered the building, they spotted Secretary Xiao Liu carrying a stack of materials, about to head upstairs. Meng Wan immediately called out to stop him.

Secretary Xiao Liu paused mid-step and turned to look at the two of them, puzzled. "Section Chief Zhou, Comrade Meng Wan—how come you two are here together?"

Meng Wan hurried forward to explain why she'd come. Secretary Xiao Liu said Deputy Mayor Wang was in a meeting; he was on his way up to deliver some materials, so she should wait here for a bit and he'd take her to the office to wait afterward.

It really was easier to get things done when you knew people. With Secretary Xiao Liu's help, Meng Wan thanked Zhou Quan and told him to go on back to his work—she'd take it from here.

After Zhou Quan left, Meng Wan was shown by Secretary Xiao Liu to Deputy Mayor Wang's office. Uncharacteristically demure, she sat in one corner of the sofa with both hands resting on her knees, swiftly marshaling her words in her head.

She hadn't yet figured out how to put it when Deputy Mayor Wang came back together with Secretary Xiao Liu. He'd originally been about to head out, but hearing from Secretary Xiao Liu that Meng Wan was here to see him—and since he had a decent impression of the young woman—he was willing to spare a quarter of an hour to hear her out.

"Deputy Mayor Wang." Meng Wan stood up at once, showing a rare hint of timidity.

Wang Zhongxian made a downward pressing gesture. "Sit, sit and talk."

With that he settled into the head seat, took the tea Secretary Xiao Liu offered, and began to drink.

"Deputy Mayor Wang." Meng Wan wrung her hands, faltering. "I came today to, to..."

Secretary Xiao Liu saw her nervousness, poured a cup of water and handed it to her, giving her an encouraging smile at the same time.

Meng Wan took the cup, shot Secretary Xiao Liu a grateful glance, and felt the tension in her chest ease a little.

Wang Zhongxian set down his cup and, dropping his usual sternness, looked at her with a kindly smile. "Weren't you quite the talker before—bold enough to give the city bureau your suggestions right to my face? How come you've gone mute now?"

Meng Wan set down her cup, drew a deep breath, straightened her spine, and said gravely, "Deputy Mayor Wang, I'd like to ask your help in specially approving a procedure."

Meng Wan laid out the whole matter from the Tourism Bureau for Wang Zhongxian, hoping he might have a word with the Industry and Commerce Bureau to get her permit approved quickly.

The thing Wang Zhongxian hated most was people asking him to pull strings; in the past he'd refused every such request without exception. But Meng Wan's matter was something she could accomplish even without his help—it would just take a bit longer.

Right now it was peak tourist season, and the whole city was working to receive visitors. The bilingual signage was already being rushed into production and would soon be in place. The city had never had enough foreign-language guides to begin with—he'd personally gone to the foreign-languages department to recruit for this very purpose.

So having one more agency, Meng Wan's, able to receive foreign tourists could only benefit Yuyang's economic development at this moment. This favor he'd definitely grant.

For Wang Zhongxian, this matter was just a phone call away. But once he opened this door, he was afraid others would swarm to him too, so he asked Meng Wan to promise that this would stay between the two of them and not get out.

Meng Wan promised over and over that she'd keep her lips sealed. She wasn't Qian Zhenyu—she didn't go blabbing everything about.

Deputy Mayor Wang phoned the leader at the Industry and Commerce Bureau then and there. The other end agreed to expedite it—she'd send the materials over today, and they'd try to have it done by tomorrow afternoon.

It really was easier to get things done when you had people, Meng Wan mused silently.

Leaving Deputy Mayor Wang's office, Meng Wan rushed straight back to the agency without a moment's pause to get the materials the Industry and Commerce Bureau needed in order, then went charging over there. The staff handling it had clearly already received word from above and, without wasting a second, slotted her into the expedited queue.

The next afternoon, when Meng Wan went back, the permit was ready. Carrying these documents, she went to the Tourism Bureau again to file. She'd run around for a solid day, and pushed every single thing through. At last her agency could receive foreign tour groups legitimately and aboveboard.

Watching everything fall into place, Meng Wan felt an inexplicable sense of ease in her heart.

That afternoon, the office was completely empty. Meng Wan, the manager, doubled as the cleaning crew. Lately everyone had been run off their feet—Old Bi and Xu Qiuyun, besides doing the accounts, also had to handle distributing the ads—so the agency hadn't been cleaned in a long while.

Meng Wan finished mopping the floor, washed the mop, and hung it out to dry against the wall facing the street, then hopped on her bike and headed for Xiao Hai's home.

Qin Yi'e and Zhou Da had returned home not long ago after their surgeries and were recuperating at home now. Meng Wan went to the market, bought some fresh vegetables and meat to visit the sick, and brought along some things she'd specially prepared to "pass the time."

The first time she'd visited Xiao Hai's home, the place had been full of the cluttered chaos of bachelor men. This time it was completely transformed—Qin Yi'e had tidied the whole home spotless, full of warmth and life, with plenty of new things added. It felt very cozy.

The moment Qin Yi'e saw her, she started clamoring to go home. Living here with nothing to do all day, she said, she'd practically gone stir-crazy from idleness—she'd only feel settled back in her hometown, feet on the soil and hands snapping ears of corn.

Zhou Da, however, wouldn't hear of it, snorting indignantly. "Living so comfortably here, why on earth would you want to go back to that little mountain gully? You want to go, you go—I'm not going back."

Meng Wan knew Qin Yi'e was just bored and needed something to do, so she pulled out the red thread and pattern sheets she'd bought and told Qin Yi'e and Zhou Da to braid red cords in their spare time—twenty cents apiece.

The instant she heard each cord earned twenty cents, Qin Yi'e stopped grumbling, grabbed the thread, and asked Meng Wan to teach her how to braid it. Working from the pattern, Meng Wan taught her for a whole afternoon, and at last Qin Yi'e graduated.

Zhou Da, after years cooped up at home, had finally made it to the big city and long since hit it off with the neighbors; by now he was already out playing chess.

Meng Wan stayed busy until evening, leaving only after eating the meal Qin Yi'e cooked. She also gave Qin Yi'e Qin Yizhong's address, telling her to drop by for a visit whenever she got bored.

The busy days of summer flew by. Meng Wan also threw herself into the ranks of the guides, leading several boutique small groups and giving them an in-depth tour of Yuyang.

The first month, tourists came in an endless stream; later, after the other agencies copied their brochure, things leveled off into a steady period. Over this one summer, the guides of Youth Travel Agency took practically no days off, fighting on the front lines the whole time.

By the time summer ended, every one of them had tanned a shade darker.

When everyone had finished the last group on their hands, Meng Wan gathered all the guides in that little agency office to hold the one major ceremony everyone cared about most—the dividing-up-the-money assembly!

Chapter 66

Meng Wan had bought two standing floor fans for the agency, but they'd hardly been used all summer. Now both were switched on at once, one on either side, tirelessly swinging their heads back and forth.

She'd also prepared watermelon and popsicles for everyone. The watermelons were soaking in a bucket of water; just now Xiao Hai had pulled out a cool one, cut it into chunks, and handed them around.

Meng Wan held a sheaf of papers rolled into a cylinder, tapping it lightly against the crook of her other arm.

"Eat up, everyone—let me say a few words."

Hearing that the manager was about to speak, everyone looked up as one, the sweet juice of the watermelon bursting in their mouths putting them in an even better mood.

"First of all, thank you all for your hard work these past two months. Every drop of sweat you've shed will be rewarded." Meng Wan launched into her summing-up speech. "Over these two months, our agency achieved a perfect record of no days off for anyone. Each of you stuck diligently to your post, and in the end you pushed our agency's earnings to a record high, while also making a tremendous contribution to Yuyang's tourism."

Luo Zhigang chuckled. "Manager Meng, is the next step handing out the pay?"

This was the topic on everyone's mind. For these two months they'd left early and come home late, battling on the front lines every day—all so they could earn a big haul. Now the moment of final reckoning had finally arrived, and everyone looked at Meng Wan with anticipation written all over their faces.

Meng Wan's brows lifted. Putting on a mock-serious air, she cleared her throat, unrolled the papers in her hand, and said, "These papers are a performance sheet I drew up based on the groups you've all led over this period. They spell out in detail how many groups each of you led and what the commission was on each. I'll hand them out for you to look over in a moment—check carefully whether anything's been left off. If it all looks right, Old Bi will hand out the money to everyone on the spot."

Xu Qiuyun took the stack of papers from Meng Wan's hand and distributed them one by one according to the names—it felt a bit like getting back an exam paper back in school days.

Just as Meng Wan had expected, after everyone saw their commission slip, each broke into a smile—some reserved, some unabashed.

The one who'd taken the most groups and turned the most profit was Jiang Ke. The young man was sharp and quick-witted, with a smooth way of talking that kept the tourists happy. One auntie had even insisted on introducing her daughter to him—it came to nothing in the end, but she did go and buy a jade Buddha at Old Bai's jade workshop.

Lan Xiuli and Yu Fei did well too. Because their clients were foreign, they spent more freely and even had a tipping habit, so even though the two of them led fewer groups, their profits were no worse.

At the bottom were Liu Fei and Xiao Hai with their inspection tours. They'd actually started out receiving three inspection groups, but when the numbers ran short, they'd switched over to tourist groups. Meng Wan had already submitted the application paperwork for the inspection-tour subsidies, but it'd likely be a while before the money came through, so for now their commission was a bit less than the others'.

"Besides all this," Meng Wan went on to announce, "I've also prepared a bonus for each of you. It's my way of recognizing how well every one of you has performed lately—just a small token of my appreciation."

Jiang Ke's eyes lit up, and he sprang to his feet, thrilled. "Really? How much?"

Yu Fei gave his shin a swat, a look of disdain on her face—who asked something like that to someone's face?

Meng Wan didn't mind. Smiling, she flashed a "peace" sign.

Luo Zhigang said in surprise, "Two hundred?"

Some people's monthly salary was only three hundred, and here Meng Wan was, generously handing out a two-hundred-yuan bonus to each of them—a rare thing even among bosses.

Truth be told, Meng Wan had agonized between one hundred and two hundred, and in the end had gritted her teeth and gone with two hundred. Not counting herself, her agency had eight people; two hundred apiece came to sixteen hundred—no small sum.

But the most important thing for a leader was to instill loyalty in the staff, so they'd take the job seriously, which could only benefit the agency's future development. So Meng Wan reckoned that sixteen hundred was money well spent.

Meng Wan had Accountant Bi prepare an envelope for each person and hand them out one by one.

Xu Qiuyun opened her envelope and stared in surprise at the money inside—more than double what she'd expected. "Manager Meng, you've given me too much, haven't you?"

"It's not too much. You and Old Bi have been doing the ads and the back-office support lately—that's basically two jobs' worth of work. You've more than earned this bit of pay."

Old Bi hurriedly thanked Meng Wan with a beaming smile.

From the back row, Jiang Ke said excitedly, "Thank you, Manager! I can take this money home and use it to cover my little brother and sister's school fees!"

Jiang Ke had come from a farming village on the outskirts of Yuyang, with a younger brother and sister still at home. As the eldest son, he'd been put through university by the whole family's combined efforts, so during his school years he'd taken on part-time work too, all to ease the family's burden.

"Good big brother." Meng Wan gave Jiang Ke a thumbs-up. "That education wasn't wasted on you. Keep working hard from here on out—aim to make a home in the city and bring your whole family over to enjoy the good life."

"I'll work my hardest!" Jiang Ke answered, brimming with drive.

After Old Bi finished handing out the agency's pay, he passed Meng Wan the salary envelopes for the jade workshop, the vehicle fleet, and the restaurant as well.

The day before, he and Meng Wan had worked late into the small hours settling everyone's wages. These old bones of his hadn't been this worn out in a long time—but seeing everyone so happy to receive their pay today, he figured the exhaustion was worth it.

"Peak tourist season is over now, and the weather's gradually cooling. Weekend tourists will make up the majority from here on, so over the next while, once everyone's wrapped up the work on hand, start rotating through your days off according to the duty schedule on the second sheet I gave you. Of course, if you don't want to rest, I won't force you—the more you do, the more you earn."

——

That afternoon, Meng Wan went to the vehicle fleet to pay Lin Dong and the others, and made trips to the jade shop and the restaurant too.

In the materials Meng Wan had submitted to the Industry and Commerce Bureau, she'd given the restaurant a name: "Yuyang's Finest Eatery." The name sounded grand, with a period flavor and an air of authority. She'd even come up with the slogan: "Your first meal in Yuyang—a must at Yuyang's Finest Eatery."

Once that slogan got out there, any tourist coming to Yuyang would be sure to have a meal at the Finest Eatery.

A month earlier, Sun Jing had resigned from the school, finally making up her mind to set out on the path of business. Over this past month, with tourist numbers hitting their peak, the restaurant had been so busy receiving guests it could barely keep up.

Like Meng Wan, Sun Jing now left early and came home late every day. Meng Xingyang had privately grumbled to Meng Wan more than once, saying that now that she had her own career she'd stopped caring about the family—not cooking, not doing the laundry, the child left to her grandmother to raise.

Meng Wan gave Meng Xingyang a stern talking-to. Everyone had the right to choose how they wanted to live their life. Sun Jing had made her own choice and was bearing its consequences, so no one had any business standing on moral high ground to criticize her.

As for the housework—anyone with two hands could do it. There was no rule anywhere that housework had to be done by women, and no rule anywhere that a child had to be raised by its mother.

When she received her salary, Sun Jing's eyes brimmed with tears. She cradled the envelope in both trembling hands—this was the first paycheck she'd earned since resigning. Before this she'd pictured failure countless times in her mind: losing her school job and having the restaurant's business flounder too, busting her back for nothing and ending up with nothing to show for it.

But ever since she'd started at the restaurant, there'd been an unending stream of customers every single day—not just tourists, but plenty of repeat local diners who'd tasted Master Hai's cooking. When it got busy, there wasn't a spare moment all day even for a sip of water.

This gave her tremendous confidence. Though the work was tiring, she was full of drive—she was determined to get her daughter into a home with a bedroom all her own!

With summer over, Lin Xi was about to embark on a new chapter of her life too. With excellent marks, she'd been admitted to a medical school in the capital. The day the acceptance letter arrived, Hao Xiangping clutched it and wept the whole day before the spirit tablet of her late husband, Lin Xinhai. She'd finally lived up to his charge and raised both children safely into adulthood.

Hao Xiangping laid out a banquet and invited all the neighbors from the whole compound to celebrate Lin Xi's admission. Meng Wan was among the guests invited.

Zhou Nian's two-month small-class tutoring had wrapped up smoothly too. Lin Xi gave her a test paper covering the two months' worth of material, and the results weren't half bad—as long as she listened carefully in class once school started and put in some extra effort afterward, she'd surely be able to catch up.

With Sun Jing's help, the two daughters of Qin Yizhong and Ding Chunling also got their Yuyang school registrations sorted out. Because Ding Chunling was a local, the registrations went fairly smoothly.

Qin Yizhong was getting the hang of the renovation crew, and had already put together a little crew of his own. Ding Chunling, meanwhile, had taken the souvenir-selling work well in hand. The whole family had more or less put down roots in Yuyang.

In a few more days it would be Old Bi's retirement ceremony. Over these past days Meng Wan had been discussing with Xu Qiuyun how to throw Old Bi a send-off party, so that this old comrade who'd worked half his life could have a dignified, warmhearted farewell.

The renovation of James's hotel was in its final stages too, so he'd been swamped lately. The last time the two of them had seen each other was at the Finest Eatery, snatching a hurried meal together.

Having finished her work for the day, Meng Wan decided to drop by James's hotel and check in on the progress.

The renovation crew had had a master craftsman calculate the timing, and the best completion date would be a week out, so everyone had been putting in overtime to push the work along these past few days. Meng Wan had Master Hai make twenty boxed meals and bought a few big watermelons, bringing them all to the work site to reward the crew. Once the renovation was done, she'd treat everyone to a big feast at the restaurant.

Meng Wan had been by a few times before, so most of the people at the site recognized her.

She'd barely gotten out of the car when a familiar-looking young fellow recognized her.

"Sister Xiao Meng."

Meng Wan waved at him. "There's an extra meal for everyone in the trunk. Round up a few people to carry it down and pass it out."

The moment the young fellow heard there was food, he scampered off delightedly to call people, and in less than a minute had hollered together four or five of them to haul the food in from the trunk.

James came striding out of the hotel, a yellow hard hat on his head, having just finished his rounds. His face lit up with delight at the sight of Meng Wan, and he walked toward her in long strides. "How'd you find the time to come?"

Meng Wan dangled a special set meal she'd had Master Hai make just for him in front of his face, smiling sweetly. "Came to bring my dear boyfriend a meal, of course."

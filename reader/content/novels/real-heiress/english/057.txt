Chapter 57

Meng Wan laid out her plan for Wang Zhongxian. It required him to play along in a little piece of theater with her: stick to the original meeting program, sternly criticize the agency in front of everyone, then announce its punishment, while Meng Wan partnered with him in a double act to achieve his original aim.

This wasn't out of the question. Wang Zhongxian could pretend he knew nothing of the matter and penalize them in the original manner. The trouble was, once Xue Guangming's apology letter hit the papers, everyone would know the truth, and the importance everyone attached to the punishment of Meng Wan's agency would surely fall far short of before.

Keeping him in suspense, Meng Wan said, "Don't worry, leave the rest to me. I guarantee that from this time on they won't dare reoffend."

Watching Meng Wan's mysterious yet utterly assured air, Wang Zhongxian decided to give this little lady a chance and see how young people did things these days.

Those attending the meeting this time were all heads of travel agencies. According to Old Bi, the tourism bureau organized a meeting like this every year, aimed at developing Yuyang's tourism industry with all their might—issuing new regulations for everyone to study in spirit and then carry back and pass along.

In the meeting room stood a long wooden table. As soon as everyone came in they found their seats, and the only one left empty was that single chair at the very end, sitting there in lonely, somewhat forlorn isolation.

Meng Wan looked around the room, then sat down in this seat that everyone had specially left for her—out of consideration for her as a junior.

A middle-aged woman seated not far from her, finding her face unfamiliar, asked in a low voice, "Which agency are you from?"

Without the slightest hesitation Meng Wan announced her name. The moment the woman heard it, her expression changed three times over, her voice instantly rising an octave as she blurted out in a shrill, piercing tone, "You're from Youth Travel Agency?"

That cry made the whole room turn its head as one to look, and a murmur of gossip about Meng Wan rustled up through the meeting room in an instant.

"That's the agency whose guide got caught red-handed."

"They got splashed all over the newspaper—if it were me I wouldn't have the face to show up at this meeting."

"Exactly. If it weren't for their agency, none of us would have to come to this meeting today. It's plain as day—they're going to make an example of someone to scare the rest."

In one ear and out the other—Meng Wan kept a big smile on her face as she faced the crowd, thinking quietly to herself: go ahead and speak your minds a while longer. There'll be a moment soon enough that has you crying.

With a creak, the meeting room door was pushed open, and the room fell silent at once, every head swinging back around.

In first came the tourism bureau leaders—five in all, headed by the bureau chief, Qi Hongwei. One of them, rather younger, cast a pointedly meaningful glance at Meng Wan the instant he came in.

Meng Wan guessed this had to be Song Qingyang's friend, Zhou Quan. Song Qingyang had said he'd had a word with his friend, and that he would speak up for her during the meeting.

Deputy Mayor Wang came in last and sat down in the center, with the leaders ranged across the front rows.

Qi Hongwei cleared his throat and spoke first. "We've gathered you all here today because our Yuyang tourism industry has recently seen a serious incident—a guide forced tourists to spend and even came to blows with them. The leadership above takes this very seriously, so today Deputy Mayor Wang, who oversees our tourism industry, has come in person to hold this meeting for us. Let's welcome him."

With that, Qi Hongwei led off the applause himself, and the rest immediately picked up his rhythm, clapping in a clatter.

Wang Zhongxian, the picture of composure, pressed his hand downward in the air to signal everyone to stop, and said in a deep voice, "First, thank you all for taking time out of your busy schedules to attend this meeting of ours. I'm sure you've all heard that recently, here in Yuyang, there was an incident of a guide forcing tourists to spend. The impact of this matter can only be called appalling—it has seriously damaged the image of Yuyang's tourism industry. So gathering you all together today is precisely to purge the bad practices that exist within Yuyang's tourism industry, to sound the alarm for all our colleagues in the tourism trade, and to put a stop to anything similar happening again."

When this opening, all-encompassing statement was done, one of the people seated near the front immediately answered the leadership's call, never one to let a stir die down. "We can't let our Yuyang tourism industry be ruined by people like that—they absolutely must be heavily punished!"

Meng Wan followed the voice and looked over. It was a man with a fat, jowly face, ruddy in complexion. She recognized him—his name was Qian Zhenyu, the number-one boss of Yuyang's second-largest travel agency.

Wang Zhongxian picked up where he'd left off, his tone cold and hard. "Of course they'll be dealt with—dealt with severely, in fact, so that everyone knows the determination of our leadership to wipe out this practice, and so that every tourism worker keeps that string in their heart pulled taut. There are red lines that must not be crossed!"

Qi Hongwei jumped in at once. "Deputy Mayor Wang is right. This time we absolutely must carry out our work with the resolve to eradicate this phenomenon. Everyone present is the head of a major travel agency. Your understanding and your conduct will, to a great degree, shape the future development of Yuyang's tourism industry. So today you must all listen carefully to the spirit conveyed by Deputy Mayor Wang, and go back and conscientiously put it into practice."

The boilerplate over, Wang Zhongxian let his eyes sweep imperceptibly across Meng Wan. "Then let's begin."

With that, Secretary Little Liu handed a document up to him.

"This is the handling procedure the city has worked out, decided upon for the agency involved." Wang Zhongxian gave the document in his hand a wave and began reading aloud.

Much of it he'd already conveyed just now. The most important parts were the final three measures of punishment.

First, the head of the agency would publicly conduct self-criticism and pledge never to reoffend.

Second, the agency would suspend operations for rectification for three months.

Third, an administrative penalty: a fine of five thousand yuan in full.

The severity of these penalties was no light matter. The woman comrade not far from Meng Wan couldn't help drawing a sharp breath, and the look she turned on Meng Wan held a few more shades of sympathy.

Meng Wan put on a heavy-hearted air and stood up, eyes brimming with tears, pleading aggrievedly, "Deputy Mayor Wang, isn't this punishment a bit too severe? Please, you and all the leaders, in light of the fact that this is our first offense, don't penalize us so heavily this once."

In fact the original terms had been a one-month suspension and a two-thousand-yuan fine. It was Meng Wan who'd said it wasn't heavy enough and had Secretary Little Liu change a few characters.

Wang Zhongxian assumed an air of stern authority and said sharply, "Only if it's heavy will it teach you a lasting lesson. We gave repeated orders before, forbidding the forcing of tourists to spend, and you, for the sake of profit, treated the leadership's words as wind past your ears. Now that something's gone wrong you ask me to lighten the punishment—where was all this earlier?"

Qi Hongwei instinctively exchanged a glance with Zhou Quan. This was a bit different from the level of punishment they'd agreed on beforehand—could it be that the city leadership felt the show of resolve wasn't strong enough and had upped the severity?

Although the incident's impact really was appalling, Youth Travel Agency was just a small operation of modest scale. A suspension for rectification plus a fine, all at once—they feared the agency wouldn't survive it. But this was a decision the deputy mayor had made personally, and they didn't dare lightly open their mouths to plead leniency.

Zhou Quan likewise knew nothing of the talk between Meng Wan and Deputy Mayor Wang, so he too assumed the deputy mayor was dead set on a heavy penalty, and could only fret helplessly off to the side, utterly unable to understand why Meng Wan didn't just speak the truth of the matter.

The only one who spoke up in agreement was Qian Zhenyu, who repeated like a machine, "Deputy Mayor Wang is right. Where were you earlier? Such an appalling matter must of course be severely punished, or you'll never learn your lesson!"

Everyone else held their tongues. Seated across from Qian Zhenyu was Bai Yuexi, manager of Yuyang's largest travel agency. He'd dealt with Deputy Mayor Wang several times and knew the man's character. These punishments today really were too harsh—not like something Wang would do. But then on the other hand he talked himself round: perhaps this affair had so angered the deputy mayor and the other city leaders that a few heavy-handed measures weren't out of the question after all.

Meng Wan had been worrying she couldn't find an outlet for her emotions. Riding the momentum Qian Zhenyu had built, she worked herself up. "Deputy Mayor Wang, I'm willing to sign a written guarantee of my own accord, to show our agency's resolve to rectify ourselves. In light of how sincere we're being, couldn't you find some room to ease the punishment?"

"What written guarantee?" Wang Zhongxian asked—genuinely in the dark.

Meng Wan promptly produced a signed document from her bag, and Secretary Little Liu, quick-eyed and quick-handed, took it over and gave it to the deputy mayor to see.

Everyone watched this document curiously, observing the deputy mayor's expression as well. He read it from start to finish, and at last a touch of ease softened his iron-dark face.

When he'd finished, Wang Zhongxian passed the document to Qi Hongwei beside him, who, with the other leaders around him, began reading it word for word, and then the whole office began passing it around.

Apart from the few leaders, everyone else's face was less than pleased.

The document read that Youth Travel Agency voluntarily accepted the oversight of all its colleagues in the tourism trade, and that should such a situation occur again, they would shut the agency down and cease operations.

Up to here it was all fine. It was the last line: "I believe that my colleagues are likewise willing to accept the oversight of others, to reject black-market guides, overcharging, and the like, and together create a civilized, friendly tourism environment."

Apart from Meng Wan's agency, every other agency engaged to some degree in overcharging or forced spending. By writing it this way, Meng Wan had effectively dragged all those matters out into the open. From now on, if she caught them at it, she might really go and report them—and if the punishment then were as heavy as today's, none of them could stay in business.

Although no one was at all happy with that last clause of Meng Wan's, no one dared voice an opinion either.

Once everyone had finished passing the document around, Meng Wan, in a full, hearty voice, proposed, "Since no one has any objection to this written guarantee, why don't all of us agency heads sign one together? We'll oversee one another and correct one another, and I'm confident our Yuyang tourism industry can become the very best in the nation!"

Hearing he was being pulled, for no good reason he could see, into this murky business, Qian Zhenyu grew agitated all at once. "Who says we have no objection?"

Quick on the draw, Meng Wan said, "If you have an objection, you're welcome to state it in front of Deputy Mayor Wang."

Qian Zhenyu was struck dumb for a moment, his meaty face flushing crimson. He forced his discontent back down his throat, and meeting Wang Zhongxian's interrogating gaze, stammered, "Deputy Mayor Wang, I didn't mean it that way. I meant—I meant, I just don't want to sign on the same sheet of paper as her."

"That's easily solved." Meng Wan came up with a fix at once. "We can each sign our own copy. We're in an office building anyway—printing's no trouble at all."

By now Zhou Quan had collected himself somewhat, and he immediately seconded Meng Wan. "I know where the printer is. I'll go prepare a copy for everyone right away."

And so, while everyone else was still in a hazy fog, not yet having caught on, Zhou Quan had already pushed open the door and walked out, carrying the written guarantee Meng Wan had prepared the day before.

Chapter 55

Song Qingyang brought Meng Wan an even bigger bolt from the blue.

The newspaper story about their travel agency had been seen by the deputy mayor in charge of culture and tourism. Furious that such a damaging incident could harm the development of Yuyang's tourism economy, he had delivered a stern rebuke to the leadership of the tourism bureau at a meeting that very day, ordering them to rectify the agency involved at once. Song Qingyang's friend worked at the tourism bureau and had already been notified that he was the one assigned to investigate. The moment he heard the news from his friend, he had rushed over to tip Meng Wan off. By his estimate, it would be only a matter of days before someone from on high came looking to have a talk with her, and he had come so she could brace herself.

Meng Wan had never imagined their little agency would make a name for itself in front of the leadership this way. It was almost laughably absurd.

But there was no dodging what was coming. After all, they had been wronged, so deep down she still had some confidence. Worst case, when the time came she would simply bring Xue Guangming along to clear things up for them.

That afternoon Meng Wan stuck to her morning plan and went hunting for a location for the restaurant. After winding all over town she settled on three promising spots, deciding to go back, compare the prices, the floor space, and the foot traffic nearby, then make up her mind.

The moment she got back to the agency, she found James sitting in the front room, chatting away happily with Old Bi.

"How are you here?" Meng Wan asked, delighted.

"I came to find you, but you were out. I didn't have anything to do anyway, so I waited here a while."

Old Bi's gaze swept over the two of them, and tactfully he said, "This old man is off the clock. I won't stick around playing the third wheel."

"Me too, I'm clocking out as well, leaving the place free for you two to have your private little world," Xu Qiuyun said briskly, packing up her bag and falling in behind Old Bi.

Their teasing painted a flush across Meng Wan's cheeks. Putting on a mock-serious air, she pulled rank to shut them down. "I happen to be your boss. How dare you crack jokes with your boss?"

Xu Qiuyun, bag over her shoulder, breezed past Meng Wan and pointed at the watch on her wrist, grinning impishly. "It's after hours right now. There's no boss here."

With that she strode lightly out of the agency, and in an instant only James and Meng Wan were left in the room.

Meng Wan blinked her bright, clear eyes. "Did you come to take me to dinner?"

It was right around mealtime, and after running around all afternoon she was a bit hungry.

"I did." James let his eyes linger on her face, tucking back a few loose strands of her hair, and said slowly, "But there's one more person joining us. She wants to meet you."

"Who?"

Thinking he meant to introduce a friend, Meng Wan waited for his next words with a sweet smile spreading across her face.

"My mom wants to meet you."

At those words the smile froze on Meng Wan's face. A little disbelieving, she sought confirmation. "Your mother wants to meet me?"

Now it was James's turn to gaze at Meng Wan, all tenderness. "Don't put pressure on yourself. My mom's a wonderful person. She's bound to like you just as much as I do."

Even with James saying that, Meng Wan couldn't help feeling anxious. They had only been together a few days, and already they were at the meet-the-parents stage. She wasn't the least bit prepared.

"But, I—"

James reached out a hand and stroked her cheek, his eyes crinkling with a smile. "It's fine. If you don't want to go, I'll turn her down for you. I'll just say you're too busy, no time."

"That won't do." Meng Wan set her little face seriously. "When an elder invites a junior to a meal, no matter how busy you are, you keep the appointment. I do have that much manners."

James smoothed away the knot between her brows and smiled gently. "Relax. Just be your usual self, that's plenty. The two of you are sure to hit it off."

So it seemed the parent had to be met today after all.

Meng Wan threw herself into James's arms like a coquette, hugging him tightly around the waist and grumbling and fussing for a while, before the two of them finally set off.

In the car, Meng Wan took the front passenger seat.

"Is this your car?" She looked it over front, back, left, and right. If only she could have a car too, then she wouldn't have to pedal that bicycle of hers in the blazing heat, sweating all over.

"More or less." Safety-conscious as ever, James fastened his seatbelt, then set a box—which seemed to materialize out of nowhere—on the armrest between them.

"This is a present for you. Open it and see."

Meng Wan followed his example and buckled her seatbelt too. At the word "present" her eyes went wide and she leaned in to look.

"A pager?" She picked up the box, thrilled. "How did you think to give me this?"

She had actually been planning to go buy one herself in a few days. She was a manager now, with all sorts of things waiting for her every day. If something like the Luo Zhigang incident happened again and she wasn't at the agency, no one would be able to reach her.

James pressed the gas and turned the wheel. "We've only been together a few days, and I've already come looking for you twice—both times you weren't there. If something happened to you I wouldn't even know. I heard you went down to the police station all by yourself. Next time something like that comes up, you should bring Old Bi along."

"You know about that too." Then Meng Wan asked, "And the newspaper business?"

"I know about that too."

Accountant Bi really did have a fast mouth.

Hugging the pager, Meng Wan blinked her clear eyes and asked, "So do you think I handled those two things well enough?"

In Meng Wan's mind James had become something like a teacher. She had just sat two exams, and she wanted her teacher to give her a grade.

James didn't read the papers, so the article was only read to him today by Old Bi. On the back of that, Old Bi had also told him about Meng Wan going alone to the station to bail out Luo Zhigang. Though he didn't know the exact details, in the end she had resolved both matters flawlessly—you could scarcely find fault. The only thing that worried him was that she didn't know how to share the burden, taking on everything herself, doing it all hands-on. If those tourists had been unreasonable and laid hands on her too, it was more than he dared think about.

"Whether you handled it well doesn't matter. All I hope is that when you deal with work, you put yourself first. Your own safety is the one thing you most need to handle right, and it's what I care about most."

When he finished, James turned at a red light to glance at Meng Wan.

The instant their eyes met, Meng Wan felt that gaze was warm, so warm.

She nodded obediently and said softly, "I understand. I'll take good care of myself."

"Meng Wan." James hit the gas and they moved forward again. "You really are amazing."

Meng Wan looked at his profile, the corners of her mouth lifting plainly, and she found herself smiling too. "I think so as well. You're so lucky to have such an outstanding girlfriend—I'm even jealous of you."

"Then jealousy is all you'll get, because there's no room for a third party between us."

------

Madam Ye Xinzhu had asked James about Meng Wan's tastes and chosen an old Yuyang establishment for the meeting—which happened to be the Yuxiang Lou that Meng Wan frequented.

Back in this familiar setting, Meng Wan suddenly felt a little steadier, as if this place were her own home turf, quietly backing her up.

When Ye Xinzhu saw her son come up the stairs, she peered eagerly past him, and there was a fresh-faced, lovely young girl trailing behind him, walking toward her step by step.

This had to be her son's girlfriend, the girl called "Meng Wan."

When Meng Wan reached the top of the stairs and saw Ye Xinzhu, she found the woman looking at her with a face full of warmth and kindness. James's mother looked remarkably young. No wonder Meng Wan had mistaken her for James's girlfriend the other day. Seeing them walk together, she would never have taken this for James's mother—older sister was nearer the mark.

James introduced Meng Wan and Madam Ye to each other. The moment Ye Xinzhu took Meng Wan's hand she didn't let go, pulling her straight down to sit beside her.

"You must be hungry. I just ordered, the food will be up soon."

"I'm all right." Meng Wan had barely finished the words when her stomach betrayed her with a loud growl. She laughed awkwardly and admitted, "I am a bit hungry, actually."

Drawn by Meng Wan's adorable manner, James kept fighting to keep the corners of his mouth from rising, doing his best not to laugh out loud so as not to embarrass her.

Ye Xinzhu smiled and glided past the moment, then said warmly to Meng Wan, "I heard you ran into James and me going back to the hotel and mistook me for his girlfriend. I nearly drove a wedge between a pair of lovers—what a disaster that would've been."

Meng Wan's face went scarlet at once. She turned and shot James a glare—why did he have to blurt out everything!

She laughed awkwardly by way of explanation. "That day I saw him going back to the hotel with a young, beautiful woman, the two of you laughing and chatting—naturally my imagination ran away with me. You look so young, I never once connected you with being James's mother. Older sister, at the very most."

Meng Wan spoke the plain truth, though with a touch of wanting to flatter the elder.

"This Little Meng has such a sweet tongue." Coaxed into good spirits, Ye Xinzhu said, "You've no idea—the greatest regret of my life is that I never had a second child, a daughter. As for this son..."

At that she shot James a glance, then let her eyes fall back on Meng Wan's face. "A son is fine too, only he's far too dull. Things like shopping for clothes, strolling around town—those are only fun to do with a daughter."

Meng Wan offered, "Auntie Ye, if you have time, I could keep you company shopping around Yuyang—though the styles here are surely nowhere near as fashionable as the ones where you're from."

"Yes, yes, yes." Ye Xinzhu's eyes curved with her smile. "You've no idea—ever since I came to Yuyang this fellow's been busy every single day, not even one person to keep me company. I'd like to go out for a stroll, but Yuyang's changed so much it's long since stopped being the place I remember."

Meng Wan came to James's defense. "His hotel's in its construction phase right now, so he does have to keep a close eye on it. There's a whole heap of things waiting for him to deal with there every day—just thinking about it makes my head spin. Your son really is very capable."

Ye Xinzhu glanced at her praised son and saw him gazing at Meng Wan with the corners of his mouth lifted. Her boy really was blessed, to have found such a fine girl for a partner.

"You're no slouch yourself. James tells me you're running your own travel agency now. These days girls can study and work, true, but it's still a rare few who make it to a leadership position as young as you. The difficulties you have to face are surely greater than most people's."

As a woman herself, Ye Xinzhu knew all too well a woman's place in this society, especially a woman who wanted to climb. She had to endure what ordinary people could not. The road Meng Wan walked was anything but easy.

Those words struck Meng Wan right in the heart, leaving her thoroughly at ease, and made her warm to Ye Xinzhu all the more. "Auntie Ye, you're a kindred spirit!"

The three of them ate the whole meal in warm good cheer. All through it Ye Xinzhu kept chatting with Meng Wan and putting food on her plate, the two of them talking up a storm—from the changes in Yuyang to the latest fashionable skirt styles, holding nothing back. As for James, the go-between who had introduced them, he couldn't get a word in edgewise, and could only sit quietly to one side admiring the two ladies' soaring spirits.

Meng Wan even received a jade bracelet from Ye Xinzhu. Although she knew nothing about jade, just from its all-over translucence she could tell the gift was very precious.

Today's meeting had come together too suddenly, and Meng Wan hadn't prepared a gift in return, so she asked which zodiac sign Madam Ye was, planning to commission Old Bai to make a jade pendant to give back to her.

Before they parted, Meng Wan made a date with Ye Xinzhu to go shopping with her in a few days and tour around the city of Yuyang while they were at it.

The meeting had gone far more pleasantly than Meng Wan had expected. Madam Ye Xinzhu was nothing like a woman her age. Had she been born in this era, she would surely have accomplished great things.

Back at the Meng household, Meng Qingyan told Meng Wan that the inscribed plaque for the agency was nearly finished, and asked which day she had time, so he could deliver it to her in person and take a look at his daughter's workplace and working environment while he was at it.

Meng Wan thought it over, then asked Meng Qingyan—a university professor—to help pick an auspicious day suitable for an opening, when they would hang the plaque and reopen with a brand-new face.

Meng Qingyan flipped through the calendar and set the date for the end of the month. He notified every member of the household, hoping they would all keep that day free and come together to attend the opening ceremony of Meng Wan's agency.

Perhaps because she had talked so much about the opening before bed, Meng Wan dreamed she was standing beneath the plaque with a big red flower pinned to her, giving an opening speech—only the plaque, not hung securely, came into free fall and crashed straight down onto her head.

Meng Wan jolted awake from the dream, terrified. Luckily she woke quickly and didn't go on to dream anything more gruesome.

Perhaps because the dream had been too real, Meng Wan couldn't shake a bad feeling. Her heart thudded the whole way there, and her mood was heavy.

The moment she arrived at the agency, she saw Director Zhang Yuqing of the office sitting in their workspace in a towering temper.

Meng Wan's chest tightened. It seemed the trouble she couldn't dodge had come to her door.

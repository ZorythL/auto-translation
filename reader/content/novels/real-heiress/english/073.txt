Chapter 73

Bai Yuexi had always projected the image of an all-around good guy: never losing his temper, forever playing the peacemaker.

But today, for the first time, he lost his composure in front of Meng Wan, shattering his usual mild-mannered facade. The man who was always so gentle now turned a cold face on her.

He gave a sardonic, mirthless snort. "Manager Meng, what impressive maneuvering. Off you went to Linjiang with Deputy Mayor Wang without a word to anyone, and now I imagine your client list is booked solid clear into next year."

Meng Wan poured him a glass of water, still perfectly polite. "Manager Bai, you flatter me. The Deputy Mayor only took me along because I'm new and have so much still to learn. He wanted me to broaden my horizons, that's all. And honestly, there really weren't as many tourists as you seem to think—split one apiece among the guides and there's nothing left."

"Hmph!" Bai Yuexi sneered. "I used to think of you as a young one too, made allowances for you in everything, kept playing the go-between so you and Qian Zhenyu wouldn't come to blows. And how did you repay me? Behind my back, you first bought out Qian Zhenyu's travel agency, then went off and hogged all the clients for yourself. Little girl, that's not how business is done."

Inwardly Meng Wan rolled her eyes. She hadn't snatched or stolen anything—how was that not the way to do business? Was she supposed to wait until he'd eaten all the meat and then content herself with sipping a little of the broth?

No. She wanted to eat the meat too!

"Manager Bai, since you've put it that bluntly, I won't mince words either." Meng Wan dropped the smile and adopted a tone of plain fact. "Didn't I come to you a while back about them poaching clients with cut-rate prices? But you acted like you knew nothing about it, like you couldn't be bothered. Since you didn't want to deal with it, I had no choice but to deal with it myself. I have no reason to stand by and watch our agency's clients dwindle away bit by bit. I have my own employees to protect—I can't let them go hungry!"

"Who said I wasn't going to deal with it? That was my strategy, don't you understand? Just a few more days and Qian Zhenyu would've gone under on his own. That's when I'd have gone to him and talked him into selling. Once I finished that buyout, there'd be no more of this undercutting and price-warring!"

Meng Wan clapped her hands with a crisp smack and said brightly, "Then Manager Bai and I were thinking exactly alike! I went and bought it out for you—so now your agency doesn't have to fight a price war either. Everybody wins, all around."

Bai Yuexi could have spat blood, he was so furious. How was that the same thing at all?

But anything he said now was too late. The agency had already been bought out from under him, and she'd reeled in a good number of tourists besides. If he didn't move soon, his number-one position was about to be lost for good.

"I can't be bothered to argue with you. We'll settle this in the marketplace!"

So be it—who's afraid of whom?

Meng Wan bowed and scraped him out the door. As soon as he was gone, Li Yang sidled up beside her and laid on the flattery in a low voice. "Manager Meng, I believe that under your leadership, we'll overtake them in no time."

Meng Wan didn't bother with false modesty. "I think so too."

*

In the days that followed, every last guide at Youth Travel Agency went out into the field, ferrying every tourist they could reach over to Yuyang.

The moment the friendship-and-mutual-aid agreement between the two cities took effect, the Linjiang leadership threw its full weight behind promoting it, and the citizens of Linjiang made Yuyang their top pick for outings. Over the National Day holiday, you couldn't take a few steps in Yuyang without spotting a guide waving a little flag, leading a group of Linjiang residents around the sights.

And Youth Travel Agency, seizing exactly this moment of opportunity, vaulted in one stroke to become the most profitable agency in Yuyang, overtaking Bai Yuexi's outfit.

Yet our Manager Meng had no time to savor the triumph. She was short-staffed now and had to hire more people—foreign-language guides especially. Not long ago, after James's friend had visited and taken such a liking to Yuyang's culture, scenery, and historic sites, he'd referred a whole string of relatives and friends. One brought two, two brought three, and before long there were forty or fifty names on the list.

On top of that, there was the website James had mentioned earlier. He really had gotten a friend of his to help build one for their travel agency, with the email address James had registered for her listed at the bottom.

Meng Wan set up a computer in the office, and within a few days she was receiving emails from across the ocean. She knew her way around a computer, so replying to emails and the like was no trouble at all. She'd also been teaching herself basic English lately, and—muddling along somehow—she actually managed to close a deal: seven people, coming to Yuyang in December.

James's hotel business was thriving too. Thanks to its secluded setting, spotless cleanliness, and attentive service, its reputation had steadily climbed to the top of the industry. Some tourists, the moment they stepped off the plane, would hail a cab and ask for the best hotel in the city—and the driver would take them straight to Yuyin Lodge.

From there the hotel's front-desk staff would recommend Meng Wan's travel agency, and the agency, after asking what the clients had in mind, would bring them to Yuyin Lodge in turn—each side helping the other prosper.

Then there was Yuyang's Premier Eatery, which had now expanded; Meng Wan was getting ready to open a second branch in the north of the city.

Lin Dong's fleet of vehicles kept growing larger, becoming a fine place for many laid-off workers to find work again. And at the jade shop, too, Meng Wan made a point of setting aside positions for laid-off workers. For this, Deputy Mayor Wang even praised her, saying she'd done a great thing that benefited ordinary people.

From travel-agency guides to the restaurant, the fleet, the jade shop, and the hotel, Meng Wan had essentially linked up every key link in the tourism chain, achieving full coverage across the whole travel experience.

No more kickbacks to pay out; all the income from the gray middle ground was wiped clean. It was all kept within the family now—whatever they earned was theirs to keep. Everyone pulled together, pushing in the same direction, and they truly created a healthy, self-sustaining cycle for the tourists.

By the end of that year, Youth Travel Agency had not only multiplied its headcount several times over, but its revenue was such that every single person took home a handsome year-end bonus. Everyone agreed: life had never felt so full of promise.

Work for Manager Meng, and you've got a future ahead of you!

After the better part of a year of stumbles and setbacks, Meng Wan had somehow built a four-person travel agency into the best in the entire city—and through everyone's combined effort, had pulled Yuyang's tourism industry up to third in the whole country.

At the awards ceremony celebrating the development of Yuyang's tourism industry, Deputy Mayor Wang presented Meng Wan with an Outstanding Contribution Award, commending her for the remarkable contributions she'd made to Yuyang's economic development and to the work of its citizens. The provincial governor himself presented her the trophy.

Wearing a red sash and holding aloft the golden trophy and the red certificate, Meng Wan stood shoulder to shoulder with the governor for a one-of-a-kind photograph. In the picture, her smile was radiant, her whole bearing high-spirited and brimming with confidence.

The photo even ran as the front-page headline of the Yuyang Broadcasting and Television News, the reporting journalist being an old acquaintance of hers—the reporter Xue Guangming.

Even as Meng Wan found success in her career, her love life, too, began to come together with its own pursuit and passion.

On the evening of the day the awards ended, James proposed to Meng Wan, and the two of them, witnessed by friends and family, walked together into the hall of matrimony.

Before that, too, Meng Wan had explained the whole matter of the real and fake heiresses to the Meng family. Qin Yi'e came to their door in person to apologize, repenting of the deep sins she'd committed; on the spot she cut off her hair and went to a nunnery to become a nun.

When the Meng family learned the truth, they couldn't accept it for the longest time. Meng Wan brought Zhou Nian to the Meng household to be reunited with them.

The moment Cheng Xuehua laid eyes on Zhou Nian, it was as though she were seeing her own younger self. She threw her arms around her and wept bitterly, while Meng Wan stood to one side at a loss. Calm as she appeared on the surface, inside her heart had long since churned like seas turned upside down with grief.

Sun Jing could tell that Meng Wan was having a hard time of it too—anyone caught up in something like this would ache inside. So she quietly drew Meng Wan away. Both sides needed space to be alone; staying together would only deepen the sorrow.

After nearly half a month of adjustment, everyone attended Meng Wan's wedding. James threw her a grand Western-style ceremony. Because Zhou Da couldn't walk, Meng Wan still took Meng Qingyan's arm and walked slowly toward James.

Meng Qingyan stroked Meng Wan's hand, his eyes reddening, and said softly, "Child, no matter what, you must be happy."

Choking back tears, Meng Wan nodded, her bright clear eyes brimming with crystalline tears. Meng Qingyan placed Meng Wan's hand in James's and charged him, his voice thick with emotion, "Child, today I give my beloved daughter into your keeping. The two of you must be happy."

James took Meng Wan's slender, fair hand, and said in earnest, with firm resolve, "Dad, set your mind at ease. I will make Meng Wan happy, I promise."

Bride and groom, hand in hand, looked into each other's eyes and smiled.

"James, thank you. I think I forgot to tell you—I love you."

"You didn't forget. Every single day, you tell me so with everything you do." James wiped the tears from the corners of Meng Wan's eyes, his voice gentle and strong. "Meng Wan, I love you!"

The End!

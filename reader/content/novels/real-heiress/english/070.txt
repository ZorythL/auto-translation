Chapter 70

Meng Wan couldn't make out what game Bai Yuexi was playing. This kind of price war clearly hurt more than just her agency—his was the biggest of them all, with the most business, which meant he stood to lose the most too. Yet here he was, the eunuch fretting harder than the emperor—except it was the other way around, the one who should have been anxious wasn't.

Watching Bai Yuexi's air of lofty detachment, as if none of it had anything to do with him, Meng Wan knew he wasn't going to offer any concrete advice today.

Her busy day over, Meng Wan soaked in a leisurely bath at James's hotel.

James brought out a bottle of champagne, and under the moonlight the two of them swirled their glasses and drank together. The mood was so romantic, and yet Meng Wan's face was clouded with worry, her brows knit, the wine seeming to hold no flavor at all as she tipped one glass after another down her throat.

"That's not how you drink this. Keep going like that and you'll be drunk soon. If something's on your mind, just say it." James set down his glass and turned to look at her. "Tell me and I'll help you think it through."

Meng Wan couldn't hold back a hiccup, a mouthful of alcohol fumes rising up, and only then did she feel her head going a bit fuzzy—she really couldn't drink any more. She set down her glass too, sat there blankly mulling over what James had just said, and decided, fine, she'd tell him. Then, while her head was still clear, she laid out for James everything that had happened lately, from the beginning.

"Tell me, why isn't this Bai Yuexi the least bit worried?" Meng Wan had been puzzling over it all evening and simply couldn't find a thread to pull.

James folded his arms across his chest, his brown eyes flickering faintly. He pondered a while, then said, "Unless he stands to profit from it."

"How could that be?" Meng Wan found it hard to believe. "It obviously hurts his interests."

James tapped a finger lightly against himself, his gaze fixed on the half-full bottle before them. "His immediate interests do take a hit. But the future—that's another matter entirely."

"The future?" Meng Wan followed the thought further, and just as she brushed against the possible truth she stopped short, looking at James in disbelief. "You're saying Bai Yuexi wants to use this chance to swallow up Qian Zhenyu's agency?"

Meng Wan had heard earlier that Qian Zhenyu's agency, owing to internal upheaval and a business strategy that hadn't kept pace with the changing market, was already pouring a lot of effort just into holding onto second place. The summer season had given them some relief, but for all anyone knew they'd already been quietly slashing prices back then; now, with fewer tourists, the poaching had simply grown more brazen. If no strong-handed leader stepped up to set things in order, then at this rate of bleeding, shutting down really was only a matter of time.

James shrugged, very calm. "As things stand now, it's highly likely. This is a very common phenomenon abroad—a big company swallowing a small one to gain a monopoly position and seize the whole market."

"You're saying Bai Yuexi wants a monopoly?" Half of Meng Wan's tipsiness vanished in an instant, her heart thudding faster.

"Every country's circumstances are different. An outright monopoly probably won't fly in Yuyang, but a single dominant firm is a real possibility. Once Bai Yuexi acquires Qian Zhenyu's second-largest agency, his operation will be bigger than all the remaining agencies combined."

At that point it would be next to a monopoly. The big agency could set its own prices, with its own quality customer base and stable channels, while a small agency like Meng Wan's could only scrape up the scraps it left behind, with no say at all. Her agency had only just begun to find its footing, and now it looked set to be sacrificed in this business war.

Meng Wan's eyes rolled as a bold idea sprang into her head. "Tell me—what if I bought Qian Zhenyu's agency?"

Then her agency would land in the number-two seat and be able to go toe-to-toe with Bai Yuexi's.

But the next second, struck by the practical problem, she deflated. "But I don't have that kind of money."

Take out a bank loan? With an agency the size of hers, she couldn't borrow much anyway.

Meng Wan slumped back on the sofa, her eyes going vacant as she stared up at the openwork chandelier overhead, and let out a long sigh like the wail of an ape.

"God of Wealth, drop a little money down for me!"

A soft laugh curved James's lips. "Don't you see there's a living, breathing person sitting right beside you?"

Meng Wan turned to look at him without much enthusiasm—then the next second sprang upright off the sofa. "That's right! You're my God of Wealth!"

James's expression turned wry. "God of Wealth is a stretch. But I can certainly come up with the money to buy a travel agency."

Meng Wan's eyes sparkled, brimming with hope. "How much are you planning to lend me?"

"I'm not planning to lend." James said it without a moment's hesitation.

Meng Wan hissed through her teeth. Was he toying with her?

James sat up straight, his voice unhurried. "Don't be in such a rush. I won't lend—but I can buy in as a shareholder."

"Buy in?"

"Yes. I put up the money and you run it. You can buy in with your expertise."

Meng Wan sank into thought, then couldn't help asking, "And what about my own agency?"

"Still yours, of course."

"Then wouldn't I be working two jobs?" She could barely keep up with even the one.

"You could merge Youth Travel in and run them together—that way your share would be bigger," James suggested.

Meng Wan discovered that this man, when he talked business, was all business—not a trace of the boyfriend left in him.

"You'd really trust me to manage an agency that big?"

She'd only just begun to get the hang of running a small one. A big agency, from guides to logistics, meant several dozen people at least—did she have the ability for that?

"Of course I trust you." James gave her his firm approval. "Nobody knows how to manage right out of the gate, and you've already got experience. What you need to do is cut the rot away immediately—excise the tumor at once, get the body back to strength as fast as you can, and then go to work in earnest."

A deep-seated, long-festering malady did call for a clean cut, and that was exactly when you needed an outsider with strong methods and decisive nerve to step in—and Meng Wan was a very suitable choice for the role.

Meng Wan was daydreaming, riding James's words, picturing herself driving sweeping reforms, leading a new agency to its peak—when James doused her with a bucket of cold water.

"The problem right now is how we compete with Bai Yuexi."

Since he'd settled on this plan, it meant he'd already done his calculations and was confident he could snap up Qian Zhenyu's agency in one stroke and become Yuyang's largest. But of the two of them, one had no resources in this area and the other was a raw beginner; money alone wouldn't do much good.

Meng Wan's heart was like a child on a slide, plunging from the highest point straight to the lowest—then, a few minutes later, it surged back up. She stood right up on the sofa and swore an oath to James. "Leave it to me. I'll take it down for sure!"

------

Meng Wan was a woman of strong follow-through. Early the next morning she went to the agency and instructed all the guides to start spreading word that Chaoyang Travel was about to go under—and, finally, to spread it to the faces of Chaoyang's own guides.

Sure enough, within a few days the talk reached Qian Zhenyu's ears, and before Meng Wan even had to seek him out, he came storming over.

"To what do I owe the honor of Manager Qian's grand visit?" Meng Wan played the innocent.

Qian Zhenyu all but jabbed a finger in her face. "Was it you who had your guides spreading rumors about us out there?" he roared furiously. "Have you no professional ethics at all?"

Meng Wan sneered inwardly. She honestly couldn't believe such words could come out of his mouth.

"Manager Qian, don't get worked up just yet. Have a seat and let me explain it to you slowly." As she spoke, Meng Wan pulled out a chair, then turned and instructed Xu Qiuyun, "Qiuyun, hurry and pour Manager Qian a cup of tea—use my best tin of pre-Qingming Longjing."

"Got it, right away."

Qian Zhenyu snorted coldly, dropped onto the seat with a thump, and shot Meng Wan an eye-roll for good measure.

Meng Wan took the hot tea Xu Qiuyun had poured and set it down in front of Qian Zhenyu. "Manager Qian, drink some tea, cool your temper."

"I won't!" Qian Zhenyu turned his head to her. "I just want to know whether you did this—whether you're the one who spread the rumors all over the place!"

"I did." Meng Wan owned up readily, then quickened her pace before Qian Zhenyu could blow up. "But it was all Manager Bai who told me—so it shouldn't really count as a rumor, should it?"

Qian Zhenyu had been about to flare up, but the moment he heard Bai Yuexi's name he slammed on the brakes. "Bai Yuexi? What did he say to you?"

Seeing the fish was on the hook, Meng Wan began, unhurried. "Nothing much, really—we were just chatting. Manager Bai said he had a mind to expand his agency, and then he mentioned that Chaoyang Travel's earnings didn't seem to be doing too well lately..."

Meng Wan didn't go any further. With Qian Zhenyu's suspicious mind, only what he worked out himself would he truly believe.

"You mean to say he wants to swallow our agency whole?" Qian Zhenyu shot to his feet as if stung. "In his dreams!"

"Don't get ahead of yourself—Manager Bai may not have meant that at all. Maybe I misread him." Meng Wan kept stoking the fire, unable to resist watching his reaction.

One mountain can't hold two tigers. The last-place and second-to-last can be good friends, but the first-place and second-place are bound to be locked in a quiet contest. The previous few times, Meng Wan had seen the two of them keep up an outward peace, but Qian Zhenyu plainly looked down on Bai Yuexi, and Bai Yuexi had even less liking for a man like Qian Zhenyu, who did things flashily and without regard for consequences.

"That's exactly what he means!" Qian Zhenyu bellowed, red in the face and thick in the neck. "He just wants to watch me make a fool of myself. He just wants to prove that he's the boss of Yuyang's tourism industry. Well, I refuse to give him the satisfaction!"

"Manager Bai is only thinking of what's good for Yuyang's tourism. After all, Chaoyang Travel's habit of poaching customers at a loss really has hurt the other agencies' business."

"If they've got the guts, let them cut their prices too! He won't cut his own, but he wants to dictate whether others cut theirs. Does he think my agency can't hold on? I'll hold on extra long just to spite him—and even if I couldn't hold on, even if I had to sell my agency, I'd never sell it to him!"

There it was—that was exactly the line Meng Wan had been waiting for.

"Manager Qian, I happen to have an idea. Would you care to hear it?"

"Out with it!" Qian Zhenyu sat down, breathing hard, anger crackling off the top of his head.

"James of Yuyin Lodge—my boyfriend, that is—has taken a bit of interest in running a travel agency lately. Perhaps you could approach him about putting up the capital to buy yours. That would solve your funding problem and keep Manager Bai from getting the upper hand over you."

Meng Wan didn't mention herself—otherwise Qian Zhenyu would surely suspect this had all been her design.

Qian Zhenyu seemed to be genuinely weighing the idea's feasibility. He met Meng Wan's eyes and said suspiciously, "This isn't some trap you've set for me, is it?"

How could it be such a coincidence—her boyfriend wanting to run a travel agency at exactly this moment?

Meng Wan clicked her tongue, sincerity all but spilling off her face. "Manager Qian, do I look like someone with scheming like Manager Bai's?"

Sorry, Manager Bai—but you're the one who chose a policy of appeasement. Since it had come to harm her interests, she couldn't very well sit by and do nothing. She had a whole crew of people to feed too!

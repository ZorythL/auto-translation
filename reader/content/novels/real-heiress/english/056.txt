Chapter 56

"Director Zhang, to what do we owe the honor of your visit?"

Seeing how grim his face was, Meng Wan figured he had most likely come about the agency making the papers.

Sure enough, Zhang Yuqing's expression was dark as he rapped hard on a newspaper laid out on the desk and fumed, "Look at the fine mess you've made. Before, you swore up and down to me that you'd make our West City district the vanguard, the trailblazer of the whole city's tourism industry. Is this how you blaze a trail? You've blazed a fine trail of bad practices, all right!"

Accountant Bi hurried to bring him a cup of tea, setting it close at hand, and said placatingly, "Director Zhang, calm down, calm down. In this whole affair, our agency is a victim too."

"You've been splashed all over the newspaper by someone, and you call yourselves victims? Then me, hauled into the office first thing this morning for a tongue-lashing from the leadership—aren't I even more pitiable than you?"

Early this morning, no sooner had Zhang Yuqing set foot in the building than the section chief's secretary summoned him to the office. Before he had even grasped what was going on, he was raked over the coals from head to toe. He had been at the office for less than three months, and he prided himself on being conscientious in his work, yet he had never imagined he'd be tripped up at Meng Wan's hands.

"Director Zhang, don't be upset just yet." Meng Wan handed her bag to Qiuyun and pulled over a stool to sit down. "This really is a misunderstanding. Hear me out."

Calmly and evenly, Meng Wan explained the whole thing to Zhang Yuqing. He listened it all through, half believing, half doubting, finding it hard to credit her account—how could a newspaper reporter make such a foolish mistake?

Meng Wan spent ages talking, but it won her no trust from Director Zhang. It seemed this matter still needed Xue Guangming, the man at the root of it all, to explain everything to everyone face to face, so as to dispel all doubt completely.

Just as she was thinking this, that very Xue Guangming actually arrived, and with him came a middle-aged man in a stand-collar culture shirt.

"And you are?" Meng Wan rose to greet the guest.

"Hello, Manager Meng. I'm the chief editor of the Yuyang newspaper, Sun Xiangrong. I've come today specifically to offer my apologies to you and your agency. Due to an oversight by our reporter, you and your agency suffered a negative impact, and we extend our sincere apologies."

With that, he bowed to Meng Wan. Watching the chief editor's gesture, Xue Guangming immediately followed suit.

Meng Wan promptly shot Zhang Yuqing a look. See? Everything she'd said was true. Their agency really was the victim.

Zhang Yuqing's brows drew tight together as he drew a sharp breath. He hadn't expected it to actually be so.

"Chief Editor Sun, please, get up. As long as the matter's been cleared up, it's all fine."

Sun Xiangrong straightened, turned his head to glance at Xue Guangming beside him, then looked back at Meng Wan and said earnestly, "Truth be told, part of the responsibility here is mine. I failed in my duty as a mentor, and that's how an article lacking proper verification came to be published. Guangming knows he was in the wrong too. I'll personally supervise him as he writes a clarification and apology, to be published in next week's paper."

"Then I'll have to trouble you with that. After all, this paper of ours has the largest circulation in the whole city—it really wouldn't do to let a falsehood keep spreading."

"It's just that..." Sun Xiangrong hemmed and hawed, wringing his hands. "It's just that, about Little Xue's job—Manager Meng, might we discuss it a little further? It was no easy thing for him to get into university, no easy thing to get into our newspaper. It's hard to train up a good reporter these days. Couldn't you give him another chance, let him stay in this post and put his talents to good use?"

Meng Wan shot a glance at Xu Qiuyun beside her. See? She'd said he couldn't bear to give up that job.

Feigning ignorance, she said, "Huh? Reporter Xue is resigning?"

Seeing that Meng Wan looked entirely in the dark, Sun Xiangrong couldn't help looking to Xue Guangming, asking with his eyes what was going on—hadn't Xue said it was Meng Wan who wanted him to resign?

Xue Guangming was just as baffled. This time everything he'd said was true. "Didn't you tell me to resign on my own once I'd finished writing the apology article?"

"I said that?" Meng Wan blinked her big eyes, the picture of innocence. "What I said was for Reporter Xue to seriously consider the importance of his profession. I never meant for you to resign. Could there have been a gap in your understanding?"

Before Xue Guangming could get a word of rebuttal out, Meng Wan shut him down again. "No, that's not right—a reporter's comprehension must be excellent. It must have been that I didn't express myself clearly, and that's what led Reporter Xue to misunderstand. I do hope that in the future Reporter Xue won't encounter an interviewee like me, one who speaks so unclearly and misleads your judgment of the objective facts."

There was a barb beneath Meng Wan's words. Even the dullest person could hear that she meant to teach Xue Guangming a lesson.

Xue Guangming's face cycled through shades of green and purple. Usually so glib, this time he couldn't get out a single word. For this favored son of fortune, the lesson dealt a tremendous blow.

Meng Wan went on, "Chief Editor Sun, the leadership above me already knows about this. By my guess it'll be only a matter of days before I'm dragged into a criticism meeting. Could you help me write a letter of proof, certifying that this really wasn't the fault of our agency or our guide? Otherwise, on my word alone, the leadership might not believe me."

Zhang Yuqing felt Meng Wan was pointing at him, and his expression twitched a few times, unnatural.

"No problem, I'll write it right now." Sun Xiangrong agreed at once—this was only what they ought to do.

"Let me do it instead." Xue Guangming jumped in, vying to write it. After all, the one at fault was him.

"No." A refusal flew out of Meng Wan's mouth. "This letter can only be written by Chief Editor Sun."

Sun Xiangrong's name carried weight before the city leadership. Say what you would, he was a senior chief editor of the Yuyang newspaper. As for Xue Guangming—who among the leadership knew who he was? A letter from him would be worthless.

Sun Xiangrong understood Meng Wan's meaning. He took the pen and paper Xu Qiuyun handed him and began writing away. In the end he not only signed his own name but also stamped it with the newspaper's seal that he carried on him.

With everything in order, Meng Wan tucked the letter away, thanked Chief Editor Sun, and saw the two of them off.

Not long ago Zhang Yuqing had been all anger; now he turned sympathetic. "Manager Meng, you really are magnanimous. This reporter caused you such a serious negative impact, and a single apology settles it?"

If it were him, he wouldn't let it go so easily. Women really were softer touches.

"Harmony breeds prosperity, you know." Meng Wan turned to Zhang Yuqing. "And I've nothing to say against you either. Before, I said I'd become the trailblazer of Yuyang's tourism industry, and I will deliver. When that day comes, you just wait for your promotion and your meteoric rise."

Of course she knew a printed apology was hardly much of a punishment, and it was something Xue Guangming should have done anyway. The reason Meng Wan didn't press the matter further was that the other side were newspaper people. You could offend anyone, but never offend those who held the reins of public opinion.

"Meteoric rise, she says—my dear young lady, let's get the matter at hand sorted out first and I'll thank Buddha for that."

He had come today to tell Meng Wan that the city had convened all the heads of the travel agencies for a meeting, aimed specifically at black-market guides forcing tourists to spend and the string of bad practices that had cropped up lately—a rectification assembly.

The meeting was to be held the very next day. The leadership still didn't know the truth, so tomorrow Meng Wan was certain to be named and criticized as a negative example.

Meng Wan's heart lurched. Forewarned though she had been by Song Qingyang, hearing she was to be singled out and criticized by the leadership still gave her a twinge of dread.

Seeing Meng Wan's expression turn rarely solemn, Zhang Yuqing couldn't help feeling for her. After all, she was just a young girl. "How do you plan to handle it?"

The atmosphere fell quiet for a moment. Xu Qiuyun and Old Bi cast worried looks at Meng Wan. Damn that Xue Guangming for landing their agency in such a passive spot.

Meng Wan shook her head, then heaved a heavy sigh. "The road to Shu is hard—harder than climbing to the blue sky!"

Zhang Yuqing shook his head. Great—another one's gone off her rocker.

------

Yuyang had seventeen travel agencies, large and small, and the heads of all of them would attend this meeting. The vast majority of those agencies had themselves engaged in forcing tourists to spend or in overcharging, and Meng Wan couldn't tell whether, on hearing of this, they would take more of a warning from it or more of a laugh.

After all, it wasn't they who'd been caught, and it wasn't they who had to take the criticism. Most likely they were all just coming to watch the show.

Meng Wan couldn't sit and wait for doom. Though she held in her hands the evidence to prove her agency's innocence, if she produced it while the leadership was criticizing her, wouldn't that be slapping the leadership in the face? And if she went to see the leadership beforehand and explained everything, then the very purpose of calling the meeting would be defeated. Was she supposed to make the leadership explain and clear her name in front of everyone?

Whether to speak, and when to speak, all required finding a clever moment. The one thing that fell in line with the leadership's wishes was for her to be criticized and lectured before everyone, then heavily penalized, so that she, as the negative example, could serve as a warning to all.

In that case, she might as well play along and turn their scheme to her own ends!

The meeting had originally been set for half past nine, but Meng Wan turned up outside the tourism bureau before eight.

This meeting was led by the tourism bureau, with the deputy mayor in charge of tourism, Wang Zhongxian, in attendance. It was this big leader that Meng Wan was waiting for—the very one who was to criticize her.

Meng Wan stood outside the gate with her bicycle, waiting in faint nervousness. She had never met this deputy mayor and didn't know his temperament, so she had trouble gauging how to pitch her words to him. If she said something wrong, or if he wasn't pleased with her proposal, she'd truly be out of options.

As she was mulling it over, a black sedan came rolling slowly past in front of her. A middle-aged man sat in the back seat, and judging by the car's appointments, the passenger inside had to be a fairly high-ranking official.

Meng Wan reckoned this had to be the deputy mayor, Wang Zhongxian.

She quickly wheeled her bicycle aside and parked it, patted her clothes down with a touch of nerves, clenched her fists, and walked to the door of the car where the deputy mayor was getting out.

"Deputy Mayor Wang, hello."

Wang Zhongxian shut the car door and was startled by this sudden voice, his hand trembling for an instant. Then he turned and looked Meng Wan up and down, asking with mild puzzlement, "And you are?"

He hadn't denied the title she'd addressed him by—so she had the right person.

In a high, spirited tone brimming with feeling, Meng Wan introduced herself. "Deputy Mayor Wang, hello. I'm the head of Youth Travel Agency. My name is Meng Wan."

Clearly Wang Zhongxian hadn't anticipated Meng Wan seeking him out alone before the meeting. First a flicker of surprise crossed his eyes, then a flash of displeasure. "The handling of your agency will be addressed at the meeting shortly. There's no need to come ahead of time begging for leniency. Everything's being done by the rules."

With that he made to leave.

Seeing him about to go, Meng Wan stepped up at once to keep pace, quickening her words. "You've misunderstood. I've come to give you evidence that can prove our agency's innocence. That report in the newspaper is fake news. Our agency's guide never forced any tourists to spend."

Wang Zhongxian's just-lifted foot halted. His expression turned grave. "Fake news?"

"Yes, that report is false." Meng Wan hurriedly drew from her bag the clarification letter Chief Editor Sun had written yesterday. "This is a clarification letter written by Chief Editor Sun Xiangrong of the Yuyang newspaper. Please take a look—the contents prove our agency was wronged."

Somewhat disbelieving, Wang Zhongxian took the letter and read it through carefully. Indeed, just as Meng Wan said, their agency had been wronged by this reporter named Xue Guangming, because an interviewee had given false testimony.

He knew this Chief Editor Sun Xiangrong. The man was an old comrade, and over Wang Zhongxian's years in office the two had worked together many times. He trusted Sun's character, which lent Meng Wan's letter far greater credibility.

If things were truly as the letter described, then the meeting he'd called today had entirely lost its original purpose. For a moment Wang Zhongxian was at a loss, his eyes drifting back and forth between the letter and Meng Wan's face.

"Come inside with me first." Wang Zhongxian folded the letter and turned to the secretary waiting nearby. "Little Liu, find me an empty office. I want a word with this comrade."

"Yes, Deputy Mayor."

After coordinating with the tourism bureau staff, Secretary Little Liu found a small, unoccupied meeting room. Wang Zhongxian had Meng Wan sit down, wanting to understand her current request and intentions.

"Thank you for being willing to trust me, and for setting aside your valuable time to hear me out." Meng Wan exchanged pleasantries. "The main purpose of your calling this meeting is to crack down hard on the phenomenon of forcing tourists to spend, and to safeguard the healthy development of Yuyang's tourism industry—am I right?"

"That was the plan. But now there's no negative example left, and the educational impact is greatly weakened all at once."

He knew Yuyang had black-market guides forcing tourists to spend, but he'd never found the right opportunity to clean it up. Although the newspaper incident had angered him, it served conveniently as a hook for rectification, a way to make everyone understand the gravity of the problem and recognize their errors.

But now that the news was false, he couldn't use punishment to achieve a deterrent effect on the crowd. Simply shouting slogans was meaningless—saying one thing to your face and doing another behind your back was a trick these people used all the time.

"Who says there's no negative example left?" Meng Wan curved her lips into an ingratiating smile and blinked her bright, lively eyes. "Our Youth Travel Agency is the negative example!"

Wang Zhongxian couldn't keep up with this young person's leaps of thought. "But didn't you say your agency was wronged?"

"But the others don't know that. We can turn the scheme to our own ends!"

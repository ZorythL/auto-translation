Chapter 58

By the time everyone belatedly came to their senses, they realized this was a pit Meng Wan had dug for them. They were all roasting on the fire now—not signing this guarantee simply wasn't an option.

Wang Zhongxian's face still wore a grave expression, but inside his heart had long since burst into bloom. So this was the game Meng Wan had been playing. Before the clarification ran in the paper, she'd get these agency heads to sign the guarantee first. Once they learned the truth, the rice would already be cooked. Then he could call another meeting, express his regrets to Meng Wan's agency, and cancel their punishment. This one move killed several birds at once.

A problem that had given him a headache for ages, solved in a few sentences by such a young slip of a girl. It seemed this youngster's future was boundless.

Who'd have thought that the very next second, the Meng Wan he'd just praised would toss him a thorny problem of his own.

"Deputy Mayor Wang, I have a few more suggestions. I wonder if you might let me say a few words?" Meng Wan was thinking: she couldn't have come all this way for nothing. She'd already made enemies of the agency heads, so she had to do something to improve her image in everyone's eyes.

"Go ahead."

Meng Wan launched into a torrent of words. "I think the development of Yuyang's tourism industry can't rest solely on the efforts of us agency heads sitting here. The government's side needs to lend more support too. Only when top and bottom are of one mind can our city's economy be full of vitality, and the people of Yuyang live good lives."

Raise the lofty principle first—that way the suggestion that follows becomes hard to refuse.

Wang Zhongxian heard the meaning beneath her words and narrowed his eyes. "What is it you want the government to support you with?"

"Not me—Yuyang's tourism industry." Meng Wan promptly drew a set of materials she'd compiled from her bag. "This is a summary I've put together of the current shortcomings of Yuyang as a tourist city, along with targeted solutions. Please take a look."

Secretary Little Liu stepped forward to take it and laid it before Wang Zhongxian.

Wang Zhongxian leafed through the stack of more than a dozen pages, every one of them densely and neatly filled in. Clearly a great deal of effort had gone into it.

The atmosphere fell quiet for a time, with only the rhythmic rustle of pages turning one after another echoing through the room.

Roughly a dozen minutes later, Wang Zhongxian finally lifted his head again and stared thoughtfully at Meng Wan for a long while, then passed the materials to Qi Hongwei beside him. "Everyone take a look. This is all firsthand material from someone working on the front line. We who sit in offices all day really should be ashamed by comparison."

Meng Wan hurriedly waved her hand in modesty. "No, no, I just paid a little extra attention, that's all."

For Deputy Mayor Wang to go so far as to say "ashamed by comparison"—Zhou Quan, intensely curious, leaned in close and read along too.

Meng Wan's material listed out, one by one, the existing problems of Yuyang's tourism industry: transportation, lodging, the development of scenic spots and attractions, and more.

Some of it Zhou Quan already knew. He'd raised these very problems with the bureau chief himself, but for lack of funds the higher-ups kept dragging their feet without resolving them. The public restroom problem, for instance—he'd raised it many times, yet it never got implemented.

Other parts Zhou Quan hadn't even thought of—only someone like Meng Wan, working on the front line, would run into such concrete, real-world problems. Things like insufficient convenience for overseas tourists, the lack of bilingual Chinese-English city signage.

Finally she even put forward an idea of her own: she wanted to build a large amusement facility in Yuyang, which would draw more people to come and tour.

Indeed, every point in there was something Yuyang urgently needed to improve and upgrade right now—things that required the leadership and the higher-ups to do the work and fight for financial backing.

In the end it all came down to a lack of money. With money, never mind a few dozen public restrooms, even an amusement park could be built. But where was the money to come from?

This was what worried the leaders, and what all the people of Yuyang were desperate to know.

Wang Zhongxian tapped his fingers and pondered for a while, then said solemnly, "These materials of yours—I'll take them back and study them carefully together with the other city leaders. Some of these problems we're already working hard to solve, and some I'm hearing about for the first time today. As long as something benefits Yuyang's economic development and benefits the common people of Yuyang, we'll do it."

At this point he paused, looking around at everyone.

"But between wanting to do something and actually achieving it, there's still a long stretch of cliff we must keep climbing. I hope you can all trust our Yuyang leadership and give us some time. As you all surely know, Yuyang is now vigorously developing commercial housing. Once the developments open and the homes are sold, the fiscal revenue gained from this will be poured into our infrastructure construction. We stand together with every person who contributes to Yuyang's development, and I hope you agency heads will likewise have the awareness and the sense of responsibility not to fixate only on the petty profits in front of you. Before making any decision, you must first consider the image of our city. In the tourism business, image represents reputation, and only with a good reputation will people be willing to come and spend. It's all positive feedback."

Wang Zhongxian spoke these words with great sincerity, and Meng Wan knew well enough that leaders had their own hardships. Since reform and opening up, every corner of the country had been throwing itself into building the economy in a fever of activity. To make money you first had to put money in—there was no such thing as catching a wolf with empty hands.

Where was the money to come from? From local government finances, of course. In some places the treasury might bring in less than a single small boss from Xiangjiang made in trade—essentially destitute. Yuyang, leaning on this blessed patch of land, could just barely squeeze into the middle tier, already ahead of quite a few cities.

Once again Qian Zhenyu was the first to respond, brimming with energy as he boasted, "Deputy Mayor Wang, rest assured—our agency will absolutely set an example, answer your call, and devote ourselves to Yuyang's tourism industry for life!"

In the end Meng Wan gave a satisfied nod. Say what you would, sometimes these bootlickers really did have a pleasant ring to their words.

The meeting ended, and once most people had left, Meng Wan stayed behind to thank Zhou Quan.

"Section Chief Zhou, thank you for responding so promptly just now."

Zhou Quan glanced at the now-empty office and said, "I didn't really do anything. It was you and Deputy Mayor Wang who played it off so well together."

After that performance of Meng Wan's, anyone could see she'd had it premeditated. She and Deputy Mayor Wang had pulled off a borrow-the-force-to-strike-the-force maneuver, and the others, grievances or not, didn't dare say a word.

Meng Wan waved a hand and said with a laugh, "It was all thanks to Deputy Mayor Wang's good leadership. I just rode the slope to lead the donkey down—did my best to make sure his purpose for the meeting wasn't spoiled by me."

Zhou Quan's expression turned earnest. "What you did really can improve the bad practices in Yuyang's tourism industry. But cutting off people's livelihood—I worry that sooner or later someone's going to trip you up."

For all that those agency heads were fawning and pledging loyalty before Deputy Mayor Wang today, in their hearts they probably already hated Meng Wan to the bone. With just a few sentences from her, they'd signed some inexplicable guarantee that cut off more than half their income. There was no way they'd take it lying down.

It wasn't that Meng Wan hadn't considered this, but it was already the best solution she could think of for the moment. She couldn't have the fish and the bear's paw both—she had that much sense.

"Thank you for your concern, Section Chief Zhou. I'll stay on my guard at all times. I believe that as long as our agency keeps itself upright and aboveboard, and every cent we earn is clean, then there's nothing to fear."

"All right." Seeing how confident she was, Zhou Quan caught a bit of her spirit. "Then here's wishing your agency grows bigger and bigger, adding bricks and mortar to Yuyang's economic construction."

"Thank you, Section Chief Zhou."

"Oh, by the way." Zhou Quan thought of his friend again. "How far along have things gotten between you and Qingyang?"

Yesterday Song Qingyang had come to ask him to help explain the matter of Meng Wan's agency, and to put in a few good words when the time came. From his manner and tone, Zhou Quan had drawn a conclusion: Song Qingyang liked Meng Wan. He'd never seen him run himself ragged so attentively for any girl. Since his friend clearly had intentions, he had to put in some effort for the man's lifelong happiness too.

"How far along between us?" Meng Wan was puzzled. "Did Song Qingyang say something to you?"

"He didn't, no." Zhou Quan said smugly, "I guessed it all myself."

"You guessed he's helping me because there's something special between us?"

"Isn't there?"

With a stiff, mirthless smile, Meng Wan explained awkwardly, "Section Chief Song is my father's student, so he's probably paying a bit more attention to my affairs than usual. There's nothing at all between us. And besides... besides, I already have a boyfriend."

Even though Meng Wan knew Song Qingyang's concern for her really did exceed that of an ordinary friend, she now had James, so she had to draw a clear line. These words today Zhou Quan would surely relay to Song Qingyang, and on hearing them he'd understand what she meant.

Now it was Zhou Quan's turn to feel awkward. He rubbed the tip of his nose and apologized with mild surprise, "Sorry, I overstepped. I didn't realize there was that kind of connection between you. I wish you and your boyfriend every happiness."

------

Once the brief awkwardness passed and everything was explained, Meng Wan returned to the agency with her heart at ease, gurgling along like a babbling brook. The moment she stepped in the door she saw Lan Xiuli, Luo Zhigang and Jiang Ke, Liu Fei and Xiaohai, Xu Qiuyun and Old Bi—everyone was there.

"Such a full house today?" Meng Wan said happily, lifting the bag strap up over her head. "Perfect—now I don't have to notify everyone one by one."

A guilty, worried look on his face, Luo Zhigang asked, "How'd it go? Did you get it explained to the leadership? They didn't criticize you, did they?"

Old Bi asked anxiously too, "Did that letter of proof do any good?"

Xiaohai was even more protective. "Master, did they bully you? I'll go win it back for you!"

Meng Wan looked at the roomful of people, her eyes brimming with smiles. "Don't worry, everything's fine. Not only is everything fine, I even did the leadership a big favor. From now on our agency's name will carry weight before the mayor himself."

"You're Jiang Ke, right?" Meng Wan looked at the sunny young man beside Luo Zhigang. "Are you here to report for duty today?"

Jiang Ke nodded nervously. "Brother Luo told me—he said our agency's short on guides, and since I've got experience leading tours, you had him come ask whether I'd be willing to join."

He'd never imagined that the person he'd glimpsed in passing that day at the train station would turn out to be his future boss—and such a young, pretty one at that.

"So are you willing?"

Fidgeting with his hands, Jiang Ke said in a daze, "I'm very grateful to Manager Meng for giving me this job opportunity. I'll definitely cherish it, work hard, and not let you down."

Out of university, work was hard to find. There were no assigned placements anymore; everyone had to seek their own way out. His cohort had it even worse, hitting the wave of layoffs on top of it. Most of his classmates still hadn't found jobs, so the day he heard Luo Zhigang's offer, he'd agreed on the spot without a second word. Having a job and earning money mattered more than anything—worst case, if it didn't suit him, he could look for other work later.

"Then you'll start by shadowing Luo Zhigang on a tour, learning our agency's style. You've got the basics, so next time you'll be able to lead a tour on your own."

"Thank you, Manager Meng." Jiang Ke looked at Meng Wan, opening his mouth as if to say something, then closing it again.

"If you've got something on your mind, just say it. The people at our agency are all straight shooters—none of that beating around the bush."

"It's just—I have a classmate, a girl. Her spoken English is excellent, and she hasn't found a job yet either, so I wanted to ask on her behalf whether you still need a foreign-language guide here."

Jiang Ke meant a female classmate of his named Yu Fei, one of the top students in their major. Because her family circumstances were poor, she urgently needed to find a paying job to support herself.

"That'd be wonderful—I've been fretting over not having enough hands. Bring her by first so I can have a look. If she's a good fit, she can start on the spot."

"Then I'll thank you on her behalf. I'll bring her by tomorrow."

That settled, Meng Wan thought back to the meeting in the office just now. "Oh, Old Bi—back when you all went to the tourism bureau for meetings, how were the seats assigned?"

Old Bi glanced at Meng Wan and said in a low tone, "At first it was just sit wherever. Then one time Qian Zhenyu proposed seating people by the size of their agency, and our agency got put dead last."

He gathered Meng Wan must be feeling a bit indignant over being stuck in that godforsaken last seat. The first time Old Bi sat there, his mood had been sour too—but with their agency ranking dead last in both scale and profit, he hadn't had the standing to push for a seat further up.

Meng Wan nodded thoughtfully. So that was it. It seemed they'd have to pick up the pace and inch their way forward, bit by bit!

Seeing Meng Wan's tone light and her expression relaxed, everyone gathered she hadn't suffered any punishment, and they all breathed easier too.

Who'd have thought that within just a few days of the agency changing hands, they'd be hit with fake news in the papers and then summoned by the leadership for a meeting—not a single day's peace, one thing after another.

Meng Wan collected her thoughts and called out, "Everyone find a seat first. I've got something to say to you all."

Chapter 47

When Meng Wan arrived, the Lan family was already at supper. She'd been worn out all afternoon, and the moment she caught the savory smell of stir-fried food, her stomach gave a loud growl. She hesitated for all of a second before deciding to cadge a meal there.

She wheeled her bicycle into the courtyard, patted the dust off her clothes, and walked straight up to the door. It wasn't shut, so she gave it a polite knock.

Knock, knock, knock.

A whole roomful of seven or eight people turned their heads toward the door at once.

Lan Xiuli looked at this acquaintance she'd only just seen that afternoon, unable for a moment to grasp how she came to be here.

"Meng Wan?"

"Sister Xiuli." Meng Wan flashed her a brilliant smile. "I had some business over this way, and I heard from my brother that your family lives around here. I thought I'd try my luck and see if I could find the place—and look, I really did."

Lan Xiuli's sister-in-law, Hu Xiuxiang, looked Meng Wan up and down. In the lamplight she saw the red dot still on the girl's forehead and the dust all over her, and, taking her for some ordinary working girl, paid her no further mind and went on eating.

Lan Xiuli looked at Meng Wan, somewhat at a loss. She knew the matter was surely not as simple as she'd made it sound, but the girl had come all the way to her door, and as host she couldn't very well say nothing.

"What a coincidence. Have you eaten? If not, why not join us?"

Hu Xiuxiang's face dropped at once and she shot Lan Xiuli a glare. The food was made to fit exactly; add one more bowl and pair of chopsticks and everyone else got a mouthful less. And today of all days they'd made braised pork—there wasn't even enough for themselves, yet here she was playing the gracious hostess.

Anyone with sense knew Lan Xiuli's words were a mere courtesy, the kind to which the other party normally replied, "No, no, I've eaten," or "I won't, I'll just say a couple of words and be on my way."

But Meng Wan, of all people, was someone who never played by the script. She patted her belly with a smile. "Perfect, I happen to be hungry. Then I won't stand on ceremony."

Lan Xiuli's mother was a reasonable enough woman. A guest was a guest, and since the girl didn't turn up her nose at their food, why not have her sit and eat too.

The old lady, seated nearest the kitchen, got up at once to set out a bowl and chopsticks for Meng Wan. "Come, child, you sit beside Xiuli."

So saying, she nudged her younger daughter, seated nearby, over to one side, and the girl, looking thoroughly unwilling, shuffled aside to make room.

Meng Wan said her thanks and promptly pulled up a stool to squeeze in between the two of them, picked up her chopsticks and bowl, and fell to eating with gusto.

"This braised pork is really good."

She didn't forget to offer a review between bites, either.

Hu Xiuxiang watched Meng Wan pick up piece after piece of the meat, grinding her back teeth in fury, and jabbed her husband beside her with an elbow, signaling him to say something.

She couldn't even bear to eat this meat herself; little Fei had barely had a few pieces. And here this girl was, brazenly stuffing herself with relish.

Lan Xiuli's elder brother had never seen anyone make herself so at home either, but he was a grown man—how could he say anything to a young girl? So he just lowered his head and went on eating.

Meng Wan paid not the slightest attention to the way everyone at the table was looking at her. As she ate, she even picked out food for Lan Xiuli's bowl. "Sister Xiuli, you eat more too. You're far too thin—it's not good for your health."

Lan Xiuli smiled awkwardly, her own chopsticks hovering, unable to bring themselves down. To eat was wrong, and not to eat was wrong.

What on earth had this Meng Wan come for? Wasn't the Meng family a household of scholars? How had they raised a daughter so... so casual about decorum?

The Lan family hardly chatted at all over meals—perhaps too, with this outsider Meng Wan present, they didn't feel free to discuss family matters. The whole meal passed in silence.

Meng Wan, all but full, set down her chopsticks in contentment and, taking in the looks on every face at the table, said cheerfully, "Go on, eat—don't just watch me."

Hu Xiuxiang pursed her lips and angrily picked up a chopstick-full of greens to chew.

You've eaten to your heart's content, all right—the meat's all gone, every last bit in your stomach.

Afraid her sister-in-law's temper might boil over into something unpleasant, Lan Xiuli quickly drew Meng Wan into conversation. "Who is it you came to see around here?"

"Actually, I came specially to see you."

"To see me?"

They'd only met for the first time that afternoon; surely they weren't close enough for her to come calling at the house. Or had Meng Xingyang told this little sister of his something?

That didn't seem right, though. She knew Meng Xingyang—the man prized his reputation above all and would never breathe a word of something like this to anyone.

Meng Wan said, unhurried, "I came to recruit talent."

"Surely the talent you mean isn't me?" Lan Xiuli puzzled. How did this girl leap from one thing to the next?

"It's exactly you." Meng Wan smiled and explained for the whole family's benefit. "I run a travel agency of my own, and right now I'm hiring tour guides who can speak English. I heard from my brother that Sister Xiuli studied English—and fertile water shouldn't flow into someone else's field, am I right? So I came today to talk over a job with Sister Xiuli. I was wondering whether she'd be interested in trying something more challenging, work that could bring her greater rewards."

The moment she heard Meng Wan was a young boss, Hu Xiuxiang's attitude changed on the spot, and she asked with a smiling face, "Miss, when you say 'greater rewards,' just how much are we talking?"

Money, of course, was what she cared about most.

"The rewards I mean aren't measured in money alone. An English-speaking guide deals every day with the upper and middle classes from abroad—it's bound to broaden your horizons and show you a far wider world. Sometimes an open mind matters more than money."

That part was meant for Lan Xiuli's ears.

Then she turned to the rest of the Lan family. "Of course, if Sister Xiuli does well, the money certainly won't be lacking. Right now there's a real shortage of English-speaking guides here in Yuyang—plenty of people want to do it, but they don't have the ability. Once Sister Xiuli joins my agency, I'll train her up properly and make her our star guide. Earn while you're young: at Sister Xiuli's age, she could retire by thirty-five."

"That much money?" Hu Xiuxiang's eyes lit up instantly. "Then what's there to discuss? Go, hurry up and go!"

Lan Xiuli's mother was swayed by Meng Wan's words too, and looked at her daughter. "Xiuli, why don't you give it a try?"

She had no idea what a tour guide actually did, but anything that made money was surely good work.

Having heard the whole spiel out, Lan Xiuli finally understood what medicine Meng Wan was peddling in that gourd of hers.

She was trying to get her to resign from the Surveying and Mapping Bureau—to put her well clear of her brother.

Not a chance!

"Come outside with me a moment." Lan Xiuli stood up, her expression cold, and walked out of the courtyard without a backward glance.

Meng Wan said a few words to the Lan family, picked up her bag, and followed her out into the yard.

Hands on her hips, Lan Xiuli turned on her without ceremony. "You came today because you want me to quit and stay away from your brother, isn't that right? You came specially to my house to say it because you knew my family would pressure me, so I'd find it hard to refuse you—isn't that it?"

Meng Wan neither confirmed nor denied it. Lan Xiuli had it essentially right, but there was one more point she did want to clear up. "I do want you to stay away from my brother. But my travel agency genuinely does need a guide who can handle foreign tourists. So please, don't let your feelings run away with you—give my offer some serious thought."

"Ha." Lan Xiuli wore an I-knew-it look. "No need. I'm not going."

Meng Wan went on, patient and earnest. "What is it you see in my brother? Just because he said a few comforting words to you? You've been married once before—you of all people should know how hard a marriage is. Your own wasn't whole; why break apart another family? If my brother divorced my sister-in-law for you, what's to stop him divorcing you for someone else tomorrow? You're so lovely, and so well educated—why waste yourself on a small-time section chief like my brother? Just let go of this one fixation, and I promise your future will be bright as anything."

"But I'm a woman!" Lan Xiuli argued stubbornly. "A woman's best destiny is to find a good man and marry him—and your brother is that good man!"

"Completely and utterly wrong!" Meng Wan squared off to reason with her. "A woman's destiny was never to be someone's wife or someone's mother. A woman is a person, and a person is born to be herself—every other identity is a false notion society has drilled into you. Break out of it, and you can be a tour guide, you can have a travel agency of your own like me. I know a few friends down south—some run clothing shops, some little eateries—and every one of them is worth ten thousand yuan now. With money, what kind of man can't you find? With money you can meet a better man—you might even marry a foreigner and move abroad, and then you'd never have to deal with all this household mess again."

This long lecture left Lan Xiuli's head spinning. She wanted to retort but couldn't get the words out—she'd always been the slow one, and the moment an argument started she fell apart completely.

"Whatever you say, I'm not going. You can talk all you like, it won't change anything."

With that she spun around in a temper and stalked back into the house.

To her ears, everything Meng Wan had said was heresy and crooked talk. She really couldn't fathom how the Meng family had brought up a child like this.

"You think it over carefully. I'll be waiting," Meng Wan called after her furious, retreating back. "I'll give you a week to decide. Miss this village and there won't be another inn!"

She deliberately called it out at the top of her voice, precisely so the family inside would hear clearly. Lan Xiuli was very obedient to her family; this wasn't a decision she could make on her own—and that was exactly the point of Meng Wan's visit today.

Before leaving, Meng Wan set twenty yuan on the windowsill. She couldn't very well eat their meal for nothing, after all.

Back home, the matter of Zhou Nian's schooling was on Meng Wan's mind. The moment she walked in and saw Sun Jing, she took her by the hand and pulled her into her own bedroom.

Sun Jing said she'd spoken to the dean of students about Zhou Nian's situation, and what he'd told her matched roughly what she'd expected.

In a case like Zhou Nian's, he could only attend as a transfer student, and would have to return to his place of household registration to sit the college entrance exam. This semester was already nearly over; if they truly meant to have him study in Yuyang, then they should get his paperwork ready and bring both the boy and his documents over together next semester.

The school would also be assessing Zhou Nian—if his foundation was too weak, they might not be willing to take him.

Meng Wan assured Sun Jing she'd have everything ready before the start of next semester, and would make up all the coursework Zhou Nian had fallen behind on, so that he could begin from the second year of high school.

It was Sun Jing's reminder that made Meng Wan realize the day after tomorrow was the college entrance exam.

She'd have to make a trip to the Lin household tomorrow to rally Lin Xi's spirits. It hadn't been easy for the girl to come this far—she had to get through this one exam without a hitch.

After a full day of running about, now that she'd finally come to rest, Meng Wan looked at the jade dog sitting on her desk, the gift she'd never managed to deliver, and an indescribable bitterness rose in her chest.

Her mind wandered wildly. Was James with that woman right now? How far had the two of them already gone?

Were they getting married?

Lying on the bed, Meng Wan twisted herself into a wormlike curl, guessing at it all.

At last she shot upright. No—she had to find James and have it out plainly. Even if he already had someone he loved, the matter of the birthday still needed clearing up. She hadn't missed it on purpose, and even ordinary friends deserved a proper ending.

That settled it!

Chapter 45

Meng Wan held the same pose, her tone severe. "Don't you have something you want to tell me?"

Meng Xingyang looked utterly mystified, frowning at her. "I should be asking you that—don't you have something you want to tell me?"

Had she taken the wrong medicine? Yesterday he'd done her such a huge favor and not gotten so much as a thank-you, and today she'd come marching to his door to take him to task. As if he owed her something.

"As a matter of fact, I do." Meng Wan lowered her hands, met his eyes levelly, and curved her lips into a textbook-perfect arc. "I'd like to thank my brother, Comrade Meng Xingyang, for arranging the inspection delegation for me yesterday. Your little sister was in rather low spirits last night and so failed to express her gratitude in good time, and has come specially today to thank you in person."

Meng Xingyang pulled a face. Now that was more like it.

The next second Meng Wan resumed her original posture and said expressionlessly, "Now it's your turn."

Meng Xingyang was driven half mad. "What on earth do you want me to say?"

Meng Wan had no interest in dragging it out. She cut straight to it. "Tell me about you and that Miss Lan just now."

Meng Xingyang froze for a moment, his eyes evading hers. "What's there to tell about me and her? What nonsense are you talking?"

If he wouldn't shed a tear till he saw the coffin, fine. Meng Wan carefully fished the wavy strand of hair out of her pocket and held it up in front of him. "I found this on the back of your chair. Give me a reasonable explanation."

So that was all—he'd thought she'd discovered something. It was just a hair.

Meng Xingyang let out a breath of relief and gave a mocking snort. "That day she came to deliver some documents while I was watering the plants. I told her to set them on the desk for me, and then she tripped and sat down in the chair. The hair must've caught on it then."

"That's not it."

"What's not it?" Meng Xingyang was telling the plain truth. What could a single hair possibly prove?

"The way she looks at you—that's not right."

The hair could be explained away as he said, for now. But a person's eyes don't lie, and the way Lan Xiuli had looked at Meng Xingyang was unmistakably the loving gaze a woman gives the man she's fallen for.

"Am I supposed to control how she looks at me?" Meng Xingyang was rather taken aback that his usually careless, happy-go-lucky sister could be so sharp-eyed. He grabbed his teacup and took a tactical sip of tea.

Meng Wan knew Meng Xingyang understood exactly what she was getting at—he was just playing dumb now, trying to muddle his way through it. But since she'd found out today, she wasn't about to let it slide.

"Brother." Meng Wan began to play the emotional card. "You're the only brother I have. I want you and Sister-in-law and Zhizhi to be happy, the three of you together. You and Sister-in-law are my model of what a husband and wife should be. So please, treasure this marriage. Don't let some little temptation lure you away. The wildflowers out there aren't right for you, and there's no room in our family for a single wildflower."

"What is it you've found out?"

So there really was something!

"I already know all about you and Lan Xiuli," Meng Wan said, casting her line.

"She told you everything?" Meng Xingyang was thoroughly startled.

"That's right." Meng Wan nodded. "She told me everything."

"How did she put it to you?"

"However she put it to you, that's how she put it to me."

The two of them traded gloriously empty nonsense back and forth.

Meng Xingyang seemed genuinely to believe Lan Xiuli had told Meng Wan everything. Since his sister already knew, there was nothing left to hide. He let out a long sigh and prepared to lay it all out.

Around the time Lan Xiuli's husband died, Meng Xingyang happened to be working on a project that had him running over to the Archives Section constantly. Every time, he'd see her quietly wiping away tears, looking each day as though the sky had fallen—as if her whole life had ended along with her husband's.

Seeing such a young woman in the bloom of life like that, he couldn't help offering her a few words of consolation. He talked things through with her and urged her to look forward.

One thing led to another, and the two of them gradually grew familiar, and Lan Xiuli's spirits slowly recovered.

Later, when that project of his ended, he had less reason to visit the Archives Section, and naturally the two of them saw each other far less often.

After that, he began to notice that he'd run into Lan Xiuli now and then on his way to and from work. At first he'd assumed it was mere coincidence, but as time went on, even a man as dense as he couldn't fail to see that she was doing it on purpose.

As for why she was doing it—well, between a man and a woman, there's only ever the one thing.

Meng Xingyang didn't want to spell it out. After all, Lan Xiuli was a young woman, and he had to spare her dignity.

But in Lan Xiuli's eyes, this became Meng Xingyang tacitly permitting her advances, and so she grew bolder still. Just a week ago, using the excuse of delivering archives, she'd come to his office and laid bare her heart.

Meng Xingyang had refused her on the spot, explaining that he had a family and was truly no good match for her. But Lan Xiuli had said she didn't care—she could wait, and she didn't even need the title of wife.

This had scared Meng Xingyang badly. For all that he carried himself outside as a poised, unflappable cadre, the very picture of calm, when faced with something like this he genuinely had no idea what to do.

Sun Jing was the only girlfriend he'd ever had, and then the two of them had married. As for relations between men and women, apart from his mother Cheng Xuehua there was only his sister Meng Wan, and now Zhizhi besides.

Every other woman in his life was either a colleague or a classmate, kept to a surface-level courtesy and nothing more. Now that someone had suddenly broken through that boundary, he really was at a loss.

Watching a beautiful, fragile young woman weep before him, all pear-blossoms-in-the-rain—say he wasn't moved in the slightest, that wasn't possible either. But the difference between man and beast is this: however great the temptation before him, reason would still surface to stop him, to tell him this was wrong.

Of course, none of this last part did he say to Meng Wan. In his telling, he had refused Lan Xiuli righteously and firmly on the spot, without the slightest hesitation.

After Meng Xingyang's flat refusal, today had been the first time the two of them had met again—and who'd have thought Meng Wan would run into it. The nerve of her, coming to say all this to his family. Truly shameless.

Listening to her brother's account, judging from his tone and expression, Meng Wan was provisionally willing to believe nothing had happened between them. But clearly Lan Xiuli hadn't achieved her aim—she would come again. It was only because Meng Wan happened to be there today that the two of them had had no chance to be alone. Once they met often enough, with the woman simpering and shedding a few more tears, there was no guarantee Meng Xingyang wouldn't waver.

Meng Wan thought for a moment and decided it would be best to have Meng Xingyang get Lan Xiuli transferred away. That was the simplest solution.

"Transfer her away?"

Meng Xingyang felt his sister was making a mountain out of a molehill. There was nothing between them—transferring her away would only make it look as if he had something to hide.

"What's the matter—can't bear to part with her?"

"Nonsense!" Meng Xingyang flushed with anger. "What's there to bear? She's doing her job perfectly well. What reason do I have to transfer her?"

A reason was indeed needed. Right now nothing had happened, and abruptly transferring her like this would only make Meng Xingyang look like a petty section chief throwing his weight around.

She'd have to think it through carefully. "What's Lan Xiuli's family situation?"

Meng Xingyang was about to answer, but meeting Meng Wan's probing gaze, he hastened to clear himself first. "I only heard about it secondhand—I never went and looked into it on purpose."

"All right, all right, just tell me."

A grown man, dithering on and on.

"What I heard is, her family's not in great shape. She's got an older brother and a younger sister. After her husband died she moved back to her parents' home, where it was already crowded as it was—adding her made it even more packed, everyone crammed in together. Her brother and sister-in-law have been hunting for a new match for her too, wanting to marry her off quickly so she'll free up some space at home."

A hard-luck case as well, then.

Meng Wan asked again, "How much does she earn on her own?"

"How would I know that?"

"Just roughly."

"My salary's eight hundred a month. She's a lower rank than me, so probably four or five hundred."

"Do you know what her education is?"

Meng Xingyang thought about it, uncertain. "I think she's got a junior-college degree. I don't know the school, but I remember she told me she studied English."

"She studied English?" Meng Wan's voice rose with sudden excitement.

"That's right, why?"

What did that have to do with transferring her away?

"I've got a plan. Leave this to me." Meng Wan rubbed her hands together, raring to go. "What you need to do is steer clear of her for now, keep yourself in line, and don't do a single thing to wrong my sister-in-law. Otherwise—"

She drew a hand like a blade across her own throat. "—this'll be your end."

Meng Xingyang was startled into laughter by the childish gesture. A grown woman in her twenties, and her way of threatening people was still stuck at fourteen or fifteen.

Meng Wan hadn't forgotten the actual business that had brought her here either. She asked Meng Xingyang for the inspection delegation's roster, their arrival time, and contact information.

Meng Xingyang had long since prepared it all for her; he'd been planning to bring it home for her after work that day anyway.

Once everything was settled, Meng Wan pedaled her bike back to the travel agency.

When she got there, both Luo Zhigang and Liu Fei happened to be in. Meng Wan handed Liu Fei the delegation's roster and told him to take Xiao Hai with him to receive them tomorrow, making sure to get all the paperwork and receipts in order—when it came to claiming the state subsidy, the most important thing was having complete documentation.

She had a few words for Luo Zhigang too, warning him not to take it upon himself to steer the guests into jade shops to spend money. There was a crackdown on right now, and getting caught would hurt the agency's reputation.

Luo Zhigang nodded with a look of impatience, and there was no telling how much of it had actually sunk in.

"Old Bi." Meng Wan couldn't be bothered with him. "I've found you an assistant. She should be able to start tomorrow. When she does, you'll have to teach her properly—go through the books with her, sort everything out so she understands where we currently stand, so that when you retire she can take over from you."

Old Bi had his head down over the account book. At her words he looked up and pushed up his glasses. "I was just about to bring this up with you, and here you've gone and found someone already. As expected of a leader—always thinking of everything."

Meng Wan smiled, took her seat, and got down to her own work.

She might be leading fewer tours these days, but her workload hadn't shrunk one bit. The whole agency, inside and out, was hers to run. Whatever she left undone today would still be her work tomorrow—there was no slacking off even if she wanted to.

So far, the first two of the new chief's three fires were burning along in good order. But the third still had no clear shape. A makeover took money, and money was exactly what she lacked most right now.

Their agency had two rooms, front and back. The front room was the everyday office, with four desks, a filing cabinet, and a small counter near the door. The back room served as a rest room and a storeroom both, with a single bed, a water basin, and the rest of the space piled with all manner of odds and ends.

The day she'd gone in and rummaged through it, she'd even dug up newspapers twenty years old.

Meng Wan planned to replace all these battered, rickety desks and chairs, give the walls a fresh coat of paint, clear out all that junk in the back room, swap in a new bed, and add a spot for washing up—so that if anyone ever found themselves stuck too late to get home, they could just stay at the agency.

Everything else was easy enough; it was the furniture that was pricey. Meng Wan mulled over where she might scrounge up some secondhand pieces—cheap and practical both.

Mulling it over, she thought of the desk in her own bedroom, which had been hauled back from Meng Qingyan's school.

That was it!

What a leader she was. The transfer fee had come from Cheng Xuehua, the inspection delegation from Meng Xingyang, and now for the furniture she was counting on Meng Qingyan. She really didn't let a single useful person slip by.

But then again, she reflected, it'd be foolish not to use ready resources laid right in front of you. This was simply making rational use of every resource to grow and strengthen herself.

And only once she'd grown and strengthened herself could she go on to help even more people profit!

Right—that was exactly the logic.

Hypnotizing herself as she went, Meng Wan grabbed her bag, and before her seat had even warmed up she went hurrying out the door again.

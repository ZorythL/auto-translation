Chapter 29

In the span of a single hour, Meng Wan's situation had been turned completely on its head. She didn't know how to explain things to Cheng Xuehua, and she didn't know what place she truly held in James's heart.

"Meng Wan!" Seeing her walk faster and faster, James reached out to catch her.

In a fit of temper, Meng Wan flung off his hand and kept marching ahead, head down, sulking and seething.

She didn't even know what she was angry about. Angry that James had teamed up with Xu Tian without telling her? Angry that all he had eyes for was profit? Or angry that he didn't hold her in his heart?

Meng Wan couldn't put it into words. There seemed to be two little figures arguing above her head, one pleading her case, the other defending James. There was nothing between them—he was a businessman, of course he had to put profit first. How could he be expected to give up gains within easy reach for the sake of one ordinary friend?

"Meng Wan!"

Afraid that in her recklessness she'd get hit by a car, James seized her by the wrist and made her stop.

"Where are you going?"

"Where I go is none of your business!" Meng Wan strained hard to wrench free of his grip, but he was far stronger than she was, and her wrist stayed clamped fast in his hand.

"Won't you calm down for me?" Half-bending almost in entreaty, James braced his hands on her shoulders, lowering his head to her level, his eyes fixed straight on her face. His voice gentled. "Let's go wash up and change first, and I'll explain everything to you bit by bit—all right?"

Meng Wan was the sort who yielded to softness but not to force. James's near-pleading words were like a ladleful of cool water, dousing seven or eight tenths of the fire smoldering in her heart.

This man was truly infuriating—always speaking so gently that you couldn't stay angry even when you wanted to.

Seeing Meng Wan's ruffled feathers gradually smoothing back down, James took off his own jacket to drape it over her.

Meng Wan instinctively put up a hand to block him. "It's dirty."

His clothes were clearly not cheap. No sense getting them soiled over a dress of hers.

James's lips curved up faintly as he carried on regardless, settling the jacket over Meng Wan's shoulders. Then he hailed a taxi and took her back to the hotel where he was staying.

The whole way there, Meng Wan kept her left shoulder propped up with her hand, carefully cradling his jacket. James's citrus scent surrounded her, the fragrance drifting into her nose, soothing her mood considerably from the storm of moments ago.

At the hotel, James went to the front desk and asked the manager for a fresh set of toiletries and towels. Meng Wan trailed behind him and the two of them took the elevator up.

"You wash up first. I'll wait in the lobby. When you're done, call the front desk and I'll come up."

Meng Wan understood he was worried she might have reservations. "Just wait in the sitting room—no need to go to all that trouble."

That much trust they had in each other.

James turned his head and looked at her, his eyes full of tenderness. "All right. As you say."

Inside the room, Meng Wan carefully took off James's jacket and inspected it closely for stains. Even though she'd been so careful, some splashes of soup had inevitably gotten on it.

"It's fine, just leave it there."

James helped her set the toiletries in the bathroom. "Go on and shower first—I've laid everything out for you. The blue bottle is shampoo, the orange one's body wash."

To Meng Wan's ears, these words sounded terribly intimate, like a couple long settled into life together, exchanging the small ordinary details of the day—and the more ordinary, the more intimate.

Meng Wan bit lightly at her lower lip and gave a dazed little nod. She was just turning to go when something occurred to her. "I don't have anything to change into!"

What was she supposed to wear after her shower?

Stumped by her question, James suddenly remembered something. "Wait here a moment."

He turned and went into the bedroom, and when he came back out he was carrying a bag.

"This dress is for you."

Curious, Meng Wan took the bag and peered inside. Within was a pale pink dress, its color neither too bright nor too cloying, like rose tea steeped through a few rinses of water.

She looked up at James, her face alight with surprise, then lifted the dress out of the bag and held it up by the shoulders against herself.

The style was the modified suit-dress popular these days—a small turned-down collar, gently cinched at the waist, the skirt falling away naturally. Just the kind she liked.

Meng Wan held the dress up to herself to compare. The size was a perfect fit.

"Did you buy this?"

Why would he buy a girl's dress? Was he meaning to give it to someone? Wan Qing?

The thought sent Meng Wan's mood plunging like a roller coaster, diving straight to the lowest point.

James smiled and stroked Meng Wan's head. "The other day when I went to the department store, I passed a clothing stall, and the moment I saw this dress it made me think of you, so I bought it. I'd been meaning to find a chance to give it to you—I just never expected the chance would come so suddenly."

That day he'd originally gone to the department store to scout it out. Passing the dress on display, he couldn't help stopping, imagining how Meng Wan would look wearing it, and on impulse simply bought it then and there, planning to give it to her later.

Meng Wan went blank for a moment, her eyes round, and then it was as if fireworks ignited deep in her eyes, bursting out with the light of joy. Her ears went hot too.

She clutched the dress and said bashfully, "So it was for me after all."

"Well? Do you like it?" It was James's first time buying clothes for a girl; he had no idea whether it would suit her taste.

Meng Wan couldn't keep the corners of her mouth from lifting. She nodded shyly. "I do."

Their eyes met in midair, and it was as if countless tiny currents crackled and sparked at the place where they crossed. James's blazing gaze made Meng Wan's heart skip a beat, as if she'd been singed; her eyes darted away—only to sneak another look after she'd dodged.

For a moment the air turned dense and viscous, like a marshmallow that wouldn't melt.

Meng Wan felt waves of heat washing over her, her cheeks beginning to burn. Then all at once a breeze came in through the window, stirring her hair faintly, and she snapped back to her senses.

"Um." She fumbled for words. "I'll go shower now."

And with that she fled, dress in arms, hurrying off toward the bathroom.

James, who'd been holding his breath all the while, quietly let it out now too.

His gaze followed Meng Wan all the way to the bathroom, his heart still racing and leaping. He pressed a hand to his chest and couldn't help asking himself in secret.

Was this what it felt like to fall for someone?

——

Meng Wan felt as if this shower had taken her nearly a century. In truth she'd long since finished, but she'd kept dawdling inside, unwilling to come out.

The steam in the bathroom hadn't yet cleared. Meng Wan looked at herself in the fogged-up mirror—a flushed little face set with a pair of bright, watery eyes, the fitted pink dress tracing the line of her waist cleanly, her fair chest bared to the air lending her an extra allure. This dress fit her as if it had been made to measure.

The thought that James had bought it specially for her kept tugging the corners of Meng Wan's mouth irresistibly upward.

It confirmed all the more for her the place she held in his heart. Even if it hadn't yet reached the point of love, fondness there certainly was.

When she came out at last, holding the dress that had been soiled with soup, James was at the writing desk in the sitting room. She watched him quietly and didn't go over to disturb him.

James wore an especially earnest expression when he wrote, each stroke careful and deliberate. Some characters had too many strokes; one pass through clearly wasn't enough for him to remember, so he'd write a whole neat row of them, and when one was too hard to remember he'd even form a word with it—a word being easier to fix in memory than a single character on its own.

Golden sunlight poured in through the glass window. Now and then the chatter of birds drifted in from outside, while inside all was still, only the soft scratch of the pen on paper to set the heart at peace.

She didn't know how long it had been when James, feeling a soreness in his wrist from writing, set down the pen and flexed his hand a few times. Only then did he look up and see Meng Wan standing not far off.

At that moment the sunlight fell across her skin, making her fair complexion brighter still, as though her whole body radiated light. The pink dress traced her figure in graceful curves, her fluffy black curls spilling over her shoulders, framing her face to make it look smaller, more delicate.

For a moment James was struck dumb, gazing in a daze at the loveliness before him, momentarily forgetting how to speak at all.

His stare made Meng Wan a little shy. "Well? Does it look good?"

"Amazing—"

James couldn't find any adjective to capture how radiant this beautiful girl before him was. Hers was an unadorned, effortless splendor; no word of praise for beauty applied to her could express even a ten-thousandth of it.

Meng Wan blinked her wide eyes at him blankly, not understanding.

"I mean—it's gorgeous. You look just like an Oriental Audrey Hepburn." James explained himself somewhat incoherently, lavishing his praise on Meng Wan without restraint.

Meng Wan felt flattered. Comparing her to Audrey Hepburn was surely going too far—but the next second she puffed out her lips, floating on air. Too far? Not really. After all, they were both great beauties.

Meng Wan draped her old clothes over the back of a chair and went to sit beside James, hands resting on her knees. "Thank you for the dress. I really like it."

"A lovely dress needs a lovely person to bring out its full charm."

The compliment sent Meng Wan's heart blooming with joy. This man must have eaten honey today—how else could one sweet line after another come tumbling out of him in a string?

James paused, hesitated a moment, then spoke up after all. "About what happened just now—I owe you an explanation."

Meng Wan's giddy thoughts were yanked back; she'd nearly forgotten the real matter at hand.

"Today's meeting was set up by Xu Tian. He knew I had my eye on the Yuhu Hotel, so he proposed that we jointly buy out the hotel and run it together."

The Yuhu Hotel was the very one James was now staying in.

Memories from her past life flashed swiftly through Meng Wan's mind. She remembered that in her last life, because Xu Tian had bought up that plot of land to build a hotel, he'd been short on capital and unable to take part in investing in the Yuhu Hotel.

Later the Yuhu Hotel had come to be jointly run by a mysterious foreign investor and the original state-owned unit. Because policy at the time permitted joint ventures but required the Chinese side to hold a controlling stake of more than 51 percent, the mysterious businessman had brought in his own "foreign brand" and management team, insisting his people must lead operations. Nominally the Chinese side held the larger share, but in practice purchasing, finance, and personnel were all in the foreigner's hands.

With an advanced set of foreign operating methods and philosophies, the Yuhu Hotel's business had grown ever more thriving, becoming one of the most talked-about hotels in Yuyang City at the time—the other being Xu Tian's resort hotel.

Just as everyone was scrambling to imitate the Yuhu Hotel's new operating model, the foreign investor suddenly died. Overnight the hotel lost its anchor, like a hungry, wailing infant robbed of its mother—it became an orphan.

By that time Xu Tian's hotel was already turning a profit, and he had capital enough to take over the Yuhu Hotel the foreigner had left behind. So not long after the foreigner's accident, Xu Tian bought the hotel out cheaply and became its true master.

So the mysterious foreigner had been James?

In her last life Meng Wan had helped Xu Tian secure that plot of land, leaving James empty-handed. He'd then turned to the Yuhu Hotel—and afterward died by accident?

The hairs on Meng Wan's body suddenly stood on end, a chill prickling up her back. If her guess was right, then James would die one year from now!

James noticed something was off with her. "What's the matter?" he asked, concerned.

Meng Wan grabbed James's hand, almost begging. "Could you please not invest in the Yuhu Hotel?"

James knit his brows in puzzlement, not understanding why she'd make such a request. "If this is because of Xu Tian, let me tell you—I only agreed to meet him today in order to turn down his offer. I have no intention of going into business with him."

Xu Tian didn't fit his criteria for a business partner—especially after the scene he'd staged at the restaurant today, which had laid his narrow, sinister nature utterly bare. Do business with a man like that, and you'd have to live in constant fear of betrayal, fear that some greater gain would tempt him to stab you in the back.

Meng Wan took small breaths, trying to calm herself. Investing in a hotel was a huge piece of business—how could James give it up so easily? Maybe she was reading too much into things. James might not have died because he took over the hotel; perhaps it was simply an ordinary accident.

But still she couldn't talk herself out of how monstrous a coincidence it would be. Xu Tian had long wanted to muscle into the Yuhu Hotel's business, and the moment he had capital enough, the mysterious foreigner died, letting him take the whole thing over painlessly. Where in the world did such convenient luck exist?

A blood-chilling thought slowly surfaced in Meng Wan's mind. Could it be... Xu Tian?

Her scalp prickled all over, her hands trembling uncontrollably, beads of cold sweat breaking out on her forehead.

"Meng Wan?" James gripped her shoulders. "What's wrong? Are you feeling ill somewhere?"

"James, I'm so cold, I'm so cold." Meng Wan's lips quivered, cold sweat slicking her back.

James pulled the blanket off the sofa and wrapped it around Meng Wan, gathering her tightly into his arms, his hands stroking ceaselessly over her back, his eyes full of worry. "Are you still cold? Should I take you to the hospital?"

"James, don't go." Meng Wan buried her head in James's chest, both arms wound around his neck, trying to steady her own spirit with his scent.

James coaxed and soothed her. "I'm not going anywhere—I won't go anywhere. I'm right here with you."

The two of them stayed like that, holding each other, until the sun slowly sank toward the west and Meng Wan, restless, fell asleep in James's arms.

It was not a peaceful sleep. She had a nightmare.

In the dream, Xu Tian had taken James up to the top floor of the hotel, his face contorted with malice as he shoved him over the edge. Meng Wan leapt after him too, straining with all her might to catch James's hand—but no matter how far forward she reached, there always remained a fist's width of distance between them. She just couldn't reach him, could only watch the gap between him and the ground shrink smaller and smaller.

In the instant before he hit the ground, Meng Wan jolted awake, her heart slamming like a drum that wouldn't stop, her forehead beaded with sweat, gasping for breath in great heaves.

"What is it—did you have a nightmare?" James's heart rose and fell with her every emotion.

Meng Wan looked at the living, breathing James before her, and only then did her heart settle back into place. He was still safe and sound—there was a whole year before his accident. She still had time enough to change James's fate. She would never let anything happen to him.

She reached up to caress James's cheek and, in a flat, even tone, dropped a bombshell. "James, let's be together."

Chapter 33

When school let out at noon and the front gates opened, the students poured out like fish flooding into a pond, slipping and sliding their separate ways home.

Sun Jing pushed her bicycle along with the crowd toward the exit. Normally she didn't go home at midday—the school was a fair distance from the Meng house, and the round trip took her over forty minutes by bike.

But today was different. Yesterday her sister-in-law had a furious row with her mother-in-law, and the atmosphere at home had turned to ice, a chill so deep it set her shivering. Early that morning everyone had gone about their day as usual—those with jobs to work, those with school to attend—but every face was pulled long. The sight of it left a knot in her chest, and she'd let out more than a few sighs during her classes.

Her sister-in-law had taken nothing with her when she left yesterday, so Sun Jing meant to slip home while the house was empty at noon, pack a few sets of clothes, and bring them to Xu Qiuyun at the cultural center. Xu Qiuyun was the only friend of Meng Wan's she knew. She figured perhaps Meng Wan was staying at Xu Qiuyun's, or that, at the very least, Xu Qiuyun would know where she was.

It was honestly the sort of thing to make people laugh: here she was, a sister-in-law who didn't know which work unit her own sister-in-law was at, didn't know where she lived—couldn't even figure out where to send her some clothes.

Sun Jing went first to Xu Qiuyun and asked if she knew where Meng Wan was. Xu Qiuyun guessed she might have gone to the Lin family's, and paged Lin Dong. Lin Dong got the message and called her back, telling Xu Qiuyun that Meng Wan was staying at his home.

Knowing where Meng Wan was, Sun Jing finally set her mind at ease. She handed the clothes over to Xu Qiuyun, said a few words of thanks, and asked her to talk some sense into Meng Wan—a bowed head and a quick apology, and the whole thing would blow over.

Xu Qiuyun said she'd pass the message along. On the phone Lin Dong had offered to drive over and pick up the clothes, but she said she'd rather go in person and see for herself how Meng Wan was doing; only then could she rest easy.

The moment she got off work, Xu Qiuyun pedaled hard for the Lin home. She hadn't turned in her wages for the past two months, and money held in hand was bound to draw covetous eyes, so she'd simply gone and bought a bicycle. Now her commute was a breeze.

She had been to the Lin home once before. The last time, after she and Meng Wan had visited a patient at the hospital, she'd kept worrying about Auntie Hao's health, and once she heard the older woman had been discharged, she'd had Lin Dong take her to look in on her again.

Sun Jing had been vague in her account, and the reason Meng Wan had left home remained a mystery to Xu Qiuyun. Still, she could vaguely guess—perhaps it was over the job.

As a friend, Xu Qiuyun supported every decision Meng Wan made. But support was one thing; worry was another. A young, pretty girl like her, out every day dealing with strangers, was bound to cross paths sooner or later with someone harboring ill intent. It was fine while nothing happened, but if something ever did, regret would come too late.

Thinking how soon she'd get to see Meng Wan, Xu Qiuyun pedaled faster and faster. It had been so long since they'd last met. Surely she'd lost weight lately, and out in the wind and sun all day, she must have gotten darker too.

By the time she reached the Lin home, the sun had already slanted low. Xu Qiuyun gripped the handlebars with one hand and lifted the back of the bike with the other, stepping over the threshold of the front gate and wheeling the bicycle into the yard.

Meng Wan was in the kitchen helping Hao Xiangping wash vegetables. She lifted her head and there stood a lovely figure.

"Qiuyun!"

Meng Wan turned off the tap, set the washed Chinese toon into a basin, and walked out with both hands held loose at her waist, swinging as she came. Her whole face crinkled with joy at the sight of the friend she'd been missing. "How did you know I was here?"

Xu Qiuyun looked at her the way one looks at a mischievous child, feigning anger. "Look how long it's been since we've seen each other. You run away from home and don't even come find me."

"Aiya." Meng Wan smiled at her playfully. "Your place wasn't exactly convenient, was it?"

The two joked back and forth with easy familiarity, closing the distance between them in no time.

"Your sister-in-law found me, asked me to bring you some things." With that, Xu Qiuyun untied the cloth bundle lashed tight to her rear rack. "She told me you'd had a falling-out with your family and left home."

Sun Jing?

Meng Wan stared at the bundle, taken aback. This was rather more than she'd expected. "Come inside first."

Xu Qiuyun set the things down in the room, then went back out to greet Hao Xiangping.

Meng Wan carried the bag into the bedroom and pulled open the zipper. Inside were several sets of the clothes she usually wore, along with undergarments and socks, toiletries, skincare, makeup—anything that could be crammed in had been crammed in. No wonder the bag had been stuffed so full.

Looking down at these things, her eyes grew unexpectedly warm. In her past life the two of them had never gotten along. Meng Wan had rather looked down on Sun Jing, thought her too plain, too old-fashioned, while Sun Jing thought Meng Wan too rebellious and free with money. The two simply couldn't stand each other.

But for Meng Xingyang's sake, the two had kept up a surface peace—until the business with the Barbie doll, which left Zhizhi crying so hard she could barely catch her breath. Sun Jing couldn't take it anymore, and for the first time she lost her temper at Meng Wan in front of the whole family.

After that their conflict was out in the open, neither willing to give the other a kind face. When Meng Wan married Xu Tian, Sun Jing hadn't said a single word of congratulations.

And yet now, here she was, showing concern for her. Who could have known that the source of all this change was that one beautiful Barbie doll?

In the kitchen, Xu Qiuyun had already taken over Meng Wan's work. With nothing left to do, Meng Wan poured herself a cup of tea and went out to drink it in the yard.

Lin Dong had wrapped up work early today, and he'd even brought back an old friend he'd run into by chance.

Meng Wan was sipping her tea when James, without any warning, walked right into view.

"James, what are you doing here?"

James handed the takeout food he'd brought from a restaurant to Lin Dong, then walked over to Meng Wan, dragged a little stool over, and sat down beside her.

"I heard a certain someone had run away from home and thought I'd come comfort her." He glanced at the teacup in her hand. "But it looks like she's doing just fine—doesn't need my comfort at all."

Meng Wan followed his gaze to the cup in her hand and quickly moved it behind her back. "How does drinking a cup of tea mean I'm doing fine? Honestly, I'm miserable inside—I'm in desperate need of comforting."

James smiled, and said with a doting tenderness, "And how would you like me to comfort you?"

"Mm..." Meng Wan put on a thoughtful air. "Then keep me company for dinner."

"I'm already here. Do you really think Lin Dong would let me leave on an empty stomach?" James smiled and offered, "How about I take you out to the countryside tomorrow for a look around—and while we're at it, you can see that plot of land you secured for me? They've already broken ground."

The suggestion delighted Meng Wan at first, but then she remembered tomorrow's plans, and the light in her eyes dimmed.

Of course she wanted to go see it—only tomorrow she was leaving for Dongshan Province with Lin Dong.

"Next time." Meng Wan felt a deep pang of regret. "Tomorrow I have to make a trip out of town with Lin Dong. I probably won't be back for a few days."

"Out of town? Where to?" Lin Dong hadn't mentioned it just now.

Meng Wan hissed softly through her teeth, unsure how to explain. Her eyes drifting, she said vaguely, "There's a bit of family business to deal with. I was worried it might not be safe on the road, so I asked Lin Dong to come along."

Something unreadable flickered in James's eyes. "What rotten timing," he said, a note of disappointment in his voice. "We'll talk about it when you're back, then."

His birthday was this coming Monday. Would she make it back in time?

He'd even been planning, on his birthday, to...

"James, Sister Wan. Dinner's ready."

Lin Dong stood in the doorway with bowls and chopsticks, calling to the two of them. His brow furrowed without his meaning it to—the two of them had been all smiles a moment ago, so how had their faces fallen all at once?

It was James's first time at the Lin home. Before the meal he greeted Hao Xiangping politely. It was also Hao Xiangping's first time meeting a foreigner, and she pulled him aside and talked with him for a good long while.

After dinner, the group settled into the yard to drink tea. James had sensed Meng Wan's low spirits ever since they'd sat down to eat, and he couldn't shake the feeling she was keeping something from him.

Before he could even ask, Meng Wan spoke up on her own.

"There's something I want to tell you all."

Xu Qiuyun assumed it was about the quarrel with her family. She turned to glance at her, then tilted her face up toward the sky, waiting for the rest.

The matter of learning she wasn't the Meng family's biological daughter felt, to Meng Wan, like something from a century ago. In the days right after she'd found out, she'd been like a madwoman—hysterical, refusing to believe it, like a balloon ready to burst at any moment, one that anyone could pop with the lightest prick.

Even now she couldn't bring herself to face it with composure. The matter was like a gauze swab left behind inside her body after surgery: to take it out meant cutting open again, but to leave it in meant forever feeling its foreign presence.

Yet though she couldn't be as serene as if nothing had happened, she had at least grown calm.

Meng Wan let out a long breath and said, with a kind of release, "The truth is...I'm...not the Meng family's biological daughter."

Xu Qiuyun's head snapped back toward Meng Wan's face like a spring released. "You're joking with us, aren't you?" she asked, her face full of disbelief.

Meng Wan's tone was too flat—so flat that Xu Qiuyun felt she was talking about someone else's affairs.

James had imagined countless reasons she might be unhappy. The one thing he'd never imagined was a cause this staggering.

Endless questions raced through his mind, but his throat felt stuffed with cotton; he couldn't get a single one out.

Lin Dong was even more stunned. He stared at Meng Wan in astonishment, trying to read something from her expression.

Meng Wan kept her face turned up to the stars, both arms wrapped around her drawn-up knees. "I'm not joking. The place Lin Dong and I are going tomorrow is where my birth parents live. And the Meng family's real daughter is there too."

"What are you going there for?" Xu Qiuyun frowned. "To reunite with them?"

A bellyful of questions fizzed and bubbled in Xu Qiuyun's heart like an effervescent tablet dropped into water. What on earth was all this?

"More or less." Meng Wan didn't know how to explain. "That Meng family daughter is about to get married. She could have had a much better life—I don't want her to just marry that man."

Xu Qiuyun was thoroughly lost. How had Meng Wan even learned of her own origins? "Did your birth mother come looking for you?"

"No."

"Did the Meng family find out?" But when she'd seen Sun Jing earlier that day, she hadn't read anything so monumental on her face.

"Neither." Meng Wan turned to look at her and gave a small, unburdened smile. "They don't know yet. Only I knew—and now the few of you do too."

A wave of confusion washed over Xu Qiuyun's heart. Even as an outsider she found this hard to accept; she couldn't fathom how Meng Wan could speak of it so lightly. She must have endured a long, lonely stretch of struggle, pain, and self-blame all on her own.

She didn't want to probe any further. All she wanted now was for her friend Meng Wan to get through this hurdle in one piece. Whether or not she was the Meng family's daughter didn't matter. What she treasured was Meng Wan herself, a living, breathing person—with no conditions attached.

"Thank you for telling us." Xu Qiuyun reached out and cupped her cheek. "I won't ask anything more. Do whatever you want to do. No matter what happens, you're my very best friend—and that's enough."

Meng Wan had thought herself long since numb to such things, yet she was moved all the same by Xu Qiuyun's heartfelt devotion. If only she'd had someone like this standing behind her in her last life—perhaps she wouldn't have died so young.

The darkness hid the redness in Meng Wan's eyes. She leaned her face into Xu Qiuyun's hand, and the tears at the corners of her eyes ran down along her thumb.

Lin Dong sat to one side at a loss, reaching out clumsily to pat Meng Wan's shoulder in comfort. What Xu Qiuyun had said was everything he'd wanted to say too, but he was hopeless with words and could only fret helplessly from the sidelines.

James, who had stayed silent all this while, finally spoke up. "Tomorrow, I'm coming with you!"

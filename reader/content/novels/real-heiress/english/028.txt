Chapter 28

By late May the weather in Yuyang had eased into warm spring, sunny all day long, a single layer of clothing enough to go out in.

Today Meng Wan wore a cream-white chiffon dress. Her curls had gradually relaxed toward straight, a faint wave falling loose about her shoulders. She'd swept her fine wisps of fringe to either side of her forehead, baring a pair of bright, lively eyes. A touch of pale rouge at her lips, paired with the pearl earrings at her ears, made her at once vivid and understated.

Cheng Xuehua had arranged to meet Song Qingyang's mother at a newly opened Western restaurant. Meng Wan remembered that in her last life, when this restaurant first opened, she'd dragged Xu Tian here several times. But Xu Tian hadn't cared for it much, complaining that he had a Chinese stomach and couldn't get used to foreign fare.

It was Saturday at noon and the restaurant was crowded, nearly every seat taken. Most of the patrons were young people who liked to try new things.

When they arrived, the Song mother and son were already seated. This was the first time Meng Wan had met Song Qingyang's mother. She looked nothing like Song Qingyang—a broad, full forehead, a round face and big eyes, the very picture of a woman blessed with good fortune.

Meng Wan first gave Song Qingyang a simple nod of greeting. The last time the two of them had parted hadn't been pleasant, so meeting again now was a touch awkward.

Song Qingyang's mother, Qiu Mei, broke into a grin the instant she saw Meng Wan that she couldn't hold back. She took Meng Wan's hand and looked her over for a good long while. "Before, I'd only ever heard little Wan's voice on the phone—and lovely as it was, I never imagined you'd be even prettier in person."

A girl of Meng Wan's looks matched well with her son, a fine pairing of talent and beauty. The more Qiu Mei looked, the more she liked this prospective daughter-in-law.

"Auntie Qiu, you're the real beauty here—you look just like Song Qingyang's older sister," Meng Wan returned the compliment.

"What a sweet-mouthed girl." The more Qiu Mei looked, the happier she was.

"Auntie Cheng, Meng Wan, please, have a seat," Song Qingyang said from the side.

"Yes, sit, sit." Qiu Mei sat down too. "I get so happy I forget myself."

Song Qingyang sat across from Meng Wan. Their eyes met, and he quickly looked away again. The last incident had been a misunderstanding on his part; he'd find a moment today to apologize to her.

He picked up the menu and handed it to mother and daughter. "Auntie Cheng, little Wan, take a look at the menu—what would you like to eat?"

"Your mother says you've been very busy lately." Watching Meng Wan flip through the menu, Song Qingyang's mother probed indirectly.

"There's a new shipment of books at the library lately, so I've had to go through the inventory several times over." Meng Wan stole a slightly guilty glance at Song Qingyang.

Song Qingyang knew about Meng Wan changing jobs, so lying like this felt rather awkward.

"That's a good job you've got—steady, and you can read in your spare time. It suits a girl."

It suited a wife, too. After marriage one party always had to mind the home, and her son was so busy; if he married someone who was out all day as well, what would be the point of marrying at all?

Meng Wan gave an awkward smile and didn't answer. Song Qingyang, beside her, promptly changed the subject. "Mom, didn't you have something to ask Auntie Cheng?"

"Oh, that's right." Qiu Mei immediately produced her lab results. "These are the test results I got last time. The doctor said all the indicators are basically normal now, but I'm still not at ease—I'd like you to have a look for me."

Cheng Xuehua took the report and began to read.

Meng Wan knew Song Qingyang was rescuing her from the spot, and gave him a small, grateful nod.

"Meng Wan?"

A crisp male voice suddenly rang out behind her. Meng Wan's ears twitched—she'd know that voice anywhere. It was James.

Her first reaction was delight; she hadn't expected to run into him here. The next second she caught the puzzled looks on Cheng Xuehua's and Qiu Mei's faces, and sucked in a cold breath. James knew nothing of her situation—what if he let something slip?

Steeling herself, Meng Wan stood up and introduced the two parties to each other.

Cheng Xuehua sized up this foreign young man, thoroughly mystified. When had her daughter befriended a foreigner? The young man was handsome, though—handsome enough that it was hard to look away.

Qiu Mei took the exact opposite view from Cheng Xuehua. She eyed the crisply suited young man with open hostility, her gaze darting back and forth between Meng Wan and James, anxious to read in their expressions some clue to the nature of their relationship.

Afraid James would steer the conversation onto her, Meng Wan spoke first. "Did you come on your own?"

"There's someone else." James paused, and the look he gave Meng Wan carried a hint of apology. "I'm here on business today."

Every nerve in Meng Wan was strung tight; she completely missed the flicker in James's eyes. "Then you go take care of it. We'll talk another time, when there's a chance."

Before James could answer, a man's voice came from behind him.

"James!"

The whole party's attention was drawn to the voice, and they all turned to look toward the door.

Meng Wan looked at this familiar figure striding in and felt utterly exasperated, fury surging up out of her chest. How was he here too?

The person James said he was meeting on business—was it him? What business could the two of them possibly have together?

Full of questions, Meng Wan turned to look at James, and all at once understood the meaning behind the look he'd given her. Could it really be this?

James spread his hands, his expression complicated as he tilted his head slightly, his throat seeming caught like a stuck spring—the words bounced out and bounced back, but wouldn't come.

Xu Tian, oblivious to the subtle tension in the air, took in the gathering of old acquaintances and assumed James had invited the others.

Meng Wan looked especially pretty today. Now he felt the faintest twinge of regret—if she'd never found him out back then, the two of them might be close to married by now.

"Little Wan, long time no see." He plastered on a smile and greeted her of his own accord.

Just the sight of him made Meng Wan's chest tighten. She shot him a withering glare, that breath stuck in her chest that wouldn't go up or down. If not for this man, she'd never have lived such a miserable last life. The wound he'd left her—even smashing his car in revenge hadn't been enough to smooth away a single corner of it.

Seeing Meng Wan ignore him, Xu Tian kept up his eager reminiscing. "I hear you quit your job at the cultural center and you're a tour guide now. Want big brother to help you out, send some clients your way?"

Out of a clear blue sky, it was as if a giant bolt of lightning split open the restaurant roof and struck straight down onto Meng Wan's head, cleaving her in two, her hair singed and crackling with sparks.

Who was she? Where was she? She wanted nothing more than to find a crack in the ground and crawl into it!

"Little Wan." Cheng Xuehua's face had gone grave. "What's all this about?"

What on earth was going on—never mind what these two men were to her daughter, but a thing as big as quitting her job, and she'd had to learn of it from a stranger's mouth. As her mother, Cheng Xuehua was deeply displeased.

"Mom." Meng Wan clenched her fists and turned around woodenly, forcing out the ugliest smile imaginable. "Let me explain."

It was the sort of thing a cheating cad would say when caught. She'd never imagined the day would come when she herself would feel like a cheater caught in the act.

Qiu Mei looked at her son in astonishment, asking with her eyes what was going on. Had Meng Wan just been lying to her face?

Song Qingyang gazed at his mother, his expression complicated, momentarily too dazed to speak.

By now things had dissolved into utter chaos, every faction gathered under one roof, each person sitting on a bellyful of words with no opening to speak them, all of it quietly bubbling away.

A waiter carrying dishes saw so many people standing in the way and wanted them to make room. But the moment "Excuse me" left his mouth, his foot caught on something he couldn't see, and his whole body, along with the tray of soup in his hands, came toppling toward where Meng Wan was standing.

In an instant the restaurant filled with the clatter of collision and a chorus of rising screams. Everyone turned to look this way.

"Are you all right?"

James was closest to Meng Wan. Though he'd glimpsed the danger from the corner of his eye, the waiter went down too fast for him to mount any rescue. He could only watch helplessly as she was doused head to toe in liquid, then snatched up a napkin from the table and hurried to wipe her down.

"Little Wan!" Cheng Xuehua, too, was frightened by the sudden splash, throwing out her hands as if to stop it.

Meng Wan was so angry she laughed. She took the napkin from James. She really must not have checked the almanac before leaving the house today. She glanced down at the spectacular "artwork" all over herself—yellow soup soaked into the skirt from her left arm down to her thigh, and a warm trickle running over the left side of her neck and the ends of her hair.

She consoled herself: at least the soup was lukewarm.

Face set hard, eyes blazing with fury, Meng Wan turned a vicious look on Xu Tian.

A moment ago, out of the corner of her eye, she'd clearly seen it—this man had stuck out his foot and tripped the waiter!

Xu Tian guiltily looked away, yet was still rattled by the look in Meng Wan's eyes.

Seeing the trouble, the restaurant manager came trotting over to handle it.

"I'm so sorry, miss—let me take you to change clothes first." Then he turned and savagely scolded the waiter, who was still on the floor grimacing in pain. "What kind of walking is that? Can't even serve a dish without causing a catastrophe this big!"

"It wasn't his fault." Meng Wan crouched down to help the man up. "This person tripped him." She pointed at Xu Tian with her other hand.

Xu Tian's eyes went guiltily wide and he immediately waved his hands in denial. "It wasn't me—what nonsense are you spouting? Just because the two of us had some bad blood once, you can't take your revenge on me like this."

Meng Wan let out a "Hah," rolled her eyes hugely at him. "Call the police. I'm a witness—this is attempted murder."

"How is it attempted murder? All I did was trip him!" Xu Tian scrambled to throw off the heavy accusation Meng Wan had pinned on him—and the words were barely out before he realized he'd walked straight into her trap. He floundered to backpedal. "I didn't, it wasn't me."

Every eye locked onto the culprit. The manager didn't want things blowing up—after all, the place had only just opened, and dragging the police in now would look terrible. Whoever had done it, he just wanted to smooth a big matter into a small one and a small one into nothing.

The manager ventured a solution. "How about this—the restaurant will compensate you for your clothes, and this meal will be on us. We hope you'll give us a chance and not make too big a thing of it."

Meng Wan wasn't satisfied with his proposal. She hated nothing more than people who just papered over things. "It's clearly his fault—why should you pay for his mistake? If he killed someone today, would you take the blame for that too?"

The manager was struck dumb, standing there at a loss.

"You!" Meng Wan rounded on Xu Tian and shouted, "Pay up!"

Xu Tian's bluster instantly collapsed. Meng Wan was like a lioness right now, cornering him, a scrawny little jackal, into a tight, cramped spot.

He muttered under his breath, "Fine, I'll pay."

He took out the wallet from his pocket, first drawing out a fifty and looking it over—then, feeling it might not be enough, swapped it for a hundred.

"Here!"

Meng Wan patted the waiter beside her. "Go on, take it. This is your medical fee."

Cradling his injured arm, the waiter glanced at the manager like a startled rabbit. The manager pressed his lips together and gave him a nod. With his superior's permission, the waiter timidly reached out his other hand and took the money.

"And the rest?" Meng Wan barked at Xu Tian again.

Xu Tian lowered his head, rolled his eyes, and grudgingly pulled another hundred from his wallet. "That enough?"

Meng Wan, fed up with his dithering, stepped right up and snatched the wallet straight out of his hand.

"What are you doing?" Xu Tian lunged frantically to grab his wallet back.

Meng Wan twisted away, dodging him as she flipped it open.

A fair bit of cash in there. She pulled out the whole stack of bills, peeled off five hundred, and flung the rest in Xu Tian's face like scattered petals, right in front of everyone.

In an instant banknotes of every color drifted down in scraps across the floor, sketching out a "beautiful" little scene.

"Xu Tian, you're just like your money—forever too cheap and shabby to be seen in decent company!"

And with that, amid a crowd of astonished faces, Meng Wan gripped her five hundred yuan and walked out of the restaurant without a backward glance.

James cast a cold, sidelong glance at Xu Tian, then in two long strides went after Meng Wan, who'd already left the restaurant.

Song Qingyang had just stepped forward, only to pull his foot back again, watching with a dark, unreadable look as the two figures vanished one after the other beyond the restaurant doors.

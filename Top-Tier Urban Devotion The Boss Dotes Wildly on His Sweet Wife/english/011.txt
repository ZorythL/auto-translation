The next morning, Su Qingyuan went to see Zhou Wanyin.
It was again that studio beneath the ginkgo tree. Autumn sunlight streamed in through the windows, and the worktables were spread with new design sketches.
Zhou Wanyin was as brisk and capable as ever, with no wasted pleasantries. She asked outright, "Made up your mind?"
"I have." Su Qingyuan sat down and handed over a revised cooperation framework. "I accept a fifty-fifty split, but I'd like to add one clause—two years from now, the split ratio gets renegotiated based on my market valuation."
Zhou Wanyin raised an eyebrow and glanced at the document.
Then she smiled.
"Did you come up with this yourself?"
"Someone gave me advice."
Zhou Wanyin let out a meaningful "Oh," but didn't press further. She picked up a pen, made a few changes to the document, and pushed it back. "Two years is too short. Make it three. If your personal brand is valued at over fifty million three years from now, the split can be adjusted to sixty-forty—sixty for you, forty for me."
Su Qingyuan was taken aback.
She had only dared to imagine sixty-forty in Zhou Wanyin's favor—forty for herself.
She hadn't expected Zhou Wanyin to simply hand her the sixty.
"Teacher Zhou, you…"
"I have faith in you." Zhou Wanyin cut her off, her tone flat but her gaze earnest. "If you can't reach a fifty-million valuation in three years, then it means my judgment was off. And there's nothing I trust more than my own judgment."
Su Qingyuan looked at the woman before her, and a complicated rush of emotion suddenly welled up in her chest.
All her life, she had met far too many people who wanted to milk her for gain, who wanted to climb over her to get ahead, who wanted to use her as a stepping stone.
But Zhou Wanyin wasn't one of them.
Neither was Lu Shiyan.
"All right." Su Qingyuan picked up the pen and signed her name on the contract. "Teacher Zhou, I won't let you misjudge me."
"Just call me Sister Wanyin." Zhou Wanyin tucked away the contract, stood up, and extended her hand. "Here's to a happy partnership."
"To a happy partnership."
Their two hands clasped together.
A brand-new beginning.

But now—
He had done it all.

That afternoon, Su Qingyuan went to look at the studio.
Chengnan Creative Park—red brick walls, steel structures, glass curtain walls, an arts district converted from old factory buildings, full of galleries, design studios, and independent brand shops, steeped in artistic atmosphere.
The spot Lu Shiyan had chosen was tucked deep within the park, down a quiet little alley. By the entrance stood an osmanthus tree, in full bloom this season, its sweet fragrance drifting through the air.
Su Qingyuan stood at the door, slid the key into the lock, and turned it.
The moment the door swung open, she froze.
The eighty-square-meter space had been cleverly divided into three zones: a display area, a work area, and a small lounge.
The walls were painted a warm white, the floor a light-gray self-leveling finish, clean and crisp. The display area had an entire wall of glass display cases with built-in light strips—perfect for showcasing jewelry pieces.
The work area held a huge solid-wood worktable, already fitted with professional lighting equipment, a magnifier lamp, and various tool-storage racks.
The lounge sat by the window: a small sofa, a little round table, a floor lamp, and a potted plant in the corner.
Minimalist, warm, clean.
Exactly the way she liked it.
Su Qingyuan walked in, each step light, as if afraid of disturbing some dormant possibility sleeping within the space.
She stepped up to the worktable and reached out to touch its surface. The wood was warm and smooth to the touch, sanded to a fine polish, its corners rounded off—every detail brimming with care.
She turned around and surveyed the entire space.
Sunlight poured in through the windows, lighting everything bright and warm.
And all at once she had a powerful feeling—
This place would be her starting point.
Not the kind of "make-do for now" starting point, but a starting point that truly belonged to her, where she could put down roots and grow.
Her phone buzzed again.

The scent of osmanthus came pouring in, carrying the warmth of an autumn afternoon.
Somewhere in the distance, someone was playing a guitar, the broken melody drifting over—she couldn't quite make out the tune, but somehow it sounded lovely.
Su Qingyuan leaned against the windowsill, looking out at the quiet little alley and the golden osmanthus tree, her heart utterly at peace.
At last she had a place of her own.
A place where she needn't read anyone's expression, needn't live under another's roof, needn't grovel just to survive.
And the key to this place had been handed to her by Lu Shiyan.

At that very moment, in the top-floor office of the Lu Group.
Lu Shiyan stood before the floor-to-ceiling windows, phone in hand, the screen still lit and frozen on the last two words Su Qingyuan had sent—
"Deal."
The corner of his mouth tilted up faintly, and he slid the phone into his pocket.
Outside, the Nancheng sky was slowly steeping in dusk, orange-red light spreading across the entire city like an enormous oil painting.
"Mr. Lu." His assistant knocked and came in, respectfully holding out a document. "The bidding proposal for that plot of land you wanted—it's ready."
"Leave it on the desk."
The assistant set the document down, hesitated, then couldn't quite resist asking, "Mr. Lu, that plot is near the creative park, and its commercial value isn't the highest. Why are we so set on acquiring it?"
Lu Shiyan didn't turn around. His voice was light:
"Because that park is going to house a very important studio someday."
The assistant blinked, took the hint and asked nothing more, then bowed and withdrew.
The instant the door closed, Lu Shiyan turned, walked back to his desk, and picked up the bidding proposal.
He opened to the first page and saw the project name: Chengnan Creative Park Expansion Project.
Su Qingyuan's studio was in Phase One of the park.
If he secured Phases Two and Three, he could control the planning and the entire commercial ecology of the whole park.
It wasn't that he wanted to fence her in.
It was to make certain that wherever she was, the environment would always be the very best.
Lu Shiyan picked up his pen and signed the proposal.
Outside, the dusk grew steadily deeper.
The Nancheng night was coming once again.
But he knew that from this day forward, there would be someone in that little studio in Chengnan, keeping a lamp lit.
And he would be the one who, from the eighty-eighth floor, across the entire city, watched from afar as that lamp flickered to life.

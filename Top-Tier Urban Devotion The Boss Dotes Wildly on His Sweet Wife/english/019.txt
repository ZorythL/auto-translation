Chapter 19 — The Undercurrent Stirs Again, and She Meets It Head-On

The first week after returning home, Su Qingyuan barely rested at all.
News of the "First Light" pre-sale spread like wildfire, boosted by the Biennale gold medal. The instant she posted the pre-order link, the official website of Zhou Wanyin's studio crashed—not because of a technical glitch, but because the flood of simultaneous orders was so massive that the server simply went down.
"It's fixed, it's fixed!" Zhou Wanyin's voice came down the line, brimming with excitement she couldn't suppress. "Qingyuan, do you know how many orders came in during the first five minutes of the pre-sale?"
"How many?"
"Fifty-three million."
The hand holding the phone went still for a moment. "…Fifty-three million?"
"Fifty-three million. In yuan. In five minutes." Zhou Wanyin drew a deep breath. "No independent designer in this country has ever hit a number like that. You broke the record."
Su Qingyuan leaned back in her studio chair, staring up at the ceiling, and didn't speak for a long while.
Fifty-three million. Five minutes.
She thought back to a few months ago, when she'd been a small-time designer who could barely scrape together rent, with a bank balance in the triple digits, looked down on by everyone. And now, a piece she'd designed had generated fifty-three million in sales within five minutes of going live.
This wasn't a dream. This was real.
"Wanyin, can production keep up?"
"It can. The three suppliers Mr. Lu lined up are all up and running. The first batch of orders should ship within two weeks." Zhou Wanyin laughed. "Your Mr. Lu even has your entire supply chain sorted out for you. Shouldn't you treat him to a meal?"
The corners of Su Qingyuan's mouth curved up. "Treating him to a meal is impossible—he grabs the check every single time."
"Then thank him some other way, hmm?"
"Wanyin!"
Zhou Wanyin laughed heartily on the other end. When she'd laughed it out, her tone suddenly turned serious. "Qingyuan, there's something I need to tell you. The last couple of days, people in the industry have been spreading a rumor—that you won the gold medal because Lu Shiyan pressured the Biennale judging committee. They're telling it in convincing detail, claiming someone saw people from Lu Group in Paris making contact with the judges."
Su Qingyuan's smile slowly faded.
"Who started this?"
"No one can trace the source so far, but I have a guess—the people behind Su Yurou. They couldn't beat you on the work itself, so they've started playing games with public opinion." Zhou Wanyin's voice sank. "Do you want to put out a statement?"
Su Qingyuan was silent for a few seconds, then said, "No statement needed. The truth is plain for everyone to see. The judging committee is a top-tier international independent body—it won't change its verdict because someone applies pressure. If anyone has doubts, let them produce evidence."
"That's all well and good, but public opinion is a tricky thing—"
"Wanyin," Su Qingyuan cut her off, her voice calm but resolute, "if I had to issue a statement every time someone threw mud at me, I'd never have time to do anything else for the rest of my life. 'The clean stay clean'—those four words aren't there to comfort yourself. They're there to face the world with."
Zhou Wanyin was quiet for a moment, then let out a soft laugh. "All right. I won't try to talk you into it. You handle it your way."
After hanging up, Su Qingyuan sat by the window, gazing out at the osmanthus tree.
Its petals had nearly all fallen, leaving only bare branches swaying faintly in the autumn wind.
A saying came to mind—"The tree that stands tallest in the forest is the one the wind will break."
She was that tree standing tall above the rest now. The wind had come—from every direction. But she was no longer the fragile little sapling that toppled at the first gust.
Her roots were planted deep.
That afternoon, Su Qingyuan received an unexpected call.
The number was unfamiliar, but the moment she answered, the voice on the other end made her freeze for a heartbeat.
"Ms. Su, this is Lawyer Fang."
Su Yurou's lawyer.
The hand holding the phone tightened slightly, but her voice held steady. "Lawyer Fang. What is it?"
"Ms. Su, I'd like to have a talk with you." Lawyer Fang's tone was courteous—courteous to the point of being excessive. "Regarding the matter with Su Yurou, perhaps we can find a solution that benefits both parties."
"What solution?"
"It's not convenient to discuss over the phone. Could we set up a time to meet in person?"
Su Qingyuan was silent for two seconds. "Lawyer Fang, if you've come to negotiate terms on Su Yurou's behalf, then I can tell you right now—I won't accept any terms. The Ripple Cut is my original technique. Su Yurou used it without authorization. Either she publicly apologizes and stops using it, or I sue. There's no middle ground."
Lawyer Fang paused, his voice still courteous but now carrying a subtle, probing edge. "Ms. Su, have you considered that a lawsuit would also drain your time and energy? Your career is on the rise—why distract yourself over things in the past?"
"Distract myself?" Su Qingyuan let out a soft laugh. "Lawyer Fang, aren't you in the intellectual property field? You should understand better than I do—when a designer's original technique is stolen, that's not a 'thing in the past.' It's a thing of the present, and a thing of the future too. If I don't pursue it now, there'll be a second Su Yurou, a third. I'm pursuing it not for revenge, but to protect myself—and to protect everyone in this industry who does honest, original work."
The line was silent for a long time.
At length, Lawyer Fang sighed. "Ms. Su, I understand. I'll convey your position to my client."
"Good. If there's nothing else, I'll hang up now."
The call ended.
Su Qingyuan set down the phone and realized her hand was trembling slightly. Not from fear—from anger.
She drew several deep breaths, picked up her carving knife, and bent back over the worktable.
The sound of the blade scraping across metal was fine and fragmented, yet unwavering—like a vow that no storm could ever interrupt.
That evening, Lu Shiyan came to pick her up.
Once she was in the car, Su Qingyuan told him about Lawyer Fang's call.
Lu Shiyan listened without responding right away, driving in silence, his fingers tapping the steering wheel twice.
"Lawyer Fang isn't the type to act without certainty," he said, his voice steady and low. "The fact that he called you means Su Yurou's side has realized how serious the problem is. They want to settle—not out of kindness, but because they're afraid of losing."
"I didn't agree to settle." Su Qingyuan watched his profile. "I told him: either Su Yurou publicly apologizes and stops using the Ripple Cut, or I sue."
Lu Shiyan turned his head to glance at her, an emotion in his eyes that was hard to name—not surprise, but the kind of admiration that said "of course you would," with something else pressed beneath it, something like tenderness, something like pride.
"Su Qingyuan, do you know how many people in your position would choose to settle?"
"I know," Su Qingyuan said softly. "Settling is easier, cheaper, faster. But settling means compromise, and compromise means they get to keep going. I don't want to give them any signal that they 'get to keep going.'"
Lu Shiyan reached out and took the hand resting on her knee. He didn't say anything more—he just gripped it, hard.
The strength of that single clasp was worth a thousand words.
The car stopped in front of a restaurant.
Su Qingyuan got out and found that it was a perfectly ordinary hotpot place—not the kind of high-end restaurant you had to book a month in advance, just one of those steaming, bustling little neighborhood hotpot joints you saw everywhere in Nancheng.
She turned to look at Lu Shiyan. He was locking the car, his expression as calm as if to say "I come here all the time."
"How did you know I like hotpot?" Su Qingyuan asked.
"Last time you ordered takeout, I saw you pick the spicy mala broth." Lu Shiyan walked over and took her hand naturally. "Come on, I've eaten here before. The food's good."
Letting him lead her into the hotpot place, Su Qingyuan was still digesting that line in her mind—last time you ordered takeout, I saw you pick the spicy mala broth.
He'd been watching when she ordered takeout. He'd been watching, and he'd remembered. Not only had he remembered, he'd found a good hotpot place and brought her here on purpose.
This feeling—of being quietly watched over, of having her small details remembered—made her heart flutter more than any rose or diamond ring ever could.
The private room in the hotpot place wasn't large, but it was clean. The red-oil broth bubbled and gurgled, steam rising up and flushing both their faces a rosy pink.
Su Qingyuan blanched a slice of tripe—dipping it in and out seven, eight times—then put it in her mouth, and her eyes lit up.
"Good, isn't it?" Lu Shiyan watched her shining eyes, the corner of his mouth lifting.
"It's delicious!" Su Qingyuan blanched another slice and dropped it into his bowl. "Try it."
Lu Shiyan looked down at the tripe in his bowl and slowly smiled. He thought of that private kitchen restaurant before, where she'd also served him food. Back then, their relationship hadn't yet been settled, and there'd been a hint of cautious hesitation in the way she picked up the food for him.
Now, the motion was as natural as if she'd done it countless times.
That change softened some place inside him until it was very, very tender.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"Tell me—Gu Changfeng, the man behind Su Yurou. What is it he actually wants?" Su Qingyuan set down her chopsticks and looked at him earnestly. "Does he want Lu Group's share of the jewelry sector? Or something else?"
Lu Shiyan set down his chopsticks, wiped his hands with a napkin, then picked up his teacup and took a slow sip.
"Gu Changfeng went up against the Lu family once when he was young. He lost. That single loss dropped him from a position where he could have stood as the Lu family's equal down into the second tier. For twenty years, he's watched the Lu family grow stronger and stronger while he himself grew more and more marginalized." He put the cup down, his voice flat. "It's not just market share he wants. He wants to win. He wants to prove to everyone—that he, Gu Changfeng, is no lesser than anyone in the Lu family."
Su Qingyuan listened, slowly nodding.
Suddenly she understood.
Gu Changfeng was helping Su Yurou not because Su Yurou had any great talent, but because Su Yurou hated her—and she was Lu Shiyan's woman. Su Yurou's hatred and Gu Changfeng's resentment had twisted together into a single rope, and the target was her—or rather, was Lu Shiyan.
"So what do you plan to do?" Su Qingyuan asked.
"Nothing." Lu Shiyan blanched a slice of beef and set it in her bowl. "He makes a move, I counter it. If he doesn't move first, I won't go stirring up trouble. That's the rule in business—it's not children brawling, where whoever throws the first punch is in the wrong."
Su Qingyuan looked at the beef in her bowl, and the corners of her mouth suddenly lifted. "So you're waiting for him to expose a weakness on his own?"
Lu Shiyan looked at her, a flicker of admiration in his eyes. "You're understanding me better and better."
"Stand by tar and you turn black."
"Stand by cinnabar and you turn red. You've got it backwards."
"I didn't get it backwards," Su Qingyuan said, smiling as she put the beef in her mouth. "Spend long enough with you, and I've learned how to talk in circles too."
Watching the way she smiled, Lu Shiyan suddenly reached over and lightly wiped a little dab of sesame sauce from the corner of her mouth.
The motion was as natural as if he'd done it countless times.
Su Qingyuan froze for a heartbeat, then lowered her head and went on eating her hotpot, pretending nothing had happened.
But her ears had gone red again.
[System: Just popping in for a sec. This scene is too domestic—so domestic the System nearly cried. Could you two please not be this sweet? Never mind, don't answer that, carry on.]
Su Qingyuan silently cursed in her heart: Didn't you say you'd retired to the background?
[System: I come back to check in now and then. Seeing the two of you doing so well puts the System's mind at ease. This time I'm really retiring. Bye-bye.]
Holding back a laugh, Su Qingyuan went on blanching tripe.
After finishing the hotpot, the two of them stepped out of the restaurant. The night wind of Nancheng swept against them, carrying the chill of late autumn.
Su Qingyuan instinctively hunched her shoulders, and Lu Shiyan immediately took off his coat and draped it over her.
"You don't have to—"
"Wear it." Two words, curt, brooking no argument.
Su Qingyuan pulled the coat—still warm with his body heat—closer around her shoulders, and didn't refuse again.
The two of them walked slowly down the street, neither of them speaking. The hotpot place receded further and further behind them, and the streetlights stretched their two shadows long, very long.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"How do you think Gu Changfeng will make his move?"
Lu Shiyan pondered for a moment. "He won't target you directly. Targeting you directly would be too obvious—everyone knows Su Yurou is connected to him. He'll come from the side—through industry channels, say, squeezing your supply chain, or poaching your partners out from under you."
Su Qingyuan frowned. "The supply chain is handled by Lu Group. Can he even touch it?"
"Not directly, but he can poach. Of your three suppliers, one of the bosses has a personal friendship with Gu Changfeng. If he offers better terms, that supplier might tear up the agreement." Lu Shiyan's tone was very calm, as if laying out a contingency he'd already prepared. "But don't worry. I set up a backup plan in advance. If that supplier wants out, another alternate can step in seamlessly within twenty-four hours."
Su Qingyuan stopped walking and turned to look at him.
Under the streetlight, his features were deep and gentle, his eyes reflecting the warm yellow glow.
"Lu Shiyan, exactly how many backup plans have you made?"
"A lot." He looked down at her, his voice very soft. "Enough that you don't need to worry about a single thing."
Su Qingyuan gazed at him, and that feeling of being steadily held up welled in her chest again.
She rose onto her toes and pressed a very, very light kiss to his lips.
"Thank you for the backup plans." She stepped back, her voice soft and laced with a smile. "That's the deposit."
Lu Shiyan reached out, lightly circled her waist, and pulled her back.
"Deposit received," he said, lowering his head, his voice dropping until it seemed meant for her ears alone. "When do you pay the balance?"
Su Qingyuan's face went scarlet in an instant.
"You… you pay the balance first, and then I'll pay."
"All right." Lu Shiyan smiled, his brows and eyes relaxing into a smile like the first crack splitting across a frozen lake in spring. "I'll owe the balance for now. Interest calculated at the highest rate."
Su Qingyuan turned her face away, pretending to study the shop window across the street.
But her hand stayed in his the whole time, fingers interlaced, palm pressed to palm.
Like two hearts, separated only by skin and bone, listening to each other beat.
The night deepened.
In the distance, the lights of Nancheng merged into a single warm sea of glow.
Lu Shiyan's car was parked at the foot of Su Qingyuan's apartment building. The two of them sat in the car for a while, neither willing to be the first to say "I'm heading up."
"What's on your schedule tomorrow?" Lu Shiyan asked.
"Studio in the morning, and in the afternoon Zhou Wanyin's taking me to meet a brand." Su Qingyuan counted on her fingers. "In the evening I might have dinner with Wanyin—not sure what time I'll be back."
"Message me when you're done. I'll come get you."
"If it's too late, don't bother—I'll just hail a cab—"
"I'll come get you," Lu Shiyan repeated, his tone gentle but firm.
Su Qingyuan looked at him and relented. "All right. I'll message you when I'm done."
She pushed open the car door, got out, took two steps, then looked back. Lu Shiyan was watching her through the car window.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"Goodnight."
"Goodnight."
Su Qingyuan turned and headed upstairs, every step feeling weightless.
At her door, she pulled out her keys but didn't open it right away. Instead she leaned against the door, listening to her own heartbeat.
One beat, two beats, three beats.
Every beat spoke the same name.
She curved her lips into a smile, opened the door, stepped inside, and turned on the light.
On the windowsill, the white moth orchid was in full bloom under the lamplight.
Su Qingyuan walked over and gently touched a petal, then picked up her phone and sent Lu Shiyan a message:
[Home now. The matter of the balance—we'll talk about it next time we meet.]
The reply came in a flash:
[All right. I'll have the interest ready.]
Su Qingyuan pressed the phone to her chest, gazing out at the night, smiling like she'd been handed all the candy in the world.
Outside the window, the moon over Nancheng was round and bright.
Tomorrow, there would be new storms.
But tonight, there was only moonlight—and two people who slept apart, yet met each other in their dreams.
[System: Truly retired to the background now. One last word—may the two of you, year after year, never let your devotion change.]

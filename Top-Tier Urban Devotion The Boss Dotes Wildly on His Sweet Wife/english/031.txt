Chapter 31 — Before the Journey, the Dust Settles
https://www.xbanxia.cc/books/417510/72626233.html

On the third day after the smear-campaign affair, Dingfeng Group released a short announcement: Gu Changfeng, citing "personal health reasons," was stepping down as chairman of Dingfeng Group, with the vice-chairman serving in the interim. The wording was dignified—no tearing, no scandal, like a carefully rehearsed curtain call.
But everyone knew what those four words, "personal health reasons," were hiding. When Lu Shiyan told Su Qingyuan the news over the phone, his voice was very calm—no victor's glee, no contempt for the loser. He simply stated a fact, and then added a single sentence: "He won't lay a hand on you again."
Su Qingyuan held her phone, standing at the studio window, looking out at the gray, overcast sky. Gu Changfeng had resigned. Not because of her—because of himself. He'd backed the wrong person, used the wrong methods, played the wrong cards at the wrong time. Every wrong thing stacked on top of another until it became the final straw that broke him.
She didn't pity him. Nor did she pity Su Yurou. She only felt that the world was, after all, quite fair—you reap what you sow. It's not that retribution never comes; it just isn't time yet. And when the time comes, no one can dodge it.
After hanging up, Su Qingyuan walked back to her workbench and picked up her sketchbook. Her hand was steady, the tip of her pen meeting the paper, the lines flowing and sure. She was drawing the fifth piece of the "Roots" series—a necklace. The centerpiece was an irregular pearl, shaped like a teardrop, but its luster wasn't a cold white; it was a warm white, as if a beam of light were hidden inside the tear.
She gave the piece a name: "Healing."
Wounds heal. They don't vanish; new flesh grows in and covers the old injury. The color of the new flesh isn't the same as the original skin—it's a little paler, a little more tender—but it's a part of you, proof that you survived.
After the final stroke, Su Qingyuan leaned back in her chair and let out a long breath. The sky outside was still gray, but the sun was about to come out.
That evening, when Lu Shiyan came to pick her up, he brought a suitcase.
Su Qingyuan looked at the silver-gray carry-on in his hand and froze for a moment. "You're going on a business trip?"
"Not a business trip." Lu Shiyan set the suitcase down at the studio door and walked in. "I'm getting the Paris things ready ahead of time. I'm afraid we'll run out of time later."
Su Qingyuan looked at the case, then at him, and couldn't help laughing. "There are still over ten days until we leave, and you're already packing?"
"I'm not packing luggage." Lu Shiyan came to stand before her, looking down at her. "I'm packing my mood."
The line made Su Qingyuan's heart skip a little faster. "What mood?"
"The can't-wait mood." Lu Shiyan reached out and tucked a stray strand of hair behind her ear. "Ten-some days is too long."
Su Qingyuan's face went red. She turned her face toward the window, pretending to study the color of the sky outside. "When did you get so sappy?"
"Since the day you said, 'You're better-looking than the night view.'"
Su Qingyuan's ears went red too. She remembered that night on the mountaintop—she really had said those words. It was the sappiest thing she'd said in her whole life, and she'd assumed he'd long forgotten it. She hadn't expected him to remember, and to remember it so clearly.
"Lu Shiyan, with a memory this good, it's a shame you don't go on Super Brain."
"My memory only works for you." Lu Shiyan's tone was serious, not at all like he was joking.
At last Su Qingyuan couldn't hold it in and burst out laughing. She reached over and gave him a light punch. "Hurry up and move your suitcase—it's blocking the doorway."
Lu Shiyan curved his lips, turned, and carried the case inside, setting it in a corner of the lounge area. The silver-gray suitcase rested there quietly, like a silent promise—I'll go with you.
Su Qingyuan glanced at the case, warmth spreading in her chest. She turned back to her workbench and went on polishing the samples for the "Roots" series. Lu Shiyan sat down on the sofa, opened his laptop, and dealt with documents. The two of them each busied themselves, occasionally looking up to meet each other's eyes, smiling, then bowing their heads again to continue.
This kind of understanding needed no words.
Over the next ten-some days, Su Qingyuan entered "Paris countdown" mode.
All five pieces of the "Light Traces" series were finished and already crated and shipped to Paris. Every day she received on-site photos from Anna—display cases in place, lighting adjustments complete, every detail being prepared for her work. The "Roots" series was also ahead of schedule: three of the five pieces were done, the fourth was in the finishing stage, and the fifth was still being polished. On Qu Ya's end, all the photos for the joint exhibition had been shot, and she'd moved into post-production.
Everything was proceeding according to plan. But there was always a string in Su Qingyuan's heart, pulled faintly taut. Not nerves—reluctance. The Paris trip would be ten-some days, the first time she'd be away this long since getting together with Lu Shiyan. Even though he'd be going with her, being away from the studio for so long left her feeling a little hollow inside.
Three days before departure, Zhou Wanyin came to the studio to drop off promotional materials for the joint exhibition. She sat on the lounge sofa, leafing through Qu Ya's draft of the exhibition catalog, studying it carefully page by page.
"Qingyuan, come take a look." Zhou Wanyin pointed to a page in the middle of the catalog.
Su Qingyuan walked over, looked down, and froze. On that page, the left side held Qu Ya's photograph—the roots of a great tree, thick roots driving deep into the earth, like an old man's hands, like the veins of time. On the right was the first piece of her "Roots" series—the tree-ring brooch. The photo and the jewelry placed side by side, one a creation of nature, one a work of human hands, yet the theme, the emotion, even the composition were astonishingly alike.
"Qu Ya said that when she chose this photo, she hadn't seen your brooch yet. It was only after she placed them together that she realized they looked like they were in conversation." There was a suppressed excitement in Zhou Wanyin's voice. "Qingyuan, this joint exhibition is going to take off."
Su Qingyuan looked at the two side-by-side images in the catalog, and a scalding emotion welled up inside her. What Qu Ya had photographed were the roots of nature; what she had made were her own roots. One turned outward, one turned inward, but both arrived at the same place—the place of origin.
"Sister Wanyin, could I have a copy of this catalog? I want to bring it to Paris."
"Of course. It was always meant for you." Zhou Wanyin closed the catalog and handed it to her. "When you get to Paris, rest well—don't just bury yourself in work. You've lost weight lately."
Su Qingyuan took the catalog and hugged it to her chest. "I know."
Zhou Wanyin looked at her and suddenly smiled. "When you go to Paris with your Mr. Lu, are you going to shoot your wedding photos while you're at it?"
Su Qingyuan's face went red in an instant. "Sister Wanyin!"
"I'm joking. But really, the two of you ought to settle things. That Lu Shiyan—he never says anything, but I can tell, he's been waiting a long time for this day."
Su Qingyuan lowered her head, fingers gently brushing the cover of the catalog. Of course she knew he'd been waiting. From the glass door to the rainy night, from the hotel lobby to the mountaintop, from Paris to Nancheng, he'd been waiting all along. Waiting for her to be sure, waiting for her to be ready, waiting for her to turn back and see—he'd been there the whole time.
The day before departure, Su Qingyuan rarely gave herself a day off.
She didn't go to the studio; she slept until she woke naturally. By the time she woke it was already past nine, sunlight slipping in through the gap in the curtains and falling golden on the quilt. She lay in bed, staring at the ceiling, lost in thought for a while. There were a lot of things turning in her mind—tomorrow's flight, the weather in Paris, the schedule on launch day, the exhibition plan Qu Ya had sent over. After a while, she suddenly remembered something.
She picked up her phone and sent Lu Shiyan a message: "What time is the flight tomorrow?"
Lu Shiyan's reply came quickly: "Ten in the morning. I'll come get you at eight."
Su Qingyuan: "Okay. Have you finished packing?"
Lu Shiyan: "All packed. Two cases."
Su Qingyuan: "Two? What did you bring that needs two cases?"
Lu Shiyan: "One mine, one yours."
Su Qingyuan froze. She hadn't even gotten around to packing yet, and he'd already prepared her case too? She was about to ask when his next message came: "Don't bring too many clothes, you can buy them in Paris. But I packed your carving knives and sketchbook for you—they're in the carry-on bag, won't be checked."
Su Qingyuan gripped her phone, and her nose suddenly stung. Her carving knives and sketchbook were the things she cared about most. She'd planned to pack them herself, afraid they'd get lost in checked baggage, wanting to carry them with her. Before she could even say a word, he'd already thought of it for her—and done it.
Su Qingyuan: "Lu Shiyan, did you set the combination lock on my suitcase too?"
Lu Shiyan: "Your birthday."
Su Qingyuan stared at the words "your birthday," and her eyes grew hot. Her birthday—even she herself rarely marked it. Back in the Su household, no one remembered her birthday. After she'd struck out on her own, she'd never gotten into the habit of celebrating it. She'd assumed no one would ever care. But he remembered. Not only did he remember, he'd set it as the combination to her suitcase.
Su Qingyuan pressed her phone to her chest, gazed up at the ceiling, and smiled.
At noon, Su Qingyuan went to the studio. Not to work—to say goodbye.
The studio was very quiet, the heating already turned off, and the room felt a little cold. The cyclamen on the windowsill was still in bloom, its pink-white petals trembling faintly in the chilly air. She watered the flowers, tidied the tools on the workbench one by one, and draped them with a dust cloth. Then she stood at the door and took one last look at the whole space.
Eighty square meters—not large. But this place held the lifeblood of countless days and nights of hers. "First Light" had been born here, "Light Traces" had taken shape here, "Roots" had taken root here. She picked up her phone, snapped a photo, and saved it into the album called "Roots."
As she locked the door, she suddenly remembered the day she'd first come to see this studio. That day the osmanthus had been in bloom, and Lu Shiyan had stood beneath the osmanthus tree waiting for her, a key in his hand. He'd said, "The key's here, you can come look anytime." When she took the key, her fingers had brushed his fingertips, and her heart had skipped a beat. Back then she'd thought he was just being kind, helping her find a studio. Only later did she learn that for the sake of this one studio, he'd seized control of the entire creative park.
She locked the door and slipped the key into her pocket.
After Paris, she would come back. She would return here with new work, new stories, a new self.
That evening, Lu Shiyan came to take her to dinner. He didn't take her to a restaurant—he drove straight to his own home.
Su Qingyuan had been there once—a long time ago, before they'd defined their relationship, she'd come to drop off a document. Back then she'd stood at the door, too shy to go in. Today, Lu Shiyan drove the car straight into the garage, then took her hand and rode the elevator up to the top floor.
The instant the door opened, Su Qingyuan froze.
This wasn't the kind of "bachelor's mansion" she'd pictured—cold, lifeless, like a show home. This place had warmth. The living-room lights were a warm yellow, a pale beige throw draped over the sofa—exactly like the one in her studio's lounge area. On the coffee table sat a bunch of white chamomile, and beside it an open book, looking as if someone had just been reading it. On the kitchen counter stood two cups, one large and one small, as if they were used every day.
"This is your home?" Su Qingyuan asked.
"Mm." Lu Shiyan set his car keys in the tray by the entryway. "I live alone, it's a bit big. But I'm used to it."
Su Qingyuan changed into slippers—they were her size, pale gray, soft and fuzzy. She walked into the living room and sat down on the sofa. The sofa was very soft, far more comfortable than the one in her studio. She sank back into it, hugging that pale beige throw, and suddenly felt that this place didn't feel like a "bachelor's mansion" at all—it felt like a home that had been waiting a long time for her.
Lu Shiyan brought two glasses of water out from the kitchen, set them on the coffee table, and sat down beside her.
"The reason I asked you over today is that I wanted you to see it, so that when you come in the future, you won't feel like a stranger here."
Su Qingyuan turned her head to look at him. "Will I be coming often in the future?"
"Come whenever you want. If you don't want to come, I'll go to you." Lu Shiyan's voice was very soft. "But here—you're welcome anytime."
Su Qingyuan lowered her head, her fingers slowly tracing circles in the fuzz of the throw. She understood. He was telling her—my home is your home. Not a courtesy, not romance, but a very earnest invitation about "the future."
"Lu Shiyan, when you go to Paris tomorrow, are you going with me, or going 'while you're at it'?"
The corner of Lu Shiyan's mouth lifted slightly. "Going specifically."
Su Qingyuan laughed. She set down the throw, stood up, and walked to the floor-to-ceiling window. Outside lay Nancheng's night view, the lights of ten thousand homes merging into a warm sea of light. She saw a very low building in the distance, looking especially quiet amid the lights.
"What's that?" She pointed in that direction.
Lu Shiyan came over and stood beside her. "The creative park. Your studio is in that direction."
Su Qingyuan looked at that blurred patch of lights, and a strange feeling welled up inside her. Standing at the floor-to-ceiling window of his home, she could see her own studio. This building stood at one end of the city, the studio at the other, half a city between them. But standing here, they seemed very close—because she was standing at his side.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"When you lived here alone, did you stand here at night looking out the window?"
"I did."
"Looking at what?"
"Looking at the whole city. Wondering when there'd be one more person here."
Su Qingyuan tilted her head to look at him. The light from outside fell on his face, his profile especially gentle in the night. "Now there is."
Lu Shiyan turned to look at her, his gaze as deep as the night. "Mm. There is."
The two of them looked at each other for a few seconds, and then Su Qingyuan rose onto her tiptoes and pressed a very, very light kiss to his lips.
"See you tomorrow."
"See you tomorrow."
Su Qingyuan turned, picked up her bag, and went to the door to change her shoes. Lu Shiyan saw her to the elevator. When the elevator doors opened, she stepped in, turned around, and looked at him.
"Lu Shiyan, your home is beautiful."
"It'll be even more beautiful in the future."
"Why?"
"Because it'll have your things in it."
The elevator doors closed. Su Qingyuan leaned against the elevator wall, looked at her own flushed face in the mirror, and smiled. This man—when he said sweet nothings, he never seemed to think they were sweet nothings. What he was talking about was the future. A future the two of them would share.
The next morning, Lu Shiyan showed up on time downstairs at Su Qingyuan's apartment.
When Su Qingyuan came down, she saw his car parked at the entrance, the trunk open, and him loading the suitcases inside. Two cases, one black and one white, leaning against each other like a pair.
She walked over and looked at the white case. "Is this mine?"
"Mm. The combination's your birthday. I've already packed it for you—take a look and see if anything's missing." Lu Shiyan shut the trunk and pulled open the car door.
Su Qingyuan got into the passenger seat and fastened her seatbelt. "If you packed it for me, nothing will be missing."
Lu Shiyan started the car, the corner of his mouth lifting slightly.
The car drove toward the airport. Nancheng's winter was still very cold, but the sunlight was lovely, falling on the windshield, dazzlingly bright. Su Qingyuan leaned back in her seat, watching the streetscape rush backward outside the window. The creative park, the Cartier building, the Hunan restaurant, the hotpot place, the art museum, Qu Ya's studio, the floor-to-ceiling window of his home—these were all part of her map of Nancheng. Every one of them held his shadow.
"Lu Shiyan, when we get to Paris, should we go check out the Cartier flagship first?"
"Sure."
"Then the Eiffel Tower?"
"Sure."
"Then go eat croissants?"
"Sure."
"Why do you say sure to everything?"
Lu Shiyan turned his head to glance at her, a smile in the depths of his eyes. "Because whatever you say is fine by me."
Su Qingyuan turned her face toward the car window, pretending to look at the scenery outside. Her face was reflected in the glass, the curve of her lips refusing to come down no matter what.
The airport, the departure lounge.
Su Qingyuan sat by the floor-to-ceiling window, watching the planes on the tarmac. Lu Shiyan had gone to check the luggage, and she sat there alone, holding two cups of coffee—an Americano for herself, a latte for him.
She looked down at the names on the coffee cups and suddenly remembered a time long ago, when she couldn't even bring herself to buy a single cup of coffee. Back then she'd worked at the Su family's shop, walking a long way every day, passing a café and slowing her steps when she caught the scent of coffee. She'd never once gone in, because the price of one cup of coffee was enough to feed her two meals.
Now she could buy many cups of coffee. But the one she most wanted to buy was the one in the hand of the person beside her.
Lu Shiyan came back and sat down beside her. Su Qingyuan handed him the latte. "Yours."
Lu Shiyan took it and drank a sip. "Thanks."
The two of them sat shoulder to shoulder, watching the planes beyond the window. Sunlight poured in through the enormous glass curtain wall, falling over them, warm and bright.
"Su Qingyuan."
"Mm."
"When we get to Paris, besides the launch and the Tower, there's one more place I want to take you."
"Where?"
"Montmartre."
Su Qingyuan turned to look at him. "Why Montmartre?"
Lu Shiyan looked out the window, his voice very soft. "Because there's a wall there, called the Wall of Love. The same sentence is written on it in over three hundred languages—'I love you.' I want to take you to see it."
Su Qingyuan's heart skipped a beat.
"Which line do you want to see?"
Lu Shiyan turned to look at her, his gaze as deep as the night. "The Chinese one."
Su Qingyuan's eyes grew hot. She lowered her head and took a sip of the Americano, using the bitterness of the coffee to press down whatever was welling up in her throat. "Lu Shiyan, you really do know how to pick a place."
"It's not that I know how to pick it—it's that that place was always meant to be visited with you."
Su Qingyuan said nothing. She reached out and clasped his hand. Ten fingers laced together, palm against palm. Outside the window, the planes' engines roared and rumbled. The sunlight grew brighter and brighter, lighting the entire departure lounge like a vast, transparent greenhouse.
The announcement came: "Passengers traveling to Paris, please prepare to board."
Su Qingyuan stood up and picked up her bag. Lu Shiyan stood up and gathered both of their coats.
"Let's go."
"Let's go."
They walked shoulder to shoulder toward the boarding gate, Nancheng's sunlight behind them, the unknown of Paris ahead.
The two of them's shadows fell on the floor, drawn close together, so close they almost overlapped.

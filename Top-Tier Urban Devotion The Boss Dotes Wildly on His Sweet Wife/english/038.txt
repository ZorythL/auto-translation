Chapter 38 — The Ring and the Faraway
https://www.xbanxia.cc/books/417510/72626240.html

In the first week back from Scandinavia, Su Qingyuan felt like a clock that had been wound up tight all over again. The final proposal for the Cartier New York tour had to be confirmed before the end of the month, the third production batch of the "Light Traces" collection needed her signature to go ahead, and the portfolio for the "Roots" series was being assembled, ready to submit to the review committee for next year's Venice Biennale. One thing piled onto another, rolling toward her like a snowball. But she didn't feel tired, because all of it was hers by choice.
That evening, Lu Shiyan messaged to say he had a business dinner and couldn't come pick her up. Su Qingyuan replied with an "Okay" and kept working. When she finally lifted her head from the workbench, she realized the sky had gone completely dark. She glanced at her phone — 8:47. Lu Shiyan hadn't messaged; he must still be busy. She packed up her things, locked the door, and walked to the gate of the complex to hail a cab. The early-spring night wind was still cold, and she shrank her neck, tugging her scarf up higher.
The taxi pulled up outside her apartment building. She paid, pushed the door open, and got out. The stairwell lights were on, warm yellow light spilling through the windows and falling across the steps at the entrance. She climbed the steps and was just about to reach for her keys when she suddenly stopped.
There was a box sitting at her door. Small, white, velvet, tied with a champagne-colored ribbon. No sender's name, no card — just that box lying there quietly, like a secret that had been kept for a long time.
Su Qingyuan crouched down, picked up the box, untied the ribbon, and opened it.
A ring.
Not a diamond ring — the center stone was a moonstone, not large, but she knew the cut all too well. The Ripple Cut, three layers of halo: deep blue on the outside, silver-white in the middle, warm gold within. Exactly like the moonstone in her "Daybreak." The band was a thin line of platinum, and engraved on the inside, in a clean, sharp hand, were the words: "From a glass door to the aurora — from now on, it's all you."
Su Qingyuan's tears fell.
She cradled the ring, crouched at her apartment door, and cried like a fool. Not because the ring was so precious, but because he had remembered everything — the glass door, the aurora, every single thing she had ever said.
Her phone buzzed. Lu Shiyan's message: "Did you see the box?"
Su Qingyuan wiped her eyes and typed: "I saw it. When did you leave it here?"
Lu Shiyan: "This afternoon. Your studio door was locked, I couldn't get in, so I left it at your door."
Su Qingyuan: "Why didn't you just give it to me directly?"
Lu Shiyan: "Because I wanted you to see it alone. You don't look good crying in front of other people."
Su Qingyuan laughed through her tears. She picked up the ring and slid it onto the ring finger of her left hand — a perfect fit. He'd known her size all along.
Su Qingyuan: "When did you measure my size?"
Lu Shiyan: "While you were asleep. With a piece of string."
Su Qingyuan thought back to those days in Scandinavia — on the plane, in the car, in the hotel, she had so often fallen asleep leaning against him. So that was when he'd quietly measured her ring size.
Su Qingyuan: "Lu Shiyan, is this a proposal?"
Lu Shiyan: "Yes."
Su Qingyuan: "Last time you said a proposal needs a ring."
Lu Shiyan: "I brought one. This time I brought one."
Su Qingyuan: "Then where are you?"
The other end went silent for a few seconds. Then she heard footsteps behind her. She turned around, and Lu Shiyan stepped out from around the corner of the staircase, a bunch of white chamomile in his hand, wearing a dark grey coat, his scarf blown crooked by the wind.
He walked up to her and looked down at her. "You put the ring on. Does it fit?"
Su Qingyuan lifted her hand; the moonstone glowed softly under the streetlight. "It fits."
"And the answer?"
"What answer?"
"Whether you're willing—"
"I'm willing." Su Qingyuan cut him off, the tears falling again. "Willing. I've been willing since the day you said, 'Tell me once you're sure.'"
Lu Shiyan looked at her and smiled. He reached out and gently wiped the tears from her face, then handed her the chamomile. "These are for you. I didn't pick them up on the way — I bought them specially. From now on, every time will be on purpose."
Su Qingyuan took the flowers and buried her face in the bouquet, breathing in the faint scent of chamomile. Then she rose onto her toes and kissed him.
The light in the stairwell was warm and yellow, falling over the two people holding each other. Outside the window the night wind was cold, but neither of them felt cold at all.
Su Qingyuan couldn't remember how she got upstairs. She only remembered that he held her hand the whole way, fingers laced through hers, the ring pressing against each other's fingers — cool at first, but soon warm.
Inside, she changed her shoes, set the chamomile in a vase, and placed it on the dining table. Then she turned and looked at him.
"When did you get the ring ready?"
"The day we got back from Scandinavia."
"That fast?"
"Not fast. I'd been thinking about it for a long time." Lu Shiyan stood in front of her, helping her undo her scarf. "Since the day you signed the contract with Zhou Wanyin, I'd been thinking about it."
Su Qingyuan paused. "That early?"
"Mm. Even back then I was thinking — once you were standing firmly on your own, I'd ask you."
Su Qingyuan lowered her head and looked at the moonstone on her finger. "How did you know I'd stand firm?"
"Because you're you."
Su Qingyuan's nose stung again. She reached out and hugged him, burying her face in his chest. "Lu Shiyan, thank you for waiting for me."
Lu Shiyan tightened his arms, resting his chin on the top of her head. "I waited. It was worth it."
They stood in the entryway, holding each other for a long time. Long enough for the moon outside to peek out from behind the clouds, long enough for one of the streetlights below to go out.
Su Qingyuan didn't post the news of the proposal to her social feeds, didn't put it on Weibo. She told only two people.
The first was Zhou Wanyin. Zhou Wanyin's reply was a long voice message; Su Qingyuan tapped to play it and heard Zhou Wanyin's voice cracking with excitement: "Ahhhhhh! He proposed!!! A moonstone!!! The Ripple Cut!!! Did he scour the entire internet to find someone who could make this ring?!!" Su Qingyuan laughed after listening and replied: "Sister Wanyin, calm down."
Zhou Wanyin: "I can't calm down!! When's the wedding? I'm going to be a bridesmaid!!"
Su Qingyuan: "Not decided yet. I'll tell you when it is."
Zhou Wanyin: "Okay!! I'll be waiting for your news!!!"
The second was Qu Ya. Qu Ya's reply was very short, just one line: "He deserves it. So do you. Congratulations."
Su Qingyuan looked at these two messages, and her heart felt warm.
She didn't tell Su Yurou, and she didn't tell Chen Xiaoman. It wasn't that she was deliberately hiding it — she just felt that some people no longer needed to know.
Preparing for the New York tour was more complicated than Su Qingyuan had expected.
Cartier's Fifth Avenue flagship store in New York was one of the global landmarks of the jewelry industry. Su Qingyuan's "Light Traces" collection would be exhibited there for two weeks. She needed to go to New York in person to oversee the display, and she had to give interviews to several American media outlets. In the week before departure, she practically lived in the studio.
Lu Shiyan didn't try to talk her out of working so hard. He simply showed up on time every day, bringing food, watching her finish it, then clearing away the containers. One night, deep in the early hours, Su Qingyuan lifted her head from the workbench and saw him sitting on the sofa, laptop open, the screen's glow lighting up his earnest profile.
"Why haven't you left yet?"
"Waiting for you."
"You don't have to wait for me. I might be up all night."
"Then I'll stay up all night. I'll keep you company."
Su Qingyuan looked at the faint shadows under his eyes, and her heart softened. She walked over, sat down beside him, and leaned against his shoulder. "Lu Shiyan, if you keep doing this, I'm going to get used to it."
"Then get used to it."
"And if I get used to it and then you're not there?"
"I won't not be there."
Su Qingyuan closed her eyes and listened to his heartbeat. One beat, two beats, three. Each one steady and strong, like some kind of promise.
Spring came earlier to New York than to Nancheng. Su Qingyuan stood at the entrance of the Cartier flagship on Fifth Avenue, looking up at that elegant building, sunlight reflecting off the glass curtain wall so brightly that she had to squint. Lu Shiyan stood behind her, pushing the suitcase, her handbag hanging off it.
"Nervous?" he asked.
"A little," Su Qingyuan said. "But more than that, I'm excited."
"Same as what you said before going to Paris."
Su Qingyuan turned to look at him and smiled. "How do you remember everything?"
"Because every word you've ever said is worth remembering."
Su Qingyuan smiled and took his arm, and they walked through Cartier's doors.
The response to the New York tour exceeded all expectations. The headline of the New York Times review read: "Light from the East, Illuminating Fifth Avenue." The social media account of Vogue's American edition posted three updates in a row, calling the "Light Traces" collection "the most noteworthy jewelry work of the year." A Hollywood star even placed an order on the spot at the exhibition, buying the diamond necklace with the half-suspended setting.
Su Qingyuan stood in a corner of the exhibition hall, watching the people gathered before her work, and a strange feeling welled up in her — not pride, but calm. Once she had longed to be recognized; now she had been recognized. But she discovered that being recognized didn't feel as earth-shaking as she had imagined. It was very quiet, like a river that, after a long, rushing journey, finally flows into the sea — not an ending, but a vaster beginning.
"Miss Su, congratulations." A familiar voice came from behind her. Su Qingyuan turned and saw Jean-Pierre standing there, holding two glasses of champagne. "Jean-Pierre! How did you get here?"
"A Cartier tour in New York — how could I not come?" Jean-Pierre smiled and handed her a glass of champagne. "Miss Su, the response to the 'Light Traces' collection in New York has been even better than in Paris. Do you know what that means?"
"What?"
"It means your light belongs not only to the East, and not only to the West. It belongs to the whole world."
The hand holding the champagne glass trembled slightly. "Jean-Pierre, that's too much to live up to."
"It's not too much. You can carry it." Jean-Pierre clinked his glass against hers. "Congratulations, Miss Su. I look forward to our next collection."
Su Qingyuan took a sip of champagne, the bubbles bursting on the tip of her tongue with a hint of sweetness.
On the night the tour ended, Lu Shiyan took Su Qingyuan up to the observation deck at the top of the Empire State Building. All of New York spread out beneath their feet, ablaze with lights, like a golden sea.
"Beautiful?" Lu Shiyan asked.
"Beautiful." Su Qingyuan looked at the city below. "But not as beautiful as the aurora."
Lu Shiyan smiled. "Then next year we'll go see the aurora again."
"Okay."
"Every year."
"Okay."
"Every year we'll go to Paris, eat croissants, see the Wall of Love."
"Okay."
"Every year we'll come to New York, see the Empire State Building."
"Okay."
"Every year we'll go back to Nancheng, eat hot pot."
Su Qingyuan turned to look at him and smiled. "Why does everything have to be once a year with you?"
"Because I want to spend many years with you. One year isn't enough, two isn't enough either. It has to be many, many years."
Su Qingyuan leaned against his shoulder, looking at the thousands of lights below. "Lu Shiyan, where do you think we'll be in the future?"
"Anywhere. Wherever you are, that's where I'll be."
Su Qingyuan closed her eyes, feeling the night wind brush her cheeks. New York's night wind was drier than Nancheng's, carrying the city's distinctive clamor. But she felt at peace, because he was here.
By the time they got back to Nancheng, it was already mid-April. Spring had finally come.
The osmanthus tree outside the studio window had put out new leaves, tender green, glittering in the sunlight. Su Qingyuan stood by the window, looking at those new leaves, and thought back to last autumn when she'd first moved into this studio, when the osmanthus had bloomed all over the tree, its fragrance sweet to the point of cloying. Back then she'd only just gotten together with Lu Shiyan; everything had only just begun, everything was unknown. Now, new leaves had sprouted on the once-bare branches of the osmanthus tree, and new leaves had sprouted on her career too — the global tour of the "Light Traces" collection had won its opening battle, the "Roots" series had been selected for the Venice Biennale, and the "Roots and Light" joint exhibition had been named the year's best cross-disciplinary art project.
Everything was new.
Her phone buzzed. Lu Shiyan's message: "What do you want for dinner tonight?"
Su Qingyuan thought for a moment and typed two words: "Your cooking."
Lu Shiyan: "Okay. I'll come pick you up."
Su Qingyuan's lips curved up. She put her phone in her pocket, picked up her carving knife, and went back to polishing the first sample of the new collection. This series didn't have a name yet; she'd come up with several and wasn't satisfied with any of them. Maybe she shouldn't rush to name it — let it grow slowly. Once it had grown, the name would come on its own.
That evening, Lu Shiyan came to pick her up. The car stopped outside his building; Su Qingyuan pushed the door open, got out, and stepped into the elevator. The doors opened, she changed into those pale pink fuzzy slippers, and walked into the living room.
On the dining table sat two dishes and a soup: stir-fried seasonal greens, braised pork ribs, tomato egg-drop soup. All simple dishes, but plated with care. Su Qingyuan sat down, picked up a piece of rib, took a bite, and her eyes lit up.
"Good?"
"So good! When did you learn to make ribs?"
"While you were in New York. I watched a few videos, tried a few times."
"Tried a few times?"
"Five times."
Su Qingyuan looked at him and laughed. "You tried five times to make one batch of ribs?"
"I tried five times to make one batch of ribs for you."
Su Qingyuan set down her chopsticks and looked at him. "Lu Shiyan, why are you so good to me?"
Lu Shiyan thought for a moment, then said one word: "Worth it."
Su Qingyuan's eyes grew hot. She lowered her head and kept eating the ribs, pressing the heat in her eyes back down.
After dinner, Su Qingyuan went to wash the dishes. Lu Shiyan stood in the kitchen doorway, leaning against the frame, watching her profile as she washed. The warm yellow light fell over her, her hair loose over her shoulders, her sleeves rolled up to the elbows, revealing her slender wrists and that moonstone ring.
"Su Qingyuan."
"Mm?"
"The Venice Biennale — I'll go with you."
"Okay."
"The New York tour — I'll go with you."
"Okay."
"The Paris launch — I'll go with you."
"Okay."
"Everything from now on — I'll go with you."
Su Qingyuan turned off the faucet and turned to look at him. "Then it's settled."
"It's settled."
She walked over, rose onto her toes, and pressed a very light, very gentle kiss to his lips.
Outside the window, Nancheng's night was deepening. But their sky stayed bright, always.

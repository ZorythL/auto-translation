Chapter 18 — Home Again, the Storm Not Yet Over

The flight from Paris to Nancheng cruised smoothly through the air, ten thousand meters above the ground.
In the privacy of her first-class pod, Su Qingyuan curled up in the wide seat, a blanket draped over her, the crystal trophy still clutched in her hands. She hadn't been willing to check it; she'd held it against her chest the whole way, cradling it like a fragile treasure.
Lu Shiyan sat beside her, a document spread open on the tray table in front of him, though his attention was clearly nowhere near it. He turned his head to glance at her and found her eyes still bright, without a trace of sleepiness.
"Still not asleep?" he asked quietly.
"Can't sleep." Su Qingyuan lifted the trophy and held it up to the moonlight beyond the window. The crystal scattered the light into fine, glittering shards that fell across her face. "Lu Shiyan, do you think that once I get back, I'll find out all of this was just a dream?"
Lu Shiyan reached over and gently pinched the back of her hand.
"Does it hurt?"
"No."
"Then it's not a dream."
Su Qingyuan's lips curved. She carefully set the trophy back into the storage compartment beside her seat, then leaned against his shoulder. He slipped an arm around her in perfect understanding, settling her into a more comfortable position.
"Lu Shiyan, you said before that you were looking into the person behind Su Yurou."
"Mm."
"Did you find them?"
Lu Shiyan was silent for two seconds, his fingers tapping lightly against her shoulder. "I found the general direction. That investment firm is called Shengheng Capital. On the surface it operates independently, but trace its ownership deep enough and it has countless threads tying it to Lu Group's old rival—Dingfeng Group."
Su Qingyuan lifted her head from his shoulder and looked at him. "Dingfeng Group?"
"Dingfeng's chairman is named Gu Changfeng. Twenty years ago, Lu Group and Dingfeng were competing in the same arena. In the end, Lu Group came out on top, and Dingfeng was forced to pivot. Gu Changfeng has always believed the Lu family stole his place. All these years the two families have kept up a polite front, but underneath, there's never truly been a ceasefire."
Su Qingyuan slowly digested all of this. She had never once cared about the feuds of the business world before, but now those feuds had surged like an undercurrent, lapping right up to her feet.
"So he used Su Yurou to go after you? No—to go after me? Because I'm yours?"
Lu Shiyan looked down at her, his gaze still and calm. "Gu Changfeng isn't a man who does anything without a return. Helping Su Yurou wasn't only about getting at me. He has a far bigger aim."
"What aim?"
"The Biennale's award carries the weight of a bellwether in the international jewelry industry. If he could win at the Biennale through Su Yurou—or through any designer he controls—he could shift the entire landscape of the domestic jewelry market. Jewelry has always been a major source of profit in Lu Group's culture-and-entertainment division. He wanted to tear an opening in it from the inside."
Su Qingyuan fell silent.
She suddenly realized that the gold medal she'd taken wasn't just her own personal glory—it had also, indirectly, blocked a blade aimed at Lu Shiyan.
Because the gold medal was hers, not Su Yurou's. Gu Changfeng's plan had fallen through.
"So that's why you were so tense earlier about Su Yurou making the shortlist?" she asked.
"It wasn't her making the shortlist that worried me." Lu Shiyan's fingers threaded through her hair, his touch very light. "What worried me was whether I'd lose control if you got pushed down."
Su Qingyuan's heart gave a tremor.
"Lose control?"
"Lose control to the point of… striking back no matter the cost." Lu Shiyan's voice was low, low enough that it seemed meant for her ears alone. "That's exactly what Gu Changfeng was waiting for. He doesn't care whether Su Yurou wins or loses. What he cares about is whether I can stay calm."
Su Qingyuan gazed at him, then suddenly reached out and pressed her hand gently against his chest. Through his shirt, she could feel his steady, powerful heartbeat.
"And now? Are you calm?"
Lu Shiyan caught the hand she'd pressed to his chest and lowered his head to drop a feather-light kiss on her fingertips.
"With you here, very calm."
Su Qingyuan's face slowly flushed. She withdrew her hand, leaned back against his shoulder, and pretended to watch the clouds outside the window.
But the curve at the corners of her mouth simply wouldn't be pressed back down.
---
Nancheng. Touchdown.
The moment she cleared customs, her phone wouldn't stop buzzing. Su Qingyuan opened it to find a barrage of messages from Zhou Wanyin:
[Qingyuan! You're on the cover of the Chinese edition of Vogue! Next month's issue!]
[Cartier has formally sent over a letter of intent for collaboration. I looked it over for you—the terms are very generous!]
[The provisional patent for the Ripple Cut—Lu Group's legal team has already filed it for you. It's entered the PCT process at the international stage.]
[When do you land? Should I come pick you up?]
Su Qingyuan read through them one by one, and just as she was about to reply, her phone buzzed again. This time it was a text from an unknown number:
"Congratulations, Miss Su. But the game has only just begun. Next time, I won't lose again."
No signature. But Su Qingyuan knew exactly who it was.
Su Yurou.
She handed the phone to Lu Shiyan to see. He gave it a glance, his expression unchanged, and handed it back. "She's not wrong—the game really isn't over. But you won't be giving her another chance, either."
Su Qingyuan nodded, deleted the text, and switched off her phone.
At the airport exit, the Lu family's driver was already waiting. Beside the black Maybach, a uniformed chauffeur respectfully pulled open the car door.
Su Qingyuan slid in and only then noticed there was an extra person in the car—Lu Shiyan's special assistant, surnamed Wang, a capable man in his early thirties, a tablet in his hands.
"Mr. Lu, the background check you had me run on Su Yurou's new lawyer—I have the results."
Lu Shiyan took the tablet and scanned it quickly. Su Qingyuan leaned over to look. On the screen was a tangled relationship chart, with Lawyer Fang at its center, several lines branching outward, all of them eventually pointing to the same node—Shengheng Capital.
"Lawyer Fang's wife is the younger sister of a senior executive at Shengheng Capital," Assistant Wang said. "And last year Shengheng Capital had a sum of money flow into an offshore company under Dingfeng Group. It went through several layers of cover, but the trail was still left behind."
Lu Shiyan handed the tablet back to Assistant Wang, his expression mild. "Keep watching them. Don't tip them off."
"Understood."
The car started up, pulled out of the airport, and merged into Nancheng's afternoon traffic.
Su Qingyuan looked out the window at the familiar streetscape and was suddenly struck by a sense of having stepped back into another lifetime. She'd been gone less than a week—nothing about this city had changed, yet she was no longer the same.
"Assistant Wang, would you mind taking me to my studio first?" she said.
Lu Shiyan turned his head to look at her. "You just got off the plane. You don't want to rest?"
"I want to go see it." Su Qingyuan smiled. "I've been away a few days—I've started to miss the place."
Assistant Wang glanced at Lu Shiyan, who gave a slight nod.
The car turned toward the creative park district.
---
The studio door opened, and Su Qingyuan stood in the doorway, stunned.
Not because it had gathered dust, not because it had changed—
It was because it had changed. Changed into something even better than before.
The worktable had been replaced with a larger one, fitted with a brand-new lighting system and a tool rack. The glass display cases in the showcase area had been swapped for professional cabinets with built-in temperature and humidity control. The sofa in the lounge area had been replaced with a more comfortable one, and beside it stood a new little bookshelf, packed full of jewelry-design books and art albums.
On the windowsill, the pot of pothos she'd casually set down before had been replaced with a few delicate succulents and a small pot of white moth orchids. A heat-insulating film had been applied to the windowpanes, blocking the harsh glare without dimming the natural light.
The entire space looked as if someone had carefully redone it from scratch.
Su Qingyuan walked slowly inside, every step soft and light. She turned to look at Lu Shiyan, who was leaning against the doorframe, hands tucked into his trouser pockets, his expression calm.
"You did this?"
"Mm." Lu Shiyan's tone was as casual as if he were remarking that the weather was nice today. "While you were away these few days, I had someone come tidy it up. The old equipment was getting a bit dated—not fitting for a gold-medal designer."
Su Qingyuan walked over to the new worktable and ran her hand across its surface. The wood felt warm and smooth, the edges rounded into gentle arcs—exactly matching the "I like rounded lines" she'd mentioned to him once before.
She turned and walked to the bookshelf in the lounge area, pulling out a book at random. She opened the cover and found a bookmark tucked inside, a single line of small print across it: "For a future master."
The handwriting was clean, elegant, and sharp—Lu Shiyan's hand.
She looked back at him. He was still leaning against the doorframe, his posture relaxed, but there was a sliver of barely perceptible tension at the bottom of his eyes—like he was waiting for her verdict, yet afraid she'd find it all too deliberate.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"Did you, over these few days I was gone, memorize every single corner of my studio, and then have someone redo all of it to suit my tastes?"
Lu Shiyan neither denied it nor admitted it. He simply straightened up a little, stepped away from the doorframe, walked over to stand in front of her, and looked down at her.
"You don't like it?"
"I like it." Su Qingyuan's voice came out a little muffled. "But could you please stop moving me to the verge of tears every single time?"
Lu Shiyan reached out and gently ruffled her hair, his voice very soft. "Then cry. I won't laugh at you."
Su Qingyuan tilted her head up to look at him, her eyes rimmed red, but no tears fell.
She rose onto her toes and pecked him lightly on the lips.
Not the cheek, not the forehead. The lips.
It lasted only an instant, but it was enough to throw both their heartbeats into disarray.
Su Qingyuan stepped back, her ears flushed red enough to bleed, and pretended to study the books on the shelf.
Lu Shiyan stood where he was, lifted a hand to touch his own lips, and then, slowly, ever so slowly, he smiled.
"Su Qingyuan."
"What?"
"This kind of thank-you gift—feel free to give it more often from now on."
Su Qingyuan buried her face in the book and mumbled, "In your dreams."
Above the bookshelf, that pot of white moth orchids bloomed perfectly in the afternoon sun.

Meanwhile, in an office building in the southern part of the city, in Lawyer Fang's office.
Su Yurou sat on the sofa, a cup of coffee gone stone-cold in front of her. She looked at Lawyer Fang, her gaze unnervingly calm.
"So, Shengheng Capital won't be putting in any more money?"
Lawyer Fang pushed up his glasses, his expression awkward. "It's not that they won't invest—they want to hold off for a while. Mr. Gu's side says Su Qingyuan is riding too high right now, and a head-on clash isn't the wise move. He suggests you lie low for a while, wait for the heat to die down, and then look for another opportunity."
"Lie low?" Su Yurou let out a cold laugh. "I've already been lying low for more than twenty years. How much longer am I supposed to lie low?"
Lawyer Fang sighed. "Miss Su, I can understand how you feel, but what Mr. Gu means is—"
"What Mr. Gu means is that this chess piece of mine is no use for now, so put it away and bring it back out the next time it's needed." Su Yurou stood up and picked up her bag. "I understand."
"Miss Su, where are you going?"
Su Yurou didn't answer. She pulled the door open and walked out.
The corridor was deserted, the sound of her high heels striking the floor crisp and lonely.
She stood before the elevator, looking at her own reflection in the elevator doors—gaunt, pale, with shadows beneath her eyes that wouldn't fade.
She reached up to touch her own face, and suddenly she smiled.
"Su Qingyuan, you must be feeling pretty pleased with yourself right now, aren't you?"
"That's fine."
"People who are pleased with themselves are the easiest to trip and fall."
The elevator doors opened. She stepped inside and pressed the button for the ground floor.
The instant the doors closed, her smile vanished, replaced by a cold, almost obsessive certainty.
She would not give up.
Never.
Because to give up would be to admit that these past twenty-odd years had all been wrong.
And she would absolutely never admit that she'd been wrong.
---
That evening, when Su Qingyuan came out of the studio, she found that Lu Shiyan still hadn't left.
His car was parked beneath the osmanthus tree by the door, the window half down, and he was sitting in the driver's seat on a phone call. His tone was even, with the occasional nod—he looked to be handling business.
Su Qingyuan didn't disturb him; she leaned against the studio doorway and waited.
The setting sun had dyed the entire creative park a warm orange. The osmanthus blossoms had withered, but a faint trace of their sweetness still lingered in the air.
She watched the side of his face and suddenly remembered that line the System had once said—"Every exception he makes, every bit of tenderness, patience, and devotion in his whole life, all of it began on that rainy night, and all of it comes home to one Su Qingyuan."
She used to think that line was a little too cloying.
Now she thought it wasn't cloying at all.
Because it was true.
Lu Shiyan hung up the phone, turned his head, and saw her leaning against the doorframe, not knowing how long she'd been waiting.
"Why didn't you call me over?"
"You looked busy." Su Qingyuan walked over, pulled open the passenger door, and got in. "Watching you work so seriously—it's actually quite a good look on you."
Lu Shiyan started the car, the corner of his mouth lifting slightly. "Meaning, ordinarily I don't look good?"
"Ordinarily you look good too, but that kind of good-looking is—mm."
He didn't let her finish. He leaned over, one hand gently cupping her chin, and kissed her.
Not a fleeting brush, not a tentative taste.
It was the kind of kiss that came after waiting a whole day, holding back a whole day, finally getting that moment alone—a kiss laced with possessiveness and tenderness.
Outside the car, dusk was gradually falling.
Inside the car, their breaths tangled together.
After a long while, Lu Shiyan released her, pressed his forehead to hers, his voice low and husky. "Ordinarily I look good too? Go on."
Su Qingyuan leaned against his shoulder, catching her breath, and it took her ages to squeeze out a single line. "…Lu Shiyan, you've turned wicked."
"I learned it from you."
Su Qingyuan wanted to argue, but the curve at the corners of her mouth gave her away.
Dusk grew thicker and thicker, and the streetlamps of the creative park flickered on one by one.
That black Maybach still sat beneath the osmanthus tree, and for a long, long while, it didn't drive away.

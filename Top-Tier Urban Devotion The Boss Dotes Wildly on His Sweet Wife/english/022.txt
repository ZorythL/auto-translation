Chapter 22 — On Both Sides of the Glass Door, Old Scores Settled Anew
https://www.xbanxia.cc/books/417510/72626224.html

After that night, Su Qingyuan felt as though life had taken on a soft-focus filter.
It wasn't that things had gotten easier—work was still hectic, and challenges still came one after another—it was that the way she looked at the world had changed. Before, it had been "I have to prove myself"; now it was "I know who I am." Between those two mindsets lay the sense of security that came from being steadily held up, for three whole years (counting from that first glance in front of the glass door), by one particular person.
The Cartier contract was officially signed on Wednesday morning.
The signing was held in a conference room at Cartier's Greater China headquarters—small, but very formal. Anna attended on behalf of the brand, and Su Qingyuan sat alone on the other side of the long table—she'd brought no lawyer, because she had already read every clause of the contract word by word three times over, and every figure, every definition was etched into her memory.
Anna signed her name on the contract and slid it across. Su Qingyuan took the pen and wrote the three characters of her name, "Su Qingyuan," in the space for the second party.
The instant the nib touched the paper, she remembered that just a few months ago, the most formal document she'd ever signed had been a rental lease. Now she was signing a partnership agreement with a top-tier international jewelry brand. Anna stood and extended her hand. "Ms. Su, here's to a happy collaboration." Su Qingyuan clasped her hand. "Here's to a happy collaboration."
There were no media at the signing, no champagne, no flowers. Anna said Cartier's style was "celebrate after the deal is done." Su Qingyuan liked that style—no fuss, no showing off, letting the results speak.
When she walked out of the Cartier building, Lu Shiyan's car was parked at the entrance. He hadn't gone up, because "that was your occasion, not mine." Su Qingyuan opened the door and climbed in, handing him a copy of the contract to look over. Lu Shiyan took it, flipped through it, nodded, and handed it back.
"Signed?" he asked.
"Signed."
"Congratulations."
Just one word. But Su Qingyuan could hear the weight in it—it wasn't a pleasantry, he was genuinely happy for her.
The car headed toward the creative park. Su Qingyuan settled back into her seat, then suddenly remembered something. "Oh, right—any movement from Gu Changfeng lately?"
Lu Shiyan's fingers tapped twice lightly on the steering wheel. "He's bidding on a commercial real estate project in Nancheng recently, and Lu Group is in the running too. His earlier attempt to sound you out is probably connected to that bid."
"Connected how?"
"He wants to know whether, if I got distracted—say, distracted over something to do with you—his chances of landing that plot would go up."
Su Qingyuan frowned. "So he wants to use me to keep you in check?"
"Not 'wants to.' He's 'currently trying.'" Lu Shiyan's tone was flat, like a man describing an equation he'd already solved. "He came to see you, to find out what kind of person you are. If you were easily provoked, easily swayed by the terms he dangled, he'd have used you to make trouble. But you turned him down, and you did it cleanly. So now he knows two things: first, you're not his pawn; second, my read on you wasn't wrong—you won't become my weakness."
Su Qingyuan let that settle for a moment, then asked the question she'd been wanting to ask all along. "If… and I mean if, if I hadn't turned him down back then—what would you have done?"
Lu Shiyan didn't answer right away. The car stopped at a red light, and he turned to look at her, his gaze very deep.
"You wouldn't have," he said.
"I said if."
"There's no if." The light turned green, and he faced forward again as the car moved on. "Su Qingyuan, I understand you—more deeply than you think. You wouldn't work with him, not because he's Gu Changfeng, but because you don't work with people who don't respect originality. That's your bottom line, and it has nothing to do with who I am."
Su Qingyuan fell silent.
He was right. Her refusal had nothing to do with Lu Shiyan; it had only to do with herself. She would never allow anyone to trample on originality, and Gu Changfeng helping Su Yurou steal her craft was, in itself, a red line that could never be crossed. That line would not shift by a hair, no matter what terms the other side offered.
She turned her head to look at Lu Shiyan's profile, then suddenly said, "Lu Shiyan, have you ever noticed that you understand me better than I understand myself?"
The corner of Lu Shiyan's mouth lifted slightly. "Not better. I've just been watching longer."
Watching longer.
From that glass door onward, he had watched her for three months. In those three months, what he'd seen was her at her most disheveled, her most unremarkable, her least "successful." But it was precisely those moments that made up the "Su Qingyuan" he understood—a woman who would draw even while standing in a corridor on the other side of a glass door, a woman the whole world had rejected yet who had never rejected herself.
Su Qingyuan drew her gaze back and looked at the streetscape rushing past the window. Outside, the sunlight was just right.
That afternoon, when Su Qingyuan returned to her studio, she found an unexpected person standing at the door.
Chen Xiaoman.
She was wearing a faded gray hoodie, jeans, and canvas shoes, her hair tied carelessly behind her head, and she looked far thinner than she had the last time, when she'd "posed as an assistant." Her head was lowered, her fingers clutching the hem of her clothes, not daring to look at Su Qingyuan.
"Qingyuan jie…" Her voice was tiny, like the whine of a mosquito.
Su Qingyuan glanced at her, said nothing, and took out her key to unlock the door. "Come in."
Chen Xiaoman froze for a second, clearly not expecting Su Qingyuan to let her inside. She hesitated for a few seconds, then followed her in.
The furnishings of the studio made Chen Xiaoman's eyes light up for an instant—the pieces displayed in the cases, the tools and design sketches scattered across the worktable, the moth orchids blooming just right on the windowsill—but she quickly lowered her head, not daring to look long. Su Qingyuan sat down at the worktable and gestured to the chair across from her. "Sit."
Chen Xiaoman sat down gingerly, hands on her knees, like a grade-schooler waiting to be lectured.
"You didn't come today to ask me to forgive you, did you?" Su Qingyuan asked.
Chen Xiaoman shook her head. "No. I came… to return the money."
She pulled an envelope out of her hoodie pocket, set it on the worktable, and pushed it across. The envelope was bulging, looking like it held a fair amount of cash.
"The fifty thousand Su Yurou gave me—I haven't spent a cent of it. The money for my mom's treatment was money I earned myself, working. Not this money. This money I've never dared to touch; it felt like it was burning my hands. Today I've made up my mind—I'm giving it back to you. I know fifty thousand can't buy back your craft, but it's the only thing I can do."
Su Qingyuan looked at the envelope but didn't reach for it.
"How is your mother?"
Chen Xiaoman's eyes reddened all at once. "She… passed away last week."
Su Qingyuan's heart clenched.
"I'm sorry."
"Don't be." Chen Xiaoman's tears fell, but she didn't sob aloud; she just wiped them away clumsily with her sleeve. "Before she went, she told me a person has to be able to face their own conscience. She never knew I did something like that. If she had, she would have been so disappointed."
The studio was quiet for a long while.
Su Qingyuan stood, walked over to the lounge area, pulled a few tissues from the bookshelf, and handed them to Chen Xiaoman. Chen Xiaoman took them, covered her face, and her shoulders trembled faintly.
"Chen Xiaoman." Su Qingyuan's voice wasn't loud, but it was very clear. "Take the money back. It's not that I won't accept it—it's that you need it more than I do. Your mother is gone; from here on you have to walk your own road. Keep this fifty thousand and make a good life for yourself."
Chen Xiaoman lifted her head from behind the tissues, her eyes red, and looked at Su Qingyuan.
"Qingyuan jie, I…"
"I don't forgive you." Su Qingyuan cut her off, her tone calm. "What you did was wrong, and I won't say 'it's fine.' But I won't pursue legal action against you. Not because I pity you, but because you handed over the evidence of your own accord and admitted your mistake. In legal terms that's called a 'mitigating circumstance'; in human terms it's called 'knowing your wrong and being able to change.' But I want you to understand—'not being pursued' doesn't mean 'you did the right thing.' The road ahead, you need to think clearly about how you want to walk it."
Chen Xiaoman nodded hard, and her tears fell again.
She stood, tucked the envelope back into her pocket, bowed deeply, then turned toward the door. When she reached it, she stopped and looked back at Su Qingyuan.
"Qingyuan jie, that man—Gu Changfeng—he's no good. Su Yurou working with him won't end well. You… you should be careful."
"I know. Thank you."
Chen Xiaoman left. The instant the door closed, the studio fell quiet again.
Su Qingyuan sat at her worktable, looking at the closed door, silent for a long time.
She didn't feel she'd done some great act of kindness. She just felt that—Chen Xiaoman wasn't worth her time to hate. Hatred was a kind of drain, and her energy needed to be saved for creating, for the people who loved her, for the things that were worth it.
Her phone buzzed.
Lu Shiyan: 【There's a dinner tonight, a few of Lu Group's business partners. Do you want to come along? It's not a social obligation, just a meal. They want to meet you.】
Su Qingyuan looked at the message, a little surprised. Lu Shiyan had never let her take part in his business socializing before; he always kept her world and his world clearly separate. Inviting her himself today—what did it mean?
【Why the sudden urge to bring me?】
Lu Shiyan: 【Because Gu Changfeng might be there. It's not a coincidence—someone arranged it. Rather than have you alone in the studio, better to have you at my side.】
Su Qingyuan understood.
With Gu Changfeng there, Lu Shiyan didn't feel easy about her being alone. Not because he didn't trust her abilities, but because a man like Gu Changfeng was capable of any tactic. At his side was the safest place to be.
【All right. What time? Where?】
Lu Shiyan sent an address and a time, followed by one more line: 【No need to dress up specially. You're perfect as you are.】
Su Qingyuan looked down at herself—white shirt, black trousers, canvas shoes, hair in a ponytail, no makeup. She gave a little laugh; this man could even say "no need to dress up" with such unshakable conviction. But she still changed into a dress and put on light makeup. Not for Gu Changfeng—for Lu Shiyan. She wanted him to know she was willing to put a little thought into his world.
Seven in the evening, Nancheng's most exclusive private club.
Su Qingyuan walked into the lobby on Lu Shiyan's arm and immediately felt the eyes turning toward them from every direction. Most of the people here wore tailored suits and gowns, champagne in hand, their smiles polished and aloof. Their eyes landed on Su Qingyuan with scrutiny, curiosity, and a hint of "what makes her think she can stand at Lu Shiyan's side."
Su Qingyuan didn't shy away from those looks. She lifted her chin slightly, a polite, faintly distant smile at the corner of her mouth, her steps unhurried. She hadn't worn an evening gown, only a simple black floor-length dress, but its cut traced her figure just right—understated, yet powerful.
Lu Shiyan turned his head to glance at her and said in a low voice, "You changed your clothes."
"Mm."
"You look beautiful."
Just two words, but the tips of Su Qingyuan's ears went faintly red.
Deep in the lobby, in a corner by the window, several sofas formed a semi-open, private space. A few middle-aged men were already seated there, and seeing Lu Shiyan approach, they all rose.
"President Lu."
"President Lu is here."
Lu Shiyan gave a slight nod, not shaking each hand, simply stepping aside to let Su Qingyuan sit first, then taking the seat beside her. The gesture was small, but everyone present noticed it—Lu Shiyan let this woman sit first. What that meant, the shrewd people in the room understood perfectly.
"This must be Ms. Su? I've heard so much about you." A round-faced man seated across from them smiled and extended his hand. "I'm Zhao Zhicheng of Hengtong Group, an old friend of President Lu's."
Su Qingyuan clasped his hand with a polite smile. "Hello, President Zhao."
The others beside him introduced themselves in turn. Su Qingyuan responded to each, neither obsequious nor aloof. She noticed that Gu Changfeng was not among those present. She glanced at Lu Shiyan; his expression was very calm, and he gave a slight shake of his head—not shaking his head to say "he's not coming," but to say "no rush, he'll come."
Sure enough, a dozen-odd minutes later, a faint stir came from the lobby entrance.
Su Qingyuan turned to look. Gu Changfeng was walking in through the door. He wore a tailored navy-blue suit, his hair combed without a single strand out of place, and beside him followed a young woman—not Su Yurou, but a woman she didn't recognize, about twenty-five or twenty-six, in a wine-red gown, her makeup exquisite, her smile polished.
Gu Changfeng's gaze swept the lobby and landed precisely in Lu Shiyan's direction. Then he smiled, with an air of "just as I thought," and walked straight over.
"President Lu, it's been a long time." Gu Changfeng extended his hand, his face full of smiles, like an old friend who hadn't been seen in years.
Lu Shiyan stood and clasped his hand, his own smile equally polished. "President Gu, it has been a while."
The two men's hands clasped together—the pressure, the duration, the angle all just right, like a carefully choreographed performance. But Su Qingyuan could feel a subtle tension in the air, like two strings stretched to their limit: whoever let go first would lose.
Gu Changfeng released his hand and turned his gaze to Su Qingyuan. His smile didn't change, but a hint of scrutiny had entered his eyes. "Ms. Su, we meet again."
Su Qingyuan gave a slight nod, not rising and not offering her hand. "Mr. Gu."
The young woman beside Gu Changfeng glanced at Su Qingyuan, a flash of hostility in her eyes—Su Qingyuan caught it, but paid it no mind.
"And this is?" Lu Shiyan looked at the woman.
"My newly hired special assistant, Ms. Lin." Gu Changfeng introduced her with a smile. "Ms. Lin, this is President Lu of Lu Group, and his girlfriend—Ms. Su Qingyuan, the newly crowned Biennale gold medalist."
Assistant Lin smiled and held out her hand. "Ms. Su, I've heard so much about you. I read the coverage of your 'First Light' collection—it's truly beautiful."
Su Qingyuan clasped her hand and felt that the woman's palm was a little damp—nervousness? Or something else? She didn't dwell on it, let go, and smiled politely.
Everyone took their seats again. Gu Changfeng sat across from Lu Shiyan, the wide tea table between them. Assistant Lin sat beside him, quietly lowering her head, like an exquisite ornament.
A server brought fresh tea and refreshments. Gu Changfeng picked up his teacup, took a slow sip, then set the cup down and looked at Lu Shiyan.
"President Lu, that plot of land in the city's south—Lu Group's bidding on it too?"
Lu Shiyan picked up his teacup and took an unhurried sip. "We are."
"So am I." Gu Changfeng smiled. "Looks like we'll be opponents once again."
Lu Shiyan set down his cup, his tone flat. "Competition is what drives progress in business. President Gu, this isn't the first time we've been opponents, and it won't be the last."
The two exchanged a glance, and that one glance held more than any words could—probing, contesting, declaring war, conceding; every emotion flowed through in that single instant, then was drawn back in again.
Su Qingyuan sat quietly beside Lu Shiyan, not interjecting. She noticed Gu Changfeng's gaze sweeping over her from time to time, like a snake flicking its tongue in the dark. Outwardly she betrayed nothing, but inwardly she was wondering—what exactly did this man want from her? Or rather, what did he want from Lu Shiyan?
Halfway through the dinner, Su Qingyuan got up to go to the restroom.
The corridor was carpeted thick, and her footsteps were completely absorbed, so quiet it was like walking on clouds. The restroom door was dark wood; she pushed it open and went in, and had just turned on the faucet when she heard the sound of high heels behind her.
In the mirror she saw Gu Changfeng's Assistant Lin walk in.
Assistant Lin stood beside her, turned on a faucet, and pretended to wash her hands. But her eyes stayed on Su Qingyuan the whole time, through the mirror.
"Ms. Su, President Gu asked me to pass along a message." Assistant Lin's voice was very low, low enough that only the two of them could hear.
Su Qingyuan turned off her faucet and looked at her in the mirror. "What message?"
"He said that last time at the café, he remembered what you said. But he wanted to tell you—beyond pawns and discarded pieces, there's another role, called 'the one who moves the pieces.' If you don't want to be a pawn, you can be the one who plays the game. He's willing to play the game together with you."
Su Qingyuan turned around and looked into Assistant Lin's eyes.
"How long have you been with Gu Changfeng?" she asked.
Assistant Lin blanked for an instant. "What?"
"How long have you been with Gu Changfeng? Not a working relationship—that kind of relationship." Su Qingyuan's tone was very calm, as if she were asking about the weather.
Assistant Lin's face changed—from pale to flushed, from flushed to pale.
"I… I don't know what you're talking about."
"Your lipstick." Su Qingyuan pointed at the corner of her mouth. "The color isn't the same as when you arrived. When you came in it was a true red; now it's more of a rose. There was no time to touch up your makeup midway through the dinner, so the only possibility is—someone, somewhere outside this restroom, smudged your lipstick. And before you came into the restroom, the only place you'd been was at Gu Changfeng's side."
Assistant Lin instinctively raised a hand and touched the corner of her mouth, her face deathly pale.
Su Qingyuan looked at her, her tone neither mocking nor pitying. "You're relaying his message for him; he didn't come himself, because he knows I won't listen to him. But has he ever considered—sending you to deliver the message, and if I caught hold of something to use against him, how would he treat you then?"
Assistant Lin said nothing, her lips trembling faintly.
"Go back and tell Gu Changfeng," Su Qingyuan dried her hands with a tissue and tossed it into the trash, "the role of 'the one who moves the pieces'—I'll handle that myself, I don't need him to teach me. And I don't need him to do it 'together with me.' He should mind his own pieces and just not cross the line."
She pushed the door open and walked out of the restroom, leaving Assistant Lin alone in front of the mirror.
In the corridor, Lu Shiyan was leaning against the wall, waiting for her. Seeing her come out, he straightened up. He didn't ask why she'd taken so long, only held out his hand.
Su Qingyuan placed her hand in his palm and let him hold it.
"Let's go," he said.
"Okay."
The two walked back to the lobby side by side, the long, quiet corridor behind them—like the long road they'd walked together, from the abyss to the clouds.
By the time the dinner ended, it was nearly ten o'clock.
When Gu Changfeng left, he shook hands with Lu Shiyan and gave Su Qingyuan a nod, his smile as polished as ever. Assistant Lin followed behind him, head lowered, not daring to look at Su Qingyuan.
Su Qingyuan watched their departing backs and suddenly thought that Gu Changfeng was a truly pitiful man. He had money, status, and resources, but not a single person who followed him out of genuine feeling. Assistant Lin feared him, Su Yurou used him, those business partners stood in awe of him—but not one of them loved him.
He thought power could buy everything, but the very thing he couldn't buy was exactly what Lu Shiyan had—someone willing to walk side by side with him.
"What are you thinking about?" Lu Shiyan's voice pulled her back to reality.
"I'm thinking how lucky I am." Su Qingyuan turned to look at him and smiled.
"Lucky how?"
"Lucky enough to have met you."
Lu Shiyan looked at her smiling face, the light in his eyes as gentle as moonlight.
"It's not luck," he said, his voice low. "It's fate."
The night of Nancheng spread out behind them, the lights of ten thousand homes like a warm, glimmering sea.
Su Qingyuan leaned against his shoulder, gazing at that sea of lights, her heart very still, and very full.

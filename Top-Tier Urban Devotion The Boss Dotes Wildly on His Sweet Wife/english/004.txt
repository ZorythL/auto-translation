New Life From the Cracks
For the last three days before the submission deadline, Su Qingyuan locked herself in her study and barely came out.

The study's floor-to-ceiling windows faced the Capital's CBD skyline—sunlight poured in by day, lights flowed like a river by night. But she had no time to admire the view. Her entire attention was fixed on the project laid out before her, the one she had titled "Cracks and Rebirth."

It was her entry for the International Young Designers Competition.

A complete conceptual design: a community cultural center rebuilt from rubble. The old brick walls were preserved, new steel structures sprouting up through the cracks, glass curtain walls stretching out from broken masonry like branches and leaves grown from time itself. The building wasn't pristine, wasn't smooth—it bore scars, but those scars had been designed into patterns, transformed into the most beautiful part of the structure.

When Su Qingyuan finished the last stroke of the final detail drawing, it was already dark outside.

She set down her pen, leaned back in her chair, and closed her eyes.

Images from the past twenty-three years surged through her mind—the small skylight in the attic, the sketches Zhao Xiulan had thrown out and she had retrieved, her sobbing and screaming the day her university acceptance letter was hidden away, Su Yurou's smile as she cradled the gold trophy at the design competition, the cold, indifferent stares in that hotel banquet hall…

Every crack had become nourishment for new life in this project.

【Ding—Main quest "The Road to Rebirth" progress: 95%. Overall project rating: SSS-tier. Exceeds the average score of the global grand champion works from the past five competitions by 37%.】

Su Qingyuan opened her eyes, the corner of her mouth curving slightly.

It wasn't smugness.

It was a certainty that had grown from the marrow of her bones.

She knew how good this project was. Not because the system told her—she knew it herself. Her eyes, her hands, her heart all told her: this was the finest piece of work she had created in her twenty-three years of life.

Her phone screen lit up.

A message from Lu Shiyan: "Done drawing?"

Su Qingyuan paused. She looked down at herself—her T-shirt was covered in pencil dust, her hair hadn't been washed in three days, dark circles ringed her eyes. She looked just like a soldier who had crawled straight out of a trench.

How did he know?

She walked to the window and glanced down.

Below the Yunxi building, a black Maybach was parked at the curb. Lu Shiyan leaned against the car, phone in hand, his gaze lifted toward the top floor.

Across the distance of several dozen meters, Su Qingyuan couldn't make out his expression, but she could feel his eyes resting on her window.

Just as he had said—however long she drew, he would wait downstairs that long.

Su Qingyuan's heartbeat suddenly quickened.

She turned and rushed out of the study, ran to the bathroom, and looked at herself in the mirror—she really was a mess. She washed her face as fast as she could, redid her hair, changed into clean clothes, then ran down to open the door.

By the time the door opened, Lu Shiyan was already standing at the elevator landing.

Today he wore a thin black sweater and dark gray trousers. He didn't look like the man who controlled a global commercial empire—more like an ordinary young man who had just gotten off work.

But in his hands he carried two bags.

One was a food container, the other a shopping bag.

"You haven't eaten yet," he said. Not a question—a statement.

Su Qingyuan stepped aside to let him in. She wanted to ask "how did you know I hadn't eaten," but what came out instead was: "How long did you wait?"

Lu Shiyan's hand paused as he was changing his shoes.

"Not long."

Su Qingyuan looked at him. There was a small patch of dark water stains on the shoulder of his coat—it was raining outside. She glanced at the time again: nine forty in the evening.

"What time did you start waiting?"

Lu Shiyan didn't answer the question. Carrying the food container, he walked straight to the dining table and took out the dishes one by one.

Stir-fried seasonal vegetables, blanched shrimp, a bowl of corn and pork rib soup, and a small serving of rice.

All of it light, all of it hot.

Su Qingyuan followed behind him, watching as he laid out the food, unwrapped the chopsticks, and set the soup bowl in front of her.

Her nose suddenly stung.

"Lu Shiyan."

"Mm."

"You still haven't answered my question."

Lu Shiyan turned and looked at her.

The light fell on his face, softening the sharp lines of his features. His eyes were deep, her reflection mirrored within them.

"Four in the afternoon," he said.

Su Qingyuan's heart felt as if someone had clenched it hard.

Four in the afternoon until now—more than five hours. He had waited downstairs all that time, in the rain, until she finished the last stroke of her drawing.

"Why didn't you call me?" Her voice was a little hoarse. "You could have sent a message, rung the doorbell…"

"You were drawing." Lu Shiyan cut her off, his tone as flat as if stating something self-evident. "You can't be disturbed while drawing."

Su Qingyuan opened her mouth, but not a single word came out.

This man truly took her affairs to heart. Not empty words, not surface politeness—a genuine concern carved into his very bones.

"Eat," Lu Shiyan said, pulling out a chair.

Su Qingyuan sat down and lowered her head to take a sip of the soup. It was still hot, the sweetness of the corn blending with the savory flavor of the pork ribs, sliding down her throat to warm her entire stomach.

She drank two mouthfuls, then looked up at him. "What about you? Have you eaten?"

Lu Shiyan leaned against the edge of the table, hands tucked into his pockets, his posture casual and relaxed.

"No."

Su Qingyuan set down the soup bowl, stood up, walked to the kitchen, and brought back a fresh bowl and pair of chopsticks, placing them across from her.

"Sit down. Eat with me."

Lu Shiyan glanced at her, the corner of his mouth moving slightly.

He sat down and picked up the chopsticks.

The two of them ate this belated dinner face to face, the Capital's countless lights and the fine, drizzling rain framed in the window outside.

Halfway through the meal, Su Qingyuan suddenly spoke. "Su Yurou signed up for this competition too."

Lu Shiyan's chopsticks didn't pause.

"I know."

"Her work doesn't look like her own style."

"I know that too."

Su Qingyuan looked up at him. "Have you already looked into it?"

Lu Shiyan set down his chopsticks, picked up a napkin from the table, and wiped his hands, his movements unhurried.

"The person ghostwriting for Su Yurou is named Zhou Wantang. Three years older than you, graduated from the same university as you, then went to Milan Polytechnic. After returning to China she opened her own design studio. She's won two minor international awards and has a certain reputation in the industry."

Su Qingyuan's eyes narrowed slightly.

It really was her.

"Why would Zhou Wantang help Su Yurou?" she asked.

"Money," Lu Shiyan said. "Su Yurou gave her two million, plus a share of the prize money and the credit if they win."

Su Qingyuan let out a cold laugh.

Two million. To make her lose, the Su family really was willing to spend.

"What do you plan to do?" Lu Shiyan looked at her.

Su Qingyuan picked up her soup bowl and slowly took a sip.

"Nothing," she said. "If they want to plagiarize, let them plagiarize. If they want to use a ghostwriter, let them. I don't need to win by tearing others down. My work will speak for me."

Lu Shiyan looked into her eyes, his gaze deep.

He had seen too many people. When facing unfair competition, most either flew into uncontrollable rage or swallowed their grievances and compromised. But Su Qingyuan was different. She wasn't angry, wasn't aggrieved, wasn't even in a hurry.

She simply, quietly, with certainty, believed in her own ability.

That kind of confidence wasn't put on. It had grown from the marrow of her bones—something that, after being denied, stripped away, and trampled countless times, still hadn't been broken.

"Su Qingyuan," he said.

"Hmm?"

"You're far stronger than you think."

Su Qingyuan paused, then smiled.

"I know."

The corner of Lu Shiyan's mouth curved.

She really did know. This was what set her apart from everyone else he had ever met—she didn't need anyone to tell her "you can do it"; she had always known she could. What she lacked had never been ability—it was opportunity. And he happened to be the one with no shortage of opportunity to give.

After the meal, Lu Shiyan went to the kitchen to wash the dishes.

Su Qingyuan leaned against the kitchen doorframe, watching him. This man's hands were beautiful—well-defined knuckles, long fingers, hands made for signing ten-billion-yuan contracts—yet at this moment they were diligently scrubbing a soup bowl. Hot water ran down between his fingers, his sleeves rolled up to his forearms, revealing a stretch of lean, powerful wrist and a watch whose logo was so understated as to be almost invisible.

"Lu Shiyan."

"Mm."

"You don't have to do this."

"I know." He set the washed bowl in the drying rack, turned off the faucet, and turned to look at her. "But I want to."

Su Qingyuan's heart gave a sudden leap.

"Want to do what?" she asked, her voice softer than she'd intended.

Lu Shiyan walked over to her and stopped.

The distance was very close. Close enough that she could see the curve of his eyelashes, close enough to feel that clean, almost frosty scent about him.

"Want to be good to you," he said.

Four short words, his voice not loud, but every one of them perfectly clear.

Su Qingyuan's fingers curled slightly.

She lifted her head and met his eyes. Those eyes, always cold as frost, now held a warmth she had never seen before. Not scorching, not searing, but warm and lasting—like magma in the depths of the earth's core that never goes out.

"Lu Shiyan."

"Mm."

"Do you know that saying things like this could make someone misunderstand?"

"Misunderstand what?"

Su Qingyuan opened her mouth, wanting to say "misunderstand that you like me," but those words were too heavy—too heavy for her to be sure she could bear the answer.

She chose to retreat.

"…It's nothing."

Lu Shiyan lowered his head, his gaze falling on her faintly trembling eyelashes. His Adam's apple bobbed, as though he were restraining something.

In the end he only said, "Tomorrow you submit your work. I'll go with you."

Su Qingyuan nodded.

He turned toward the entryway, changed his shoes, and pulled open the door.

"Get some rest," he said.

"You too."

The door closed.

Su Qingyuan leaned back against the kitchen doorframe and slowly slid down to sit on the floor.

Her heart was pounding like a drum.

She raised a hand and pressed it over her chest, where something seemed to be breaking through the soil to sprout.

【Ding—Host's heartbeat value: 78→85. Note: when the heartbeat value exceeds 80, the host's feelings for the target have upgraded from "fondness" to "love." This system advises the host to face her own emotions. If needed, this system can provide a "Confession Phrasing" module, including—】

"Shut up," Su Qingyuan said inwardly, gritting her teeth.

【Understood. Wishing the host sweet dreams tonight.】

At ten o'clock the next morning, Lu Shiyan appeared punctually below the Yunxi building.

When Su Qingyuan walked out of the elevator carrying her drawing tube, she saw Lu Shiyan standing by the car, holding a long-handled black umbrella.

It wasn't raining today; the sun was bright.

"What's the umbrella for?" Su Qingyuan asked.

Lu Shiyan glanced at her, his tone level. "The weather forecast says rain this afternoon."

Su Qingyuan couldn't help laughing. "You're getting more and more like Qin Zhou—wanting everything prepared down to the last detail."

A faint cough came from inside the car.

Su Qingyuan leaned in to look—Qin Zhou was sitting in the driver's seat, his expression complicated.

"Miss Su," Qin Zhou's voice carried a barely perceptible note of grievance, "there's still a fundamental difference between me and Mr. Lu. I'm just an employee. Mr. Lu is…"

"Is what?" Lu Shiyan asked mildly.

Qin Zhou, with an extremely strong survival instinct, shut his mouth and silently started the car.

Su Qingyuan got into the back seat with a laugh; Lu Shiyan climbed in from the other side and sat down beside her.

The car drove toward the competition committee's submission point. Su Qingyuan held the drawing tube, her fingertips gently tracing the patterns on its surface.

"Nervous?" Lu Shiyan asked.

"Not nervous." Su Qingyuan looked out the window. "I'm just wondering what the Su family's reaction will be once I submit this work."

"Whatever the reaction," Lu Shiyan's voice came from beside her, low and steady, "you have me."

Su Qingyuan turned her head and looked at his profile.

He wasn't looking at her when he said it; his gaze rested ahead, his expression as calm as if he were discussing what to have for lunch. But Su Qingyuan heard the weight of those words—not courtesy, not consolation, but a promise made to her by a man who could mobilize countless resources across the globe.

She didn't say thank you, didn't say there's no need.

She only gave a soft "mm," then turned her gaze back to the window.

But the corner of her mouth curved into a very small arc.

The submission point was set up on the second floor of the Capital International Convention and Exhibition Center. By the time Su Qingyuan arrived, the hall was already crowded—contestants there to submit their work, some students from the country's major design schools, others young designers who had already made a name for themselves.

The moment Su Qingyuan walked in, she felt several unfriendly gazes.

She recognized two of them.

One was a classmate from her university days, named Lin Zhixia. Lin Zhixia had been a top student in their class back then, but several of her projects had been pointed out by professors as "too similar" to Su Qingyuan's assignments. Though it was never officially ruled plagiarism, the rumors had persisted in the department.

The other was a former colleague from her previous design firm, named Fang Min. Fang Min was three years older than her and had made plenty of trouble for her at the company. On the day Su Qingyuan resigned, Fang Min had said to others in the break room: "Someone at her level will starve out there."

These two were now standing together, their gazes resting openly on Su Qingyuan—appraising, contemptuous, and tinged with a jealousy that was hard to put into words.

"Oh? Su Qingyuan," Lin Zhixia spoke first, her voice pitched just loud enough for everyone around to hear, "you got eliminated too? No—you made it past the preliminaries? What work did you get in with? Don't tell me you plagiarized it again?"

Su Qingyuan didn't stop walking. She passed right in front of her, tossing out a flat remark: "Lin Zhixia, the signature in the bottom-right corner of your junior-year 'Eye of the City' assignment is still in my handwriting. Should I pull it up right now and show everyone?"

Lin Zhixia's face flushed scarlet in an instant.

She opened her mouth to retort, but meeting Su Qingyuan's eyes—calm to the point of being almost emotionless—all her words stuck in her throat.

Seeing this, Fang Min refused to be outdone and cut in: "Su Qingyuan, don't think you can do whatever you want just because someone's backing you this time. The competition is about skill, not about whose backer is the toughest."

Su Qingyuan stopped and tilted her head to look at her.

"Fang Min, that office-space design you submitted to the 'Huachuang Cup' last month—at least three of the details in it were copied from the Italian designer Luca Bianchi's work from last year. Did I ever mention that Luca Bianchi and I know each other?"

Fang Min's face went white in a flash.

She knew Luca Bianchi? That world-class master designer?

The onlookers around them began to whisper among themselves.

Su Qingyuan paid them no more attention and walked straight to the submission window.

She took out the "Cracks and Rebirth" portfolio from her drawing tube and placed it in front of the staff member.

That portfolio was something she had spent three days crafting by hand—the cover used imported cotton-linen paper, embossed with a raised texture that felt like the brick wall of an old building. The layout, typeface, and white space of the inner pages—every detail had been carefully designed. The entire portfolio was itself like a small work of art.

The staff member was a middle-aged woman, and her eyes lit up when she saw the portfolio.

She opened the first page, and her expression changed.

Not the kind of polite smile, but a genuine, awestruck expression.

She flipped quickly through several pages, then looked up at Su Qingyuan. "Did you… complete this all by yourself?"

"Yes."

The staff member drew a deep breath, entered Su Qingyuan's information into the system, and stamped it "Submitted."

"Miss Su, your work is the best I've seen this year."

The surroundings fell silent for a moment.

The expressions on Lin Zhixia's and Fang Min's faces could no longer even be described as ugly.

Su Qingyuan took the receipt, turned, and left.

As she reached the doorway, Lu Shiyan came walking toward her from the other end of the corridor.

He had waited outside the submission point, not going in. He had said this was her battlefield alone, and he didn't want to influence anyone with his status.

But as Su Qingyuan walked out, he was on the phone, his expression cold and incisive, his tone clipped and forceful: "…I said that clause is non-negotiable. If the other party isn't satisfied, terminate the negotiation."

Seeing Su Qingyuan emerge, he immediately said into the phone, "Do as I said," then hung up and strode over.

"All done?" His tone softened instantly, a world apart from the ruthless, decisive king of the business world he'd been on the phone moments ago.

"All done." Su Qingyuan raised the receipt in her hand.

Lu Shiyan glanced at the receipt, the corner of his mouth moving slightly.

"Come on, I'll treat you to a meal."

"Eat what?"

"Whatever you want."

Su Qingyuan thought for a moment. "Hot pot."

Lu Shiyan's steps faltered.

He couldn't handle spicy food.

But he said, "Sure."

Su Qingyuan looked at his slightly stiffened back and couldn't help bursting into laughter.

"Just teasing you. Let's have Japanese—your stomach isn't good."

Lu Shiyan turned and looked at her.

The afternoon sunlight poured down through the glass dome of the convention center, falling over her. She stood in the light, smiling with her eyes curved into crescents, the whole of her bright as a painting.

Lu Shiyan looked at her for three seconds, then shifted his gaze away, his Adam's apple bobbing.

"…Sure."

Qin Zhou was waiting for them in the car. When he saw his boss walk out with the tips of his ears flushed red, he silently made a mental note.

Today the boss's ears had turned red twice.

The first time was when Miss Su said "your stomach isn't good."

The second time was when Miss Su smiled.

Qin Zhou quietly opened his notes app and added another entry to the "Mr. Lu Falling in Love Observation Diary."

The news of Su Qingyuan submitting her work reached the Su family that very afternoon.

Zhao Xiulan saw it in a design-circle WeChat group. Someone had posted a photo—Su Qingyuan standing in front of the submission window, holding that exquisite portfolio, her profile lit bright and resolute by the convention center's lights.

A string of comments followed beneath the photo:

"This girl's portfolio is so high-end. Who is she?"

"I heard she's Su Yurou's older sister—the one whose work got stolen at the last design competition."

"Really? Then won't her work get stolen again this time?"

"No way—this is an international competition, the review is extremely strict."

Zhao Xiulan's face went as dark as the bottom of a pot.

She rushed into Su Yurou's room with her phone and shoved the screen in her daughter's face: "Look at this! Look at her! She actually entered the competition too! Didn't you say she couldn't afford to register?!"

Su Yurou was lying in bed with a face mask on. When she saw the photo, her fingers suddenly clenched the bedsheet.

Of course she knew Su Qingyuan had entered. Not only did she know—she had prepared a backup plan for it.

"Mom, don't panic." Su Yurou sat up and peeled off the mask. "So what if she entered? What kind of waves can she stir up on her own?"

"But the last incident already let her steal the spotlight once. If this time she's allowed to—"

"There won't be a this time." Su Yurou cut her off, the corner of her mouth curling into a cold, sinister arc. "Mom, what do you think I've been doing all this time? I've already found someone. This time, she won't even make it past the preliminaries."

Zhao Xiulan was stunned. "What do you mean?"

Su Yurou didn't answer. She picked up her phone and dialed a number.

The call connected quickly on the other end.

"Senior Zhou," Su Yurou's voice was sweet as if dipped in honey, "the thing I mentioned to you last time—you can go ahead now."

There was silence on the other end for two seconds.

"Has the money arrived?" Zhou Wantang's voice was cold and direct.

"All two million will be in your account before tomorrow."

"Good."

The call ended.

Su Yurou set down her phone and looked out at the night beyond the window, the smile at the corner of her mouth deepening.

Su Qingyuan, you think you've won just because you have Lu Shiyan backing you?

This world has never been about whoever's in the right winning.

It's about whoever has the money winning.

And this time, she would not give Su Qingyuan any chance to turn things around.

She lowered her head and looked at that photo of Su Qingyuan on her phone, a streak of viciousness flashing in her eyes.

"Sister, this time, I'll make you fall completely into the mud."

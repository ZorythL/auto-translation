The First Crack Blooms
The first draft of "Cracks" was finished on a rainy night.
When Su Qingyuan set down her brush, the first rain since the start of winter was falling outside her window. The fine, dense threads of it tapped against the floor-to-ceiling glass with a soft rustling, like someone sighing gently in some faraway place.
She stepped back a few paces and looked at the painting before her.
On the paper, a building grew out of the darkness. It was not whole—its walls were riven by countless cracks, as if something had torn it apart from within. But within the cracks there was no emptiness; there was light. The light seeped out from the fissures, fine and faint, like sparks that refused to go out. The base of the building was old, weathered brick on the verge of collapse, but the higher it rose, the fewer the cracks and the more the light, until at its summit the entire structure looked as though it had been cast anew out of light—transparent, weightless, almost ready to take flight.
This was "Cracks."
Not a complaint, not a play for pity, not a wound torn open for others to gawk at.
It was telling you—no matter how deep the crack, light can still shine through.

Su Qingyuan didn't look at those numbers. She looked at her painting, and her eyes stung faintly.
This time it wasn't from grievance. It was from pride.
Pride in herself.
Her phone buzzed. A message from Lu Shiyan: "Done with the painting?"
Su Qingyuan glanced out the window—there was no Maybach downstairs. He wasn't here tonight.
"How did you know?" she replied.
"A feeling."
Su Qingyuan stared at those two words for several seconds, and the corners of her mouth curved up. This man—now he was even using words like "feeling." The old Lu Shiyan's dictionary had probably held only cold, clinical terms like "data," "analysis," "results."
"It's done," she typed. "The first draft. I start refining it tomorrow."
"I want to see it."
"I'll show you tomorrow. It's too late tonight—get some rest."
His reply paused for a few seconds.
"I'm downstairs at Yunxi."
Su Qingyuan froze, then hurried to the window and looked down—the street below was empty. No car, no one.
"You're lying to me," she typed.
"I'm not lying. I'm in the basement garage, level B1."
Su Qingyuan drew in a deep breath.
This man wasn't sleeping in the middle of the night—he'd driven over to the garage beneath her building, just to look at her painting?
She picked up her phone and walked out the door still in her slippers. The elevator descended all the way down, and when it reached B1 the doors opened onto the dim light of the garage, the air thick with the faint smell of gasoline.
Lu Shiyan was leaning against his car, wearing a thin charcoal-gray sweater, phone in hand, the blue glow of the screen reflected on his face. Hearing the elevator doors open, he raised his head.
Their eyes met.
"Come up," Su Qingyuan said.
Lu Shiyan put away his phone and walked over.
The two of them rode the elevator back up to the top floor together. Su Qingyuan opened the door, Lu Shiyan changed his shoes and went straight to the study. He stopped before the drafts, crouched down, and studied them one by one.
Su Qingyuan stood behind him, her heart racing. She'd shown him her work many times before, but every time, she still grew nervous. Because every piece was different, every one a sliver of her life from some particular stage.
Lu Shiyan looked for a long time.
So long that Su Qingyuan felt she was about to suffocate.
At last he stood, turned around, and looked at her.
"Su Qingyuan."
"Mm."
"Do you know how much this painting would sell for at auction?"
Su Qingyuan was taken aback. She'd never once considered the question.
"No."
"Ten million. U.S. dollars."
Su Qingyuan's heart gave a violent jolt. Not because of the money—though ten million dollars truly was a great deal—but because of how calmly he said the figure, so calm it was as if he were saying, "It rained today."
"You're flattering me again," she said.
Lu Shiyan frowned slightly, his expression too serious to be joking. "I'm not flattering you. At Christie's last year, a contemporary artwork of the same caliber as this painting sold for twelve million dollars. Yours has more power than that one."
Su Qingyuan opened her mouth and didn't know what to say.
Lu Shiyan walked back to the drafts and lowered his head to look at the most fully realized rendering.
"But this painting can't be sold."
"Why not?"
"Because it belongs to the whole world." He turned his head to look at her. "You painted this not to hang in someone's living room. You painted it so that everyone who feels they have cracks of their own can see—cracks aren't something to fear. A crack is where the light gets in."
Su Qingyuan's tears fell again.
This man could always say, in the calmest tone, the very words most likely to make her cry.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"Can you stop always saying things like that?"
"Things like what?"
"Things that make me want to cry."
Lu Shiyan looked at her red-rimmed eyes and her stubbornly pressed lips, and the corner of his mouth curved.
"All right. From now on I'll say things that make you laugh."
"What kind of thing could make me laugh?"
Lu Shiyan thought for a moment.
"Qin Zhou confessed to Wen Ruan today."
Su Qingyuan froze, tears still on her face, but the corners of her mouth had already started lifting beyond her control. "What?!"
"Wen Ruan was being harassed by an actor on set. Qin Zhou went over, picked the guy up, and threw him out." Lu Shiyan's tone was as flat as if he were reading a news bulletin. "Wen Ruan asked him, 'Do you like me?' He said, 'Yes.' Wen Ruan said, 'Then take me to dinner.' He said, 'Okay.' They should be out for a late-night meal right now."
Su Qingyuan couldn't help laughing, laughing until she doubled over.
She pictured Qin Zhou's eternally deadpan face and tried to imagine his expression when he said "Yes." She pictured Wen Ruan's startled look. She pictured the two of them sitting at a late-night food stall, Qin Zhou eating skewers without batting an eye while Wen Ruan chattered away beside him.
"When did they get together?" she asked, still laughing.
"About three hours ago."
"How do you know?"
"Qin Zhou sent me a message: 'President Lu, I'm taking the night off.' I asked why, and he said: 'Personal matter.'" Lu Shiyan paused. "He's been with me five years. It's the first time he's ever taken leave for a personal matter."
Su Qingyuan laughed even harder.
She wiped her eyes—this time tears from laughing—pulled out her phone, and sent Wen Ruan a message: "I hear someone confessed to you?"
Wen Ruan replied instantly: "AAAAAAAH how did you know!!!"
Su Qingyuan showed the phone to Lu Shiyan. He glanced at it, the corner of his mouth twitched, but he said nothing.
Wen Ruan's messages kept bombarding in: "Qin Zhou never says anything normally, and when he finally does it's just 'Yes'!!! He answered with one single word!!! But he's so handsome, what do I do, Qingyuan, I'm done for, I've fallen!!!"
Su Qingyuan replied, laughing: "Didn't you always want someone who says little but gets things done?"
Wen Ruan: "But he says WAY too little!!! We've been sitting here eating for half an hour and he's said exactly three things: 'What do you want to eat,' 'Okay,' and 'I'll take you home.'"
Su Qingyuan: "Isn't that great? Concise and to the point."
Wen Ruan: "But I want to hear him say other stuff!! Like 'You look so pretty'!!! He won't even look at me!!!"
Su Qingyuan turned to Lu Shiyan. "When Qin Zhou's chasing a girl, does he never look at her?"
Lu Shiyan considered. "He looked. Twenty-seven times. I counted."
Su Qingyuan: "……"
This man—he'd even count how many times his assistant glanced at someone.
She relayed Lu Shiyan's words to Wen Ruan. Wen Ruan sent a long string of exclamation marks, then said: "He looked at me and I didn't even notice!!!"
Su Qingyuan: "Because you were looking at him too."
Wen Ruan went silent for five seconds, then sent a "shut up" sticker.
Laughing, Su Qingyuan set down her phone and looked at Lu Shiyan. He was studying her drafts, the profile of his face especially gentle in the light of the desk lamp.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"Thank you."
Lu Shiyan turned his head to look at her.
"For what?"
"For letting Qin Zhou go after Wen Ruan."
Lu Shiyan raised an eyebrow slightly. "I didn't make him go after her. He went on his own."
"Then how many hints did you give him?"
Lu Shiyan was silent for a second.
"……Three."
Su Qingyuan laughed.
This man—he even managed his assistant's love life. But his way of managing it was peculiar—not orders, not arrangements, but creating opportunities and then letting everything happen naturally.
Just like what he did with her.
"Come here," Su Qingyuan said.
Lu Shiyan walked over and stopped in front of her.
Su Qingyuan rose on her tiptoes, reached out, and lightly brushed his hair. His hair was very soft, not what she'd imagined. She'd thought a man as cold and hard as him would have hair just as hard, but it actually felt like the down of some small animal.
Lu Shiyan's body stiffened slightly.
"Your hair is so soft," Su Qingyuan said.
Lu Shiyan didn't answer, but the tips of his ears turned red.
Su Qingyuan drew her hand back, her eyes curving with her smile. "All right, you can head home now. It's very late."
Lu Shiyan looked at her, his gaze deep.
"Su Qingyuan."
"Mm?"
"You just touched my head."
"Mm, what about it?"
"Do you know what it means to touch a man's head?"
Su Qingyuan's heart suddenly raced, but she didn't shrink back. Instead she tilted her face up, looked into his eyes, and said with a smile, "It means I wanted to touch it."
Lu Shiyan's throat bobbed.
He reached out and gently pinched her earlobe. The pad of his finger was cool, but the instant it touched her earlobe it was as if he'd been scalded, and he pulled it back.
"Now we're even," he said.
Su Qingyuan's ears turned red enough to bleed.
The two of them stood facing each other in the entryway, both with red ears, neither looking at the other.
"Goodnight," Lu Shiyan said first.
"Goodnight."
Lu Shiyan pulled the door open and walked out. The instant the door closed, Su Qingyuan leaned against it and slowly slid down to sit on the floor, burying her face in her knees.
Her ears were still burning.
She reached up and touched her own earlobe—it seemed to still hold the warmth of his fingertip.

Su Qingyuan sat on the floor for quite a while before she stood and walked back to the study.
She carefully put away the "Cracks" drafts and slid them into a drawing tube. Then she opened her laptop and began writing the first business plan for Yanyi Culture.
If she was going to start a company, it couldn't be just a name on paper. She would have her own team, her own projects, her own brand. She didn't want to live forever beneath Lu Shiyan's wing—warm as that was, what she wanted more was to stand shoulder to shoulder with him.
She wrote the plan until three in the morning.
She wrote out the company's vision, its scope of business, its organizational structure, its three-year development roadmap. She didn't go into too much detail, since many things could only be settled once the company was actually up and running. But she made one thing crystal clear—Yanyi Culture's core competitiveness wasn't "Lu Shiyan's investment," it was "Su Qingyuan's design."
She wanted everyone to know that this company would survive and grow not because someone powerful was backing it, but because her work was good enough.
After writing the last word, she saved the document and shut down the laptop.
Outside the window, the night over the Capital was deep. In the distance, a few scattered lights still glowed in the office towers, like the eyes of people who couldn't sleep.
Lying in bed, before she closed her eyes, Su Qingyuan sent Lu Shiyan a message: "I finished the business plan."
To her surprise he was still awake, and replied instantly: "What business plan?"
"Yanyi Culture's business plan."
His reply paused for a few seconds.
"Send it to me."
Su Qingyuan sent over the document.
A few more minutes passed, and Lu Shiyan sent a message: "Su Qingyuan, you don't need to write this."
Su Qingyuan's heart sank.
"Why not?"
"Because at Yanyi Culture, you have the final say in everything. You can do whatever you want, however you want, no business plan required."
Su Qingyuan stared at that line, a tangle of emotions welling up inside her. There was the warmth of being moved, of being trusted, but also a little stubbornness.
"But I want to prove to you that I don't have to rely on you."
Lu Shiyan's reply came quickly.
"You never need to prove anything to me. You only need to be yourself. When you're being yourself, you're already the very best there is."
Su Qingyuan pressed the phone to her chest and closed her eyes.
This man really did know how to make her heart go soft.
She rolled over, buried her face in the pillow, and muttered something only she could hear: "Lu Shiyan, you're really such a nuisance."
But she was smiling.
Smiling so hard her smile pressed a little hollow into the pillow.
The next morning, Su Qingyuan got a video call from Philippe Delort.
The old man sat in his study in Milan, a whole wall of design sketches behind him. Today he wore a red sweater, looking full of spirit, his eyes bright as two stars.
"Su, how far along is your 'Cracks'?" he came straight to the point.
Su Qingyuan aimed the camera at the drafts and showed them to him one by one.
Philippe watched intently, asking many questions with each one: "Why this material here?" "Why does the line run in this direction here?" "How did you handle this effect of light?"
Su Qingyuan answered each in turn.
After Philippe had heard her out, he leaned back in his chair and let out a long breath.
"Su."
"Mr. Delort."
"You don't need me anymore."
Su Qingyuan was taken aback.
"Your work has already gone beyond the scope of what I can teach." There was no regret in Philippe's voice, only pride. "What you need now isn't a teacher, it's a stage. And you already have one—Milan Design Week. Su, you've graduated."
Su Qingyuan's eyes reddened.
"Mr. Delort, you'll always be my teacher."
Philippe laughed, laughing like a child.
"All right. Then I'll wait for you to come to Milan. When you do, I'll sit in the front row to see your exhibition."
"You'll definitely be in the front row."
After the video call ended, Su Qingyuan sat at her desk, gazing out the window, lost in thought for a long while.
She suddenly realized something—from the moment she met Lu Shiyan until now, in less than two months, her life had been turned completely upside down.
Two months ago, she was still living in a thirty-square-meter rented room, eating milk five days past its expiration date and wilted cucumbers, with only three thousand yuan left in her bank account.
Two months later, she lived in the most expensive apartment in the Capital, with two million yuan in her bank account—the first advance payment on the "Listening to the Sea" project, which Lu Shiyan had insisted on paying early—and when she couldn't refuse, she'd accepted it.
Two million.
She'd never seen so much money in her life.
But she hadn't spent it recklessly. She saved most of it, taking out only a small portion to buy herself new design tools and a few decent pieces of clothing. She wasn't the kind of person to let money go to her head—her experiences with the Su family had taught her that money mattered, but money wasn't everything. What truly mattered was ability, was the work, was the things no one could ever take from her.
Her phone buzzed. A message from Qin Zhou.
"Miss Su, all of Yanyi Culture's registration procedures are now complete. The business license, the company seal, the bank account—everything is ready. The company is located in the CWTC Tower in the CBD, an entire floor. Mr. Lu says the décor style is yours to decide."
Su Qingyuan looked at the message and took a deep breath.
The company—it was really up and running.
Not a shell company under Lu Shiyan's name, but a company truly her own, one where she had the final say.
She sent Lu Shiyan a message: "Thank you."
Lu Shiyan replied: "It's not thanks. It's investment."
Su Qingyuan laughed. This man—he always had to dress up something emotional as something rational. But she knew that wasn't how he really felt inside.
"Then I'll do it well. I can't let you lose money," she replied.
"You won't let me lose money."
"You believe in me that much?"
"It's not belief. It's knowing."
Su Qingyuan stared at the word "knowing" for a long time.
Knowing. Not believing. Belief could be wrong; knowing was certain. When Lu Shiyan said "knowing," it meant that in his eyes, her success wasn't a matter of probability—it was a matter of inevitability.
This man believed in her more than she believed in herself.
That afternoon, Su Qingyuan went to the CWTC Tower.
Yanyi Culture's office was on the forty-seventh floor, the entire floor wrapped in floor-to-ceiling glass curtain walls, looking out over the whole CBD. Standing at the window, Su Qingyuan felt as if she were standing on the very heart of the Capital.
Qin Zhou had come with her, standing behind her, waiting quietly.
"Qin Zhou." Su Qingyuan didn't turn around.
"Yes."
"You and Wen Ruan… are you really together now?"
Qin Zhou was silent for a second.
"Yes."
"Are you happy?"
Qin Zhou was silent again for a second.
"……Yes."
Su Qingyuan turned her head and looked at Qin Zhou's eternally unchanging poker face. His expression was no different from usual, but she noticed his eyes were a little brighter than usual, and the curve of his mouth a fraction higher—something no one who didn't know him very well would ever catch.
"That's good then." Su Qingyuan smiled. "Wen Ruan is my best friend. If you ever mistreat her, I won't let you off."
Qin Zhou's expression finally shifted a touch—his brow moved slightly, as if he wanted to say something but held it back.
"Miss Su," he said, "I won't mistreat her. I'll treat her the way Mr. Lu treats you."
Su Qingyuan was taken aback, then smiled even wider.
"Qin Zhou, you don't talk much normally, but when you do, you make people want to cry."
Without any change in expression, Qin Zhou took a pack of tissues from his pocket and handed it to her.
Su Qingyuan took the tissues, laughing until she doubled over.
On her way out of the CWTC Tower, Su Qingyuan ran into someone.
Lin Haoyu.
Her ex-ex-boyfriend—no, "ex-boyfriend" no longer fit; he should be called her "cheating ex-boyfriend." The man who had left her when she was at her lowest and gotten together with Su Yurou.
He stood at the entrance of the CWTC Tower in a poorly fitting suit, a bouquet of flowers in his hand, "regret" written all over his face.
The instant Su Qingyuan saw him, her steps didn't pause, didn't even slow.
"Qingyuan!" Lin Haoyu chased after her and blocked her path.
Su Qingyuan stopped and looked at him.
Two months without seeing him, he'd lost a lot of weight, dark circles heavy under his eyes, stubble he hadn't shaved clean on his chin. He was no longer the spirited student council president from their university days; he looked more like a middle-aged man beaten down by life, ragged and wretched.
"What are you here for?" Su Qingyuan's tone was as calm as if she were asking a stranger.
Lin Haoyu held out the flowers. "Qingyuan, I came to apologize. I wronged you before. Getting together with Su Yurou was a mistake. She's not a good person at all—she just used me…"
"And so?" Su Qingyuan cut him off.
Lin Haoyu froze.
"And so, the moment you realized she was using you, you came running back to me?" Su Qingyuan looked into his eyes—no anger, no grievance, not even contempt. "Lin Haoyu, you didn't come to apologize. You came begging to be taken in. Su Yurou threw you away, your career is gone, your reputation stinks, you have nothing left now—so you remembered me."
Lin Haoyu's face went pale, then flushed, by turns.
"Qingyuan, I know I wronged you before, but I really do realize my mistake now…"
"You realize your mistake?" Su Qingyuan tilted her head slightly. "Then tell me—what exactly was your mistake?"
Lin Haoyu opened his mouth. "I shouldn't have cheated…"
"And what else?"
"I shouldn't have… shouldn't have gotten together with Su Yurou…"
"And what else?"
Lin Haoyu couldn't get another word out.
Su Qingyuan looked at him, the corner of her mouth curving slightly—the kind of polite smile that carried no feeling at all.
"Lin Haoyu, your mistakes are too many to count, so let me count them for you. First, you pursued me because I was a daughter of the Su family, and you thought I could help you. Second, the moment you learned Su Yurou had more say in the Su family, you dumped me without a second thought. Third, after you got together with Su Yurou, you talked behind my back, calling me a 'psycho' who 'wouldn't stop hounding you.' Fourth, you came to me today not out of regret, but because Su Yurou is finished and you want to climb your way back to me."
Lin Haoyu's face went completely white.
"Qingyuan, I didn't…"
"You did." Su Qingyuan's voice wasn't loud, but every word drove into his ears like a nail. "Lin Haoyu, why did I fall for you back then? Because you came to pick me up when I worked part-time late into the night, because you gave me a bouquet of flowers on my birthday. Back then I thought you were the best man in the whole world. It was only later that I realized none of it was real. You were just playing the role of a 'good boyfriend,' because you thought playing it well would get you what you wanted."
Su Qingyuan looked at him, her gaze as calm as a mirror, reflecting back every bit of his ugliness.
"Lin Haoyu, I don't hate you. Hating you would require me to care about you. But to me, you stopped being anything a long time ago."
With that, she stepped around him and kept walking.
Qin Zhou followed behind her, and as he passed Lin Haoyu, he paused.
"Mr. Lin," Qin Zhou's voice was very flat, "I'd advise you not to come looking for Miss Su again. Otherwise, even that last little bit of career you have left won't survive."
Lin Haoyu shuddered all over.
Qin Zhou didn't say another word, striding off to catch up with Su Qingyuan.
Su Qingyuan settled into the car, leaned back in her seat, and looked out the window.
Qin Zhou started the engine and glanced at her in the rearview mirror.
"Miss Su, are you all right?"
"I'm fine." Su Qingyuan smiled. "Better than I'd imagined. I thought seeing him would hurt, but it didn't hurt at all. If anything, I felt… relieved."
"Why?"
"Because I could never figure out before why I'd ever liked him. Seeing him today, I finally understood." Su Qingyuan watched the city scenery flying backward outside the window. "It wasn't because he was good. It was because back then I was so starved for love. Anyone who was a little kind to me felt like the whole world. But it's different now."
She didn't finish, but Qin Zhou understood.
It was different now. Because she'd met someone who was truly good to her. Because her eyes had been opened, and she knew now what real goodness looked like.
The car stopped at a red light. Qin Zhou looked at Su Qingyuan in the rearview mirror and, rarely, added one more line: "Miss Su, the way Mr. Lu treats you—it's real."
Su Qingyuan turned her head and looked at Qin Zhou.
"I know," she said, her voice soft but certain.
When she got back to Yunxi, Su Qingyuan found a cardboard box at her door.
She opened it to find a complete set of design tools—from markers to scale rulers, from drawing boards to professional-grade colored pencils, all the best brands on the market. On top of the box sat a card, the handwriting bold and forceful: "Yanyi Culture's first project—start by designing your own desk."
Su Qingyuan pressed the card to her chest and smiled.
She took out her phone and sent Lu Shiyan a message: "Got the tools. Lin Haoyu came to find me today."
He replied instantly: "I know. Qin Zhou told me."
"You're not going to ask how I handled it?"
"No need. I know you'll have handled it well."
Su Qingyuan looked at that line, her heart racing. This man never asked her "Are you okay?", never asked her "Are you all right?", never treated her as someone who needed protecting. He trusted that she could handle everything; he would only appear when she needed him.
"I told him he was nothing to me," Su Qingyuan typed.
"What did he say?"
"He couldn't say a word."
"Naturally. Because what you said was the truth."
Su Qingyuan laughed, and as she laughed her nose began to sting again.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"Thank you for letting me know what it really means to like someone."
His reply paused for a long time. So long that Su Qingyuan thought he wouldn't answer at all.
Then the screen lit up.
"Su Qingyuan, the things you've let me know are even more."
"Like what?"
"Like the fact that being alive has meaning."
Su Qingyuan's tears fell.
She held her phone, sitting on the carpet of Yunxi's top floor, the ten thousand lights of the Capital beyond the window, and in her arms a man's heart that he restrained with all his strength and that still overflowed despite him.
She cried for a while, and then she smiled.
Crying and laughing at once, like a fool.
But she didn't care anymore.
Because in this world, at last there was one person who let her never have to pretend again.

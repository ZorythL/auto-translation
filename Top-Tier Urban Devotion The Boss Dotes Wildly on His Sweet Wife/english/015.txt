Lu Shiyan parked the car downstairs from Su Qingyuan's apartment building but didn't cut the engine right away.
The car was so quiet they could hear each other breathing. The warmth from where their foreheads had touched a moment ago still lingered on her skin, like a tiny brand.
Su Qingyuan unbuckled her seatbelt, but she didn't push the door open to get out.
She turned her head and glanced at him. His hand was still resting on the steering wheel, fingertips tapping lightly without his noticing, the rhythm a little faster than usual—he was nervous.
The discovery inexplicably made her want to laugh.
"I'll head up, then," she said.
"Mm."
"You should get home early too."
"Mm."
After two "mm"s, neither of them moved.
Su Qingyuan finally couldn't hold back her laughter. "Lu Shiyan, are you going to say something to me or not?"
Lu Shiyan turned to look at her. The glow of the streetlamp beyond the window fell into his eyes, like shattered stars.
"Say what?" he asked, knowing full well.
"Such as…" Su Qingyuan drew the words out. "What exactly are we now?"
Lu Shiyan looked into her eyes, the corners of his mouth slowly curving up. The smile was impossible to suppress, so he simply stopped trying.
"You tell me."
"I'm asking you."
"I'd say," he leaned in, his voice dropping low, "it's the kind where, from this day on, I'm part of all of it—every triumph, every mess."
Su Qingyuan's heart skipped another beat.
This man. He wouldn't just say something ordinary like "boyfriend and girlfriend"—no, he had to use one long sentence to make her blush.
"Can't you just say things straight?"
"No." Lu Shiyan said it with complete conviction. "Saying 'you're my girlfriend' outright would be letting you off too easy."
Su Qingyuan: "…What kind of logic is that?"
"The logic of a man who's been chasing you this long." Lu Shiyan lowered his gaze to her, his voice suddenly going soft. "Su Qingyuan, do you have any idea how long I've waited for this moment, from that rainy night until now?"
Su Qingyuan fell silent.
Of course she knew. The system broadcast his affection level every single day. Even though he himself had no idea the system existed, she'd watched every flutter of his heart, every act of restraint, every quiet thing he'd done for her—all of it, perfectly clear.
"Not that long," she said, her voice gentle. "Barely over a week."
"To me, it felt like a year."
Su Qingyuan pressed her lips together, but the corners of her mouth refused to come down. She reached out and gave him a push. "All right, I really should go up now. I've got the studio tomorrow."
"I'll walk you up."
"No need, it's just a few steps—"
Lu Shiyan was already out of the car.
He came around the front, pulled open her door, and held out his hand.
Su Qingyuan looked at that hand, knuckles sharply defined, hesitated for a fraction of a second, then placed her own in it.
His palm was dry and warm, his grip neither too firm nor too loose—just enough to make her feel safe without feeling held captive.
The two of them walked hand in hand through the building's entrance and up the stairs.
Su Qingyuan fished her keys out of her bag, and as she slid the key into the lock, she glanced back at him.
The stairwell light was a dim yellow. He stood two steps below her, tilting his head up slightly to look at her. The angle softened the usually severe lines of his face, making him look like a boy waiting at the door, reluctant to say goodbye.
"I'm going in," Su Qingyuan said.
"Mm."
"Be careful on your way down."
"Mm."
Two more "mm"s.
Su Qingyuan bit her lip, then suddenly rose onto her toes and pecked him quickly on the cheek.
A dragonfly skimming water—light as a flower petal landing on skin.
Then she swiftly opened the door, slipped inside, and slammed it shut with a bang.
One fluid motion, fast as if she'd rehearsed it.
Inside the door, Su Qingyuan leaned back against it, both hands pressed over her face, her ears red enough to bleed.
Outside the door, Lu Shiyan stood rooted in place. Slowly he lifted his fingers and touched the spot on his skin where she'd kissed him.
Then he smiled.
Not his usual restrained, reserved little smile—a real one, rising from the bottom of his heart, impossible to suppress no matter what.
He turned and headed downstairs, his footsteps echoing in the empty stairwell, every step light as if he were treading on clouds.
【Devotion System Alert: Female lead initiated a kiss on the male lead's cheek—first kiss (cheek edition).】
【Male lead's reaction: Stood in place and smiled for seventeen seconds.】
【Affection level: Has reached 99%. The remaining 1% requires a special event to trigger.】
【System remark: You two, getting this happy over a peck on the cheek—how are you going to manage in the future?】
Su Qingyuan lifted her face out of her hands and glared at her phone screen. "System, can you stop peeking!"
【System: The system has no eyes, only data perception. By the way, a reminder to the female lead—the corners of your mouth haven't come down once since you walked in the door.】
Su Qingyuan instinctively touched the corner of her mouth.
It really was turned up.
She walked over to the window, pulled back the curtain, and looked down. Lu Shiyan had just stepped out of the building entrance and reached his car. Before pulling the door open, he raised his head and looked, with perfect precision, toward her window.
The two of them locked eyes across five floors.
Su Qingyuan didn't duck away, and Lu Shiyan didn't wave.
They just looked, for a single moment.
Then he got into the car, the engine started, and he drove out of the complex.
Su Qingyuan watched the taillights disappear at the intersection, drew the curtain shut, leaned against the window, and reached up to touch her own lips.
That kiss just now—it had been too fast. So fast she hadn't even had time to feel it.
Next time… next time, maybe linger a little longer?
She shook her head hard, flinging the dangerous thought out of her mind.
Su Qingyuan! What are you thinking! You only just made it official!
【System: But your heart rate is giving you away.】
Su Qingyuan: …System, shut up and go to sleep.

The next day, when Su Qingyuan arrived at the studio, she found something new by the door.
A plant stand.
Wrought iron, white, set neatly beside the door, already holding a pot of pothos and a bunch of white chamomile.
A sticky note was stuck to the stand, in clean, sharp fountain-pen handwriting:
"Passed a flower shop this morning, picked these up on the way. No need to thank me. —Lu"
Su Qingyuan looked at the words "on the way," and remembered how yesterday he'd said "your eyes don't look like they're just admiring it" and then bought the flowers—and now today it was "on the way" again.
This man—just how many "on the ways" did he have?
She carefully peeled off the sticky note and tucked it into the notebook on her desk, then snapped a photo of the plant stand and sent it to Lu Shiyan:
【This "on the way" is a little big.】
Lu Shiyan replied in a heartbeat: 【Do you like it?】
Su Qingyuan: 【If I say I like it, are you going to "happen to" buy something else tomorrow?】
Lu Shiyan: 【No more "on the way." Tomorrow I'll buy it on purpose.】
Su Qingyuan stared at that line of text and laughed out loud.
She sat down at her worktable, opened her sketchbook, and tried to draw a new design. But the moment her pen tip touched the paper, her mind was full of nothing but his face.
She bit the cap of her pen, struggling to focus, and drew a few strokes on the paper—then looked down to find she'd drawn the line of a man's profile.
Su Qingyuan: "…"
She flipped past that page and started over.
This time, she drew the next piece in the "First Light" series. The inspiration came fast, her pen gliding across the paper, the lines smooth and certain.
As she drew, she suddenly stopped, and in the corner of the draft she wrote a small line of text:
"For Lu."
Once she'd written it, she felt it was too sappy and wanted to scribble it out. Her pen hovered above the paper—but in the end, she didn't.
Forget it.
He wouldn't see it anyway.
【System: But the system can see it, and will record it as future "sugar."】
Su Qingyuan: Can you not treat everything as sugar?
【System: No. This is the very meaning of the system's existence.】

That afternoon, while Su Qingyuan was polishing a sample, her phone suddenly buzzed like crazy.
She picked it up and saw messages from Zhou Wanyin, several in a row, every single one with exclamation marks:
【Qingyuan!!! Check Weibo!!!】
【You're trending!!!】
【No wait—you and Lu Shiyan are trending together!!!】
Su Qingyuan's heart lurched. She opened Weibo.
Number one on the trending list: #Lu Shiyan's Relationship#
Number three on the trending list: #Who Is Su Qingyuan#
Number seven on the trending list: #Lu Group President's Suspected Relationship Exposed#
With slightly trembling fingers she tapped into the first one and saw a set of photos.
In the photos were her and Lu Shiyan. Last night. Downstairs from the apartment.
Him leading her by the hand through the entrance. Her rising on her toes to kiss his cheek. Him smiling at her.
All of it captured.
The angle was shot from an upper floor of the building across from the complex—a long way off, but the lens was good, capturing both their expressions crystal clear.
Su Qingyuan's mind went blank with a buzz.
She scrolled down to the comments.
The comment section had already exploded.
"Lu Shiyan??? The Lu Shiyan who never attends a single party??? He actually smiled???"
"Who's this girl? So pretty! Such a great presence!"
"Wait, isn't this the Su Qingyuan who went head-to-head with her foster father on a livestream yesterday?!"
"It's her!! I watched that livestream yesterday! She's the jewelry designer who was abused by her foster parents and had her designs stolen by her sister!"
"Oh my god, what kind of dream plotline is this? The bullied little designer turns the tables AND lands Nancheng's most eligible diamond bachelor?"
"Not 'lands'—look at the photo, the girl's the one who kissed the guy! So sweet ahhh!"
"The way Lu Shiyan looks at her… I can't anymore, this man's eyes are full of nothing but her."
But a small portion of comments were less friendly:
"Another gold-digger climbing the ladder."
"Designer? You mean the one accused of plagiarism?"
"Will the Lu Group shareholders even agree to this?"
Su Qingyuan stared at those comments, her mind a tangled mess.
She and Lu Shiyan had made it official less than twenty-four hours ago, and already the entire internet had exposed them. This wasn't what they had planned—it wasn't even something they'd chosen to make public themselves.
Who took the photos? Why post them? What was the goal?
Before she could think it through, her phone rang.
Lu Shiyan.
"You've seen it?" His voice was very calm—so calm it didn't quite seem like a normal reaction.
"I've seen it." Su Qingyuan forced her own voice to steady. "Do you know who took the photos?"
"I found out." There was an added trace of coldness in Lu Shiyan's tone. "Lin Haoyu."
Su Qingyuan froze for a beat. "Lin Haoyu?"
"He staked out the building across from your complex all night. He sold the photos to a marketing account—took eight thousand for them."
Su Qingyuan closed her eyes and drew a deep breath.
Lin Haoyu.
The man who'd knelt in the hotel lobby begging to get back together, whom she'd cursed back to where he came from.
The man who'd traded curses with Su Yurou over the phone, the two of them tearing each other apart.
Failing to win her back, he'd started his revenge.
Not revenge against Su Yurou—revenge against her.
Because he couldn't have her, he had to destroy her?
"What do you plan to do about it?" Su Qingyuan asked.
"It's already handled." Lu Shiyan's voice was flat. "The photos can't be taken down—they've already spread across the internet, so there'd be no point. But I've had the legal team sue that marketing account and Lin Haoyu for violation of portrait rights and reputation rights. The damages won't be small. On top of that, Lin Haoyu's company has received some news—he'll probably be laid off very soon."
Su Qingyuan was silent for a few seconds.
"You move fast."
"Not fast," Lu Shiyan said. "From learning it was him to finishing all of that took forty minutes. I thought it was too slow."
Su Qingyuan didn't know what to say.
This man—while she was still panicking, still wondering "what do we do," had already finished cleaning up every last bit of the aftermath.
"So… the trending thing, what do we do about that?" she asked.
There were two seconds of quiet on the other end of the line.
"Su Qingyuan," Lu Shiyan's voice suddenly softened, carrying a tenderness she'd never heard before, "would you be willing to post a Weibo, together with me?"
Su Qingyuan's heart raced. "Post what?"
"Go public."
Just those two words.
Su Qingyuan gripped her phone, her palm breaking into a sweat.
Go public.
Not be secretly photographed, be exposed, be forced to admit it.
He was the one choosing to go public.
After everything blew up, he didn't choose to handle it quietly, didn't choose to deny it, didn't choose to "protect her" by hiding her away.
He chose—to stand beside her, and face it together.
"Okay," Su Qingyuan said.
"Really?" There was a faint, almost imperceptible nervousness in Lu Shiyan's voice.
"Really. What do you want to post?"
"Give me a minute."

Three minutes later, Su Qingyuan's Weibo received a mention.
Lu Shiyan's account—the one verified as "Chairman of the Lu Group," with tens of millions of followers but which had never once posted an original message—put up its first piece of content.
Just one photo, and one line of text.
The photo was that moment last night when she'd kissed his cheek, the one that had been secretly captured. But he hadn't used the blurry image the marketing account had posted—he'd had someone pull the high-definition original from the surveillance footage.
The text was only a single sentence:
"I'm the one who asked for this. She isn't climbing the ladder."
A single short line, with no extra justification, none of the canned "please give us our space" lines.
Just one resounding declaration—"I'm the one who asked."
The comment section exploded instantly.
"Ahhhhh he's so bold!!!"
"What this means is: she's not climbing the ladder, I'm the one who reached above my station for her?"
"Lu Shiyan, you've changed! You're not that cold-faced president anymore!"
"This is the most masculine way of going public I've ever seen."
"So it's President Lu who pursued Su Qingyuan? Oh my god, who exactly is this woman?"
"She's no one in particular—just a very hardworking, very talented designer. President Lu has great taste."
Su Qingyuan stared at that Weibo post, her eyes growing hot.
She reposted it, adding a line of her own:
"No ladder to climb. Just a person walking forward together with me."
The moment she sent it, her phone began buzzing wildly. Her follower count surged from a few thousand to several hundred thousand—private messages, comments, likes, the numbers leaping so fast they made her dizzy.
Zhou Wanyin was the first to comment: "Congratulations!!! Should the studio throw a celebration party?"
A famous jewelry magazine's official account chimed in too: "Looking forward to the official launch of Miss Su's 'First Light' series!"
Even a few of the normally lofty industry heavyweights liked and reposted, one after another.
The tide turned completely, overnight.
The earlier voices crying "ladder-climber" and "plagiarism freak" were drowned out beneath an overwhelming flood of blessings and curiosity.
Su Qingyuan leaned back against her chair, looked up at the ceiling, and let out a long breath.
In her chat window with Lu Shiyan, a new message arrived from him:
【Nervous?】
Su Qingyuan: 【A little.】
Lu Shiyan: 【Me too.】
Su Qingyuan looked at those two words, "me too," and suddenly felt that the man standing on the eighty-eighth floor, in command of a trillion-yuan business empire, was at this moment just like her—heart racing, palms sweating.
That realization inexplicably set her at ease.
Su Qingyuan: 【Then let's be nervous together.】
Lu Shiyan: 【Okay. Together.】
She set down her phone, walked to the window, and pushed it open.
The scent of osmanthus still lingered.
The late-autumn wind blew against her face, cool, but inside her chest it was warm.
She thought of that 1% the system had mentioned.
Maybe that 1% wasn't a matter of not liking each other enough.
It was a certain moment—when they stood side by side before everyone, with nothing to hide and nothing to fear, neither groveling nor arrogant, and said "Yes, we're together"—
That moment was the 100%.

Outside the window, the Nancheng sky was awash with sunset, layer upon layer of clouds in orange-red, pink-purple, and golden-yellow, like a vast oil painting.
She picked up her phone, snapped a photo of the sky, and sent it to Lu Shiyan.
【The sunset is beautiful today.】
Lu Shiyan: 【Mm. I'm looking at it too.】
Su Qingyuan: 【You're watching from the eighty-eighth floor—it's definitely a better view than mine.】
Lu Shiyan: 【No. The view from where you are is better. Because you're the one watching.】
Su Qingyuan stared at that line, the corners of her mouth curving to their widest possible arc.
This man—nervous right up until they went public, and the moment it was official, he started dishing out sweet nothings.
She thought for a long while, then sent back one line:
【Lu Shiyan, did you secretly practice how to flirt before?】
Lu Shiyan: 【No. After meeting you, it just came to me, no teacher required.】
Su Qingyuan slapped her phone face-down on the desk and buried her face in both hands.
She was done for.
Completely done for.
In this life, she'd fallen straight into this man's hands.
【System: Told you long ago—you two are a perfect match.】
Su Qingyuan: Didn't you retreat behind the scenes?
【System: I pop out now and then to remind you I exist. All right, really retiring now. Be sweet, you two.】
Su Qingyuan shook her head with a laugh, picked up her phone again, and looked at that photo of the sunset and her chat window with Lu Shiyan. Her heart was full, as if something had packed it tight, fuller and fuller.
That something was called the certainty of being loved.
Not "maybe," not "a little."
Certainty.
The certainty she'd never once possessed in this life, now finally held firm in the palm of her hand.

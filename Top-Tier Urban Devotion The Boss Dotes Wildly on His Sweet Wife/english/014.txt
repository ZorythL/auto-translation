By the time Su Qingyuan arrived at Lawyer Liu's firm, it was already two in the afternoon.
Lawyer Liu was in her early forties, with short hair, no makeup, and sharp eyes. She went through every piece of material Su Qingyuan had brought—from the evidence of her adoptive parents docking her wages and restricting her freedom in the early years, to the court verdict on Su Yurou stealing her design drafts and framing her with false charges, all the way to the chat logs and transfer receipts of Su Guoqiang bribing a marketing account to spread slander this time around.
Page after page, Lawyer Liu's expression went from calm to grave, from grave to silent.
When she finished the last page, she took off her glasses and pinched the bridge of her nose.
"Ms. Su, with this evidence, you could put them away for the rest of their lives."
Su Qingyuan sat across from her, her expression composed. "I don't need them behind bars. I need a public apology, and an admission that the 'First Light' series is my original work. And on top of that, to sever the adoptive relationship—so we're square, once and for all."
Lawyer Liu glanced at her. "You're sure you don't want to go the criminal route? Defamation, copyright infringement—the evidence is more than enough."
"I'm sure." There wasn't a trace of hesitation in Su Qingyuan's voice. "What I want isn't to throw them in prison; it's to cut every last tie between them and me, completely. A criminal case drags on too long, and my career can't afford to wait. A civil case is fast."
Lawyer Liu nodded, then pulled out her phone to glance at the trending topics on Weibo.
"Things are still heating up on Su Guoqiang's end. The internet's split into two camps now. Plenty of people cursing you out—but more and more are starting to speak up for you, too. Especially after Zhou Wanyin put out her statement."
Su Qingyuan paused for a beat, then opened Weibo.
Zhou Wanyin's account had posted a long article, titled "Regarding the 'First Light' Series, I Stake My Career On It."
There wasn't a single wasted word in the whole piece—she laid out three things right up front: the original-work declaration Su Qingyuan had submitted when she joined the studio, the contract's guarantee clause stating that "all works are original," and—Zhou Wanyin's own professional appraisal report on the seven pieces of the "First Light" series.
The last line of the report read: "Upon appraisal by myself and the studio's professional team, all seven pieces of the 'First Light' series are Su Qingyuan's original creations, free of plagiarism, derivation, or any form of infringement. Should this prove false, I will retire from the design world permanently."
That final sentence landed with the weight of an oath.
The tide in the comments turned in an instant.
"Zhou Wanyin's staking her whole career on it? That's a heavy bet."
"I trust Sister Wanyin's eye. She wouldn't misjudge someone."
"Hold on—so it's this guy named Su Guoqiang slinging the mud?"
"Who's Su Guoqiang? Seems like he's that Su Yurou's dad?"
"God, this whole family is disgusting—they stole someone's work and then have the nerve to play victim?"
Looking at Zhou Wanyin's statement, Su Qingyuan felt her nose sting.
She sent Zhou Wanyin a message: Sister Wanyin, thank you.
Zhou Wanyin replied in a heartbeat: Thank me for what? Your work is genuine; I just told the truth. What about you—what are you planning to do next? Su Guoqiang won't take this lying down.
Su Qingyuan: I've arranged to meet him tomorrow afternoon, at the old Su family house. Lawyer Liu is coming with me. I plan to livestream the entire meeting.
Zhou Wanyin: You sure? If you livestream and something goes sideways, the whole internet's watching.
Su Qingyuan: I'm sure. If they want to make a scene, then let's make it the biggest one yet. Who's lying, who's doing wrong—let everyone see it with their own eyes.
Zhou Wanyin went quiet for a dozen seconds, then sent a voice message, her tone laced with a smile: "Su Qingyuan, I'm more and more convinced that signing you was the best thing I did all year. Want me to share the livestream link?"
Su Qingyuan: Yes, please. Thank you, Sister Wanyin.
Zhou Wanyin: No more thanks—go get ready. Tomorrow, I'll be waiting for your good news.

By the time Su Qingyuan got back to the studio, it was nearly dark.
Someone was standing at the door.
Lu Shiyan.
He was leaning against the osmanthus tree, a document envelope in hand. Seeing her approach, he straightened up, said nothing, and simply held the envelope out to her.
"What's this?"
"The evidence you wanted."
Su Qingyuan took it and opened it. Inside was a thick stack of documents—Su Guoqiang's complete transfer records with that PR company, the chat logs, and the PR company's internal emails, spelling it out in black and white: "Task: manufacture public opinion that Su Qingyuan plagiarized. Budget: fifty thousand. Goal: sink her first collection."
"These… how did you get them?"
"The PR company's boss is a supplier to one of our group's subsidiaries." Lu Shiyan's tone was flat, as if remarking that the weather was nice today. "I had someone make a phone call, and he handed over everything."
Su Qingyuan looked up at him. The evening light fell across his shoulders. His expression was placid, as if he'd done some trivial little thing not worth mentioning.
"Didn't you say you'd let me fight this on my own?"
"Letting you fight it yourself doesn't mean I won't hand you a weapon." Lu Shiyan lowered his eyes to her. "The evidence is yours to begin with. I just helped you gather it."
Su Qingyuan gripped the envelope and lowered her head, her voice a little muffled. "Lu Shiyan, can't you just not do this every single time?"
"Do what?"
"This… doing everything so perfectly. Making me feel like I'm capable, while also making me feel… like without you, I might not be able to pull it off."
A few seconds of quiet.
Lu Shiyan's voice came down from above her, very low, very soft: "Su Qingyuan, you could do it without me too. It would just be a little slower, a little more exhausting, a little more painful. I don't want you to hurt anymore."
The fragrance of the osmanthus grew especially rich in the dusk.
Su Qingyuan didn't look up. She was afraid that the moment she did, the thing welling up in her eyes would spill over.
"Two o'clock tomorrow afternoon, the old Su family house. I'll be livestreaming." She said it, her voice steady now. "Do you… want to come?"
"Do you want me there?"
"I do."
"Then I'll come." Lu Shiyan paused. "But I won't be on camera. I'll be off to the side, watching you."
Su Qingyuan finally raised her head and looked at him.
His eyes seemed especially deep in the twilight—holding lamplight, the evening glow, the shadow of the osmanthus tree, and her, too.
"Okay." She smiled.
He smiled too.
Faint, but lovely.

Two o'clock the next afternoon. The old Su family house.
Su Qingyuan stood before that familiar iron gate, not a ripple in her heart.
She had lived in this house for over a decade. Here she'd weathered the scoldings, the beatings, the countless cold stares. Here she'd been told to "let your sister have her way," to "be sensible," to "stop causing trouble for the family."
And it was here, too, that she'd been told: "You're not our flesh and blood. You should be grateful we raised you at all—don't go thinking you deserve better."
Su Qingyuan drew a deep breath and pushed the gate open.
A crowd stood in the courtyard.
Su Guoqiang, Liu Xiulan, a few of Su Yurou's uncles and aunts, and several neighbors from the street. The setup was elaborate, as if they were waiting to put someone on trial.
The moment they saw Su Qingyuan walk in, Su Guoqiang's face hardened, his voice climbing an octave above normal. "You still have the nerve to come back? Look at what you've done to your sister!"
Liu Xiulan dabbed at her tears beside him. "Yurou's been frail since she was little—how could you have the heart to send her to a place like that? She's still just a child…"
Expressionless, Su Qingyuan opened her phone, launched the livestream app, set up the angle, and spoke into the camera: "Hello, everyone. I'm Su Qingyuan. Today I've come to the old Su family house to settle some personal matters. Every conversation will be streamed live, start to finish—no editing, no pausing."
The viewer count in the livestream room shot up rapidly from a few dozen.
Zhou Wanyin had shared the link, and Lu Shiyan's PR team was quietly boosting the traffic behind the scenes. In less than five minutes, the number of live viewers broke a hundred thousand.
Su Guoqiang still had no idea what was happening, and kept on roaring: "What are you livestreaming for?! Haven't you shamed this family enough?!"
Su Qingyuan fixed the phone onto the flower rack in the courtyard, the lens capturing a full view of the entire yard.
"Mr. Su"—it was the first time she hadn't called him "Dad"—"you say I've ruined Su Yurou. Then let's get one thing straight first: how did Su Yurou end up locked away?"
"She filed a false police report, falsely accusing me of plagiarism. After investigating, the police found that she was the one who stole my design drafts, copied a key to my apartment, and fabricated evidence. Her detention is entirely of her own making. It has nothing to do with me."
Su Guoqiang's face went livid. "You—"
"Don't be in such a hurry, I'm not finished." Su Qingyuan took out the documents Lu Shiyan had given her and held them up to the camera. "This is evidence that Su Guoqiang—my adoptive father, that is—bribed marketing accounts to defame me. Transfer records, chat logs, the PR company's internal emails—all of it, right here."
The danmaku in the livestream room went wild.
"Holy crap, a father buying trolls to smear his own daughter?"
"Not his real father—his adoptive father, and the kind who plays favorites at that."
"This family is seriously disgusting, huh?"
"This girl's got guts! Calling them out on a live stream!"
When Su Guoqiang saw those documents, his face shifted from livid to ashen.
"You… where did you get those?!"
"Where I got them doesn't matter. What matters is that every bit of it is fact." Su Qingyuan put the documents away and looked at Su Guoqiang, her gaze as calm as a pool of still water. "Mr. Su, I didn't come here today to argue. I came to lay out my terms."
"First, you issue a public apology, admitting that the 'First Light' series is my original work, and admitting that you hired trolls to spread rumors."
"Second, you and Liu Xiulan sign an agreement dissolving the adoptive relationship. From now on, I have nothing to do with the two of you."
"Third, the apology Su Yurou owes me can wait until she's out. But what you two owe me gets paid back today."
Liu Xiulan suddenly shrieked: "Dissolve the relationship?! In your dreams! We raised you for over a decade, and now that your wings are strong you want to fly off? Let me tell you, don't think you'll be shorting us a single cent of support money!"
Su Qingyuan shot her a glance and spoke in an unhurried tone: "Ms. Liu, regarding the duty of support, I've consulted a lawyer. According to the law, if adoptive parents abuse or abandon their adopted child, or otherwise seriously harm the adopted child's interests, the adopted child may petition to dissolve the adoptive relationship—and is not obligated to provide support."
She pulled a stack of old photographs from her bag and held them up to the camera one by one.
"This is a photo of me at thirteen. The bruises on my body are from Liu Xiulan beating me with a coat hanger. This is my report card at fifteen—third in the entire grade—but they wouldn't let me go to high school, because 'there's no point in a girl studying that much.'"
"These are my pay stubs from eighteen to twenty-one—I worked as a designer at the shop the Su family ran, and my monthly wage was eight hundred yuan, while everyone else in the same position made four thousand."
"Is this enough to prove 'abuse' and 'harm to my interests'?"
The number of live viewers broke half a million.
The danmaku had erupted into a wall of curses.
"These aren't adoptive parents, these are mortal enemies."
"Abusing a minor—that's a crime!"
"This poor girl… my heart breaks for her…"
"Cut ties! She has to cut ties! Don't give them a single cent of support money!"
Liu Xiulan stared at the photographs, her face as white as paper, her lips trembling, unable to get a word out.
Su Guoqiang glared at Su Qingyuan, his eyes brimming with hatred. He suddenly let out a cold sneer: "You think that just because you've got money and power now, you can stamp us into the ground? Su Qingyuan, let me tell you—you've got the blood of some random wild man running through your veins. What makes you think you're anything decent?"
The air went suddenly still.
Su Qingyuan wasn't angry, wasn't agitated. She simply looked at him, calm.
"Are you finished?"
Su Guoqiang was only further enraged by her composure: "And what if I am? You think you can have me arrested?"
"No, I can't." Su Qingyuan turned, lifted the phone from the flower rack, and faced the camera. "Everyone, that's the end of today's livestream. The legal proceedings to follow, I'll announce publicly through my lawyer."
She ended the stream and turned to Lawyer Liu.
Lawyer Liu nodded and drew a set of three identical legal documents from her briefcase.
"Mr. Su Guoqiang, Ms. Liu Xiulan, this is the agreement to dissolve the adoptive relationship. You may choose to sign it voluntarily, or you may choose not to. If you don't sign, we will file suit with the court, attaching all the evidence of abuse, defamation, and copyright infringement. Criminal case and civil case, pursued together."
Su Guoqiang stared at the agreement, his hands shaking.
He couldn't read, but he knew what was written on it.
Sign it, and once his daughter Yurou got out, there'd be nothing left. No Su Qingyuan to use as a stepping stone, no source of income, nothing to lean on.
Don't sign it, and he might end up locked away. Abuse, defamation, copyright infringement—altogether enough to put him behind bars for several years.
He glanced at Liu Xiulan.
Liu Xiulan had stopped crying. Her eyes were hollow, like a husk drained of its soul.
"I…" Su Guoqiang's voice was hoarse. "I'll sign."
He picked up the pen and wrote out his name on the agreement, stroke by stroke.
Liu Xiulan signed after him.
Once she'd finished, Liu Xiulan suddenly lifted her head and looked at Su Qingyuan, tears in her eyes, along with some emotion too tangled to name: "Qingyuan… Mom is sorry."
Su Qingyuan looked at her and said nothing.
I'm sorry.
Those words came far too late.
Over a decade too late.
"Lawyer Liu," Su Qingyuan picked up the agreement, her voice very soft, "I'll leave the rest to you."
She turned and walked out through the iron gate of the old Su family house.
Outside, Lu Shiyan was leaning against the car, watching her.
He didn't ask "how did it go," nor did he say "you did well." He simply pulled open the car door and said one thing to her:
"Get in. I'm taking you to dinner."
Su Qingyuan looked at him and suddenly smiled.
And as she smiled, the tears fell.
Not tears of sorrow, but the kind that come from finally heaving off the mountain that had crushed you for more than a decade—tears of release.
Lu Shiyan didn't say "don't cry," nor did he hand her a tissue. He simply stood before her, quiet and patient, waiting for her to finish crying.
Only after she'd wiped her own tears dry did he say, "Let's go."
"Mm." Su Qingyuan sniffled and got into the car.
The car started up and pulled away from the old street she'd lived on for over a decade.
She didn't look back.
From today onward, she was no one's adopted daughter, no one's stepping stone, no one's punching bag to bully at will.
She was simply Su Qingyuan.
A jewelry designer.
Someone destined to shine, blindingly bright.

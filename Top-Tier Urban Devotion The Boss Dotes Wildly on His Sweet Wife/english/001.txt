Chapter 1 — Edge in the Rain

The instant Su Qingyuan pushed open the doors of the banquet hall, the entire room's clamor cut off as though someone had hit pause.

The brilliance of the crystal chandeliers poured down, falling on the washed-out white shirt she wore — and falling, too, on Su Yurou's carefully made-up face up on the stage. The gold trophy cradled in her stepsister's hands threw off a stabbing glint, and on the giant screen behind her, the design for "Fleeting Life" had been blown up to fill an entire wall.

That was her work. Every line, every choice of color, was three months of her own sleepless effort.

The hall was packed with the luminaries of the Capital's design scene — the front rows held the competition judges and sponsors, the middle was the media section, where a thicket of cameras and lenses pointed at the stage. Tonight was the awards gala of the Emerging Designers Competition, the design world's biggest event of the year in the Capital, broadcast live across every platform.

Su Yurou stood beneath the spotlight in a Chanel couture gown, her smile gentle and gracious. "...Thank you to the organizing committee for this recognition. The inspiration for 'Fleeting Life' came from my reflections on life itself — life is fleeting as a dream, and all we can do is hold true to our original heart..."

Before she could finish, the sound of the doors swinging open rang out, jarringly loud in the silence.

Su Qingyuan walked in.

Her appearance was like a drop of water splashed into boiling oil. People turned, one after another; some recognized her and began to whisper. In the front rows, Zhao Xiulan was the first to change color, shooting up from her seat, her expression a mix of shock and fury.

On the stage, Su Yurou saw her too. A flicker of panic crossed her eyes, but it was quickly suppressed, replaced by a look of innocence and wounded grievance.

"Sister?" There was just the right note of surprise in Su Yurou's voice. "What are you doing here? I... I sent you an invitation, but you said you weren't coming..."

Every gaze in the hall converged on Su Qingyuan.

She wore no gown — just a plain white shirt with black trousers, her hair carelessly tied back, utterly out of place in this setting. But her spine was ramrod straight, her steps unhurried, her gaze as calm as a deep, still pool.

Su Qingyuan walked to the foot of the stage, stopped, and lifted her head to look at her stepsister.

"Your trophy used my design draft. Your acceptance speech is my inspiration. Every second of applause you're soaking up on that stage should have been mine."

Her voice wasn't loud, yet it carried clearly through the entire banquet hall.

The room erupted.

The reporters' cameras swung toward her at once, flashes crackling into a wall of light. The live comment feed exploded instantly —

"Holy crap, what's going on? The sister's here to crash the party?"

"Didn't the trending topic a couple days ago say the sister was the one making false claims? When did it flip?"

"This sister is so badass — that aura is off the charts."

Su Yurou's eyes reddened in an instant, tears falling like beads off a broken string. "Sister, I know you've never liked me, because after Dad married Mom he had less attention to spare for you. I understand all of that... but 'Fleeting Life' is a piece I worked on painstakingly for most of a year. How can you say something like this..."

Her voice was choked and aggrieved, and paired with that pitiful, fragile face, the expressions of many in the room shifted at once from shock to sympathy.

A reporter pushed forward and asked, "Miss Su Qingyuan, you say the work is yours — do you have proof?"

Su Qingyuan glanced at the reporter, the corner of her mouth lifting slightly.

"I do."

She took her phone from her pocket, opened a recording, and pressed play.

Zhao Xiulan's voice came out of the phone, shrill and crystal-clear: "...Hand the original draft of 'Fleeting Life' over to Yurou. You won't be using it anyway. The family paid your entry fee for this competition — so what could you possibly use to prove anything to the committee? Listen to Mom, stop making trouble. When your sister does well, our whole family does well."

The hall fell dead silent.

Su Yurou's face went white.

Zhao Xiulan's face went green.

The recording played on, now in Su Qingyuan's voice: "This is a design I spent three months making myself. Why should I give it to her?"

Zhao Xiulan's voice turned even shriller: "You've eaten my food and drunk under my roof all these years to grow this big — what's the harm in letting your sister have one design? You ungrateful little wretch, I never should have let you set foot in this family in the first place!"

There the recording cut off abruptly.

Su Qingyuan put her phone away and looked toward the ashen-faced Su Yurou on the stage. "Do you need more proof? The submission IP records, the upload timeline, the creator information on the original files — I have all of it. Should I release them one by one?"

The air in the entire banquet hall seemed to have been sucked dry.

The comment feed had gone completely insane —

"Damn! She's even got a recording!!!"

"This stepmother is so disgusting — stealing one daughter's work for her own daughter?"

"The sister is so fierce, I love her!!!"

"Whoever just said the sister was making false claims, come out and take your beating!"

Su Yurou's lips trembled. Instinctively she looked toward Zhao Xiulan below the stage. Zhao Xiulan opened her mouth as if to say something, but the stares around her were too sharp, scraping over her like knives, and she couldn't get out a single word.

In the judges' seats up front, several judges' faces had already turned very ugly. Zhang Mingyuan, the director of the competition's organizing committee, stood up, his expression awkward and flustered. "Miss Su, this... this is something we'll still need to investigate. Please don't be upset, we'll definitely give you an explanation..."

"Director Zhang," Su Qingyuan said, looking him dead in the eye, "the submission's IP address traces back to the apartment registered under Su Yurou's name, and the upload time is two days later than the scan of my original handwritten draft. This data is recorded automatically by the server. It can't be altered."

Beads of sweat seeped out on Zhang Mingyuan's forehead.

At that very moment, the side door of the banquet hall was pushed open.

Every gaze in the room was drawn toward it.

A man walked in.

He wore a dark-gray bespoke suit, his frame strikingly tall — sharp brow bone, a high, straight nose, thin lips pressed lightly together. The whole of him was like a blade just drawn from its sheath, cold and dangerous. Only a single assistant trailed behind him, yet his presence felt like it came backed by an army a thousand strong.

The entire room went silent in an instant — quieter even than when Su Qingyuan had walked in.

When Zhang Mingyuan saw who had arrived, his expression changed drastically. He hurried out from behind the judges' table to greet him. "Mr. — Mr. Lu! What brings you here? We've had a small situation, but we'll have it sorted out right away..."

Lu Shiyan.

The sole heir of the Capital's Lu Group.

The young master of "Lu Global," the world's foremost reclusive conglomerate, holding half the kingdoms of finance, technology, luxury goods, and real estate in his grip. Twenty-eight years old, the business world called him "King Yama" — ruthless and absolute in his methods, leaving no room to spare, never once linked to any woman in a rumor, putting on gloves even before a handshake.

A man like that had no business, by any reckoning, showing up at the awards gala of a design competition.

Lu Shiyan didn't look at Zhang Mingyuan, didn't look at anyone, in fact.

His gaze cut through the crowd and landed on Su Qingyuan.

She stood in the shadow of the stage, her white shirt dotted with rainwater — it was raining outside. Everyone was watching her: some with sympathy, some gloating, some just there to enjoy the spectacle, the bigger the better. She stood there alone, facing an entire hall of strangers and the stepsister on the stage weeping like rain on pear blossoms.

She wasn't crying.

There was not a single tear in her eyes. Her spine was straight as a sword that would never break.

Lu Shiyan looked at her, and something in his chest felt as though it had been lightly plucked.

The feeling was strange. He had seen too many people, too many women — socialites, celebrities, daughters of great houses — not one of whom had ever made him spare a second glance. But this woman's eyes — he had looked only once, and he couldn't look away.

"Director Zhang." Lu Shiyan withdrew his gaze, his tone as light as a remark about the fine weather.

"Y-yes, here!" Zhang Mingyuan rushed over.

"A work plagiarized, the original creator's name stolen — this is the 'small situation' you spoke of?"

Cold sweat poured down Zhang Mingyuan's face. "Mr. Lu, we really are investigating. This came on so suddenly..."

"No need for your people to look into it." The chief assistant behind him, Qin Zhou, stepped forward and held out a tablet. "Submission IP records, upload timeline, edit logs — all retrieved in full. The submission's IP address traces back to the apartment registered under Su Yurou's name, and the upload time is two days later than the scan of Su Qingyuan's original handwritten draft. The creator information on the original files and the device serial numbers are all traceable. Furthermore, on Su Yurou's competition entry form, the signature in the 'advising instructor' field is a forgery."

When Qin Zhou finished, he stepped back, his expression professional and calm, as if he were reporting the day's itinerary.

The whole room sank into dead silence once more.

Su Yurou could no longer stay on her feet. She gripped the podium, her lips ghastly pale, tears still streaming — but this time not from grievance, from fear.

Lu Shiyan looked toward the judges' seats. "Do you need more proof?"

No one dared to speak.

The comment feed had already lagged the servers with its flood —

"Who is this man, I'm going to scream!!!"

"It's Lu Shiyan, sis! Lu Global! That Lu family, the richest in the world!!!"

"Why is he standing up for the sister? What's their relationship?"

"Whatever the relationship is, let me ship them first as a sign of respect!!!"

Su Yurou finally broke down. She flung the trophy aside, crouched on the stage, and wailed at the top of her lungs: "It wasn't me! My mom made me do it! She said Sister had lost her chance to compete anyway, so I might as well use the work! I didn't know it would turn out like this, boo hoo hoo..."

Zhao Xiulan's face collapsed entirely. She shrieked and charged onto the stage: "What nonsense are you spouting?! Shut your mouth!"

Mother and daughter started grappling on the stage, and the scene grew very ugly indeed.

The reporters mashed their shutters like mad, the flashes lighting the whole stage bright as day.

Su Qingyuan didn't spare them another glance.

She turned and walked toward the banquet hall doors.

Everything behind her — the wailing, the cursing, the apologizing, the camera flashes — had nothing more to do with her.

She had won, but it wasn't as satisfying as she'd imagined.

She just felt tired.

When she reached the doorway, a low voice called her back.

"Su Qingyuan."

She stopped and turned.

Lu Shiyan had, at some point, come up behind her, less than three steps away. The distance was too close — close enough that she could make out the grain of the black agate cufflink at his sleeve.

"Your work is very good," he said.

Six short words, and yet they made her nose sting.

It was the first time tonight that someone had genuinely, sincerely acknowledged her work — not out of sympathy, not out of guilt.

"...Thank you." Her voice came out a little hoarse.

Lu Shiyan looked at her reddened eyes, his throat bobbing slightly.

He very much wanted to reach out and wipe away the tear hanging at the corner of her eye, the one trembling on the verge of falling.

But he held himself back.

"I'll see you home," he said.

Not "would you like me to see you home," not "let me see you home," but "I'll see you home."

Su Qingyuan lifted her head and met those pitch-black eyes.

Those eyes were very deep, as if they held an entire universe. She couldn't see the bottom, but she saw her own reflection in them.

She didn't know this man.

But she was simply too tired — too tired to find the strength to refuse.

"...All right."

The corner of Lu Shiyan's mouth twitched ever so slightly.

The curve was so faint that, if Qin Zhou hadn't had a sharp eye, he wouldn't have noticed it at all.

Standing three steps away, Qin Zhou was already in inner turmoil.

He had been with Lu Shiyan for five years and had never once seen his boss say a single extra word to any woman. Once, at Paris Fashion Week, a supermodel had approached him to strike up a conversation at a gala; Lu Shiyan hadn't so much as glanced at her, just walked straight off.

And now this man was, of his own accord, saying "I'll see you home."

Qin Zhou drew a deep breath and quietly took out his phone, sending a message to the butler back home: Get things ready. Sir may be bringing someone back tonight.

After a moment's thought, he added another line: Prepare an extra set of women's toiletries. Size S, simple style.

The butler's reply was a single character: ?

Qin Zhou ignored him.

It was only when Su Qingyuan followed Lu Shiyan out of the hotel doors that she realized it was pouring outside.

Rain hammered down on the ground, kicking up a hazy white mist. The portico at the hotel entrance was crowded with people sheltering from the rain; taxis pulled away one after another, but there were never enough.

Su Qingyuan instinctively took half a step back, putting a little more distance between herself and Lu Shiyan.

She wasn't used to standing too close to anyone.

A black Maybach glided silently up to the entrance and stopped.

Qin Zhou trotted over to open the rear door and unfurled an enormous black umbrella.

Lu Shiyan didn't get in right away. Instead he turned his head and looked at Su Qingyuan.

"Get in."

Su Qingyuan hesitated for two seconds.

She didn't know this man. His name might sound impressive, and he might have just resolved a huge problem for her, but experience told her there was no such thing as kindness without a cause in this world. The lesson the Su family had taught her was that everyone who said "I'm doing this for your own good" had a bill behind it that they'd want her to pay back.

But she looked at the torrential rain outside, then looked at her own heel, the skin rubbed raw.

Forget it.

If she owed him, she owed him. She'd pay it back later.

She bent down and got into the car.

The interior of the Maybach was spacious, the leather seats so soft it was like sinking into a cloud. The car's sound system was off; there was only the muffled sound of rain on the roof, which, oddly, made her feel safe instead.

Lu Shiyan sat beside her, a person's width of space between them.

He didn't speak, didn't look at her. He kept his head down over his phone, the screen's blue light reflected on his sharply angled face.

Su Qingyuan leaned against the window, watching the rain trickle down the glass. The halos of the streetlights blurred and softened in the rain, like a watercolor painting soaked through with water.

It was very quiet inside the car — quiet enough that she could hear her own heartbeat.

The strange thing was, this quiet didn't make her feel awkward.

It was as if the two of them didn't need to say much of anything.

The Maybach drove smoothly through the rainy night, passing through the bustling city center and turning into ever narrower streets. The streetlights gradually grew sparse, the road more and more pitted; the tires rolled over standing water, throwing up splashes that struck the windows.

Qin Zhou glanced at Lu Shiyan's expression in the rearview mirror.

The boss didn't look happy.

Not because he was angry — because of this terrible road.

The young master of the Lu Group had probably never in his life traveled a road this rough.

"You can stop up ahead at the intersection," Su Qingyuan finally spoke.

Lu Shiyan raised his eyes and looked at her.

"Where do you live?" he asked.

"Up ahead, at the intersection."

"Su Qingyuan."

When he said her name, his voice was neither high nor low, as though it held a piece of warm jade.

"Where do you live?"

Su Qingyuan pressed her lips together and gave an address.

Qin Zhou silently entered the address into the navigation, and when he saw the photo of the destination, his composure nearly cracked.

It was an old residential complex, as old as old could be, the buildings looking to be more than thirty years old. The exterior paint was mottled and peeling, half the lights in the stairwells were probably broken, and the complex didn't even have a proper gate.

When the Maybach finally stopped beneath that building, Lu Shiyan looked through the window at the dilapidated residential block and fell silent for three seconds.

Su Qingyuan said thank you and pushed open the car door.

Cold wind, laced with rain, gusted in and lifted her hair.

She was about to dash out into the rain when an umbrella was held out in front of her.

Lu Shiyan's hand was sharply knuckled, gripping the black long-handled umbrella, his slender fingers closed around the handle, his posture as casual as if he were doing something trivial.

Su Qingyuan looked at the umbrella, then looked at him.

The rain was heavy; it was probably twenty steps from here to the stairwell entrance. She had no umbrella — her bag had been lost somewhere in the earlier chaos.

"I'll return it tomorrow." She took the umbrella.

"No need to return it."

"..."

Su Qingyuan didn't bother with any more polite refusals. She opened the umbrella and walked into the rain, her high heels stepping into the standing water, the splashes wetting the hems of her trousers.

Lu Shiyan sat in the car, watching her through the rain-blurred window. She didn't walk fast, but every step was steady. The umbrella was held perfectly straight, her spine perfectly upright.

When she reached the stairwell entrance, she paused.

Then she turned around and looked, from a distance, at the Maybach.

Through layer upon layer of rain, Lu Shiyan couldn't make out her expression, but he could feel that gaze land on him, light and thin as a feather.

She turned and went up the stairs.

The sound-activated lights in the stairwell came on floor by floor; her footsteps grew more and more distant, and the lights went out one by one.

It was only when the light on the sixth floor came on and then went dark that Lu Shiyan finally withdrew his gaze.

Qin Zhou asked cautiously, "Mr. Lu, where to?"

Lu Shiyan didn't answer right away.

He leaned back against the seat, his fingers unconsciously tapping the armrest twice. It was a small habit he had only when he was thinking.

"Look into who owns the property rights to that building."

Qin Zhou: "...You're thinking of?"

"Buy it. Renovate it."

Qin Zhou drew a deep breath.

He had been with Lu Shiyan for five years. He'd seen the boss take an entire commercial street with several hundred million, seen him force an opponent at the negotiating table to sign an unequal treaty, seen him cut an entire business division without batting an eye.

But he had never seen the boss buy a building for a woman he'd known for less than an hour.

"Yes, Mr. Lu." Without a flicker on his face, Qin Zhou took out his phone and sent a message to the legal department.

Then he sent another message to the butler: Not coming back tonight. Hold on to that set of women's toiletries for now.

The butler replied: So is he bringing someone or not?

Qin Zhou thought it over and replied: Soon.

He didn't actually know how long "soon" was, but he had a feeling.

That woman called Su Qingyuan was different from everyone before her.

By the time Su Qingyuan got back to her rented room, she was more than half soaked through.

The umbrella was large, but the wind was too strong, and the rain had been coming down at a slant.

The rented room was thirty square meters, the furniture old and worn, the wallpaper peeling up in a few places, the kitchen faucet that wouldn't shut off all the way, dripping endlessly. In one corner sat her drawing tools and materials, and the walls were covered with the design drafts she'd accumulated over the years.

She changed out of her wet clothes, toweled her hair dry, then sat on the edge of the bed and looked at the avalanche of messages on her phone.

The trending topics had already blown up.

#EmergingDesignersCompetitionPlagiarismScandal# had broken five hundred million views and a million comments. Su Yurou's social media account had been picked apart down to its bones; netizens had discovered that several of her past award-winning works bore a striking resemblance to practice drafts Su Qingyuan had once posted publicly.

Zhao Xiulan's phone number had been dug up by netizens and was reportedly already ringing off the hook.

Su Guoliang's silence was being read as "complicity," and the comment section under his company's official account was nothing but a torrent of abuse.

Su Qingyuan's social media account had gained five hundred thousand followers overnight.

She looked at those numbers, her expression calm.

This sort of heat came fast and went fast. Tomorrow everyone would forget her and go chasing the next hot topic. What she needed wasn't fleeting sympathy — it was genuine ability.

And just then, a cold, mechanical voice exploded in her mind.

[Ding — Omnipotent Counterattack System successfully bound. Detecting that the host is currently in a state of extreme adversity, triggering the Beginner's Gift Pack.]

Su Qingyuan sat bolt upright.

"...Who's there?"

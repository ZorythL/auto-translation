Chapter 7 — The Night of the Champion

After the gala ended, Su Qingyuan returned to her hotel and found more than a dozen unread messages on her phone.

The one at the very top was a video from Wen Ruan. She tapped it open—it was Wen Ruan in her hotel room, dancing wildly in front of the camera: "Qingyuan! That bit where you tore Su Yurou apart at the gala—I've already watched it seventeen times! It feels incredible every single time! I'm going to set it as my ringtone!"

Su Qingyuan smiled helplessly and scrolled down.

There were congratulations from university classmates, collaboration offers from peers in the design world, and job offers from a few headhunters. There was also one from a number she didn't recognize, containing a single line: "Ms. Su, I'm a reporter from Vogue magazine. I'd love to interview you. Please reply if it's convenient."

Su Qingyuan was in no hurry to reply to anyone.

She set down her phone and walked to the window.

Milan at night was beautiful, the ancient city giving off a gentle glow under the moonlight. From far away, the tolling of church bells drifted over, like some kind of ancient blessing.

She thought of what Philippe had said at the gala—"What you are seeing is not merely the work of a designer, but the beginning of an era."

The beginning of an era.

She had never once imagined that she would become the beginning of any "era." She was only a discarded daughter cast out of the Su family, a victim whose work had been stolen by her stepsister, an ordinary person who had endured countless nights in a cheap rented flat.

But now, she stood in the Milan night, holding the invitation to Milan Design Week in her hand, the global grand champion's trophy tucked in her pocket.

Everything was different now.

Her phone buzzed.

A message from Lu Shiyan: "Still up?"

Su Qingyuan replied with two words: "Can't sleep."

"Me neither."

"Why?"

"Thinking about things."

"Thinking about what?"

The reply paused for a few seconds. Then came a voice message.

Su Qingyuan tapped it open, and Lu Shiyan's low voice came out of the phone, carrying a hoarseness peculiar to the dead of night: "Thinking about you."

Su Qingyuan's fingers suddenly tightened.

She stared at those two words—"about you"—for a long time, long enough that the screen dimmed and lit up, lit up and dimmed again.

She wanted to reply with something, but she typed a few words then deleted them, deleted them then typed again, over and over, until in the end she only sent an emoji: a single period.

Lu Shiyan replied with a question mark.

Su Qingyuan didn't explain.

She didn't know how to explain. It wasn't that she didn't want to respond—it was that she wanted to respond too much, thought about it too much, and so didn't know what to say.

Her phone buzzed again.

"Su Qingyuan, open the door."

Her heartbeat suddenly sped up.

She went to the door and looked out through the peephole—the corridor was empty.

"Where are you?" she typed.

"At your door."

Su Qingyuan paused, looked through the peephole again, and still there was no one.

"You're lying to me."

"I'm not lying. I'm at your door."

Su Qingyuan took a deep breath and opened the door.

There was no one outside.

She looked down. On the floor sat a paper bag. Inside was a strawberry cheesecake, and a card.

The card read: "To celebrate your win. Not winning the competition—winning against yourself. —Lu."

Su Qingyuan crouched down, picked up the card, and smiled.

And as she smiled, the tears fell.

This man, staying up in the dead of night, all just to bring her a cake. He didn't knock, didn't ring the bell, only set the cake at her door and then left.

Because he knew she was very tired today and needed to rest.

Because he didn't want to disturb her.

Because he wanted her to know—he had always been there.

Su Qingyuan carried the cake into the room and set it on the table. She didn't eat it; instead she opened her phone and sent Lu Shiyan a message.

"Got the cake. Thank you."

"Did you eat it?"

"No. Can't bear to."

"Why?"

"Because it's the first cake anyone ever bought just for me."

After the message was sent, the other end was silent for a long while.

Long enough that Su Qingyuan thought he had fallen asleep.

Then the phone lit up.

"From now on I'll buy you one every day."

Su Qingyuan stared at those words, her heart as though brushed lightly by a feather—ticklish, soft, aching.

She held the phone, tossing and turning, reading it many times over, then pressed it against her chest and closed her eyes.

That night, she had a very long dream.

In the dream there was no Su family, no Zhao Xiulan, no Su Yurou, none of those memories that had pained her. There was only a boundless, endless sea, and a person standing at its shore.

She couldn't make out that person's face, but she knew who it was.

The next morning, Su Qingyuan was woken by a burst of knocking.

Groggy, she opened the door, and Wen Ruan rushed in like a gust of wind, holding up her phone, so excited her face was flushed red: "Qingyuan! Quick, look! You're in the Italian news!"

Su Qingyuan took the phone. On the screen was an article in Italian, the headline reading: "China's Genius Designer Su Qingyuan: A Flower Grown from the Ruins."

The article devoted a large portion to introducing her work, "Fracture and Rebirth," calling it "one of the most moving architectural designs of this century." At the end the article quoted a line from Philippe Delort: "Su Qingyuan is not China's future—she is the world's future."

Su Qingyuan looked at that line, her fingers trembling slightly.

The world's future.

That praise was too lofty—so lofty that she felt unworthy of it.

[Ding—self-doubt detected in the host. This System rules: the host's self-perception is skewed. Based on current data, the rating index of the host's work in the global design world already exceeds 97% of active designers. The host's future is not a question of "worthy or not," but a question of "how far she can go."]

Su Qingyuan said silently in her heart: "System, you're actually quite pleasant to listen to today."

[This System has always been pleasant to listen to. The host simply usually refuses to listen.]

Su Qingyuan couldn't help but laugh.

Wen Ruan watched her suddenly start laughing, utterly baffled: "What are you laughing at?"

"Nothing. Just thought of something happy."

"What thing?"

Su Qingyuan thought for a moment: "From now on, someone's going to buy me strawberry cake every day."

Wen Ruan froze, then suddenly clapped a hand over her mouth: "Oh my god oh my god oh my god!!! Did Lu Shiyan say that? He said he'd buy you cake every day from now on? Are you two together?!"

"No."

"Then why would he say something like that?"

Su Qingyuan didn't answer, but the corners of her mouth curved up and wouldn't come back down.

Wen Ruan looked at that expression, took a deep breath, and said in the tone of someone who'd been there: "Su Qingyuan, you're done for."

"What do you mean, done for?"

"You've fallen in love with him."

Su Qingyuan's smile froze for an instant.

Then she lowered her head, looked at her own fingers, and said softly: "...I know."

Wen Ruan stood there, stunned.

She had known Su Qingyuan for seven years and had never seen her like this—soft, fragile, no longer wrapping herself up tight and impenetrable.

"Qingyuan," Wen Ruan walked over and hugged her, "you're finally willing to let someone in."

Su Qingyuan buried her face in the crook of Wen Ruan's shoulder and said nothing.

But she nodded.

That afternoon, Su Qingyuan and Lu Shiyan went together to the main exhibition hall of Milan Design Week.

The hall was enormous, covering over twenty thousand square meters, one of the oldest exhibition spaces in Milan. Next March, her solo showcase would be held here, in the prime exhibition slot.

The slot was still empty for now—only one enormous white wall and an expanse of bare floor.

Su Qingyuan stood before that white wall and closed her eyes.

In her mind, images began to surface—not specific works, but an atmosphere, a feeling. Like the riot of color light cast on the ground as it passes through stained glass, like the ripples that rise when wind brushes across water, like the traces time leaves on ancient buildings.

She opened her eyes.

"I need four months," she said.

Lu Shiyan stood behind her, hands in his pockets, his gaze resting on her: "Is that enough?"

"It has to be enough whether it is or not." Su Qingyuan looked at the white wall. "This is the biggest chance of my life. I won't let it slip away."

"It's not a chance," Lu Shiyan said.

Su Qingyuan turned to look at him.

"It's the stage you deserve."

Su Qingyuan's heart gave a sudden leap.

She turned around to face the white wall and took a deep breath.

"Lu Shiyan."

"Mm."

"If I hold my exhibition here next March, will you come?"

"I will."

"Will you be in the front row?"

"I will."

"Will you..."

She stopped, not going on.

Lu Shiyan walked to her side and stood shoulder to shoulder with her, facing the white wall.

"Will I what?"

Su Qingyuan lowered her head and looked at the toes of her shoes.

"Will you... give me an answer after the exhibition is over?"

Lu Shiyan was silent for a few seconds.

"What answer?"

Su Qingyuan took a deep breath, raised her head, and looked into his eyes.

"Why are you so good to me?"

It was very quiet in the hall, only the breathing of the two of them and the faint sounds of distant construction.

Lu Shiyan looked at her, his gaze deep—so deep she couldn't see the bottom of it.

"You really don't know?" he asked.

Su Qingyuan's heart was beating so fast it felt like it would leap out of her chest.

"I want to hear you say it."

Lu Shiyan slowly raised his hand, the pad of his finger lightly brushing across her cheek, tucking a stray strand of hair at her temple behind her ear. His movement was very slow, very gentle, as though touching something precious and fragile, a piece of porcelain.

"Because you're Su Qingyuan."

Those same five words again.

But this time, Su Qingyuan heard more meaning in them.

Not "because you're Su Qingyuan, your work is good," not "because you're Su Qingyuan, you're worth investing in."

But rather—because you are you, so I like you.

Without any conditions attached.

Su Qingyuan's eyes reddened.

"Lu Shiyan."

"Mm."

"Do you like me?"

The hall was so quiet you could hear a heartbeat.

Lu Shiyan looked at her, the corner of his mouth curving slightly.

"I thought I'd already made it pretty obvious."

Su Qingyuan's tears finally fell, but she smiled, and her smile was beautiful.

"Then why didn't you say it sooner?"

"Because I didn't want to put pressure on you." Lu Shiyan's voice was low and gentle. "You'd just won the championship, just gotten your Milan solo show, just broken free of the Su family. Your life is just beginning—I didn't want to tie you down with feelings."

Su Qingyuan shook her head.

"You're not tying me down. You're holding me up."

Lu Shiyan's expression trembled faintly.

Su Qingyuan reached out and grabbed the hem of his clothes—that same little gesture again, tugging it, and then not letting go.

"Lu Shiyan, I want to be with you."

The hall was quiet for a very long time.

Long enough that Su Qingyuan thought he hadn't heard.

Then Lu Shiyan reached out and pulled her into his arms.

His embrace was tight, so tight that Su Qingyuan could feel his heartbeat—very fast, just as fast as her own.

"Su Qingyuan." His voice was a little hoarse.

"Mm."

"I've waited a long time for you to say that."

Su Qingyuan buried her face against his chest and listened to his heartbeat, beat after beat, strong and real.

She closed her eyes, and the corners of her mouth curved up.

From today on, she was no longer alone.

On the flight back home, Wen Ruan kept staring at Su Qingyuan.

Su Qingyuan got goosebumps under her gaze: "What are you doing?"

"You two are together."

Su Qingyuan didn't deny it.

"I knew it!" Wen Ruan nearly jumped out of her seat. "You two were alone in the exhibition hall for a whole hour, and when you came out your eyes were red, his ears were red, and Qin Zhou said his boss has never smiled like that before!"

Su Qingyuan glanced at Lu Shiyan, seated in the row ahead. He was reading documents, the lines of his profile sharp, his expression focused and serious, no different from usual.

But the tips of his ears were indeed red.

Su Qingyuan couldn't help but smile.

"We're together," she said, her voice very soft but her tone very certain.

Wen Ruan threw her arms around her and burst into messy tears: "Qingyuan, you finally have someone to love you... you finally don't have to be alone anymore... boo-hoo-hoo..."

Su Qingyuan's eyes reddened too from Wen Ruan's tears, and she patted her on the back: "Stop crying, your makeup's going to run."

"I don't care! My best friend is in love! I'm happy!"

Hearing the commotion behind him, Qin Zhou in the front row sneaked a glance back. Wen Ruan had cried her makeup off, and Su Qingyuan was smiling as she handed her tissues.

Lu Shiyan, without even lifting his head, turned a page of his document and said flatly: "Qin Zhou, turn around."

Qin Zhou turned back around at once.

But the corner of his mouth curved into a very small arc.

The plane passed through the clouds, and sunlight spilled in through the cabin window, falling onto Su Qingyuan's face.

She leaned back against her seat, gazing out at the boundless sea of clouds, calmer in her heart than she had ever been.

When the plane landed, Su Qingyuan turned on her phone and found that social media had already exploded.

The official Milan Design Week account had posted an announcement: "We warmly welcome Chinese designer Su Qingyuan, who will hold a solo showcase in the prime slot of the main exhibition hall next March. This is the first time this slot has been used by a designer under thirty, and the first time it has been used by a Chinese designer."

The comments section had already gone wild—

"Su Qingyuan! Pride of China!!!"

"Ahhhhh I hereby declare that from today on Su Qingyuan is my one and only goddess!!!"

"Whoever said before that she rose on a man's back—face slapped now, huh? Milan Design Week isn't run by Lu Shiyan, is it?"

"Boo-hoo-hoo I'm crying, Chinese design has finally stood up."

Su Qingyuan looked at these comments, the corners of her mouth curving up.

She didn't reply to a single one; instead she posted a new update.

Just one line.

"I'll be in Milan, waiting for you all to come."

The attached image was a photo of the Duomo, which she'd taken the night before.

After she posted it, Lu Shiyan reshared it in an instant, his caption just one word: "Waiting."

Wen Ruan reshared Lu Shiyan's reshare, with a long string of exclamation marks: "Ahhhhhhh can you two please stop being so sweet!!!"

Qin Zhou reshared Wen Ruan's reshare, captioned: "Shipping couples in the work group chat is prohibited."

Wen Ruan replied underneath: "Then leave the group."

Qin Zhou did not leave the group.

But he saved a screenshot of the exchange, with the note: First time getting roasted by Wen Ruan, a keepsake.

Su Qingyuan looked at all these interactions and laughed out loud.

Her life, from the day she met Lu Shiyan, had turned into a grand, sweeping revelry that everyone took part in.

And she, at last, was no longer the person who hid alone in a corner, drawing.

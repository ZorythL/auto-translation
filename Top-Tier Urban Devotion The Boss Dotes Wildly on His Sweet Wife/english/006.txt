The Light of Milan
On the day they set off for Milan, Su Qingyuan ran into someone she never expected to see at the airport.
Wen Ruan.
Her best friend, wearing a goose-yellow coat and pushing two enormous suitcases, stood at the check-in counter waving at her like a madwoman, an "you finally made it" expression plastered across her face.
Su Qingyuan stopped in her tracks and turned to Lu Shiyan beside her.
"Was this your doing?"
Lu Shiyan's expression didn't change. "She said she wanted to watch you accept your award. And do some shopping while she was at it."
"I haven't accepted any award yet."
"She said it's a head start on the celebration."
Su Qingyuan looked at those two oversized suitcases and went silent for a couple of seconds. This woman wasn't going to Milan to watch a competition—she was clearly going to Milan to run a personal-shopper racket.
Wen Ruan came trotting over and threw her arms around Su Qingyuan, her voice trembling with excitement. "Qingyuan! I'm sitting with you! I'm going to watch you become a legend in Milan!"
"The seats on the plane are assigned."
"I don't care, I'm sitting with you!" Wen Ruan turned to Lu Shiyan, her eyes carrying a distinct "if you say no I'll expose you" sort of threat.
Lu Shiyan glanced at Qin Zhou.
Qin Zhou immediately pulled out his phone, and three seconds later looked up. "Miss Wen's seat has been changed to the one next to Miss Su."
Wen Ruan nodded in satisfaction, leaned in close to Su Qingyuan's ear, and lowered her voice. "This man of yours—efficient, I'll give him that."
Su Qingyuan's ears went faintly red.
"He's not 'my man' yet," she corrected quietly.
Wen Ruan's eyes went wide. "You still haven't made it official? He's done all this for you—bought you a building, cleared your path, helped you start a company, flown to Milan with you—and you two still aren't together? Su Qingyuan, is something wrong with you?"
Su Qingyuan took a deep breath and decided she was not going to discuss the progress of her love life in the middle of an airport terminal.
After they boarded, Wen Ruan did indeed sit next to Su Qingyuan, while Lu Shiyan sat in the row ahead. Qin Zhou sat beside Lu Shiyan, spine ramrod-straight the whole time, glancing back at the situation in the rear row every now and then like a dutiful sentry.
Once the plane took off, Wen Ruan grabbed Su Qingyuan's arm and dropped her voice. "Seriously, what exactly is going on between you two? What do you actually feel for him?"
Su Qingyuan looked out at the cloud banks receding into the distance and was quiet for a while.
"I don't know how to describe it," she said. "It's just… before, I thought the world was a cold place. So cold I had to wrap myself up tight just to survive. But after he showed up, it didn't seem so cold anymore."
Wen Ruan's eyes reddened.
"Qingyuan, you've finally met the right person."
Su Qingyuan didn't deny it.
Above the clouds beyond the window, the sunlight was almost absurdly brilliant.
At two in the afternoon, Milan time, the plane touched down at Malpensa Airport.
Milan in November was crisp and damp. The sky was a pale grayish blue, and the silhouettes of the ancient buildings drifted in and out of the thin mist, like an oil painting steeped in the passage of years.
Lu Shiyan had arranged cars in advance—two of them: one for him and Su Qingyuan, one for Wen Ruan and Qin Zhou.
Wen Ruan protested. "Why do I have to ride with Qin Zhou? I want to ride with Qingyuan!"
Qin Zhou's face was expressionless. "Miss Wen, the back seat of that car only has two places. Are you sure you want to squeeze in with Mr. Lu?"
Wen Ruan took one look at Lu Shiyan's keep-your-distance aura and promptly gave up.
"Fine. Qin Zhou, you can fill me in on Mr. Lu's gossip on the way."
Qin Zhou's expression didn't budge an inch. "I don't have any gossip."
"Liar."
"I don't."
"Then why are your ears red?"
Qin Zhou silently started the car.
Watching the back-and-forth between Wen Ruan and Qin Zhou in the car behind through her window, Su Qingyuan couldn't help laughing.
"Qin Zhou seems pretty scared of Wen Ruan," she said.
Lu Shiyan glanced at the rearview mirror, his tone flat. "He's scared of anyone who talks a lot."
"And you? What are you scared of?"
Lu Shiyan turned his head and looked at her.
"I'm scared of you crying."
Su Qingyuan's heart suddenly raced.
She looked away, turning toward the window and pretending to take in the scenery. But her fingers curled slightly against her knee, the knuckles going white.
This man was always saying things like this without any warning, leaving her utterly defenseless.
The car wound through the streets of Milan and stopped before a nineteenth-century building. It was a private design club, open only to the world's top designers, its membership including the Zaha Hadid estate foundation, Rem Koolhaas, and Philippe Delort himself.
The doorman wore a tailcoat, and when he pulled the door open he gave a slight bow, his bearing as graceful as a dancer mid-step in some ancient court dance.
Wen Ruan climbed out of the car behind them, and when she saw the building she sucked in a sharp breath. "This place… I feel like I'd get charged just for breathing."
Qin Zhou stood beside her and said, deadpan, "Breathing's free. But staining the carpet means paying for it."
Wen Ruan shot him a glare.
Su Qingyuan drew a deep breath and stepped through the door.
The corridor was long, and the walls on both sides were hung with design drafts—a sketch of Mies van der Rohe's Barcelona Pavilion, the façade of Le Corbusier's Notre-Dame du Haut chapel, the original works of a few contemporary masters she couldn't place. Every single one was an original. Every single one was priceless.
Su Qingyuan's steps slowed.
Not out of nervousness, but because the kind of vitality these drawings gave off—a vitality that reached across time—filled her with awe.
Lu Shiyan walked at her side, without hurrying her.
He understood this feeling.
At the end of the corridor was a heavy wooden door, with no nameplate, only a brass handle polished to a shine by countless hands.
Lu Shiyan pushed it open.
Behind the door was a spacious reception room. A fire burned in the hearth, the logs crackling. Several large architectural photography pieces hung on the walls, and the light beyond the window spilled in through white gauze curtains, bathing the whole room in a soft glow.
By the window, an old man with a head of white hair was bent over something, studying it.
At the sound of the door, he raised his head.
Philippe Delort. Seventy-three years old, a titan in the global fields of architecture and design, chairman of the jury for the International Young Designers Competition. His hair had gone completely white, but his eyes were astonishingly bright, like two dark gems buffed to a gleam.
His gaze slid past Lu Shiyan and came to rest on Su Qingyuan.
He looked at her for a long time.
So long that Su Qingyuan felt her heart was about to stop.
Then Philippe stood up.
He was not tall, his back slightly stooped, and he wore a dark gray wool sweater, a pair of khaki casual trousers, and a worn pair of brown leather shoes. He looked less like an emperor of the design world and more like a kindly old grandfather from next door.
He walked over to Su Qingyuan and held out his hand.
"Su," he said. Not Miss Su, not Su Qingyuan—just "Su." A single syllable, clean and crisp, as if they had known each other a very long time.
Su Qingyuan took his hand.
The old man's hand was thin, the joints pronounced, but warm and strong.
"Mr. Delort, it's an honor."
Philippe didn't let go; instead he watched her with those astonishingly bright eyes.
"Do you know why I wanted to meet you?" he said in English, heavily accented with French.
Su Qingyuan thought about it. "Because of my work?"
"No." Philippe released her hand, turned, and picked up a stack of drawings from the table—her very own "Fracture and Rebirth" series.
He flipped to the first one, the overall rendering she had drawn. A building growing up out of the ruins, old brick walls interwoven with new steel framework, like a giant rising again from its own wounds.
"Because this work," Philippe said, "reminded me of why I chose this profession."
Su Qingyuan froze.
Philippe set the drawings down on the table, turned, and faced the flames leaping in the hearth. His voice grew slow and far-off, as though recounting a story from long ago.
"When I was eighteen, my hometown was struck by a great earthquake. The whole city was reduced to rubble. Everyone was in despair, certain that it was all over. But three months later, I saw an old man on the ruins. Beside a collapsed house, he was using bricks from the wreckage to rebuild the wall, one brick at a time."
Philippe turned his head and looked at Su Qingyuan.
"I asked him, why bother building? It's all destroyed anyway. He said, it's precisely because it's destroyed that we must build. The ruins are not the end. They are the beginning."
The old man's voice trembled slightly.
"In that moment I understood: architecture is not about putting up buildings. It is about planting hope on the ruins. I chose this profession not because I loved design, but because I wanted to become the man laying bricks on the ruins."
He walked back over to Su Qingyuan and picked up her drawings.
"Su, your work let me see that old man. Not because of technique, not because of talent, but because there's something in your work—you are laying bricks on the ruins."
Su Qingyuan's eyes reddened.
Philippe set the drawings down and patted her shoulder.
"Technique can be learned, talent can be trained, but a heart that lays bricks on the ruins cannot be learned or trained. It is given by a person's own life. Su, you must have been through a great many terrible things in your past."
Su Qingyuan's tears finally fell.
This French old man she had never met before had seen straight through her in three minutes. Seen through twenty-three years of grievance, seen through every one of her scars, seen through all the words she had hidden behind those lines and never dared to speak aloud.
"Those terrible things did not break you," Philippe said softly. "They became the bricks you build with. Su, that is your gift. Not the gift of drawing—the gift of being alive."
Wen Ruan stood in the doorway, already dissolved into a sobbing mess.
Qin Zhou handed her a pack of tissues, expressionless. "Miss Wen, your makeup is running."
Wen Ruan blew her nose and said, "Shut up."
Lu Shiyan stood off to one side, watching Su Qingyuan cry, his fingers tightening slightly and then loosening.
He badly wanted to walk over and wipe her tears away.
But he knew that, in this moment, she didn't need him. What she needed was an old man's recognition of someone younger, a master's blessing upon someone just starting out.
So he stood there, watching quietly.
Su Qingyuan drew a deep breath, swiped clumsily at her tears with her sleeve, and looked at Philippe, her voice a little hoarse. "Mr. Delort, thank you."
Philippe smiled, a smile as guileless as a child's. "Don't thank me. You should thank yourself. Because you didn't lie down in the ruins. You stood up."
He took another folder from the table and handed it to Su Qingyuan.
"Open it and see."
Su Qingyuan opened the folder. Inside was a formal letter of invitation.
"Milan Design Week—a solo exhibition. Run dates: March of next year. Venue: the main pavilion of Milan Design Week, the premier booth."
Su Qingyuan's breath caught for a beat.
Milan Design Week, the ultimate stage for designers the world over. The premier booth in the main pavilion was never given to newcomers. In all of history, no designer from China had ever claimed that booth before the age of thirty.
And now, that letter of invitation was in her hands.
"Mr. Delort, this…"
"It wasn't my decision," Philippe said, waving a hand. "It was the decision of the Milan Design Week organizing committee. After your work was passed around the jury, everyone agreed unanimously. Su, you no longer need anyone to give you opportunities. Your work is the best calling card there is."
Su Qingyuan held that invitation, her fingers trembling faintly.
Behind them, Wen Ruan asked Qin Zhou under her breath, "Is that booth a big deal?"
In a voice only the two of them could hear, Qin Zhou said, "Last year that booth went to a Pritzker Prize laureate. The year before, to OMA. The year before that, to Zaha Hadid's firm."
Wen Ruan sucked in a sharp breath.
"So doesn't that mean Qingyuan just… vaulted straight to the top?"
Qin Zhou glanced at her. "Miss Su didn't vault to the top. She was always up there—she just got seen."
Wen Ruan paused, then said quietly, "Qin Zhou, you don't say much most of the time, but when you do, it's actually pretty profound."
Qin Zhou turned his head away, expressionless, but the tips of his ears went red.
Philippe kept Su Qingyuan and Lu Shiyan in the reception room talking for a long while.
He asked Su Qingyuan in detail about her design philosophy, her creative process, her plans for the future. He was not the sort of master who looked down from on high; he was more like an old student with a voracious thirst for knowledge, every question probing deep, very deep—so deep that Su Qingyuan had to think it over carefully before she could answer.
And each of her answers made his eyes light up.
At last, Philippe leaned back against the sofa and let out a long breath.
"Lu," he said, looking at Lu Shiyan, "this girl—you'd best take good care of her. She will be the greatest designer of this century. Whoever loses her, all of humanity will hate him for it."
Lu Shiyan looked at Su Qingyuan, the corner of his mouth curving up slightly.
"I know."
By the time they left the club, night had fallen over Milan.
The old streets gave off an amber glow under the streetlamps. In the distance, the spires of the Milan Duomo stood out clearly against the night sky, like so many arrows pointing toward the heavens.
Wen Ruan and Qin Zhou walked ahead, talking about who-knew-what; Wen Ruan was laughing happily, and Qin Zhou, still expressionless, walked with a noticeably lighter step.
Su Qingyuan and Lu Shiyan walked behind, half a pace apart.
The night wind was a little chilly, and Su Qingyuan hunched her shoulders.
The next second, a coat was draped over them.
The coat still carried Lu Shiyan's warmth, and that scent of his—clean to the point of crispness.
"Aren't you cold?" Su Qingyuan asked.
"No."
Su Qingyuan glanced at his upper body, clad in nothing but a thin sweater, and said nothing.
He was definitely cold—but he would never admit it.
She pulled the coat tighter around herself and walked on with her head down. The streetlamps stretched their two shadows long across the ancient cobblestones, like two trees whose roots had grown intertwined.
"Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"When did you start thinking my work was good?"
Lu Shiyan thought about it.
"From the first glance."
"The first glance? At the awards banquet?"
"At the hotel entrance." Lu Shiyan's voice was low and steady. "When you walked out of the banquet hall, you weren't holding anything in your hands, but I saw light in your eyes. That light—it wasn't anger, it wasn't grievance. It was defiance. A person who refuses to lose can't possibly fail to create something good."
Su Qingyuan stopped and turned to look at him.
The lamplight fell across his face, softening his sharp features by a few degrees. His eyes were deep, and in them were reflected her own image and a whole sky full of stars.
"You refuse to lose," he said, "so I refuse to lose too. I'm going to make the whole world see you."
Su Qingyuan's heart gave a sudden lurch.
The night wind passed between the two of them, carrying the cool of Milan's late autumn and the toll of a distant church bell.
Su Qingyuan reached out and lightly touched the back of his hand.
Just a touch—and then she meant to pull it back.
But Lu Shiyan's hand was faster, turning to clasp hers.
His palm was large, dry and warm, wrapping her whole hand within it.
Su Qingyuan lowered her head, looked at their joined hands, and felt the corners of her mouth lift.
She didn't pull away.
And Lu Shiyan didn't let go.
The two of them walked hand in hand like that through the ancient streets of Milan. At some point Wen Ruan up ahead glanced back, then suddenly yanked at Qin Zhou's sleeve, shriek-whispering, "They're holding hands they're holding hands they're holding hands!!! Aaaaahhh!!!"
The yank made Qin Zhou stumble, and he steadied himself, expressionless. "Miss Wen, please mind your volume."
"I don't care! I've waited so long for some sugar, and now that I've finally got it, how am I supposed to not care?!" Wen Ruan whipped out her phone to take a picture, only for Qin Zhou to press her hand down.
"Mr. Lu won't allow it."
"Then I won't post it—I'll just keep it for myself!"
Qin Zhou hesitated, then let go of her hand.
Wen Ruan snapped a photo—in the distance, Lu Shiyan and Su Qingyuan walking hand in hand beneath the streetlamps, neither of them looking at the camera, yet the image was more moving than any posed shot could ever be.
Looking at the photo, Wen Ruan's eyes reddened again.
"Qin Zhou."
"Mm."
"Do you think they'll always stay like this?"
Qin Zhou glanced at those two figures walking side by side in the distance, and for once let an expression cross his face that wasn't blank.
"They will."
The next day, when Su Qingyuan woke in her hotel room, dozens of new messages had appeared on her phone.
Most of them were congratulations—somehow, someone had leaked the news of the Milan Design Week invitation, and the domestic media had already begun reporting on it.
But one message was different.
It came from an unknown number, and contained a single line: "Su Qingyuan, you think going to Milan means you've won? Dream on."
Su Qingyuan looked at the message and let out a cold laugh.
She didn't reply. Instead she screenshotted the number and sent it straight to Qin Zhou.
"Find out whose number this is."
Qin Zhou replied instantly. "Got it. Miss Su, Mr. Lu asked me to pass along that at tonight's banquet, you may run into a few people."
"Who?"
"Su Guoliang and Su Yurou."
Su Qingyuan's fingers paused.
"They came to Milan?"
"Yes. Su Yurou pulled some strings to get a ticket to tonight's banquet. The banquet is a warm-up event for Milan Design Week, hosted by the Italian Designers Association. Su Yurou applied as an 'independent designer.'"
Su Qingyuan set down her phone and walked to the window.
A Milan morning—sunlight spilling through the ancient buildings onto the streets, a sea of gold.
Su Yurou had chased her all the way to Milan.
She thought she could still do what she'd always done—snatch away the things that belonged to Su Qingyuan.
Looking at her own reflection in the glass, Su Qingyuan let the corners of her mouth lift slightly.
Things were different now.
This time, she would never let anyone snatch anything away from her again.
She turned and walked toward the dressing room, beginning to pick out a dress for tonight's banquet.
Since Su Yurou wanted to come, then she would let her stepsister get a good, long look at what a real designer was.
Outside the window, the sunlight grew brighter and brighter; a new day in Milan had begun.
Su Qingyuan looked at herself in the mirror, and in her eyes was a light she had never had before.
It wasn't anger, and it wasn't a thirst for revenge.
It was a kind of certainty—she knew her own ability, knew her own worth, knew that she would never again be trampled underfoot by anyone.
Lu Shiyan sent a message: "Are you ready?"
Su Qingyuan replied with a single word: "Yes."
"I'm waiting downstairs."
Su Qingyuan picked up her clutch and walked out of the room.
The elevator descended all the way down, and when it reached the ground floor, the doors opened.
Lu Shiyan stood in the lobby, wearing a black bespoke suit, no tie, the collar of his shirt slightly open, looking for all the world as though he'd just stepped off the runway at Milan Fashion Week.
When he saw Su Qingyuan, his gaze held for an instant.
Su Qingyuan wore a hazy-blue velvet gown, the hem flaring softly at her ankles like a flower blooming upside down. Her hair was pinned up, baring a slender, fair stretch of neck. She rarely wore makeup, and even today she had only lightly traced her brows and applied a thin layer of lipstick.
But it was precisely that understatement that made her look like a beauty who had stepped out of a classical oil painting.
Lu Shiyan's gaze rested on her for three seconds, then moved away.
"Let's go," he said.
Su Qingyuan walked to his side, and the two of them headed for the door shoulder to shoulder.
Qin Zhou was already waiting in the car.
The car drove toward the venue of the banquet—one of Milan's oldest palaces, built in the fifteenth century and now one of the core venues of Milan Design Week.
Watching the streetscape rush backward past the window, Su Qingyuan suddenly spoke. "Lu Shiyan."
"Mm."
"Su Yurou is here."
"I know."
"You're not going to ask what I plan to do?"
Lu Shiyan turned his head and looked at her.
"Do whatever you want to do," he said. "If the sky falls, I'll hold it up."
Su Qingyuan's heart skipped a beat.
She lowered her head and looked at her hands resting on her knees. Lu Shiyan's hand reached over, settled on the back of hers, and gave it a light squeeze.
"Don't be afraid," he said.
"I'm not afraid." Su Qingyuan lifted her head, a fire burning in her eyes. "I'm just thinking about how to teach her a lesson."
The corner of Lu Shiyan's mouth curved up.
"Then teach her a lesson."
The car pulled up at the palace gates.
A red carpet ran from the entrance all the way out to the street, flanked on both sides by media reporters with cameras raised. When Su Qingyuan stepped out of the car, the flashbulbs went off in a blaze.
She stood on the red carpet and took a deep breath.
Then she saw Su Yurou.
Her stepsister, in a crimson-red gown, stood at the other end of the red carpet, staring fixedly at her. Su Yurou's face was thick with makeup, but it couldn't hide the dark circles under her eyes; she looked haggard and strung tight.
Su Qingyuan looked at Su Yurou and let the corners of her mouth lift slightly.
Then she shifted her gaze away and slipped her arm through Lu Shiyan's.
The two of them walked up the red carpet together, and the flashbulbs grew even more frenzied.
Su Yurou stood where she was, her fingers curling into fists.
Her nails dug deep into her palms, hurting so much her whole body shook.
But she didn't leave.
She wouldn't leave.
She was going to watch Su Qingyuan—and then make Su Qingyuan lose.
The banquet lights were as bright as daylight.
The instant Su Qingyuan walked into the hall, a great many eyes turned to her. Some recognized her as the creator of "Fracture and Rebirth," some recognized her as Lu Shiyan's companion, and still others murmured about the grudges between her and Su Yurou.
Su Qingyuan was unmoved.
Holding a glass of champagne, she stood in a corner of the hall and quietly took in the whole room.
"Nervous?" Lu Shiyan stood beside her and asked under his breath.
"Not nervous." Su Qingyuan took a sip of champagne. "I'm just wondering when Su Yurou is going to make her move."
The words had barely left her mouth when Su Yurou appeared.
She emerged from the crowd and walked straight toward Su Qingyuan. Her high heels clicked sharply against the marble floor, every step carrying a near-obsessive resolve.
Su Yurou stopped in front of Su Qingyuan.
"Sister," she said, her voice soft and sweet as if dipped in honey, but the hatred at the bottom of her eyes too thick to dissolve.
Su Qingyuan looked at her and said nothing.
"Sister, I came today to apologize to you." Su Yurou raised her voice, loud enough for the people around them to hear. "What happened before was my fault, it was Mom's fault, it was me wanting so badly to beat you… I know I was wrong. Can you forgive me?"
Her eyes reddened, tears welling and swirling, looking pitiful and helpless.
People around them began to whisper.
"This younger sister is so pitiable…"
"Yeah, the older one looks so cold…"
Su Qingyuan watched Su Yurou's performance, and the corner of her mouth curved slightly.
She waited until Su Yurou had finished, then spoke, unhurried. "Su Yurou, you say you were wrong. So tell me—what exactly were you wrong about?"
Su Yurou faltered.
"I… I shouldn't have stolen your work…"
"And?"
"I shouldn't have… shouldn't have reported you for plagiarism…"
"And?"
Su Yurou's expression began to stiffen.
"I shouldn't have… shouldn't have let Mom throw you out of the house…"
Su Qingyuan nodded.
"Good. You've admitted to all of that. So how do you intend to make it right?"
Su Yurou opened her mouth, but not a single word would come out.
She had thought that all she had to do was apologize and Su Qingyuan would forgive her. She had thought that under the gaze of the entire room, Su Qingyuan wouldn't dare withhold face from her.
But she was wrong.
Su Qingyuan lifted her champagne glass, gave it a gentle swirl, her gaze as calm as if she were looking at a stranger.
"Su Yurou, listen carefully. I don't accept your apology. Not because I'm not magnanimous enough, but because your apology isn't sincere. You only want to use your tears to force me into forgiving you, so that you can keep using me, keep stepping on me to climb higher."
Su Qingyuan's voice was not loud, but every word came out crisp and clear.
"In the past I yielded to you because I thought we were family. But you and your mother told me through your actions—you never once treated me as family. And if we aren't family, then on what grounds should I yield to you?"
The whole room fell silent.
Su Yurou's face flushed a deep red, and the tears finally fell for real—but this time not from grievance. From hatred.
"Su Qingyuan! Who do you think you are? You think winning a championship makes you something special? You're nothing but a woman riding on a man's coattails!"
Su Qingyuan laughed.
"I'm riding on a man's coattails? Su Yurou, who drew your work for you? Who bought your competition entry for you? Who wrote your report letter for you? You're the one who relies on others more than anyone in the world—what right do you have to say that about me?"
Su Yurou's face went utterly white.
The people around them began to murmur to one another, and the looks they cast at Su Yurou turned from sympathy to contempt.
"So she was the one who wrote the report letter…"
"How could a person be like this…"
"Putting on that pitiful act just now—disgusting."
Su Yurou stood frozen in place, trembling from head to toe.
She wanted to say something, but found that every excuse was pale and powerless in the face of the facts.
At last she stamped her foot, turned, and fled.
Her high heels struck the marble floor in a frantic, panicked rhythm, like a routed soldier.
Su Qingyuan watched her figure vanish out the door, lifted her champagne glass, and took a light sip.
No smugness, no rush of triumph.
Only a faint, dust-settled calm.
"Well done." Lu Shiyan's voice came from beside her.
Su Qingyuan turned her head and looked into his eyes.
"You don't think I went too far?"
"No."
"Why not?"
"Because she deserved it." Lu Shiyan lowered his head, his gaze settling on her eyes. "And besides, you were already very gentle with her. If it had been me, she'd have come out of it a hundred times worse."
Su Qingyuan couldn't help laughing.
"So you're saying I was too gentle?"
"I'm not complaining," Lu Shiyan said. "I'm relieved. Relieved that you didn't turn into someone like her."
Su Qingyuan went still for a moment, then lowered her head and looked at the amber champagne in her glass.
She hadn't turned into someone like Su Yurou.
Not because of any inborn kindness, but because—she had seen the finest person in this world, and so she knew that becoming a better person mattered more than winning.
Halfway through the banquet, Philippe Delort took the stage to give a few words.
He stood at the podium, his gaze sweeping across the room before finally coming to rest on Su Qingyuan.
"Tonight, I would like to introduce all of you to a young Chinese designer," he said. "Her name is Su Qingyuan. Her work, 'Fracture and Rebirth,' is one of the most powerful pieces I have ever seen. This coming March, she will hold a solo exhibition at Milan Design Week. I hope every one of you here will go and see it. Because what you will be seeing is not merely one designer's work—it is the beginning of an era."
The whole room erupted in thunderous applause.
Su Qingyuan stood among the crowd, feeling the gazes converging on her from all directions. Admiration, envy, curiosity—and jealousy, too.
But there was only one gaze she cared about.
She turned to look at Lu Shiyan.
He was looking at her, the faintest, lightest of smiles at the corner of his mouth.
Su Qingyuan smiled too.
The two of their gazes met across the crowd, like two rivers finally converging, never to part again.
